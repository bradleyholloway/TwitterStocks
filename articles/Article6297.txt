 Nissan Is Seeking U.S. Exemption On Fuel Efficiency   By Jeffrey Ball and Stephen Power, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 2004  At stake, in an election year, could be jobs in some Southern states that, because of quirks in U.S. fuel-economy regulations, depend on Nissan's ability to classify its Mexican-made Sentra compact cars as "imported" vehicles, though many components for those cars are made in the U.S. The Sentra is relatively fuel-efficient, which is valuable to Nissan because it needs, for purposes of the fuel-economy law, to offset other of its imported cars that are thirstier. The fuel-economy rule divides an auto maker's cars into both domestic and imported fleets, requiring each one to average 27.5 miles per gallon.
