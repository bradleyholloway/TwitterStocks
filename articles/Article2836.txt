 Treasurys stage early rally on news of accord on Iraqi oil but end flat as price of crude jumps   By McGee, Suzanne; Doherty, Jaqueline, The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 1996  Treasury prices staged an early, half-hearted rally on May 20, 1996 as the UN and Iraq finally agreed on a deal that will allow the Iraqi government to sell some crude oil to pay for food, medical supplies and humanitarian aid. The 30-year Treasury bond ended the day virtually unchanged at 89 11/32, with the yield remaining flat at about 6.83%.
