 What's Ahead -- People & Events to Watch in the Coming Week   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 21, 2005  Consumer sentiment continues to crawl back from Katrina's jolt. The final November University of Michigan consumer sentiment index is expected to settle in the range of 80.0 to 82.0, from a preliminary reading of 79.9, and 74.2 in October. Even so, it will be more than 20% below its cyclical high of 103.8 in January 2004. The index is an important indicator for holiday shopping, as spending tends to track sentiment.  International Atomic Energy Agency's 35-nation board meets in Vienna on Iran's nuclear program, and may refer Tehran to the U.N. Security Council, which could impose sanctions.
