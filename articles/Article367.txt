 Ahead of the Tape   By By Jesse Eisinger, The Wall Street Journal,  May 23, 2003  Even if they didn't object to the sunset provisions, fans of the dividend-tax cut were disappointed the capital-gains tax will be reduced in line with the dividend tax. Rich Bernstein, strategist for Merrill Lynch, wanted a dividend-tax cut to help wring out continuing rampant speculation. "I wanted to have something to calm down the stock market," he says. Having a dividend tax that is lower than the capital-gains tax would encourage aversion to risk, he contends.
