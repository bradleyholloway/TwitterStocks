To the Editor:  Floyd Norris (Editorial Observer, May 9) says the bankruptcy reform bill passed by the House spares the wealthy and creates a bigger burden for poor and middle-class bankruptcy filers. In fact, the bill has the opposite effect. It simply requires that those who can afford to repay some of their debt, as determined by a legal standard, be required to do so. Those whose income falls below the regional median level or those who have undue hardships would not be subject to any change from the current system.  To see the full article, subscribe here.
