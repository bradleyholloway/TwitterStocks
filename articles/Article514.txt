 Corporate News: India Bars Mine in Big Ruling   By Anand, Geeta; Bhattacharya, Prasenjit, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 2010  Much of India's untapped mineral resources lie in the forests occupied by tribal groups who are believed to be India's equivalent of Australia's aboriginal people or Native Americans in the U.S. The Dongria Kondh are among India's 85 million people who live in agriculture-based societies that rely primarily on the barter system, lacking what other parts of India and the world consider basic amenities such as electricity, running water and medical care.
