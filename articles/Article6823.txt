 Finance Firms Set to Increase Outlays for IT   By Bulkeley, William M, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 10, 2008  Banks and brokerage firms are laying off workers and cutting costs as they struggle with the fallout of the subprime-mortgage crisis, but many are still planning to increase spending on information technology, according to a survey of 200 IT executives by International Business Machines Corp. In a survey conducted last month in cooperation with the Securities Industry and Financial Markets Association, IBM found that 21% of those questioned were planning to increase IT spending by up to 10% this year, and 18% saw increases of more than 10%.
