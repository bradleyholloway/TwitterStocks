 Phone Concern Tells Of Chairman's Guilt On Gambling Counts --- U.S. Long Distance Discloses He Was Put on Probation In Late 1970s, Early 1980s   By By Kyle Pope, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 15, 1993  SAN ANTONIO, Texas -- U.S. Long Distance Corp., in announcing plans for a major acquisition, made the unusual disclosure that its chairman, Parris H. Holmes Jr., has twice pleaded guilty to gambling charges.  The company, a regional long-distance and operator-services concern, said it signed a letter of intent to acquire Value-Added Communications Inc. for stock valued at about $100 million. At the same time, U.S. Long Distance disclosed the guilty pleas, adding that Mr. Holmes had served 10 months probation for bookmaking on football games. He said less than $500,000 of betting was involved.
