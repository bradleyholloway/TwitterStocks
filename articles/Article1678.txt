 Change Is Urged in Alternative Tax for Corporations   By Wartzman, Rick, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 1992  Twenty top executives from some of the largest companies in the US called on President-elect Clinton to change the corporate Alternative Minimum Tax as part of his economic plans. The AMT is a device intended to make certain that companies do not avoid tax liability by using special exclusions. The group argued that the AMT was hurting business investment because it reduces a company's cash flow during an already sluggish economy.
