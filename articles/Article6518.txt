 Regulator Wants to AlterRisk Rule on Fannie Mae   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 2001  The original rule assumed double-A-rated mortgage insurers would pay 85% of any losses on a mortgage after a five-year period, while triple-A-rated insurers, such as GE Mortgage Insurance, would pay 95%. The proposal assumes double-A rated firms would pay 91.25% of all losses, and triple-A rated firms would pay 96.50%.
