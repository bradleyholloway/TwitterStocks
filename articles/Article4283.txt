 Enterprise Dispatch   By Anonymous, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2011  Known as last-in, first-out -- or LIFO -- the strategy is used by businesses of all sizes to reduce taxable income by deducting the most recent cost of goods from sales. Since newer goods added to inventories tend to be more costly than older ones, the result gives the appearance of lower earnings, especially during periods of high inflation.
