 U.S. airlines trim routes over Atlantic as that market fails to turn around   By O Brian, Bridget, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 4, 1993  US airlines, which have spent billions of dollars for the right to fly to Europe, are suspending service on some trans-Atlantic routes, signaling that the market probably won't turn around any time soon. Planned cutbacks at American Airlines, Delta Air Lines and Trans World Airlines are discussed.
