 Dow Jones Net Grew 10% in Second Period On 7.5% Revenue Rise   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 10, 1986  Dow Jones & Co. reported that second-quarter profit rose 10% from a year earlier while the publishing company's revenue increased 7.5%.  Profit climbed to a second-quarter record of $42 million, or 43 cents a share, from $38.1 million, or 39 cents a share, a year earlier. The year-ago per-share figure is adjusted for a 50% stock dividend paid June 30 in the form of Class B common shares.  Pre-tax operating earnings increased 12% in the second quarter and 4.5% in the first half, Dow Jones said. It cited "a strong performance by the company's information services group, good gains by its community newspapers subsidiary and a company cost-containment program." Operating profit is calculated before taxes, interest income and expense and equity earnings in associated companies.
