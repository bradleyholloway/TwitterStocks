 Thomas Weisel's IPO Beats Woes, Jumps 33%; Investment Bank Gains Despite Slipping Results, Lead Underwriter's Exit [1]  By Cowan, Lynn, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 3, 2006  Weisel Partners had experienced a rocky path to the public markets. Its financial results, outlined in filings with the Securities and Exchange Commission, showed that the investment bank's performance in the first three quarters of 2005 fell compared with 2004 as it participated in fewer investment-banking transactions, and its brokerage activity declined. Last month, Goldman Sachs Group, which had signed on to co-lead manage the deal with Weisel Partners, dropped out after disagreements over when the IPO ought to be launched.
