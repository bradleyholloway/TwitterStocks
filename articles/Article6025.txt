 Letters to the Editor: Some CEOs Forced Into Glam and Glitter   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 2001  First, Mr. [Jim Collins] assumes that all such coverage is sought by the CEO and is evidence of self-promotion. Ms. [Carly Fiorina] and Mr. [Michael Armstrong] walked into two of the highest-profile industries (computers and telecommunications) populated by luminaries such as Bill Gates, Larry Ellison, Scott McNealy, John Chambers and Craig McCaw. Good media relations is part of Ms. Fiorina's and Mr. Armstrong's job descriptions, whether they like it or not.
