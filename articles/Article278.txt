Mort Pye, editor of The Star-Ledger of Newark, New Jersey's largest daily newspaper in circulation, will retire at the end of the year, the paper announced yesterday. He will be succeeded by James P. Willse, former editor and publisher of The Daily News.  Mr. Pye, 76, has had one of the longest runs in the nation as a top editor of a major daily newspaper, arriving in the newsroom at The Star-Ledger in 1957 as managing editor and holding the post of editor since 1963.
