 Sears, Roebuck & Co. Merchandise Group To Cut '85 Spending   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 1985  Sears, Roebuck & Co.'s merchandise group plans to cut 1985 capital spending 26%, to $512 million, the company said in its annual report.  Sears also has achieved its goal of having at least half its debt in fixed-rate financing, which reduced by half the company's sensitivity to short-term interest rate swings, the report said. The report also provides Sears's first statement of plans for Sears World Trade Inc. since a year ago, when Roderick M. Hills was abruptly removed as chairman and chief executive officer of the trading company.
