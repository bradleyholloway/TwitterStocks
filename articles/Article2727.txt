 House Leaders Seek Other Ways to Fight Smut on the Internet --- Proposal Forbids Regulation If On-Line Services Work To Control Racy Material   By By Daniel Pearl, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 1995  The proposal, which would bar government regulation of the Internet, could help derail a measure the Senate passed last week, which makes it a crime to post "indecent" material on computer networks without ensuring that minors don't see it. That provision, proposed by Sens. James Exon (D., Neb.) and Dan Coats (R., Ind.), was part of a broad bill revamping communications laws.  Some House Republicans want to add similar language to a House telecommunications bill. But Rep. Christopher Cox, a California conservative who heads the House Republican Policy Committee, is trying to head off such a move.
