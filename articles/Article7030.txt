 The Man Who Might Have Been 'King'   By Marlowe, Ann, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 10, 2010  Addressing the Independent Election Commission, Mr. Karzai spoke of "widespread fraud" in the Aug. 20, 2009, Afghan presidential election, which was precisely what Dr. Abdullah, a medical doctor and former government minister who came in second, had charged soon after the vote. Elite Afghans like Dr. Abdullah -- whose father was a high civil servant and a senator -- often are ashamed of the political violence of the last three decades, though there are reflexes to offload most of the blame on other countries -- the Soviet Union, Pakistan, Iran, the U.S. They are fiercely proud of their new status as a parliamentary democracy.
