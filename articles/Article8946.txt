 Tobacco Concerns Are Dealt Setback In Minnesota Case   By By Edward Felsenthal, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 1998  The Supreme Court refused to spare cigarette makers from releasing 39,000 secret documents in a closely watched Minnesota trial.  Justice Clarence Thomas had rejected the tobacco industry's bid to indefinitely delay the release, ordered March 7 by a state judge in Minnesota, but granted a postponement until 5 p.m. yesterday. The industry refiled its request with Justice Antonin Scalia. He referred the matter to the full court, which declined to intervene.
