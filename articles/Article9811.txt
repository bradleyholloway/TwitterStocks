 Samsung Faces Lower NAND Prices   By Ramstad, Evan, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 2005  If that forecast materializes, it is likely to hit Samsung particularly hard because its NAND chip business was a bright spot in what is shaping up to be a difficult year for the South Korean electronics maker. The NAND chip business had a 46% operating margin and grew strongly in revenue and volume during the first quarter. "You cannot really expect any better" results from the NAND flash business, Chu Woosik, Samsung's chief of investor relations, told analysts during a briefing on first-quarter results in mid-April. The company hasn't made a public forecast on the business since.
