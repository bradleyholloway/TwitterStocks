 Ask David Wessel   By Wessel, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 2006  If you prefer to treat the EU as a single entity -- excluding say, German exports to France the way you would New York exports to California -- then the EU is No. 1 with a 18.1% share of world exports, or $1.2 trillion worth sold to countries outside the EU. The U.S. is second at 12.3% and China is third at 8.9%.  The U.S., by the way, is the world's No. 1 importer. Nevertheless, most of what Americans produce is actually consumed in the U.S. and most of what Americans consume is produced in the U.S.
