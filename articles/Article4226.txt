 Don't Get Carried Away by the Market Rally   By Arends, Brett, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 16, 2011  The real reason we all feel an urge to buy shares after the stock market has risen has nothing to do with the economic outlook or investment risks. Most of the stock market's value is based on the profits companies will make over many decades into the future. Analysis by Ben Inker, a director at top investment company GMO, found that even the next 10 years' profits account for only about 25% of the stock market's value.
