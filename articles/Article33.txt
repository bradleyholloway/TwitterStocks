Morgan Stanley & Company said yesterday that it had appointed John D. Paulus to the newly created post of chief economist, effective Aug. 30.  Mr. Paulus, 39 years old, has been vice president and economist at Goldman, Sachs & Company. Mr. Paulus will also be a vice president at Morgan.  Before joining Goldman, Mr. Paulus was senior vice president at the Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis. Mr. Paulus holds a Ph.D in economics from the University of Chicago and an undergraduate degree in economics from the University of Michigan.  He will be responsible for management and supervision of the firm's economics group and of the economic reports produced by Morgan Stanley.
