 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 21, 1993  Central Power & Light Co. -- $200 million, two-part issue of first mortgage bonds priced through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch. The first part, consisting of $100 million of first mortgage bonds, due April 1, 2000, was priced as 6s at 99.75 to yield 6.044%. The noncallable bonds were priced at a spread of 0.55 percentage point above the Treasury seven-year note. The second part, consisting of $100 million of first mortgage bonds, due April 1, 2023, was priced as 7 1/2 s at 99.131 to yield 7.573%. Noncallable for 10 years, the bonds were priced at a spread of 0.82 percentage point over the Treasury 30-year bond. The issues are rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp. Central Power & Light is a unit of Central & South West Corp.  Gannett Co. -- $250 million of notes, due May 1, 2000, priced at par to yield 5.85%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue, won by dealers led by Goldman, Sachs & Co., was priced at a spread of 0.35 percentage point above the Treasury seven-year note. The issue is rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P.  Jersey Central Power & Light Co. -- $125 million of first mortgage bonds, due May 1, 2023, priced as 7 1/2% at 99.17 to yield 7.57%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Noncallable for 10 years, the issue was won by dealers led by Merrill Lynch & Co. The bonds were reoffered at a spread of 0.83 percentage point above the Treasury 30-year bond. The bonds were priced at a net interest cost to the borrower of 7.611%. The issue is rated single-A-3 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P.
