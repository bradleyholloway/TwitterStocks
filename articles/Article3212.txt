 Readers' Forum: 'GDP is the market driver'   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 2003  Andrew Blackman's story two weeks ago about which indicators Wall Street professionals use to gauge the health of the market ("Where's the Market Heading? Look Here") garnered a lot of responses -- including some of your own "leading indicators."  Average growth in real GDP 1930-2002 was 3.4% a year; 2002 real GDP was 2.4%, well within "normal" economic performance but not sufficient to sustain the market at its current levels. Real GDP for 2003 will come close to the 3.4% average if the fourth-quarter performance is 4% or more. The market is responding accordingly.
