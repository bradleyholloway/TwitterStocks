 Some Wood-Product Prices Hit Highs, Sparked by Spotted Owl Litigation, Low Lumber Output   By Johnson, Chip, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 1991  Some wood-product prices soared to record highs during the week ended Jun 7, 1991, reflecting a speculative frenzy sparked by efforts to save the spotted owl from extinction and low lumber production. A composite index of nine framing-lumber products hit $303 per 1,000 board feet on Jun 7, exceeding the previous record of $300 on Aug 24, 1979. Similarly, an index of plywood products climbed to $282 per 1,000 per square feet, one of the highest levels recorded. Other commodities trading prices for Jun 7 are given.
