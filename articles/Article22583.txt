 Bay State Bill Would Make Translators S.O.P. for E.R.s   By By James Bandler, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 1999  Based on the size of non-English-speaking populations in their areas, hospitals would have flexibility in determining how many interpreters to hire and what languages they would speak. In Massachusetts, where 400,000 residents speak little or no English, about a third of all hospitals currently provide interpreters.
