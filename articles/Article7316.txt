 Law Journal: The Coveted Summer Job --- As Classes Begin, Second-Year Law Students Await Tryout Slots at Big Firms   By Smith, Jennifer, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 10, 2012  After whipping through dozens of on-campus interviews with potential employers -- the law-school equivalent of speed-dating -- second-year law students are on tenterhooks as they await offers of coveted summer jobs that could determine their future. Only 27% of 2011 graduates in private practice ended up at a firm with 100 or more lawyers one year after graduation, compared with 41% from the class of 2008, according to the National Association for Law Placement, a nonprofit group that tracks legal employment figures.
