 Tip of the Week: Money Down Can Sit a Bit   By Spors, Kelly K, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 2004  If the gift money sits in your account for the full three months or longer, it indicates the money is yours and that you're able to make the down payment, says Tim Doyle, director of government affairs for the Mortgage Bankers Association of America. "Generally, what the lender will be looking for is that it's a true gift and that you have full control over the money," Mr. Doyle says. If the money sits in your account long enough -- say six months or longer -- the lender may not even bother to find out if the money originally was a gift.
