 Market Rise Extends to 5 Sessions, with Focus on Profit-Jolt Safety   By Torres, Craig; Wilson, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 5, 1990  The Dow Jones Industrial Average rose 12.37 to 2911.63, the first close above 2900 since June 21. The current winning streak in the Dow industrials is the longest since early May.  Volume on the New York Stock Exchange totaled 130,050,000 shares, compared with 130,200,000 on Monday. Advancing issues on the Big Board outpaced decliners 834 to 650. The financial markets were closed yesterday in observance of Independence Day.  Among declining stocks, Dow Jones slipped 1/2 to a new 52-week low of 22 1/2, continuing a monthlong slide. Since early June, shares of the information and publishing company, which publishes this newspaper, have fallen 20%. Several analysts, including Susan Decker of Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette, have recently lowered their earnings estimates for Dow Jones. Ms. Decker said she is concerned about a "softer profit outlook" at Dow Jones's Telerate subsidiary.
