 Dow Jones Real Estate Index   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 5, 1996  Average rates for amounts greater than $207,000, based on "1 point" loan fee and 20% down payment.  Luxury Homes Index: shows price changes of an area's most expensive houses. Actual prices vary greatly from city to city. The index shows, however, that luxury homes are up 21% in Detroit since 1990; and even in San Francisco. Put another way: A Washington, D.C., area home selling for $1 million in 1990 is, on average, worth $1,040,000 today; that year's $500,000 Denver mansion is now worth $780,000.
