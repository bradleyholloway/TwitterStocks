 Dunkelberg Voices Optimism on Small Business's Future --- Despite Slowdown and Mideast Unrest, Economist Says Prospects Are Good   By By Udayan Gupta, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 7, 1990  The 47-year-old Mr. [William C.] Dunkelberg, one of the country's best known small-business economists, still sees little to make him abandon his usual optimistic outlook for small business. His views enjoy a wide following. Currently dean of Temple University's School of Business and Management, Mr. Dunkelberg is also chief economist of the National Federation of Independent Business, the country's largest small-business group. He produces the federation's influential quarterly economic report for small business.
