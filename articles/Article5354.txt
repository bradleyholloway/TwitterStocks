 A new era dawns: With Congress Party battered, others gain greater clout in India   By Jordan, Miriam; Waldman, Peter, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 1996  Votes are still being counted, but the importance of India's 11th general election is clear: The long-ruling Congress Party has been battered by a slew of smaller sectarian parties that now stand poised to revolutionize Indian democracy. With more than 60% of the seats in the Parliament decided, the election's biggest winner, though probably well short of a majority, seems to be the Bharatiya Janata Party. The election results are examined.
