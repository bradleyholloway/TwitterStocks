 Canada Asks Agency To Reconsider Rule On Phone-Rate Plan   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 1994  The Canadian government asked federal regulators to reconsider a ruling that would allow major phone companies to boost local phone-service charges and reduce long-distance rates.  The government's decision means that the phone companies will have to put on hold their plans to raise their monthly rates for basic phone service by two Canadian dollars (US$1.44) in each of the next three years.
