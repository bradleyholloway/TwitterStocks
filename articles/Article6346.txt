 Jobless Rate Drops In Odessa-Midland   By By Neal Templin, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 12, 2000  The unemployment rate in Odessa-Midland dropped to 5.8% in May, and the oil patch area may finally be emerging from a nasty slump that cost it nearly 9,000 jobs.  When oil prices crashed in early 1999, the economy in the twin cities crashed as well. Drilling activity slowed dramatically, idling rig and production workers in blue-collar Odessa. At the same time, oil companies began moving white-collar jobs out of Midland. The combined metro area's jobless rate peaked in May 1999 at 9.3%.
