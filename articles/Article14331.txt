 GM's EDS Unit Posts 25% Rise In Quarterly Net --- Hughes Subsidiary's Profit Fell 20%; Concern Cites Reversal of Tax Credit   By By Amal Kumar Naj, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 5, 1987  General Motors Corp.'s Electronic Data Systems Corp. subsidiary posted a 25% increase in fourth-quarter net income, while profit at its GM Hughes Electronics Corp. fell 20%.  Shares representing a dividend interest in the two big non-automotive subsidiaries are traded independently on the New York Stock Exchange. GM's finance unit, whose earnings are consolidated into company results from car operations, reported that its profit rose 2.3% to $296.2 million. GM is scheduled to announce its overall corporate results today.  EDS's profit for the period was $71.5 million, or 58 cents a share, compared with $57.4 million, or 47 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue for the data-processing concern rose 7% to $1.15 billion from $1.08 billion.
