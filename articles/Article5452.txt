 'Emerging' bond play cools down   By Zuckerman, Gregory, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 10, 1997  Bonds from developing countries known as emerging markets turned heads during the past two years, racking up gains that outperformed even the stock market's impressive returns. But in just the past few weeks problems have emerged, indicating the best gains may already have been achieved. On Feb 7, 1997, the price of the benchmark 30-year bond was up 24/32 point to 97 12/32 for a yield of 6.69%, down from the previous day's yield of 6.75%.
