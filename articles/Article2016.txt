 Japan lowers its discount rate to 1%   By Steiner, Robert; Sapsford, Jathon, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 1995  The Bank of Japan cut its official discount rate by 0.75 percentage point to a postwar low of 1%, in a move to boost growth and rein in the strong yen. In addition, Japan's government announced a package of measures also meant to cap the yen.
