 Oil Firms, Expecting to Post Profit Surge, Fear Public Demand for a Windfall Tax   By Solomon, Caleb, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 16, 1991  Oil companies are expected to post such huge increases in fourth-quarter earnings that they fear a political backlash that could spur the re-enactment of a windfall profits tax. Profits of six major oil companies rose 62% to around $6 billion in the quarter, up from $3.7 billion a year earlier.
