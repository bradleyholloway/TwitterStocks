 Hugo Boss Chairman Plans to Quit Clothier; Vogt to Be Successor   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 15, 1997  The changing of the guard at German clothier Hugo Boss AG won't change its generally well-tailored prospects, its owner and analysts said following the announcement that Chairman Peter Littmann plans to quit at year's end.  Mr. Littmann will be succeeded by Joachim Vogt, 44 years old, the company's current production and logistics expert, who joined the Hugo Boss board in 1990 after seven years with the McKinsey consultancy group in Duesseldorf.
