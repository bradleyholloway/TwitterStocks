 Oil Futures Soar As Winter Nears, Greenspan Cheers   By Leah McGrath Goodman, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 2005  Major damage along the Gulf Coast after this year's brutal hurricane season will hurt U.S. economic growth in the near term, "but the economic fundamentals remain firm and the U.S. economy appears to retain important forward momentum," Mr. [Alan Greenspan] said in remarks to Congress's Joint Economic Committee.  "There's plenty of crude around and there's plenty of heating oil," said Ed Silliere, a trader for Energy Merchant Intermarket Futures LLC, a New York brokerage. "I think we are still going to work our way lower, but it may be a slow grind."
