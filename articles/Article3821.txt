 Running a Business: Quick Tips: Failing to File Online edition  By Ransom, Diana, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 2008  If taxes are owed, business owners who delay filing their federal tax returns past the due date may face penalties and interest charges that could increase their tax bills by 25% or more, according to the Internal Revenue Service. If you miss the deadline due to extenuating circumstances, such as a serious illness, a death in the family or the loss of financial records as the result of a natural disaster, the IRS may, depending on the situation, waive penalties.
