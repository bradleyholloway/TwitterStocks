 Want Regis's Job? For These Candidates, It May Cost a Lifeline   By By Elizabeth Weinstein, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 2000  Among the contenders yesterday at a New York City studio was none other than John Carpenter, the 32-year-old Internal Revenue Service employee who was the first contestant to win $1 million on the TV version of the show. Armed with his newfound celebrity status and a funny story for the judges, he whizzed through the audition and got a callback. "I've been bitten by the bug," he said, heading off to meet with his financial adviser from Merrill Lynch.
