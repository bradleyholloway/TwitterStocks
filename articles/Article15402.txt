 World Wire [2]  By Compiled by Richard L. Holman, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 1996  Italy agreed to sell its last major steel holding, 84% of Dalmine SpA, to the industrial holding group Techint Finanziaria SrL for 301.5 billion lire ($191.4 million). Dalmine is controlled by Ilva SpA, the state steel giant in liquidation and controlled by state holding company Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale SpA, or IRI. Techint will sell 49% of Dalmine to IRI-controlled Banca di Roma, which will sell 14% in a secondary placement. Banca di Roma and Techint will launch an offer for the 16% of Dalmine that is publicly traded.
