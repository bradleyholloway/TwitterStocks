 Notable & Quotable   By Anonymous, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 10, 2011  After unemployment peaked at 10.8 percent in time for the 1982 midterm election, strong economic growth in the latter half of 1983 and through 1984 dropped the unemployment rate down to 7.2 percent by the November 1984 election, allowing the president to run on the "Morning in America" theme and rack up a 49-state landslide.
