 Treasury Puts Off Sale of $9 Billion In 30-Year Bonds   By By David Wessel, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 1988  The Treasury put off the sale of $9 billion in 30-year bonds until later this month or early December, because President Reagan hasn't yet signed legislation authorizing the sale.  The fate of the 30-year bond auction has been the subject of enormous speculation among bond traders. The tax bill recently passed by Congress authorizes the sale, but the bill hasn't formally reached the president's desk yet. He is expected to sign it.
