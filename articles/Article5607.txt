 Violence in KwaZulu/Natal province drops after South African elections   By Davidson, Joe, The Wall Street Journal,  May 12, 1994  On the first full day after Nelson Mandela, South Africa's first democratically elected president, took office, the residents of the congested black township of Umlazi in the Kwazulu/Natal Province no longer must contend with the high level of political violence they faced before the election.
