 Taste: Tony & Tacky   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 12, 2001  Smoked Out: Seems the money's greener on the other side of the tort fence. On June 6 a Los Angeles jury awarded a smoker a record-breaking $3 billion in damages from Phillip Morris (later reduced by a trial judge to $100 million). Later that same month Brobeck, Phleger & Harrison -- a San Francisco-based law firm that served as chief counsel for smoking cases for R.J. Reynolds Tobacco from 1954 to 1981 -- notified smoker Laurence Lucier that the firm would represent him in a suit against its former client.
