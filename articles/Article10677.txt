 Big Firms A Drag on Pension Funds   By Corkery, Michael; Zuckerman, Gregory, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 7, 2012  According to Wilshire Trust Universe Comparison Service, among pensions with assets greater than $5 billion, the average allocation to private equity was 13% as of June 30, up from 9.5% a year earlier. Private-equity firms of all sizes managed to exceed stock-market returns over the most recent five-year and 10-year periods, but some pension managers say large buyout strategies haven't lived up to expectations.
