 Carolina town seeks a brighter--some say whiter--future   By Cooper, Helene, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1993  Emotions are running high in South Carolina where the tiny hamlet of Hemingway, located in Hemingway township--whose 2,500 residents are 80% white--has petitioned the state to dissociate itself from Williamsburg County, with a 65% black population out of 38,000 residents, and join predominantly white Florence Country to the north.
