 New Securities Issues [2]  The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 7, 1990  Metropolitan Washington Airports Authority, D.C. -- $246 million of airport system revenue bonds, Series 1990 A, due Oct. 1, 1992-2004, 2009, 2013, 2017 and 2019, with capital appreciation bonds due 2005-2009, tentatively priced by a Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. group to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.69% in 2013. Current interest serial bonds are priced at par to yield from 6.20% in 1992 to 7.30% in 2004. There are $13.6 million of 7 1/4% term bonds priced at 98 to yield 7.44% in 2009, $47.1 million of 7.60% term bonds priced at 99 to yield 7.69% in 2013, $62.6 million of 7% term bonds priced at 94 to yield 7.52% in 2017 and $38.1 million of 6% bonds priced at 83.27 to yield 7.40% in 2019. Capital appreciation bonds are priced to yield to maturity from 7.35% in 2005 to 7.45% in 2008 and 2009. The bonds are insured and triple-A-rated, except for uninsured bonds due in 2013, which are rated single-A-1/single-A. Interest on the bonds will be treated as a preference item in calculating the federal alternative minimum tax that may be imposed on certain investors.  Federal National Mortgage Association -- $500 million of Remic mortgage securities offered in 15 classes by Bear Stearns & Co. The offering, Series 1990-24, backed by Fannie Mae 9 1/2% securities, brings Fannie Mae's 1990 Remic issuance to $10.1 billion and its total to $59.8 billion since the program began in April 1987. The issue is the first Fannie Mae Remic since late 1988 to include floating-rate classes pegged to the 11th district cost-of-funds index. Prices will be at market levels. Among classes for which figures were available, planned yield spreads range from 65 basis points above two-year Treasury securities to 115 basis points above 30-year Treasurys and 130 basis points above threeyear Treasurys. The floating-rate class has a rate that adjusts at 100 basis points above the 11th district cost-of-funds rate, the key measure of thrift deposit and financing costs in California. The index adjusts monthly. The floater class is being offered at par and has generated strong investor demand, according to a Bear Stearns official. The initial rate is 9.476%. The collateral has an underlying weighted average coupon rate of 10.125% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 29.75 years.
