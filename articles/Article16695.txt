 Ask Personal Journal   By Herman, Tom, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 5, 2006  A: The general rule is that if you pay a household employee more than a certain amount, you may be required to pay Social Security and Medicare taxes, and file Schedule H. For 2006, the threshold is $1,500 or more. But not everyone is required to pay. For example, don't count wages you pay to an employee who is under 18 at any time in 2006.
