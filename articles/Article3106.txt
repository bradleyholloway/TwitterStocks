 Containing the Asbestos Blob   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 16, 2003  This week's announcement by Travelers Property Casualty of an additional $2.45 billion charge to cover future asbestos payouts is an indication, paradoxically, that the insurer sees a light at the end of the tunnel and is willing to put a number on its exposure. Much the same is suddenly true among dozens of companies, which report a new pliability among formerly rapacious trial lawyers. Halliburton, Dick Cheney's old firm, saw its shares pummeled last year but now sees them rising again on hope of a clean-sweep deal that would put the asbestos bogey to bed for a final, if steep, price of $4 billion.
