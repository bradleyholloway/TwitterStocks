 Delta, Northwest Post Strong Net Despite Fuel Costs, Higher Fares   By By Martha Brannigan and Michael J. McCarthy, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 21, 2000  Delta, which is better hedged against rising fuel costs than most of its peers, said net income rose 26% to a record $460 million, or $3.51 a diluted share, in the fiscal fourth quarter ended June 30. The latest period includes a pretax gain of $228 million, or $1.05 a share, related to a swap of Priceline.com common stock for Priceline.com preferred stock. In the 1999 fiscal fourth quarter, Atlanta-based Delta had net income of $364 million, or $2.40 a diluted share.  Besides the Priceline.com gain, Delta's fiscal fourth quarter included a charge of $86 million for an early-retirement employee medical program, and a $3 million adjustment for an accounting change. Revenue at Delta, the nation's third-largest carrier, rose 14% to $4.47 billion from $3.92 billion a year earlier.
