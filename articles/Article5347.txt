 Heard on the street: Proposed accounting rule could boost per-share earnings of some companies   By Berton, Lee, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 10, 1996  The FASB, the chief rule maker for accountants, has proposed an accounting rule that would reduce the dilutive effect of stock options, warrants and convertible securities on earnings per share. This would boost some companies' EPS and reduce their price/earnings ratio, which some analysts believe will result in higher stock prices.
