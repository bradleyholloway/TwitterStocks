 Business and Finance [4]  The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 1991  THREE STOCKS in the Dow Jones industrials will be changed, effective Monday, to reflect the economy's growing reliance on the service sector. Caterpillar will replace Navistar International; Walt Disney, will supplant USX; and J.P. Morgan, the first banking company in the widely watched stock-market index, will replace Primerica.  Switzerland's CS Holding reduced its dividend after a 1990 loss of $587 million at its CS First Boston unit. The parent also announced plans to sell a 20% stake in Credit Suisse, the third-largest Swiss bank.  A U.S.-Japanese pact was announced under which Tokyo will help finance exports of American goods and services. The Japanese apparently agreed to the program to ease trade frictions with Washington.
