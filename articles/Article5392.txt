 Central Banks Intervene to Aid Dollar, but Effort Doesn't Give It a Strong Boost   By Sesit, Michael R; Roth, Terence; Wessel, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 12, 1992  On Aug 11, 1992, 14 central banks, led by the FRB and Germany's Bundesbank, intervened repeatedly to support the sagging dollar. In late New York trading, the dollar stood at 1.4670 marks and 128.00 yen, compared with DM1.4635 and Y127.90 the day before.
