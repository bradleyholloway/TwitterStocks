 What's in a Name? Not Much For These Fans of Imported Cadbury --- Some Spurn Version of Chocolate Made in U.S., While Getting All Gooey Over British Bars   By Barrett, Joe; Martin, Timothy W, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 14, 2009  When Gayle Green has a craving for chocolate, she piles her two children into her car and drives 45 minutes to the British Pantry store in Aldie, Va., where she stocks up on Cadbury chocolates imported from the U.K. "I know -- it's sick, right?" says the 40-year-old social media director for a wedding Web site. Like Coca-Cola lovers who swear the Mexican-made version of the soda tastes better, hardcore Cadbury fans spend plenty of time in hot chocolate pursuit.
