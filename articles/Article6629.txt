 Tom Herman's Tax Tip: Get a Break From a Loser   By Herman, Tom, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 2006  Next, suppose your losses are bigger than your gains -- or you don't have any gains at all. It still might be smart to dump losers. That's because you typically can deduct as much as $3,000 of your net capital losses (or as much as $1,500 if you're married and filing separately from your spouse) from your wages and other ordinary income.  What if you're convinced that loser you've held onto for so many years is going to rise sharply next year? You can still sell it now, "harvest" your tax loss, and buy back that stock later, but be sure to watch out for the "wash sale" rules.
