 Have Knife, Will Travel: A Slaughterhouse on Wheels; 'Custom Butcher' Gives Small Farms New Option to Sell Local Produce   By Etter, Lauren, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 5, 2008  [...] Lopez Island's mobile slaughterhouse -- the first to be sanctioned by the U.S. Agriculture Department -- now shuttles from farm to farm three or four days a week, collecting fresh carcasses of cows, pigs and sheep that will become steaks, sausage, bacon and hamburgers. In 2000, the farmers formed the Island Grown Farmers Cooperative with the help of Lopez Community Land Trust, a nonprofit group dedicated to sustainable agriculture and affordable housing.
