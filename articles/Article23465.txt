 A 'Grand Bargain' Could Co-Opt Iran   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 3, 2007  Michael Rubin and Danielle Pletka argue convincingly that U.S.- Iranian relations are in a dire state, and that previous, halfhearted attempts to reconcile our differences have come to naught. Importantly, no one is contesting those facts ("Table Talk," editorial page, Feb. 21). The trouble lies in addressing the question that Mr. Rubin and Ms. Pletka largely avoid -- what to do now, a topic on which the authors offer little more than advice that we "ratchet up the pressure."
