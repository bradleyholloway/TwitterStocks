Neftali Valencia, a Colombian who has worked steadily since he arrived in New York City in 1980, should have been the perfect candidate for citzenship under the new United States immigration law. Instead he believed that applying would alert agents to an earlier run-in with the law and end with his being thrown out of the country.  ''I thought it would be impossible,'' Mr. Valencia said of his chances of becoming an American citizen.
