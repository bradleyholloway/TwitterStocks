 Chinese, Worried Over Growth, Plan Interest-Rate Cut   By By Ian Johnson and Karby Leggett, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 5, 1997  In another sign of official concern over faltering economic growth, Chinese government officials said the central bank is set to lower interest rates this month, marking the second round of cuts in two months.  The officials -- one with the People's Bank of China and the other with the State Economic and Trade Commission -- gave the first government confirmation of what has been widely rumored for several weeks. Although China's economy is set to grow 9% this year and more than 8% next year, the figures indicate that China's economy isn't growing fast enough to re-employ the tens of millions of people losing their jobs to state-enterprise reform -- one of Beijing's key concerns.
