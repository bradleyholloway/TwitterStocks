 The Week in Review   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 2006  Iran restarted its U.N.-sealed nuclear program, setting the stage for a long-threatened showdown with Europe and the U.S. Even Russia, angered at Iran for ruining the peace talks it had been brokering, said it wouldn't oppose bringing its trading partner to the U.N. Security Council. But Iranian President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad -- who has called for Israel to be "wiped off the map" and said the Holocaust was a "myth" -- remained defiant. United Kingdom Foreign Secretary Jack Straw answered the question on many people's minds: "No one is talking about invading Iran or taking military action against Iran."
