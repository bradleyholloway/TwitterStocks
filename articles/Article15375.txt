 Growth ebbs for key industrial nations as higher interest rates brake inflation   By Wilke, John R, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 5, 1995  The world's largest industrial economies slowed in 1995 after central banks raised interest rates to brake inflation, according to an IMF survey. Growth in industrial countries was expected to ease to 2.5% in 1995, and 2.4% in 1996, from 3.1% in 1994.
