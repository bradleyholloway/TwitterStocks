 New Securities Issues [1]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 1993  Bear Stearns Cos. -- $200 million of notes, due June 15, 2000, priced as 6.5s at 99.928 to yield 6.513%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 0.8 percentage point above the Treasury seven-year note. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and singleA by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by the borrower's investment-banking unit, Bear, Stearns & Co.  Dresser Industries Inc. -- $300 million of notes, due June 1, 2000, priced at par to yield 6.25%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 0.55 percentage point above the Treasury seven-year note. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.  Morgan Stanley Group Inc. -- $150 million of notes, due June 15, 1997, priced as 5.65s at 99.965 to yield 5.66%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 0.6 percentage point above the comparable Treasury note maturing in 1997. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by the borrower's investment-banking unit, Morgan Stanley & Co.
