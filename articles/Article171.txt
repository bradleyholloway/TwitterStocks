Phyllis Newman's new cabaret act is whimsically titled ''The First Hundred Years.'' Although the singer and comedian has several decades to go before actually celebrating her show-business centennial, the title reminds one that the star is indeed a trouper with vaudevillian roots.  At Tuesday's opening-night performance of a two-week engagement at Rainbow and Stars, 30 Rockefeller Plaza, Miss Newman reminisced about her early days in Atlantic City music halls where she paraded about in a red satin skirt and sang Carmen Miranda songs.
