 White House, in Bid to Reward Pakistan, Proposes Cutting Apparel, Textile Tariffs   By By Helene Cooper, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 2, 2001  The thorniest part of the package involves the tariff cuts. "The act would permit the president to reduce or suspend tariffs on textiles and textile products made in Pakistan as he determines appropriate in response to the threat to national security posed by international terrorism," the proposal says.  The proposal goes far beyond one made last month by the domestic textile industry, which asked the [Bush] administration to limit any tariff benefits to Pakistan to carpets.
