 IRS Says Stolen Art Treasures Are Subject to Estate Tax   By Schmedel, Scott R, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 1992  The IRS ruled on Aug 30, 1991 that the heirs of Tom Meador will have to pay federal estate tax on art objects that Meador stole in Germany and France at the end of WWII. The case is examined. Attorneys for the heirs say that there can be no estate tax on the art works because he never had legal title to the treasures.
