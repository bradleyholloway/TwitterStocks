 Lucky Stores Is Sued By 4 Retired Workers Over Benefits Plan --- A Wall Street Journal News Roundup   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 16, 1987  Lucky Stores Inc. was sued by four retired workers, who charged the grocery chain with illegally failing to fund a health and welfare plan for retired employees of its recently divested Gemco discount department store unit.  The suit claims Dublin, Calif.-based Lucky's Gemco unit agreed, in a 1984 contract with the United Food and Commercial Workers International Union, to fund a plan under which Gemco retirees would receive lifetime health and welfare benefits.
