 WEEKEND JOURNAL; Bookmarks   By Hughes, Robert J, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 20, 2004  THE SIX narrator-protagonists at the heart of David Mitchell's luminous novel, "Cloud Atlas," live separate lives across many centuries, but they seem to be connected by incidental details -- a recurring birthmark, a piece of music -- that suggest a continuity. Each tries to make sense of his time, confronting the effects of society's "progress" and of individual decisions. And each succeeds only partly.
