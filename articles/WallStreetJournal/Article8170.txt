 Gates Hearings Suggest Resentment Toward Israel over Iran-Contra Affair   By Rogers, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 1991  During Senate hearings on the confirmation of Robert Gates as CIA chief, Gates suggested that there was resentment by the White House towards Israel for its part in the Reagan administration's decision to permit arms sales to Iran in the mid-1980s. Gates said the weapons-for-hostages strategy was shaped more by Israeli than US intelligence.
