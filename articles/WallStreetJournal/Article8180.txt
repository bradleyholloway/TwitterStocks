 Legal Beat: Plaintiff in Tagamet Suit Claims Judge Wasn't Impartial   By Allen, Frank Edward; Hooper, Laurence, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 1991  Thomas Latimer, the plaintiff whose lawsuit alleging injuries from the ulcer drug Tagamet, which is made by SmithKline, was dismissed in 1989, has accused US District Judge Harold Barefoot Sanders of having "obvious conflicts of interest." Sanders had securities holdings in pharmaceutical companies Glaxo Holdings PLC and Squibb while the suit was going on.
