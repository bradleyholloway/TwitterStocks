 U.S. News --- CAPITAL: Emerging Markets Face New Tests   By Wessel, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 12, 2009  Freed from the commitment to defend a fixed exchange rate with high interest rates, emerging-market central banks cut rates and partially shielded domestic borrowers from turbulence. The mighty Chinese economy withstood the storm while holding its currency tightly against the dollar. [...] the Federal Reserve swapped U.S. dollars for local currencies with central banks in Brazil, Mexico, South Korea and Singapore so they could lend them to their dollar-short banks, bypassing malfunctioning currency markets.
