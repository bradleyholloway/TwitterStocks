 New York Merc plan for new site is ended by owner, Primerica   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 9, 1993  Plans for the New York Mercantile Exchange to move into a new $90 million trading facility were scuttled when the world's largest energy exchange was told that its plans could no longer be accommodated by the site's new owner, Primerica Corp. The exchange had agreed in 1992 to renovate and and lease four floors of Shearson Lehman Brothers Inc's building at 390 Greenwich St in Manhattan NY.
