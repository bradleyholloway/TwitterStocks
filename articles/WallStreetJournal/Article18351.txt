 Vietnam's Economy Rebounds from Crisis of Soviet Collapse   By Wain, Barry, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 4, 1992  Vietnam's political and economic stabilization, which was threatened by the collapse of the Soviet empire, is discussed. A year after the demise of the Soviet Union, Vietnam's inflation has been curbed, the currency is strengthening and the country is running a trade surplus for the first time ever.
