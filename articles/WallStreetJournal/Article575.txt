 Letters to the Editor: A-Bomb's Origin   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 15, 1985  It was one of Einstein's former students at the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute in Berlin, Leo Szilard, who is generally credited with the idea of adapting nuclear fission to a weapon. Peter Wyden ("Day One," Simon and Schuster, 1984) recounts how the idea came to Szilard as he was crossing a London street in September 1933. He registered a patent on it in 1934.
