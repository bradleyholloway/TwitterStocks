 Investing in Funds: A Monthly Analysis --- When Safe Places No Longer Feel So Safe --- For risk-averse 401(k) participants, the usual havens of money-market, stable-value and short-term bond funds pose new risks; Here's what you need to know   By Laise, Eleanor, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 2009  The concerns are cropping up at a time when many retirement-plan participants, facing devastating losses in their stock portfolios, want to preserve what is left of their nest egg rather than betting on a market rebound. Money-Market Funds Money-market mutual funds, which invest in Treasury bills, certificates of deposit and very short-term bonds, held more than $3.8 trillion in late April, according to Investment Company Institute, a trade group.
