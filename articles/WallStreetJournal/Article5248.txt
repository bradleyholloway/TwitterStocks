 For makers of Japanese luxury cars, stamina is key to surviving sanctions   By Hamilton, David P; Williams, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 9, 1995  Analysts believe that Japan's five biggest auto makers could weather the short-term effects of US sanctions, which would go into effect Jun 28, 1995 unless a settlement is reached, while such financially weakened companies as Nissan Motor Co and Mazda Motor Corp would almost certainly see their expected losses widen. The other three Japanese car companies affected--Toyota Motor Corp, Honda Motor Corp and Mitsubishi Motors Corp--would probably not even fall into the red.
