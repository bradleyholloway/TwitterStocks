 Letters to the Editor: Protectionism: What a Drag   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 4, 1994  Laura Tyson, chair of the President's Council of Economic Advisors, argues that Japanese structural impediments to imports are a primary cause of the U.S. trade deficit in high-technology sectors ("Japan's Trade Surplus Matters," editorial page, March 1). Ms. Tyson also asserts that Japanese trade barriers have created a sheltered climate in which its firms have been able to reap the "experience, the scale and the profits" needed for international competition. It is alarming that U.S. trade policy toward Japan is being guided by these incorrect assertions.
