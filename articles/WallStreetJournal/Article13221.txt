 Tip of the Week: Try One-Way For Air Deals   By Spors, Kelly K, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 6, 2005  One advantage to buying one-way tickets is more flexibility. "Circle" trips, where you fly in and out of several airports, are cheaper than ever before, and there's little penalty for mixing and matching airlines on the same trip. For example, a round-trip fare from Minneapolis to Los Angeles over Thanksgiving weekend recently cost $535, according to a search on SideStep.com. Two one-way tickets for the same itinerary -- bought from two different airlines for the same dates -- cut the total price to $460.
