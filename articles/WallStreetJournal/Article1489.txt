 Bear Market in Boas: Proposed Laws Strangle Sales of Mutant Snakes --- Premium Python, Once Worth $40,000, Now Sells for Half; 'It's Just Like Stocks'   By Scheck, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 2010  The origins of the snake bubble harken back to the early 1980s, by many breeders' accounts, after Florida reptile breeder Tom Crutchfield recognized a photo of an albino Burmese python in National Geographic magazine. The mutant-constrictor economy began to resemble the Dutch tulip mania of the 17th century, as snake speculators entered bidding wars for rare specimens, which they began calling "investment grade."
