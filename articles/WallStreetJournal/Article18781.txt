 Small firms get tangled in phone-service bids   By Naik, Gautam, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 1996  Unexpectedly fierce bidding has driven prices sky-high for a piece of the broadcast spectrum that will carry the new wireless phone service, aimed at competing with cellular offerings. Escalating bids favor a few small companies backed by giant corporations and are forcing independent bidders to overextend themselves--or drop out altogether. An accompanying table lists small business and big company personal communication services bids in four major markets.
