 Nondefense spending, health care targeted by rival GOP blueprints   By Rogers, David; Georges, Christopher, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 1995  Senate and House Republicans proposed competing budget plans that rely on deep cuts from nondefense spending and wholesale changes in federal health-care programs to bring the government into balance by 2002. The Senate proposal assumes more than $430 billion would be saved by slowing the growth of the Medicare and Medicaid programs.
