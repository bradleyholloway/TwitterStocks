 PURSUITS; Books: The Glossies   By Varadarajan, Tunku, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 2007  Pliny but to anyone at all until the modern development of submersibles capable of plunging to depths that are the inverse of a Mount Everest. Only 5% of the seafloor has been mapped, and scientists estimate that there are between 10 million and 30 million species in the "vasty deep" yet to be found by man. The ones that we do know -- and many of those are pictured in this book -- are gloriously bizarre critters that appear to have been fashioned by Salvador Dali.
