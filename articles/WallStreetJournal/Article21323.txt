 Tokyo Shares Continue Streak of Record Highs, But Prices Slip Back in London and Frankfurt ---- A Wall Street Journal News Roundup   The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 1989  Share prices rose Tuesday to yet another record in Tokyo, but slipped in London and Frankfurt.  Individual Tokyo stock investors, having fully discounted a possible discount rate increase by the Bank of Japan, prepared for an anticipated jump in share prices after the "Golden Week" holidays, buying ahead of the market and pushing the Nikkei index to a record close for the fifth consecutive session.  The Tokyo stock price index of all issues listed in the first section rose 14.23 points to close at 2517.49. First section volume was estimated at 1.2 billion shares, up from 920.6 million shares Monday.
