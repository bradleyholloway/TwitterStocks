 Entertainment & Culture -- Art: The Art World's Olympics --- Century-old event copes with downturn; artists' moms cook soup   By Crow, Kelly, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 2009  The biennial is the Olympics of the contemporary art world, a century-old tradition in which countries send in their best artists to exhibit in pavilions and palazzos across the city. The curators' winning proposal involved transforming the two pavilions into "homes" for imaginary art collectors, complete with faux doorbells, mailboxes and actors hired to play real-estate agents.
