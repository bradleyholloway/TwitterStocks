 Corporate News: For Data Center, Google Goes for the Cold   By Grunberg, Sven; Rolander, Niclas, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 2011  Google Inc.'s opening of a 200 million euro ($273 million) server hall in Hamina, Finland, is boosting Scandinavian hopes that other big Internet companies will choose to build data centers in the region, attracted by its cold climate and low electricity prices.
