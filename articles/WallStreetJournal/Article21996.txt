 Review of the stock market: Year-end 1994: Analysts figure either stocks or bonds must give ground to the other this year   By Kansas, Dave, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1995  Even though the bond market took a drubbing in 1994 as interest rates shot up, stocks finished mostly up and the Dow Jones Industrial Average gained 80.35 points, or 2.1%, on the year to 3834.44. But analysts say that the divergence between stocks and bonds is a warning sign to stock investors for 1995.
