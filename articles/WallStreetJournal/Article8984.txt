 Service Corp. Plans to Buy Rival Equity   By By Robert Tomsho, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 1998  Service Corp., Houston, the world's largest operator of cemeteries and funeral homes, also will assume about $300 million in debt in the transaction, which reunites it with a company it spun off in 1990.  Under the deal, Equity Corp. shareholders will receive $27 of Service Corp. shares for each of the 22 million common shares outstanding they own. Service Corp., which has about 261.8 million common shares outstanding, will account for the transaction as a pooling-of-interests and anticipates closing in the fourth quarter, pending regulatory approval.
