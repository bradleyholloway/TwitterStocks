 No headline available   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 7, 2005  Your page-one article "Popaganda: Sparking Debate, Radio Czar Retools Government Media" (June 20) got nowhere near the real crux of the controversy over Norman Pattiz's leadership in transforming U.S. government broadcasting into pop music stations. It isn't a matter of AM or FM frequencies, or of reforming a supposedly sclerotic government agency, the Voice of America, or of his munificence to the Democratic Party.
