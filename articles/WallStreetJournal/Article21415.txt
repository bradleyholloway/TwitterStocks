 Coor's Low-Priced Beer Exceeds Sales Projections at Rivals' Expense   By Charlier, Marj, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 2, 1990  The Adolph Coors Co has sold 60% more of its new, low-priced beer, Keystone, than was predicted when the beer came out in Sep 1989. Company officials claim that the marketing campaign, as well as trouble at other breweries, led to the success of the brand.
