 Your Money Matters: Money Funds May Offer Skimpy Yields, but Analysts Made a Good Case for Them   By Jasen, Georgette, The Wall Street Journal,  May 7, 1992  Investment counselors advise caution to people impatient with the low yields of money market funds and certificates of deposit, rather than a rush to the stock and bond markets. As interest rates will probably rise, bond funds should be approached cautiously; also, stock funds should be selected with care (or not at all by people who will need their money in the short term). The current money fund yields are not so low given the low level of inflation.
