 Arts & Entertainment: Experiments In Love and Film --- The Wondrous Work of Movie Explorers Mary Ellen Bute and Ted Nemeth   By Bennett, Bruce, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 2010  Featuring a never-before-seen version of Bute's feature-length 1965 Cannes entry "Passages from Finnegan's Wake," as well as the 1956 live-action short film "The Boy Who Saw Through" -- both restored through a joint effort between the Yale Film Study Center and the National Film Preservation Fund -- the three-day event sheds light on one of the lesser-known chapters in the city's long history of independent filmmaking.
