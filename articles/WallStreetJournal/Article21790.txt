 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 2, 1993  Envirotest Systems Corp. -- $125 million issue of senior subordinated notes, due April 1, 2003, priced at par to yield 9.625%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Rated single-B-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-B-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp. The notes are noncallable for five years.  Hertz Corp. -- $100 million of notes, due April 1, 2000, priced as 6.5% at 99.559 to yield 6.58%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 0.90 percentage point above the Treasury seven-year note. Rated Baa1 by Moody's and triple-B-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc.  Niagara Mohawk Power Corp. -- $295 million two-part issue of first-mortgage bonds priced through dealers led by Goldman, Sachs & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch. The first part, consisting of $85 million of bonds, due April 1, 2003, was priced as 6 7/8% at 99.713 to yield 6.915%. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 87.5 basis points above the Treasury 10-year note. The second part, consisting of $210 million of bonds, due April 1, 2024, was priced as 7 7/8% at 98.123 to yield 8.04%. The bonds, noncallable for 10 years, were priced at a spread of 110 basis points above the Treasury 30-year bond. The offering is rated Baa2 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P.
