 A British Switch on 'Swaps' Puts Banks in Uproar   By Bray, Nicholas, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 21, 1991  Britain's unpopular poll tax may be on its way out, but another row over municipal financing continues to dog Prime Minister John Major. Nearly 80 British and foreign banks face the loss of 600 million British pounds ($1 billion) in gains they thought they had made on interest-rate swap contracts.
