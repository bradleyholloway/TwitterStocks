 EU Gets Rug Pulled Out On Unifying Merger Rules --- Blanket Authority to Fight Hostile Takeover Bids Is New German Demand   By By Philip Shishkin, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 2001  Germany's sudden about-face puts into question the future of the pan-EU takeover code. Other national governments of the EU could technically overrule Germany and adopt a less-demanding position, which must be approved by the European Parliament. But even if other EU governments spurn Germany's demand, the parliament -- if history is any guide -- appears unlikely to approve the code with shareholder-friendly provisions. In a December interim vote, the parliament's deputies backed a provision strongly resembling Germany's new stance.
