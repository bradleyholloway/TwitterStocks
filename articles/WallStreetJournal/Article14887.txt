 General Mills Expects European Sales to Hit $1 Billion by Year 2000 ---- Special to The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 4, 1991  Brewster Atwater Jr., chairman and chief executive of General Mills Inc., said he expects the consumer foods company to have European sales of about $1 billion by the year 2000.  Just two years ago, General Mills took steps to break into Europe by creating Cereal Partners Worldwide S.A., or CPW, a France-based joint venture with Nestle S.A. to develop its cereal operations in Europe. CPW then bought the British breakfast cereals business of Ranks Hovis McDougall PLC that resulted in a newly formed partnership with Nestle called Cereal Partners UK, a separate business unit from CPW.
