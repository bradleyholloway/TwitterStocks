 TV: Nazi Art, Popular Jocks, Democracy Update   By By Dorothy Rabinowitz, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 1990  Friday, June 22, 9-10 p.m. EDT, on PBS (PBS air dates and times vary, so check local listings): "Evening at Pops." Melissa Manchester sings Gershwin, along with an assortment of torch songs and blues, accompanied by saxophonist Benny Carter. The centerpiece of this production is their joint rendering of the immortal "Sophisticated Lady."  Sunday, June 24, 8-9 p.m. EDT, on Arts & Entertainment: "Art in the Third Reich." The first of a two-part series on Nazi painting, sculpture and architecture. A detailed look at how fascist culture and ideology found its expression in all forms of art in the Reich, including furniture design. Nazi art, the documentary notes, drew from the styles of the period, in a sinister wedding of ideology and fashion. Repeats at midnight. (Part two airs Sunday July 1, 8-9 p.m. and repeats at midnight.)
