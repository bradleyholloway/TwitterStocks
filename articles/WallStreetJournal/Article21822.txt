 GM Says Plant In Finland to Make 6,000 Automobiles   By By Timothy Aeppel, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 2, 1993  GM Europe inherited a production agreement with the Finnish industrial group when it bought 50% of Sweden's Saab Automobile AB in 1990. Saab-Scania AB has a 50% stake in Saab Automobile. GM used Valmet's plant in Uusikaupunki, Finland, to assemble Opel Calibra models in 1991 and 1992. But with the European car market in free fall, GM told its Finnish partner in November it was concentrating all Calibra production at the company's plant in Ruesselsheim, Germany, in 1993.
