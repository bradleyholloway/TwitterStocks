 Crude Slips as Iran Fears Ease And Natural Gas Tumbles 7.2%   By Leah McGrath Goodman, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 7, 2006  The move raised the stakes in the West's diplomatic confrontation with Iran, prompting Tehran to threaten immediate retaliation. While some traders fret that a conflict with Iran could lead to a disruption in oil supplies, others said the market may have been inclined to sell off once the IAEA acted as expected.  Iran said Sunday it had ended voluntary cooperation with the IAEA and planned to start uranium-enrichment activities, which could pave the way for the development of nuclear weapons. Iran said it would bar any surprise inspections of its nuclear facilities by the IAEA -- something it had allowed.
