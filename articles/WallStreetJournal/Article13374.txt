 Politics & Economics: In Brief   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 6, 2007  The U.S. and Malaysia resumed free-trade talks under the cloud of a disputed gas deal with Iran. House Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman Tom Lantos last week urged the suspension of the negotiations until Malaysia halts a $16 billion deal to develop gas fields in Iran. Malaysian Trade Minister Rafidah Aziz said she would drop the talks if asked to scrap the Iran deal. The opening up of Malaysia's services and government contracts are also sticking points for the U.S., as are labor and environmental issues, said Deputy U.S. Trade Representative Karan Bhatia....
