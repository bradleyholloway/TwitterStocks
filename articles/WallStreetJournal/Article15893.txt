 Chase Told to Foreclose On Accounts of Firm In China Road Venture   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 4, 2000  A federal judge directed Chase Manhattan Corp. to foreclose on Traffic Stream Infrastructure Ltd.'s collateral accounts after the Hong Kong company defaulted on its $119 million debt issue.  Traffic Stream issued the 14.25% senior secured notes in May 1998 to finance a business venture involving the construction of toll roads in China. As reported, Merrill Lynch & Co. was the lead manager for the offering.
