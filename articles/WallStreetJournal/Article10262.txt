 Food & Drink -- Eating Out: Cheating on My Oven --- When the trophy range stops working, what's to miss?   By Sokolov, Raymond, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 7, 2009  If we did a lot of baking, or wanted to roast several chicken at a time, then we'd be better off with our old oven, assuming it worked. and during a power failure, the Breville wouldn't function, but then neither would the big oven, which runs on gas but won't ignite without electricity. Since we're out in the boonies, power failures are not infrequent, and part of our motivation for buying the $3,500 all-gas range in the first place had been the idea that it would heat up when the electricity was out.
