 GM Daewoo Stake Is Being Acquired By Shanghai Firm   By By Karby Leggett, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 2002  The agreement marks the first time that a Chinese company has invested in an overseas auto company. Under terms, Shanghai Automotive is expected to pay about $60 million for the stake in GM Daewoo Automotive & Technology Co., which GM formed out of the former Daewoo Motor Co. after the South Korean auto maker collapsed several years ago. GM purchased three of the Korean auto company's main production facilities for $251 million, giving it a 42.1% stake. Suzuki Motor Corp. will hold an additional 14.9%, while Daewoo creditors will hold 33%.
