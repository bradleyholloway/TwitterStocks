 Court Limits Inside-Trading Prosecution   By Lambert, Wade, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 1990  A three-judge panel of the US 2nd Circuit Court of Appeals reversed the conviction of former stockbroker Robert Chestman and set limits on who can be prosecuted in insider trading cases. The panel also threw out SEC Rule 14e-3 which is commonly used against insider trading defendants who aren't company insiders.
