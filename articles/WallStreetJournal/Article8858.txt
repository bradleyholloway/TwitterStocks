 On the move: West German riches are luring Easterners desperate for work   By Steinmetz, Greg, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 7, 1997  More than one million people have fled the former East Germany since the border with West Germany opened in 1989, or approximately one of every 16 East Germans. More people have left eastern Germany in the seven years since reunification than in the seven years prior to the Wall's construction.
