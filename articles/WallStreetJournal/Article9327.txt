 Europe Gives Muted Applause To Mergers Bill   By By Paul Hofheinz, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 2001  Germany was the holdout. Last month, Berlin shocked Brussels by withdrawing its support for the takeover bill, complaining that since other countries had antitakeover measures on their books, it shouldn't have to force its companies to stop using poison pills. EU officials decided to continue negotiating despite Germany's stance, which earlier had seemed to doom the entire project. The five-year compromise was offered to win the support of German parliamentarians, though it's unclear it will win over Berlin.
