 White House will propose its own seven-year budget   By Georges, Christopher; Stout, Hilary, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 1995  The White House, in an effort to jump-start the stalled budget talks, will offer a new seven-year balanced budget plan the week of Dec 4, 1995, Chief of Staff Leon Panetta said. The administration plan could prove a significant breakthrough in the talks. Officials said on Dec 5 that the latest draft calls for spending $70 billion less over seven years on domestic, annually appropriated spending than the nine-year plan outlined by President Clinton in the summer.
