 REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial): German Question Mark   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 7, 1988  Tomorrow West Germany is expected to approve its less-than-major tax reform. Coalition members had been fighting for weeks to make their budget calculations fit the reform, struggling like a group of Hausfrauen trying to fit a small sheet on a big bed. The statesmen finally smoothed out the project by deciding to raise an additional billion dollars with a tax on natural gas. What the German economy really needs, though, is a more thorough housecleaning.
