 Government Policy Distorts Cross-Border Labor Market   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 2006  Ours and the Mexican government's policies have distorted the rationalization of cross-border price and labor markets, contributing to if not causing the current crisis. If we cease subsidies to labor- intensive production, capital-intensive innovations will replace that production or it will migrate or be outsourced, reducing the U.S. demand for cheap labor and increasing it elsewhere. Simultaneously, business and border enforcement will cause remaining unskilled labor incomes in the U.S. to rise above the subsistence level.
