 Agencies Urge Greater Disclosure In the Sale of Variable Annuities   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 2004  Advocates of variable annuities say they offer better features than mutual funds. Assets in variable annuities swelled 20% in 2003 to $985 billion, on sales of $126 billion, according to the Web site of the National Association for Variable Annuities. A representative of the industry association said the SEC and NASD report didn't indicate that abuses were widespread in the variable annuities and insurance industry.
