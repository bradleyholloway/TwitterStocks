 Money balks: Overseas investors find Serbian leaders corralling economy   By Nelson, Mark M, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 6, 1996  Just over a month after the suspension of UN sanctions imposed on Serbia for its role in the Bosnian war, Serbia is fast retreating into the closed economy of its Communist past. President Slobodan Milosevic has replaced the sanctions with restrictions of his own, with imports and exports strictly regulated and key industries controlled by the state. The negative impact of the policy for foreign investment and the Bosnian peace process is analyzed.
