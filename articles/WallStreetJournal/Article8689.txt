 Labor Secretary Reich proposes giving tax breaks to firms that help workers   By Burkins, Glenn; Calmes, Jackie, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 7, 1996  In a speech on Feb 6, 1996, Labor Secretary Robert Reich proposed reducing or even eliminating income taxes for corporations that offer certain education and job-placement services to employees. Reich characterized his idea as a "modest-proposal" that could give companies an incentive to help workers displaced by corporate setbacks.
