 A Remembrance of Things Past--The Nixon Tapes II   By Perry, James M; Abramson, Jill, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 1991  The release of 47 more hours of tape-recorded conversations between former President Richard Nixon and his top aides, most of them made in the Oval Office at the White House, have reignited the Watergate scandal. One of the ironies of the tapes was that Nixon established the taping system himself so that a record of his presidency could be created.
