 Legal Beat: Soldiers Who Opposed   By Lambert, Wade; Hayes, Arthur S, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 3, 1992  Several military personnel who opposed the Persian Gulf War remain in prison for desertion or missing a troop movement, while others who sought conscientious-objector status before war was declared are still waiting for hearings on their requests. Lawyers for the soldiers alleged that their clients received harsher sentences than others who were charged with the same violations but who did not claim to be conscientious objectors.
