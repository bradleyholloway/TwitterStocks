 Consumer Prices Post a Modest 0.2% Rise For September as Energy Costs Decline --- Stock, Bond Markets Cheer, But Wary Analysts Cite Gains in Wholesale Index   By By Hilary Stout, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 20, 1989  Consumer prices rose a surprisingly moderate 0.2% in September, pushed up mostly by a jump in clothing costs, the Labor Department reported.  Energy costs, which drove wholesale prices up sharply during the month, continued to decline at the retail level, pulling down transportation and helping to ease housing costs.  But many economists were not nearly as jubilant. The climb in wholesale energy prices is certain to push up retail energy prices in the next few months, they warned. They also said the dollar is leveling off after a rise this summer that helped to reduce the prices of imported goods.
