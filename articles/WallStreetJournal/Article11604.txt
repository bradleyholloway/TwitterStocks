 Doing Business Still Means Doing Time for Some in Russia   By Banerjee, Neela, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 1992  Russia's prisons continue to be filled with convicted entrepreneurs, with many of the estimated 100,000 people interred for practicing what would be normal commerce elsewhere. Critics said the proposed reforms of President Boris Yeltsin do not carry enough power to stop the arrests and convictions.
