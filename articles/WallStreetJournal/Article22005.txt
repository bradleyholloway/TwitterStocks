 Review of the stock market: Year-end 1994: Markets met with frustration as U.S. rates cast shadows overseas   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1995  Led by the US, monetary policy shifted in 1994 to address inflation fears and key markets worldwide, including the UK, Germany and Hong Kong, posted declines. Mexico's market plunged 40% in dollar terms on political and currency turmoil, while Japanese stocks rose a less-than-expected 13%. A market-by-market summary of world stocks is given.
