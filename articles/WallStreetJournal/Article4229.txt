 Letters to the Editor: Whose 'Say' Can You See?   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1986  But now look at the powers you somehow neglected to mention -- the powers conferred on Congress. In addition to shared responsibility for the making of treaties and appointment of ambassadors, Congress, under Article I, Section 8, has the following exclusive powers (subject only to the general right of presidential veto): to provide for the common defense; to regulate commerce with foreign nations; to define and punish piracies and felonies committed on the high seas and offenses against the law of nations; to declare war, and to grant letters of marque and reprisal; to raise and support armies; to provide and maintain a navy.
