 Greenspan Gives Stocks, Bonds A Moderate Lift   By By Suzanne McGee, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 4, 1997  A year after delivering a warning about "irrational exuberance" in valuing financial assets, Mr. Greenspan didn't address any concerns about the U.S. stock market. That sent a sigh of relief rippling across Wall Street, where traders had fretted the central-bank chief might dent a still-fragile stock market rebound by pointing out potential pressures on profits or addressing valuations.
