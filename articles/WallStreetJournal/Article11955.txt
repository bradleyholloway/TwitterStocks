 People patterns: Elderly don't see themselves as pigeons   By Crispell, Diane, The Wall Street Journal,  May 5, 1995  According to a survey for AT&T by Princeton Survey Research Associates, the elderly are least likely to say they have been victims of telemarketing or other fraud. Eighteen percent of adults say they have been a victim of a major fraud, scam or swindle at some time, with 54% of all adults thinking that older people are most likely to be taken advantage of.
