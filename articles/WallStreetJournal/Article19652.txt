 Tax Law Offers Benefits to Real-Estate Investors   By By Ray A. Smith, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 4, 2003  -- Mixed Bag for REITs. Real-estate investment trusts don't qualify for the new law's reduction of corporate-dividend taxes because they generally don't pay corporate taxes. So investors in REIT stocks will continue to have to pay a maximum tax of 35% on a majority of REIT dividends, as opposed to the lower 15% tax rate investors in other sectors will pay under the new law. But REIT dividends will qualify for the lower 15% maximum rate under certain circumstances.
