 South Korea Growth In 1st-Period GNP Topped Expectations ---- Special to The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  May 25, 1990  SEOUL, South Korea -- South Korea recorded a larger-than-expected 10.3% growth in gross national product during the first three months of this year, according to the Bank of Korea.  The central bank's preliminary report said the nation's GNP in the first quarter of 1990 amounted to 28.4 trillion won ($40.46 billion), up 10.3% from the previous year's 25.7 trillion won, at 1985 constant prices. In the first quarter of last year, GNP rose 6.2%. The two-digit number is far higher than the government's original projection of 6.5% to 7%.
