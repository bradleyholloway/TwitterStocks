 Bush Wants to Bar Scholarships Based Entirely on Race   By By Hilary Stout, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 4, 1991  The Bush administration will propose federal regulations that prohibit colleges and universities from awarding scholarships to students based solely on their race.  The policy was formulated after seven months of study in an effort to quell a controversy that erupted a year ago when the head of the Education Department's civil rights office declared that race-exclusive scholarships were illegal under federal civil rights laws.  But the new regulations, which were first reported in the Washington Post and confirmed yesterday by Bush administration officials, affirm the civil rights office's earlier policy against race-exclusive scholarships. Administration officials contend, however, the regulations are intended as guidelines for colleges to develop financial aid programs that will attract minority students to their campuses.
