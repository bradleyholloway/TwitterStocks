 Treasury prices climb after Fed rate decision, but investors are likely to remain cautious   By Zuckerman, Gregory, The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 1997  The Federal Reserve's decision to leave interest rates unchanged lifted Treasury bond prices. In late trading on May 19, 1997, the price of the benchmark 30-year Treasury bond was up 8/32, or $2.50 for each $1,000 face amount, at 96 14/32. Its yield fell to 6.90% from 6.92%.
