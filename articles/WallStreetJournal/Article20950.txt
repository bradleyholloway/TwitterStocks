 Northern Indiana PS Discloses It Was Sued Over Coal-Supply Pact   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 3, 1986  HAMMOND, Ind. -- Northern Indiana Public Service Co. disclosed in its annual report that it was sued for more than $100 million for allegedly breaking a coal-supply contract.  The utility said Colorado Westmoreland Inc., a unit of Philadelphia-based Westmoreland Coal Co., claimed in a suit in federal court here that the utility failed to live up to a contract to buy coal from Colorado Westmoreland.
