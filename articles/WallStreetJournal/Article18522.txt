 Long yields hit highest levels since mid-August on fresh reports signaling economic growth   By Vogel, Thomas T, Jr; Colter, Gene, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 4, 1994  Yields on long-term bonds were pushed to their highest levels since mid-Aug 1993 on Jan 3, 1994 amid economic reports showing the US economy is strengthening. The Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond plunged more than one point to 97 22/32, pushing the yield up from 6.34% the day before to 6.42%.
