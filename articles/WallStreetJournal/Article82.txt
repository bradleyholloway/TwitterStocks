 Small Business (A Special Report): The Idea Factory --- Good News for Inventors   By By Paul M. Barrett, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 24, 1989  It is the Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit, formed by Congress in 1982. Back then there was little uniformity among regional appeals courts in deciding patent cases -- and growing worry that intellectual property rights weren't being sufficiently respected at a time when foreign competition was hammering U.S. industry. In its short life, the CAFC has been remarkably successful in calming those fears.
