 French vote underlines shift in Europe   By Kamm, Thomas; Lavin, Douglas, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 3, 1997  With Lionel Jospin's appointment as French prime minister following the Socialist Party's stunning electoral victory, the left is clearly Europe's dominant political force at a crucial time in the continent's race toward a common currency. With Tony Blair's victory in the UK, left-wing parties control or are part of 13 governments out of 15 in the EU. Only Germany and Spain are holdouts. The impact of the leftward lean on the EU's political and economic agenda is analyzed.
