 Census Forms Work Hard to Find Proper Way to Identify Hispanics   By By Eduardo Porter, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 21, 2003  Latinos failed to find themselves among the options. They ticked the Hispanic option in the ethnicity question. But when it came to race, 15.4 million of them, over 40% of the nation's total, chose "some other race," and three million more ticked "some other race" plus one or more of the available races. In fact, 97% of those ticking "other race" were Hispanic. Many wrote in some variation of "Hispanic" again on the form.
