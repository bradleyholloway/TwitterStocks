 India's budget keeps reforms on track   By Karp, Jonathan; Sharma, Sumit, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 3, 1997  Blending socialist subsidies with steep tax cuts, India has assembled a $64.7 billion budget that attempts to stimulate growth and open the country to greater competition and freer trade. But some businesspeople say the plan still sidesteps tough measures needed to lower the fiscal deficit and attract direct foreign investment.
