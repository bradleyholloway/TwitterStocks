 Bush Poses Changes to Medicaid To Give States Greater Flexibility   By By Sarah Lueck, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 6, 2001  The Medicaid initiative is based on a proposal by the governors' group, which has said states could expand health coverage if given more freedom from federal rules. States also have been scrambling to figure out how to deal with the rising costs of Medicaid, which accounts for about 20% of their total spending.  Critics said the move could hurt the 12 million low-income people who fall into the optional Medicaid category. "This is the worst of both worlds: no new federal money for the states and dropping the basic protections for poor people in Medicaid," said Rep. Henry Waxman, (D., Calif.), a leading congressional advocate of Medicaid expansion. "This is a policy calculated to explode."
