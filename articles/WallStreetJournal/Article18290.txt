 Crop Futures Jump on Belief Bush Will Offer Additional Food Aid to Former Soviet Republics   By Kilman, Scott, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 4, 1992  Crop futures prices responded positively Feb 3, 1992 amid speculation that President Bush is likely to offer additional food aid to the Commonwealth of Independent States. The wheat contract for March delivery rose $.0775 a bushel to settle at $4.48 on the CBOT. Commodity market activity for the day in energy, coffee and sugar is given.
