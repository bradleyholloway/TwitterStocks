 Cotton Prices Jump Daily Limit on News Report That Sharply Cut Estimates of China's Crop   By Raghavan, Anita, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 1992  A report that China's 1992-1993 cotton crop would be 19.6 million bales, down from an earlier forecast of 26 million bales, sent cotton futures soaring the daily limit of two cents a pound. The Dec 1992 contract rose to $.5493 a pound and the Mar 1993 contract rose to $.5503 a pound. Elsewhere, gold fell along with crude oil, heating oil and wheat futures.
