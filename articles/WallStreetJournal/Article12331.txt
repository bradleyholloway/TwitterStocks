 Catalog Critic: Terrycloth Indulgence: Robes to Go   By By Eileen O'Reilly, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 1998  Most of the terry robes we ordered had similar features: the shawl collar, two pockets, tailored or bell cuffs and a wraparound belt. What set them apart from one another was the quality of the cotton and the lining. The long-fibered Egyptian cotton robes from Bra Smyth and Cuddledown of Maine tended to be softer and lighter; only the Cuddledown and Chambers robes were lined with terrycloth, a must when you are soaking wet.
