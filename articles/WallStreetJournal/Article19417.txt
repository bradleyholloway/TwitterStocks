 More Great Moments in Street-Speak   By By Jonathan Clements, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 2001  As I have noted in earlier columns devoted to Wall Street lingo, professional and amateur investors don't always say what they really mean, so translations are included. The list was put together with help from investment experts William Bernstein, Kevin Bernzott, John Rekenthaler and Meir Statman.  -- "It's a sucker's rally": Please, please, please be a sucker's rally.  -- "We're waiting for the transportation index to confirm the rise in the Dow industrials": Have you ever noticed how Wall Streeters, like the British monarch, always refer to themselves as "we"?
