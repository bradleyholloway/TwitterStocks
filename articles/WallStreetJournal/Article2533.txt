 World Bank's offering in German marks shows global bonds' appeal to yield-hungry investors   By Scism, Leslie; Vogel, Thomas T, Jr, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 21, 1993  On Oct 20, 1993 the World Bank reported enthusiasm in response to the first global-bond issue denominated in German marks by selling three billions marks ($1.83 billion) of the 10-year notes priced to yield 5.9%. In the US Treasury market, the 30-year bond rose more than 1/4 point to 105 31/32 for a yield of 5.82%, down from the previous day's 5.84%.
