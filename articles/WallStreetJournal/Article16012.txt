 Berkshire's General Re Agrees to Sell Stake In Securities Business   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 5, 2001  Berkshire is the publicly traded holding company that serves as Warren Buffett's investment vehicle. Berkshire has been considering whether to sell the financial-products business since it bought the Stamford, Conn., parent -- General Re -- in a $16.9 billion stock transaction in December 1998.  Berkshire didn't disclose the size of the General Re Securities Holdings business, in which Berkshire would keep a minority stake.
