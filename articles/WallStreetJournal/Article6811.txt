 Alcoa Helps Lift Dow; Lehman, WaMu Drop; UBS, Merrill, Citi Join Banks' Decline; Mosaic Jumps 5.7%   By Curran, Rob, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 2008  Commodity stocks such as Alcoa helped the Dow Jones Industrial Average recover some losses, but the credit crunch burned again on Wall Street, with losses for Lehman Brothers Holdings, and on Main Street, with fears about Washington Mutual. It's going to be a matter of, 'Is it enough?' Bears continued to circle Lehman Brothers on the options market, where 214,000 contracts for the right to sell the stock traded about 50,000 more than the calls to buy the stock.
