 Radio Tunes Out Google In Rare Miss for Web Titan   By Vascellaro, Jessica E, The Wall Street Journal,  May 12, 2009  Several years ago, Google began discussing a bold idea: creating a "dashboard" for chief marketing officers, a tool to let them measure, compare and spend dollars across print, radio, TV and the Internet. A Google engineering executive brokered an introduction to the Steelberg brothers, who had founded dMarc Broadcasting Inc. The three-year-old company had developed technology that automated the task of transmitting ads to radio stations, scheduling them and keeping track of when they ran.
