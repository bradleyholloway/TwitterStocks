 Getting Going: How to Avoid Making Rookie Mistakes   By Clements, Jonathan, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 4, 2004  Maybe it isn't any great surprise that folks get it wrong. After all, we don't get any practice. Instead, we make the choice just once, usually based on hearsay and gut instinct.  "In one large plan I looked at, 65% of the participants didn't make any trades during the four years I studied," says Julie Agnew, a finance professor at the College of William and Mary in Williamsburg, Va. "That's a clear sign of inertia."  Do we really know how to find a good adviser? It's something most folks don't get a lot of practice at, and if they do, that suggests they didn't do a very good job on the previous occasions.
