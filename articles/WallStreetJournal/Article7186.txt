 Corporate News: Oil Drillers to Feel Pinch From Crude's Fall   By Gold, Russell; Gilbert, Daniel, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 10, 2011  Thanks to the high crude price over the past year, peaking at $113.93 in April, many energy companies decided to shift more of their resources toward drilling for oil in the U.S. That bet was reinforced by the relatively low price of natural gas, which has hovered around $4.25 per million British thermal units since the beginning of year, making oil a profitable option by comparison.
