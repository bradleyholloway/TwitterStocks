 Letters to the Editor: World Bank Leads Free-Market Reform   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 6, 1987  In his Sept. 23 editorial-page article "World Bank Confidentially Damns Itself," James Bovard quotes extensively from several of my reports to support his assertion that the World Bank's structural-adjustment lending (SAL) programs are "a dismal failure." Neither my studies, nor any of the others he cites, say this. In fact, no dispassionate observer could make such an argument. The SAL programs have been in operation for only a few years, detailed impact studies are sparse and unambiguous measures of performance do not exist.
