 Current-Account Surplus In Japan Declined in May   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 6, 1987  Japan's surplus on its current account, the broadest measure of the nation's trade, narrowed in seasonally adjusted terms in May from the previous month and also declined from the year-earlier period.  The Finance Ministry said the adjusted current-account surplus narrowed to $7.07 billion in May from $7.43 billion in April. Before adjustment, the surplus stood at $7.09 billion, declining from $7.65 billion a year earlier.
