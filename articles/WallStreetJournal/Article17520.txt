 American Monitor Says That Fiscal '86 May Be Profitable   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 1985  American Monitor said backlog and estimated contract obligations for delivery of its automated blood analyzers exceed $12 million -- double the order rate for the entire fiscal year ended June 30, 1984. "Order entries so far this fiscal year show a continued emergence from the depressed period of 1984," said Jerry W. Denney, chairman and chief executive officer.
