 Big Airlines Near White House Pact On Fliers' Rights   By By Scott McCartney, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 1999  Major airlines are close to an agreement with the Clinton administration on a package of passenger-rights reforms that would require better service during lengthy delays and loosen rules on nonrefundable tickets, people familiar with the talks said.  Negotiations between the airlines and government regulators continued late last night, people involved said. Congressional leaders who proposed passenger-rights legislation were also involved. The talks stemmed from a regularly scheduled meeting of airline chief executives at the Air Transport Association, an industry trade group.
