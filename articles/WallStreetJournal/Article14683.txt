 REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial): Goodbye to Europessimism   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 1990  "Equities Soar to Record Level on Optimistic World Outlook," proclaimed a headline in London's Financial Times yesterday, with charts to record the sharp new year's rise on stock exchanges in London, Frankfurt, Tokyo and New York. The Times of London gives Ronald Reagan and Margaret Thatcher, two "great crusading political advocates," credit for giving "momentum to a tide of political pluralism and economic liberalization that has swept the shores of all the continents." Effusive? Perhaps to some American ears, but the Times saw no need for constraint.
