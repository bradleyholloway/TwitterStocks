 MarketBeat: U.S., Europe Safe Havens in Crisis   By Kansas, Dave, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 6, 2011  Higher oil prices lead to higher energy costs, which can crimp consumer spending on nonenergy items. Since consumer activity makes up nearly 70% of U.S. economic activity, a severe spike in oil prices would have negative implications for the nascent economic recovery and could stoke inflation.
