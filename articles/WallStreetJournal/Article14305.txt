 Growth of U.S. Families Headed by Women Slows   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 5, 1986  The number of female-headed families grew at a 16% rate between 1980 and 1985, down from a 22% pace in the 1975 to 1980 period and 30% between 1970 and 1975, the Census report said. Families headed by black women increased at an even slower pace during the first half of the 1980s, compared with the early 1970s.
