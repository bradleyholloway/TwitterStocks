 Two Questions: Do I Buy or Sell, And What Can I Do About Fleas?   By By Allanna Sullivan, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1985  That type of talent is available on the "Hotline of Champions," a telephone advisory service on the likely direction of the stock, bond, gold and monetary markets. Callers receive a brief, tape-recorded message, updated twice a week, offering the consensus opinion of some 15 amateur and professional traders.  The traders are among the top scorers in three annual trading contests sponsored by the Financial Traders Association in Marina del Rey, Calif. At least 80% of contest participants work in the investment business, says Norm Zadeh, who runs the association. An elite group he calls "star traders" earn at least $3 million a year trading for their own account.
