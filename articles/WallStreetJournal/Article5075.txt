 New Securities Issues [4]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 1994  ABN Amro Bank NV -- $100 million of medium-term notes due Jan. 12, 1996, priced as 4.15s at 99.873 to yield 4.217%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 12 basis points above Treasurys. Rated double-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co.  Federal Farm Credit Banks Funding Corp. -- $75 million of medium-term notes due Jan. 16, 1996, priced at par to yield 4.125%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of one basis point above Treasurys. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.  Federal National Mortgage Association -- $1.7 billion of stripped mortgage-backed securities, Trust 254, backed by the agency's 30-year, 7.5% mortgage securities. The collateral has a weighted average coupon rate of 7.96% and weighted average remaining term to maturity of 20.7 years. Underwriters include Bear, Stearns & Co.; J.P. Morgan Securities Inc.; Prudential Securities Inc.; UBS Securities Inc.; Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.; Goldman, Sachs & Co.; Greenwich Capital Markets Inc., Kidder, Peabody & Co.; PaineWebber Inc. and Merrill Lynch & Co.
