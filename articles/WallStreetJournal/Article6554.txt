 Magazine Prizes Bestowed on Time, ESPN and Others   By Ellison, Sarah, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 2006  Time's two awards are a shot in the arm for the news weekly, which last won a general-excellence award in 1985. Time is the largest magazine by circulation in Time Warner Inc.'s Time Inc. division, with a print circulation of more than four million.  A handful of publications won awards by the pair. Harper's, New York magazine, The New Yorker, Rolling Stone and Esquire each won two awards, in addition to Time. The Virginia Quarterly Review, a small literary periodical, also won two awards. The Atlantic Monthly, which received eight nominations, came away empty-handed.  General Excellence (100,000 to 250,000 circulation): Harper's
