 Religious Turf Dispute Extends To New Battleground: Cyberspace   By By Rebecca Quick, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 1998  Jews for Jesus, a nonprofit religious group of Jews who have embraced Christianity, was powerfully perturbed last month when it learned that the Internet address "www.jewsforjesus.org" had been registered by a follower of Outreach Judaism, a group that tries to bring Jews back into the fold.  The site -- which prods readers to "come home" to Judaism -- links to the home page for Outreach Judaism, where the teachings of Jews for Jesus are disputed. Jews for Jesus -- which has its own Web site at the address "www.jews-forjesus.org" -- learned of the new site when people trying to reach its real home page stumbled across the impostor.
