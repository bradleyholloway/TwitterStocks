 Mutual Effort to Sustain Forests and Communities   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 5, 2005  The issues involved in the Endangered Species Act are more complex than Kimberley A. Strassel acknowledged in her Oct. 19 editorial-page commentary "Owls of Protest." Anyone flying into the Northwest can see that the region's forests have been logged to excess. Clear-cuts and logging roads abound. The destruction of important habitat for endangered species such as spotted owls and marbled murrelets is not the only troubling result. This past forest "management" dumped sediments into spawning beds for wild salmon species, which are now endangered.
