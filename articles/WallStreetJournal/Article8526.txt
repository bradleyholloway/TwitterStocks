 Special interests, with backing of GOP, defeat numerous White House efforts   By Wartzman, Rick, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 1994  President Clinton has been unable to keep his campaign pledge to "break the stranglehold" that special interests have on the capital in part because of a GOP strategy to prevent the Democrats from scoring any legislative triumphs before the Nov 1994 elections. As a result, interest groups intent on preserving the status quo have been handed victory after victory. The effects that proposals for lobbying reform could have on such special interest victories are discussed.
