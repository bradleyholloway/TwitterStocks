 Hillary Clinton Tries to Calm the FALN Storm   By By Jeanne Cummings, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 7, 1999  Hillary Rodham Clinton spent Labor Day at Camp David telephoning New York officials to try to calm a storm she set off this weekend by abruptly withdrawing support for her husband's clemency offer to 16 members of a Puerto Rican terrorist group.  Of primary importance to the first lady is mending relations with leaders of the Hispanic community, one of the Democratic Party's most loyal constituencies and a group Mrs. Clinton will need if, as expected, she formally enters New York's Senate race next year.
