 Sweat, Sabers and the 'Mexican Wave'; Eschewing Olympic Press Passes and Trying to Beat the Beijing Heat in the Stands, One Journal Reporter Files an Essay on Vacation Online edition  By Sanders, Peter, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 10, 2008  The stadium and its glorious framework of interconnected steel beams make for amazing architecture, good cocktail party conversation, television-friendly aerial shots, a big invoice from the general contractor and absolutely no ventilation. Call me weak, call me soft, call me Californian, but sitting at the opening ceremonies in a pool of my own liquefied salt not 100 feet from a powerful wind machine focused on keeping the Chinese flag (and absolutely nothing else) flapping at 85 miles an hour was a unique form of torture.
