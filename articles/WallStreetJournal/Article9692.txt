 Women's Mutual Vitriol Disheartening to Read   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 2004  I was dismayed, but not surprised, that the majority of the letters published in response to your article on the tribulations of professional women returning to work after staying home to raise their children ("Second Chances: After Years Off, Women Struggle To Revive Careers -- Resume Gaps, New Technology Make Transition Difficult" by Anne Marie Chaker and Hilary Stout, page one, May 6) were simplistic and unnecessarily scornful ("Women Discover the Rip Van Winkle Effect," Letters, May 20).
