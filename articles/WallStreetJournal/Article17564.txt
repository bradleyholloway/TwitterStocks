 U.S. Car Output Rose 6.5% in '85 From '84   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1986  Despite strong production, Big Three auto sales have been slumping since the expiration in September of a highly successful program of substantial consumer incentives. After subsequent, more limited incentives failed to generate big sales gains, General Motors Corp. Dec. 26 launched another broad program of low-interest-loan incentives in an attempt to pare bulging inventories. Ford Motor Co. followed with a similar program. Auto makers will report sales for mid-December today and for late December Monday.
