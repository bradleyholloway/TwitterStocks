 The outlook: Plans for big tax cuts are fast losing favor   By Murray, Alan, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 3, 1995  The latest entrant in the tax-cut bidding war comes from House Minority Leader Richard Gephardt, who during the week of Mar 27, 1995 unveiled his third tax proposal in as many months. This proposal calls for revenues to be cut by $25 billion over the next five years--far less than the $60 billion tax cut proposed by President Clinton and the $189 billion cut proposed by House Republicans.
