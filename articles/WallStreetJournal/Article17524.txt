 Ex-Aide at Nomura Charged With a Role In Fraudulent Trading   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 1985  The Securities and Exchange Commission charged a former manager at Nomura Securities International Inc. with participating in a scheme involving fraudulent trading in $19.8 million of securities.  In a complaint filed yesterday in federal court in New York, the SEC alleged that Michel Erlichson, former credit manager at Nomura, helped two customers place orders to purchase and sell the securities without delivering payment. The customers were Benjamin Chamis and Robert Malfi, owners of ITC Inc., a New York investment and tax counseling firm. The two men and their firm also were named as defendants in the complaint.
