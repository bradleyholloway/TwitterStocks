 Gas Can't Shake Its Cold   By Womack, Jason, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 10, 2010  Prices have fallen in five of the past six sessions, retreating nearly 13% in that stretch, as relatively high inventory has weighed on the market despite hot weather in parts of the U.S. Natural-gas inventories, which can be called upon in case of supply disruptions, stood at 2.948 trillion cubic feet as of July 30, some 8.1% above the five-year average, according to the latest data from the Energy Information Administration.
