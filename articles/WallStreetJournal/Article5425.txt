 Bond raters draw heat over Miami   By Gasparino, Charles, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1996  Both Moody's Investors Services Inc and Standard & Poor's Corp were caught unaware when Miami announced a whopping $68 million deficit on its $300 million budget in Sep 1996. Both rating agencies quickly downgraded the city's bonds to below-investment-grade (or junk) levels. Now fingers are pointing in every direction as the debate rages over who is to blame for the Miami mess.
