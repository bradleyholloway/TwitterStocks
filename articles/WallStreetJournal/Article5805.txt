 Pro-Forma Results for Disney's Go.com Show Loss for Its First Fiscal Quarter   By By Bruce Orwall, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 10, 2000  Walt Disney Co.'s Go.com tracking stock reported its first quarterly results with a familiar story for Internet companies: revenue increases coupled with big net losses that are projected to continue.  Go.com tracks the performance of Disney's Internet assets in the wake of the company's acquisition in November of Infoseek Corp. On a pro forma basis, Go.com, Sunnyvale, Calif., reported a net loss of $265.4 million, or $1.73 a share, on revenue of $125.6 million for the first fiscal quarter that ended Dec. 31. That compares with the pro forma loss of $233.6 million, or $1.52 cents a share, on revenue of $111.6 million for the previous year's quarter. The pro forma results assume Disney's acquisition of Infoseek occurred at the beginning of fiscal 1999.
