 Budget Office Accedes to Rise In IRS Funding --- Treasury, In Return, Agrees To Cut Fiscal '88 Money For the Customs Service   By By Paul Blustein, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1986  The Treasury Department won White House Budget Office approval to seek substantial increases in funding for multilateral development banks and the Internal Revenue Service, according to administration officials.  The agreement was reached yesterday after considerable fighting between the two agencies that was intensified by a dispute over the administration's economic forecast. At the insistence of Treasury Secretary James Baker, the new forecast was considerably less optimistic than earlier ones, causing distress at the budget office because a projection of slower economic growth results in a wider projected deficit.
