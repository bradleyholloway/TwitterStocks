 Program Trading Volume on Big Board Logs a Monthly High   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 21, 1989  Program trading volume in March on the New York Stock Exchange reached its highest monthly level since the exchange started reporting the figures, confirming that the disputed practice is on the rise.  Morgan Stanley & Co., a major program trader, said in February that it would resume index arbitrage trading -- the futures-related strategy accounting for half of program volume -- for its own account. The figures show that last month, Morgan Stanley tripled its index arbitrage volume from February, to an exchange-leading 31.9 million shares.
