 Legal beat: Mounting piles of deposition requests disrupt lives of chief executive officers   By Gerlin, Andrea, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 1994  The growing demands by plaintiffs' lawyers for depositions from the CEOs of the companies being sued are disrupting the lives and work of the company heads. In federal court and in California and New York state courts, the burden of proof rests on the plaintiff's attorney to establish need for a CEO's testimony. However, in many other state courts, little or no protection is given from deposition requests.
