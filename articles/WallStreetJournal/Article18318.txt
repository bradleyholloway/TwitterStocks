 In Canada, 1st-Period Profits Fell 21%, a Rebound from Losses in 4th Quarter   By Heinzl, Mark, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 1992  The profitability expectations for Canadian companies in the first quarter of 1992 are described. Corporate earnings showed signs of leveling off, but a flat economy continues to depress the outlook for healthy profits. A survey of 143 early-reporting Canadian companies showed a combined first-quarter profit of 742.2 million Canadian dollars ($623.5 million), down 21% from the first quarter of 1991.
