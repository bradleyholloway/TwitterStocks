 Two Men Convicted In Thwarted Scheme To Defraud 2 Banks   The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 1991  Michael Piervinanzi, of Queens, N.Y., and Daniel Tichio, of Staten Island, were convicted of conspiracy, wire fraud, bank fraud and money-laundering. They were accused of conspiring with several other defendants to arrange a fraudulent wire transfer for $14.2 million from an account at Irving Trust, now owned by Bank of New York, in July 1988 to an account in Grand Cayman Island.
