 Heavy going: Continental's CALite hits some turbulence in battling Southwest   By O Brian, Bridget, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 1995  Continental Airlines' strategy to become competitive with rival Southwest Airlines is examined. Over the past 18 months, Continental turned half of its 2,000 flights into a low-fare, short-haul service. However, Continental's "Lite" service has cost the carrier millions of dollars, has led to the ouster of its CEO and caused the carrier to drop Lite flights that are costing money.
