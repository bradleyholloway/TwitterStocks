 The guardians: New breed of investor brings more than cash to hopeful start-ups   By Mehta, Stephanie N, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 1997  Madhaven "M.R." Rangaswani hardly looks like a high-flying financier, but this 42-year-old former technology executive is representative of a new breed of prospectors scouring the US for promising companies to finance, nurture and take public. Known as "angels," these millionaires are responsible for pumping some $20 billion a year into small companies, and can offer valuable time and expertise to the startups.
