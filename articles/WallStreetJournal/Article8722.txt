 Sixteen Are Charged In Theft of $20 Million From Various Retailers   The Wall Street Journal,  May 7, 1996  The defendants, who allegedly stole the goods, sold them to fences, or middlemen, for 15% to 30% of their retail value. According to the indictment, the fences delivered the merchandise to a business in Stoughton, Mass., which purchased the goods, removed identifying information and shipped the items to distributors for the retail market.
