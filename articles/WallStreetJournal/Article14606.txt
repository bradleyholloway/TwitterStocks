 Some Myerson Kuhn Clients Questioned By U.S. About Firm's Billing Practices   By By Laurie P. Cohen, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 1989  Federal postal inspectors working with the U.S. attorney's office in Brooklyn have questioned at least two clients of the law firm Myerson & Kuhn about the firm's billing practices.  According to individuals familiar with the inquiries, investigators are seeking to determine whether Myerson & Kuhn has sent fraudulent bills through the mail to its clients.  John La Perla, an inspector with the Postal Inspection Service in Hicksville, N.Y., and at least one other inspector have contacted clients in the last month about Myerson & Kuhn's billing practices, according to individuals familiar with the inquiries. Mr. La Perla couldn't be reached for comment.
