 International Finance: At Crisis Juncture, RBS and Lloyds Diverge on Aid --- The Former Will Get Protection but Greater U.K. Oversight, and the Latter Will Go It Alone for Better or Worse   By Sara Schaefer Munoz; MacDonald, Alistair, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 2009  To avoid participating in the plan, Lloyds is expected to announce as early as Tuesday its effort to raise GBP 21 billion in capital to absorb losses on its own, through a GBP 13.5 billion rights issue, in which existing investors buy shares at a discount, and GBP 7.5 billion through the issuance of contingent capital, or securities convertible into equity if the bank hits trouble.
