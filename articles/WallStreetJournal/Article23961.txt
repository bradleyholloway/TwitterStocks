 Rule Over The Abuse Of IPOs Is Delayed   By Smith, Randall, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 2011  Remaining sections of the new Finra rule bar IPO allocations to the brokerage accounts of corporate executives if the brokerage firm has received compensation for investment-banking services from the company in the past 12 months, expects to do banking work for the company in the next three months or on condition of receiving future banking work. After the stock-market downturn following the dot-com bubble, regulators alleged that brokers at two firms, Credit Suisse First Boston and Citigroup, had engaged in spinning by improperly awarding hot IPO shares, some of which doubled or tripled in price on their first day of trading, to executives of investment-banking clients.
