 Aluminum Firms' Glut Problems Mount   By Milbank, Dana, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 21, 1992  The aluminum industry's market glut, caused by the former Soviet Union dumping about one million metric tons of aluminum onto world markets in 1991, is discussed. Even though the tonnage should lessen in 1992, the massive exports will continue to keep prices low, and if supplies don't drop substantially soon, Western producers will have to close more plants.
