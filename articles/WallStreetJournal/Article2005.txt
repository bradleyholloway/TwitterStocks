 Centennial Journal: 100 Years in Business --- Global Economics Goes Into the Woods, 1944   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 1989  EISENHOWER'S ARMIES WERE STILL FIGHTING out of Normandy when planners on both sides of the Atlantic began creating economic machinery for the postwar world. The United Nations was born early in 1944 and in July 730 delegates from 44 U.N. members packed into the rambling 485-room Mount Washington Hotel In Bretton Woods, N.H., to plan a new international economy.
