 Traders Take Profits After Rally   By Tan, Kopin, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 2004  The defensive bent was noticeable in National Semiconductor Corp., which will report earnings today. According to data from Schaeffer's Research, the ratio of outstanding puts accumulated to calls in the chipmaker has already crept up to about 1.02 from 0.65 on May 28. Yesterday, traders noticed some investors buying July puts for downside insurance through mid-July. Others sold June 22.50 calls, for instance, pocketing premiums and committing to sell shares if they surge above $22.50.
