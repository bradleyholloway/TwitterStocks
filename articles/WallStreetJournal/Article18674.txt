 Bond prices advance on positive inflation news; Dollar's continued weakness tempers market   By Pesek, William Jr; Penn, Allison, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 4, 1995  Bond prices advanced on Apr 3, 1995 as more data suggested slowing economic growth and a favorable inflation outlook. A further drop in the dollar, however, continued to temper the market's enthusiasm. The benchmark 30-year Treasury bond was up more than 3/8 point at 102 23/32. Its yield fell to nearly 7.4% from 7.43% on Mar 31.
