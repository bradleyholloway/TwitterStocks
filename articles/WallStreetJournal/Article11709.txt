 Crop futures settle lower as traders wonder what happens when food for Russia runs out   By Kilman, Scott, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 6, 1993  US crop prices fell on Apr 5, 1993 in the wake of President Clinton's offer of an $894 million food aid package for Russia. In CBOT trading, the wheat futures contract for May delivery fell $.045 to settle at $3.505 a bushel. In other commodity markets, precious metals futures and crude-oil futures prices fell.
