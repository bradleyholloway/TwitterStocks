 Commodity Prices Plunge   By Pleven, Liam, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 2011  If raw-materials prices continue to drop, it will ease pressure on companies grappling with higher production costs and on consumers reeling from $4-a-gallon gasoline and rising food prices. Silver's sudden reversal acted as a spark, igniting the concerns of investors that commodity prices had raced too far too fast, and had become detached from the fundamental forces of supply and demand.
