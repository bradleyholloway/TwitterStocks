 Business Brief: Sybase Affirms Database-Sector Woes, Warns of Weak 4th-Quarter Results   By By Bill Richards, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 1998  Sybase Inc. expects to report disappointing fourth-quarter earnings, which drove down its shares and underscored concerns about the database-software industry.  The Emeryville, Calif., company Friday said its quarterly results will range from a loss of seven cents a share to a profit of two cents a share. Following that news, Sybase's shares fell $3.375, or 25%, to $9.9375 in Nasdaq Stock Market composite trading Friday.  Sybase also warned that its revenue will fall below expectations, running between $245 million and $250 million. Wall Street had anticipated fourth-quarter revenue of about $265 million. In the year-ago fourth quarter, Sybase reported net income of $5 million, or seven cents a share, on revenue of $268 million.
