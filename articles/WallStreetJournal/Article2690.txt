 Tobacco industry, plaintiffs square off as cigarette suit is ruled class action   By Hwang, Suein L; Geyelin, Milo, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 21, 1995  The tobacco industry and a battallion of top plaintiffs' lawyers squared off for the biggest cigarette lawsuit ever, as a federal judge in New Orleans cleared a class action on behalf of every American ever allegedly addicted to cigarettes. Tobacco companies vowed to appeal the class-action ruling, warning it would create a nightmarish logjam of cases involving 40 million smokers or more.
