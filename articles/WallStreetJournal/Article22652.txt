 Car Makers Create Testing Guidelines For Side Air Bags   By By Rudy Kleysteuber, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 2, 2000  Side-impact crashes killed 9,482 people in 1998 -- almost a third of that year's crash fatalities. But like front air bags, side-impact air bags pose safety risks -- especially to children and smaller adults. To test these risks, car makers, air-bag suppliers and insurers developed crash-dummy tests and safety guidelines for side air bags. The guidelines include how powerfully bags would be deployed and the positions of dummies during the tests.
