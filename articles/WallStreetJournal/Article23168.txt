 Ask David Wessel   By Wessel, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 3, 2004  A: Although the Fed has substantial independence, it isn't politics- free. The Fed chairman and six other members of the Fed Board of Governors are appointed by the president and confirmed by the Senate for 14-year terms. Congress can and from time to time has contemplated changing the law under which the Fed operates. The Fed doesn't enjoy the same constitutional protections as the courts.
