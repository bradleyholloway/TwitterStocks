 Liffe Cancels Interval Of Trading on Plunge In U.K. Contract Price   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 5, 2001  The price of both the U.K. and the euro money-market contracts had begun falling in the wake of the National Association of Purchasing Management's monthly report, a Liffe spokesman said. But the U.K. contract then suddenly fell much more sharply than the euro contract and hit a low of 94.69. "It was clear that trading was irregular," he said.
