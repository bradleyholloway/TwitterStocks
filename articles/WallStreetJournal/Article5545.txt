 Plug and Play   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1997  Anyone whose labors take him as far as Philadelphia knows that electricity deregulation has become a confusing issue for many people. Modern life already requires a lot of them. Throw the switch, bang, the light goes on. Once a month, pay the bill. Why can't it go on being that simple?  Oh, insightful Philadelphians. Some economist could probably tell them exactly how many price-sensitive shoppers it takes to make the marketplace safe for the rest of us. If enough slip under their radar, businesses have to charge everybody the same. And enough Pennsylvanians volunteered for the pilot deregulation program that the state had to winnow the numbers down by lottery. Next problem.
