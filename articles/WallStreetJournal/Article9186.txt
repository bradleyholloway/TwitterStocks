 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 2000  Carolina Power & Light -- $300 million offering was priced via lead managers Banc One Capital Markets and Chase Securities, according to IFR Credit. Terms: maturity: April 1, 2005; coupon: 7.50%; issue price: 99.787; yield: 7.553%; spread: 142 basis points above Treasurys; call date: makewhole call at Treasurys plus 25 basis points; debt ratings: A2 (Moody's Investors Service Inc.), single-A (Standard & Poor's Ratings Group).  DaimlerChrysler -- $300 million reopening of its 7.125s of 2002 debt, via lead manager Banc of America Securities, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Terms: amount: $300 million (total now outstanding for the issue is $1.3 billion); maturity: March 1, 2002; coupon: 7.125%; issue price: 99.936; yield: 7.160%; spread: 78 basis points above Treasurys; debt ratings: A1 (Moody's), single-A-plus (S&P).  GATX Rail Corp. 2000-1 -- $119 million offering of passthrough trust certificates were priced via lead manager Morgan Stanley Dean Witter, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Terms: maturity: Jan. 13, 2020; average life: 11.8 years; coupon: 8.1%; issue price: par; yield: 8.1%; spread: 228 basis points above 6.5s of 2010 Treasurys; call date: make-whole call at Treasurys plus 20 basis points; debt ratings: A3 (Moody's), single-A-minus (S&P).
