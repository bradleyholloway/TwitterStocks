 Spelunkers must wipe their feet and avoid wearing black clothes   By Simmons, Jacqueline, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 4, 1994  Commercial caves are plagued by hundreds of pounds of lint--traces of hair, garment fibers, skin flakes and other crud left behind by millions of visitors a year. Among the proposed solutions are vacuuming off guests, using an electronic lint brush and cleanup expeditions by volunteers.
