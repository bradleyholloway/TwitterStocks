 Despite Improving, the Job Front Remains Grim   By Reddy, Sudeep, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 2009  Some past recessions, especially those with the largest employment declines, saw the job market rebound strongly once the overall economy picked up. The aftermath of the 2001 recession was dubbed a "jobless recovery" because employers continued shedding workers even as the economy showed robust growth.
