 Options Report: Options Market Spends Day Mulling If Fed Will Raise Interest Rates at Today's Meeting   By By Gaston F. Ceron and Steven M. Sears, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 5, 1999  With the latest verdict on the future of interest rates just around the corner, the options market spent yesterday pondering what today's meeting of the Federal Open Market Committee will bring.  Optimism for the interest-rate outlook fueled gains in the stock market, but the mood of options players was tougher to read, as options volatility moved up and down. The Chicago Board Options Exchange's Market Volatility Index, which tracks certain Standard & Poor's 100 option prices to determine investor sentiment, fell 0.73, or 2.8%, to 25.70.
