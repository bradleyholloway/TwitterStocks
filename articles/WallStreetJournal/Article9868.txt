 Army Weighs Slower Troop Growth To Keep Modernization on Track   By Jaffe, Greg, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 7, 2005  The Army's move to pare back growth comes as the Air Force also is exploring cutting as many as 40,000 active-duty, reserve and civilian personnel. Both actions highlight the severe budget pressures facing the Pentagon as it assembles its 2007 budget and strives to replace weapons systems that are wearing out quickly. The official said that the Army has essentially concluded that it won't be able to add as many new brigades "to mitigate the risk associated with the new [national defense] strategy as we thought we could."
