 Dollar Finishes Lower Against Rivals; Terrorism Fears Counteract Positive Economic Data; Widespread Selling Ensues   By McGeever, Jamie, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 21, 2003  The dollar had been generally recovering early yesterday from the pounding it took Wednesday -- when it fell to all-time lows against the euro -- when reports of the White House evacuation hit the wires. Sterling jumped to $1.7083, the five-year high hit on Oct. 29; the Australian and New Zealand dollars made new six-year highs of 72.65 U.S. cents, and 64.69 U.S. cents, respectively; and the euro got up to the record high of $1.1972 hit Wednesday.
