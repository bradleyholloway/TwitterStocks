 Volatility in Options Takes a Surprise Drop As Political Events Overshadow Expirations   By By Steven M. Sears, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 21, 1998  The strange confluence of events in Iraq and Washington stole the thunder from the triple expiration of December options and futures contracts.  A number of traders rolled expiring positions in December options into more distant months. Aggressive traders tried to peg the close of the Standard & Poor's 100 index by buying or selling December 585 and 590 calls or December 580 and 585 puts.
