 In France, a Mission to Return the Military's Carrier Pigeons to Active Duty --- Grounded After Modern Communication Devices Soared, Birds May Offer Low-Tech Solutions; No Round Trips   By Parussini, Gabriele, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 2012  Glorified for their roles in World War I, pigeon squadrons have long been removed from active duty because of the introduction of more reliable, all-weather communication systems. During peace times, carrier pigeons have also proved useful for civilian missions. [...]fairly recently, the hospital of Granville, a small port town in Normandy, used pigeons to ship blood samples to a test lab in Avranches, some 15 miles away.
