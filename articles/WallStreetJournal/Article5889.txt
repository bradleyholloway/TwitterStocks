 SGI Says Quarterly Results Won't Meet Estimates   By By Kara Swisher, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 2000  SGI, originally known for machines used to make Hollywood special effects, blamed the shortfall on shortages of key materials, including ceramic packaging used in its computers. Company officials said they were working with vendors to solve the problems.  SGI has been struggling to return to profitability under the leadership of Robert Bishop, its chairman and chief executive officer. Once a highflier, SGI has had a difficult time in more recent years, losing ground in the commercial market to such competitors as Sun Microsystems Inc.
