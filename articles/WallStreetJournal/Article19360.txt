 Inktomi's Earnings, Revenue Will Miss First-Period Targets   By By Ann Grimes, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 4, 2001  Shares of Inktomi dropped 22% to $14.25 in after-market trading, according to Instinet. Before the earnings warning, in 4 p.m. trading on the Nasdaq Stock Market, Inktomi shares had soared 27%, or $3.94, to $18.50.  Chief Executive David Peterschmidt cited poor macroeconomic market conditions for the shortfall. "The current conditions in the U.S. capital markets and the broader economy have resulted in a slowdown in infrastructure spending," he said. Inktomi develops infrastructure software for the Web.
