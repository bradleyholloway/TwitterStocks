 German Post Office Raises $131.8 Million On Eurobond Market --- Special to The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 14, 1986  An agency of the West German government raised money on the Eurobond market for the first time, through an offering on behalf of the federal post office.  About $609 million face value of mark-denominated, zero-coupon bonds were sold for Deutsche Bundespost. The four-part offering was made by a Channel Islands financing vehicle, Euro-DM Securities Ltd., but was secured by a fixed-rate loan from the Bundespost. The issue gives the agency the equivalent of $131.8 million of 30-year, fixed-rate funds at the lower yields prevailing on zero-coupon bonds.  Unilever Capital offered $150 million of bonds due 1998, priced at par to yield 8.875%. Sterling offered $100 million of 8 7/8% bonds due 1996, priced to yield 8.8%. Colgate-Palmolive Co. offered $100 million of 9 1/2% bonds due 1996, priced to yield 9.52%
