 Business and Finance   The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 1999  TWO VITAMIN MAKERS agreed to pay $725 million to settle Justice Department charges that they and other manufacturers engaged in a price-fixing conspiracy. Hoffmann-La Roche, a unit of Switzerland's Roche, agreed to pay a record $500 million fine and plead guilty as part of the settlement. Germany's BASF will pay $225 million and enter a guilty plea.  The trade gap continued to widen in March as higher imports of cars and oil pushed the deficit to a record $19.7 billion. Economists see the trend continuing amid anemic growth abroad.  Online retailer eToys nearly quadrupled in value in its IPO, reaching a market capitalization of $7.7 billion, 35% greater than rival Toys "R" Us.
