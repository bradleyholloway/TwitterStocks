 Options Prices Fall Slightly for Most of Day; Takeover Speculation Reappears in Market   By By Steven M. Sears, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 3, 1998  Options prices fell slightly for most of yesterday, which attracted money managers and traders looking for bargains as the stock market traded higher for much of the day.  Bargain hunters were evident in the battered oil, bank and technology sectors. Takeover speculation, which was absent earlier in the week, re-emerged on growing confidence the stock market was recovering after a sell-off. Comsat Corp. and Kroger Co. traded higher on renewed takeover speculation.
