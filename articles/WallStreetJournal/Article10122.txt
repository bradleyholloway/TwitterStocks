 Small Band of Economists Trumpet Sports Betting for Insights   By Lahart, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 7, 2008  Academic economists got a clue that this year's meeting of the American Economic Association would be different when Louisiana State University's football team -- in town for today's championship game against Ohio State -- streamed through the lobby of the main conference hotel. Ricard Gil, an economist at the University of California at Santa Cruz, and University of Chicago economist Steven Levitt, co-author of the best seller "Freakonomics," analyzed the price movement in TradeSports contracts on 2002 World Cup soccer matches to see how quickly prices reacted when goals were scored.
