 Politics & Economics: World Watch   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 10, 2006  Meanwhile, China's vice minister of commerce, Yu Guangzhou, warned the EU that it shouldn't jeopardize a great future in bilateral trade with China by wrangling over the prices of Chinese exports.  EU Trade Commissioner Peter Mandelson said at a Brussels trade conference that China must acknowledge its "massive new weight in the global economy" and stick to world trade rules, while Europe must accept the Chinese challenge to adapt and compete.  The EU is China's biggest trading partner, while China was the EU's second most-important partner in 2004 after the U.S. China is pushing for the EU to give it market-economy status, which would give it more protection in these kinds of trade disputes.
