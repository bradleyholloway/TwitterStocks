 Tech Forecasts Boost Market; Stratex Jumps 21%   By Schreiber, Cynthia, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 9, 2004  Signs of a recovery in the large-cap telecom sector made communications-equipment stocks the session's strongest performers. Cell-phone giant Nokia raised its quarterly profit and sales outlooks, as did telecom-equipment maker Andrew. Plus, Verizon Communications said it will invest $3 billion in its networks over the next two years.  The Russell 2000 Index of small stocks gained 4.99, or 0.87%, to 579.61, its highest level in more than 45 months. The Standard & Poor's SmallCap 600 Index established another record high with a gain of 0.99, or 0.36%, to 276.69. The Nasdaq Composite Index of large-cap and small-cap stocks closed up 22.57, or 1.09%, to 2100.25, the gauge's highest mark in 30 months.  Airlines also reversed course after J.P. Morgan lowered its profit estimate on large-cap JetBlue Airways. AirTran Holdings (NYSE) shed 90 cents, or 6.7%, to 12.53; Northwest Airlines eased 52 cents, or 3.7%, to 13.59, and Skywest fell 29 cents, or 1.6%, to 18.11.
