 Fair Trade 'Robin Hoods' May Just Help Themselves   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 2004  Your front-page article on fair-trade food imports ("What Price Virtue? At Some Retailers, 'Fair Trade' Carries a Very High Cost," June 8) describes what can happen when businesses prey upon consumers' desire to do good in the world via their purchasing power. Some good is in fact done for the producers in poor countries. More good is often done for bottom lines here in the first world, matched by the equivalent loss from consumers' pocketbooks.
