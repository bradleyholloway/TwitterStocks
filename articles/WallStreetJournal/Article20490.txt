 Mortgage Mystery at the FHA   By Eavis, Peter, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 4, 2010  The FHA's business actually declined in the housing boom, because it required documentation that lenders were able to avoid on subprime and Alt-A mortgages and because it stuck mainly to standard fixed-rate loans. [...] that higher level of documentation, and the fact that its business dipped during the boom, are possible reasons why the FHA mightn't have the right to collect large reimbursements. The trouble is, some firms have collapsed after being shut off by the FHA, potentially leaving no money to make good on shoddy loans anyway.
