 NEC to Put an Additional $225 Million In Packard Bell for a Controlling Stake   By By David P. Hamilton, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 3, 1998  NEC on Friday announced the additional investment in Packard Bell NEC and its intention to convert some of its preferred shares into common stock. French computer maker Groupe Bull, which has held a minority stake in Packard Bell since 1993, will also invest an additional $25 million in the unit. As a result of the changes, NEC's voting-rights ownership of Packard Bell will rise to 52.81% from 49%; Groupe Bull will hold 11.68% of the company. Packard Bell co-founder Beny Alagem and his partners will now hold 35.51% of the new NEC subsidiary.
