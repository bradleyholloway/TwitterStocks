 China Eases Rules On Stock Buying By Foreign Holders   By Linebaugh, Kate, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 2006  Key to the government effort has been an ambitious program announced last year to reform the share capital of China's 1,400 listed companies. Until recently, about two-thirds of China's total market capitalization consisted of nontradable shares, often held by the state. Since the program began, more than 300 companies have structured deals to convert that nontradable equity into regular Class A common shares.
