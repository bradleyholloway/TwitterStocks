 Sale of Oppenheimer could mean windfall for Milken, others   By Siconolfi, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 5, 1995  Oppenheimer & Co's expected sale to Bayerische Vereinsbank AG, Germany's fourth-largest commercial bank, stands to trigger a $66 million windfall for a surprising group of Oppenheimer holders--former executives at Drexel Burnham Lambert Inc, which include former Drexel junk-bond chief Michael Milken. That's because three Drexel private partnerships own a total of 16.6% of the closely held brokerage firm's parent Oppenheimer Group Inc, people familiar with the matter say.
