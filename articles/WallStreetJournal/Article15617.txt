 Bad Advice for Russia   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 5, 1997  A team of IMF officials left Moscow on Friday saying they could not recommend disbursement of the latest $700 million loan tranche because Russia has failed to collect sufficient tax revenues. The Fund wants Russia to fix what IMF Managing Director Michel Camdessus calls its "culture of nonpayment." Russia's revenue-hungry lawmakers could hardly agree more with their friends at the IMF: Deputies in the Duma want more spending to prop up loss-making industries, and more revenues collected to cover it. The IMF-Duma alliance may prove a tough match even for Russia's stalwart reformers.
