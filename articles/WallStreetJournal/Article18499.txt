 Business and Finance [3]  The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 1993  STOCKS TUMBLED as skittish investors bailed out of utility and financial stocks amid fears that interest rates are headed higher. The Dow Jones Industrial Average sank 35.77 points to 3661.87. Long-term bond prices slumped for the fourth straight day. The yield on the Treasury's 30-year benchmark rose to 6.10%.  Prudential withdrew plans to sell its CenterMark mall unit to the public, agreeing instead to sell it to a group led by another mall concern for about $870 million in cash and debt.  The Clinton administration struck deals with Mexico to help protect six politically sensitive industries, which officials hope may persuade up to 20 House members to vote for Nafta.
