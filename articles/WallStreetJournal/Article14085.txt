 Daewoo, President And Unit Plead Guilty to Fraud   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 4, 1985  Daewoo Industrial Co., its president, Kyung Hoon Lee, and a U.S. subsidiary pleaded guilty to various criminal fraud and conspiracy charges arising from the importation of South Korean steel to the U.S.  The Korean trading company pleaded guilty to 10 counts of fraudulently concealing the actual import price of various steel products between January 1980 and June 1982, in what the U.S. Attorney charged was an attempt to avoid tariffs and antidumping laws.  The U.S. unit, Daewoo International Corp., pleaded guilty to 11 similar counts and Mr. Lee pleaded guilty to one count of conspiring to avoid U.S. Customs Service regulations.
