 U.S. News: Localities Facing Merger Push   By Dougherty, Conor; Merrick, Amy, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 2009  In Indiana, the governor and the mayor of the state's largest city are going after the state's network of 1,008 townships -- small government units that predate the Civil War, averaging 36 square miles in size. Indianapolis, a city of roughly 800,000, has nine elected township boards that handle fire and emergency services, as well as what Indiana calls "poor relief," a patchwork of services providing food and shelter to residents who fall through federal and state safety nets.
