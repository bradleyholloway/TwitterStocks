 RealNetworks Says Earnings, Revenue Will Miss Forecasts   By By Nick Wingfield, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 21, 2000  Executives at RealNetworks also said some of the corporate customers that purchase its server software for broadcasting multimedia content to consumers over the Internet have either gone belly-up or are running dangerously low on money. Some analysts believe the online media companies that use RealNetworks' software haven't figured out how to make money off of it. Broadcasting audio and video "on the Web is certainly increasing in terms of its traction" among users, said Mr. [Rob Martin], the Friedman, Billings, Ramsey analyst. "Where it has not lived up to expectations is in the business models related to that fundamental growth."
