 Efforts to promote teaching of values in schools are sparking heated debate among lawmakers   By Sharpe, Rochelle, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 1994  The character-education movement has spread into thousands of classrooms across the nation and many educators claim teaching children to refrain from lying, cheating or stealing has reduced discipline problems. The policy has sparked heated debate among lawmakers, and efforts by Rep Tony Hall (D-OH) to push a character-education amendment through Congress have been defeated seven times.
