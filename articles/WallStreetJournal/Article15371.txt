 Germany's Postbank balks at a reunion   By Steinmetz, Greg, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 5, 1995  The planned $2.1 billion acquisition of Deutsche Postbank AG by Deutsche Post AG, both state-owned companies slated for privatization, has turned into the biggest takeover fight in German financial history and called into question a valuation of Postbank by Salomon Bros. The German government left the terms of the sale up to Postbank, and before it could act, Deutsche Post came out of nowhere with a consortium bid that includes Deutsche Bank AG and Swiss Reinsurance Co.
