 Latvia Strives to Establish True Interethnic Harmony   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 2005  Latvia's efforts to overcome the Soviet legacy of mass deportations, repressions and Russification have been widely recognized by the international community, with the sole exception of Russia. If the kind of discrimination you describe were to exist in Latvia, our country wouldn't have been granted accession to the European Union and the North Atlantic Treaty Organization. Indeed, these dual accession processes brought Latvia's minority policies, which were formulated with substantial input from Latvia's minority communities, under intense scrutiny by the EU and NATO.
