 Letters to the Editor: The `Void' in the British System   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 5, 2001  What Ms. [Lionel Shriver] fails to give ink to is how the parties involved got to this point in the first place. The fact is that Britons have no Bill of Rights or written civil contract with their government. People who are systematically marginalized in the British realm have no effective political means at their disposal unless the unchecked, winner-takes-all ruling parliamentary government has the fortitude to redress grievances, which they traditionally have not. Into that void step the voices of revolution, as Mahatma Gandhi, Manachem Begin, Michael Collins and John Adams have shown.
