 Aladdin Gaming Fears Loan Default, Talks to Creditors   By By Christina Binkley, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 4, 2001  The 2,567-room Aladdin had been a particular headache for London Clubs long before the property even opened. London Clubs, which owns about 40% of Aladdin, never expected to take such a major financial role in the property, but was forced to when the [Sommer] family declined to inject needed cash to complete the $1 billion construction project.  London Clubs operates six casinos in London and four others in South Africa, Egypt and Lebanon. All are dwarfed by the Aladdin, which was the company's attempt to become a major player in the U.S. casino market.
