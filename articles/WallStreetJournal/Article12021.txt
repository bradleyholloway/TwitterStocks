 Peso May Feel Pressure Through 1995 --- Mutual-Funds Sales, Problems in Mexico Cast Cloud   By By Paul B. Carroll, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 6, 1995  Mexico's peso probably will remain volatile and under pressure at least through the end of the year, economists and investors say.  After watching the peso plunge nearly 10% against the dollar in October and then decline another 4% in the first two trading days of November, many investors are simply too jittery to hold pesos now.  In coming weeks, the peso also is likely to come under pressure from mutual funds that want to get Mexican assets off the books before sending year-end reports to investors. Many traders remember how their bonuses disappeared last year after the Mexican government's Dec. 20 peso devaluation caused heavy losses in their Mexican portfolios.
