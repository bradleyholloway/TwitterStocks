 Bank of Japan lacks autonomy under reform plan   By Sapsford, Jathon, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 7, 1997  A government panel released a detailed blueprint for a new central-bank law that would boost the independence of the Bank of Japan, in what many are calling the first major step in Japan's vaunted financial-reform program. Yet the blueprint falls short of calls for total independence for the central bank's budget--a level of control that FRB Chairman Alan Greenspan specifically warned against during a recent visit to Japan.
