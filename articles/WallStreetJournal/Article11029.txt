 Senate Panel Rejects Strunk For Mine Chief   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 6, 1987  The Senate Labor Committee killed the Reagan administration's controversial nomination for a new mine-safety chief.  In a straight party-line vote of 9-7, the panel rejected President Reagan's nomination of Dorothy Strunk, a former House Labor Committee staff member, to be assistant secretary of labor for mine safety and health.
