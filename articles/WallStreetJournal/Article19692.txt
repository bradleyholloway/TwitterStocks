 Digital River, CNET and FuelCell Energy Rise   By Schreiber, Cynthia, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 3, 2003  "You shouldn't hang your hat on that because starting next week we start to get earnings numbers," said Jay Finkel, senior equity trader at Lord Abbett in Jersey City, N.J. "If there are some disappointments that people did not foresee, the market could have a problem."  The Russell 2000 rose 2.88, or 0.6%, to 503.20, and the Standard & Poor's SmallCap 600 Index tacked on 1.51, or 0.6%, to 243.61. The Nasdaq Composite Index of large-cap and small-cap stocks closed up 3.97, or 0.2%, to 1836.22.  Jewelry retailer Friedman's (NYSE) lost 1.28, or 9.1%, to 12.72, after saying the U.S. Justice Department is investigating allegations made by Capital Factors, a receivables factoring company, which recently filed a lawsuit against the Savannah, Ga., company. Also, Friedman's posted a 3.4% rise in fourth-quarter same-store sales, or sales at stores open at least a year.
