 Abreast of the market: Investment firms are trying to keep stock investors from turning skittish   By McGeehan, Patrick, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 1996  The stock market keeps climbing higher and higher in the second-longest bull market ever, but money managers are growing increasingly worried about how investors will react to what stock market historians see as a long-overdue correction or, worse yet, a prolonged downturn. The Dow Jones Industrial Average surged 50.94 points to 5536.56 on Mar 1, 1996. For the week ending Mar 2, the industrial average lost 93.93 points, or 1.7%.
