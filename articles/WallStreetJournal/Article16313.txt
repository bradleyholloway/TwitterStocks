 Cranky Consumer: A Guide to the Guidebooks; Travel Manuals Are Booming, But Which Can You Trust? A Not-So-Hot Paris Hotspot   By de Lisser, Eleena, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 5, 2003  Frommers last year unveiled a new system that labels things as a "value," "overrated" or "kids," among others. (Good news for Italy: We couldn't find a single thing in the book that Frommers called "overrated.") But in Paris, the rating system stumbled, ranking Buddha Bar as "very highly recommended" and saying "this place still remains Paris's 'restaurant of the moment.' " However, it's now filled with tourists. And Frommers -- like all the books we tried -- left us wanting more info about Italian wineries.
