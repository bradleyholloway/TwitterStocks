 The Crisis Goes From Bad to Worse   By Gongloff, Mark, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 12, 2008  When the bear market began in March 2000, the ratio of the S&P 500 stocks' prices to their past year's earnings -- a closely watched measure of market value -- was nearly 33, according to research firm Birinyi Associates. Among stocks, that means holding companies that can make money regardless of the economic climate, particularly makers of consumer staples such as canned food and toothpaste.
