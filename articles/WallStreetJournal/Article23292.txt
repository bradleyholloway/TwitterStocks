 Chicago's O'Hare Expansion Plan Is Put on Hold by Appeals Court   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 3, 2005  An appeals court has halted a $15 billion expansion of O'Hare Airport designed to ease some of the nation's worst flight delays.  The city was expecting final approval and started to break ground Friday on the airport's first runway since 1971 after Mayor Richard M. Daley said "Let's go" into a walkie talkie at a celebratory news conference.
