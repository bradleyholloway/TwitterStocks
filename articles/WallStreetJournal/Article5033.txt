 Europeans may buy fewer U.S. stocks   By Sesit, Michael R, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 10, 1993  Money managers are predicting that recent changes in Europe's semifixed currency system that will permit nine European currencies to fluxuate more widely against one another will lead to lower interest rates, more rapid economic growth and higher stock prices in Europe. If that happens, the managers warn, a reversal could occur in a six-month trend that saw overseas investors pile into US stocks after being net sellers for a year. An examination is made of the dynamics behind recent foreign investments in the US.
