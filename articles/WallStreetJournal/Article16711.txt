 Federal Injunction Will Delay Part of Drug-Tracking Law   By Tesoriero, Heather Won, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 5, 2006  The Prescription Drug Marketing Act draws a distinction between wholesalers that buy drugs directly from manufacturers, known as "authorized distributors," and those that buy drugs from other sources, or "secondary wholesalers." Manufacturers decide which wholesalers they will directly sell to. Under the law, only secondary wholesalers must supply a pedigree.  When Congress passed the law, "the Energy and Commerce Committee was attempting to shut down small wholesalers," said William Hubbard, former associate FDA commissioner. "They were clearly viewed as the problem and clearly were intended to be put out of business."
