 Tax-Exempt Workers' Savings Lag   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 2007  That is partly attributable to the fact that many tax-exempt workers still have pensions and may not feel the need to save as much on their own. But even then, the average tax-exempt-sector worker still trails the private-sector worker. Those with a defined-benefit plan have saved $47,405 on average; those without a pension plan have put away $54,898 on average, Fidelity says.
