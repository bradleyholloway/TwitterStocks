 Moody's Prepares To Raise Rating On China's Debt   By By James T. Areddy, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 25, 2002  Citing China's ability to accumulate foreign currency and contain its domestic debt problems, Moody's raised to "positive" from "stable" its view of China's foreign-currency bonds, external bank obligations and foreign-currency bank deposits -- liabilities that total $170 billion, the firm said. Friday's move serves as notice that Moody's will probably upgrade the country's A3 credit rating.
