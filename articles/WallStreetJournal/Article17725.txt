 Japanese seek role as takeover advisers   By Sesit, Michael R, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 1987  Japanese financial institutions are slowly edging into the takeover and merger advisory business because of an increase in Japanese acquisitions of U.S. businesses and a need to generate additional fee income. These banks mostly represent other Japanese companies, are willing to negotiate small deals, and do not get involved in giant hostile takeovers. Presently, Japan is no match for the powerful U.S. investment banks.
