 Dollar Declines Amid Pressure Of View of End to Fed Rate Lifts   By Vames, Steven, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 2006  The minutes released Tuesday from the Dec. 13 meeting of the Fed's rate-setting committee indicated that policy makers differ on how many more rate increases will be needed as inflation pressures lessen and with U.S. rates no longer seen as accommodative for accelerating U.S. growth.  The Fed's unwavering rate-raising efforts had provided the primary support behind the dollar's strong 2005 advance, and investors fear the dollar's interest-rate advantage will start to evaporate once the Fed stops raising rates, particularly with European Central Bank policy makers just starting to raise rates themselves.
