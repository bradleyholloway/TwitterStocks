 U.S. Census consensus: a peak in the next century   By Anonymous, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 7, 1989  In 17 of its 30 new population estimates, the Census Bureau projects that the US population will reach a high point in the next 90 years "and then begin to decline." Even though births outnumber deaths, immigration makes up an increasing share of the nation's population growth. From the 1960s to the present, the growth in population attributed to immigration has grown 16% to 27%.
