 Business and Finance   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 1998  CARGILL IS IN TALKS to take over the grain assets of its longtime rival, Continental Grain. The move would increase Cargill's lead as the world's biggest grain exporter. For Continental Grain, industry executives say, a sale would generate cash that it could put into its livestock and financial-services businesses. But a deal could raise antitrust concerns.  Intel abandoned Internet and multimedia software projects after Bill Gates said Microsoft might not support Intel's next chip, a threat that slowed innovation and hurt consumers, an Intel executive charged.  Patriot American offered partial steps, including the sale of some hotel assets, to resolve its liquidity problems as it posted a wider loss for the third quarter. Its stock fell 14%.
