 First Brands Profit Fell Below Estimates; Weak Demand Cited   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 6, 1998  DANBURY, Conn. -- First Brands Corp. said fourth-quarter profit will fall short of expectations because of weak retailer demand and foreign-currency woes.  The maker of Glad plastic bags and Scoop Away cat litter said earnings for the fourth quarter, which ended June 30, would likely be 26 to 30 cents a diluted share. Analysts expected earnings per share of 39 cents, according to First Call Corp.
