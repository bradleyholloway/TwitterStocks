 Bonds Rally on View That Today's Jobs Report May Offer New Signs of Weakness in Economy   By Mitchell, Constance; Alcorn, Sam, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 1991  Bond prices rebounded on Apr 4, 1991 as economists on Wall Street warned that the Mar 1991 jobs data may show a sharp rise in unemployment. The Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond ended at a price of 96 16/32 to yield 8.18%, compared with 95 28/32 to yield 8.24%. Shorter-term securities rose with modest gains.
