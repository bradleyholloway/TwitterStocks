 Top Russian Banker Ridicules Monday's Ruble Devaluation   By Hays, Laurie, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 1991  Georgy Matykhin, chairman of the Board of the Central Bank of Russia, ridiculed the Soviet central bank's recent devaluation of the ruble. As of Jan 1, 1992, when Russia gains control of all state banking operations, Matykhin will change the rate back to around six rubles to the dollar. Gosbank had lowered the rate to 90 rubles to the dollar from 47.
