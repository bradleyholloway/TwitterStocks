 Nations Meet to Discuss Web Rules   By Jones, Rory, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 4, 2012  A key battleground at the conference will be a proposal from Russia and several African nations to wrest control of the Internet from Internet Corp. for Assigned Names and Numbers, or Icann, an organization that helps oversee the Internet, and other groups that are primarily based in the U.S. The Russian proposal, leaked on a website set up to counter the lack of transparency, calls for countries to "have equal rights to manage their Internet including in regard to the allotment, assignment and reclamation of Internet numbering, naming addressing and identification resources."
