 Too hot to handle: High-heat arson fires swiftly raze buildings but leave few clues   By Larson, Erik, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 1993  Investigators in Seattle have discovered what seems to be a national pattern of unsolved arsons featuring white-hot flames that consume vast industrial buildings in minutes, leaving melted steel and concrete turned to glass. The investigators say the fires, now officially known as High-Temperature Accelerant fires, are caused by arson--perhaps all set by a single arsonist using something akin to rocket fuel.
