 Tom Herman's Tax Tip: A Refund Not to Miss   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 4, 2007  Or, if you think you paid more than that, you can use the actual amount of tax shown on your phone bills and other records, the IRS says. "Base your refund request on the 3% federal tax paid, not the total phone bill. Do not count tax paid on local-only service," the IRS says. To use this method, you must have phone bills "or other records adequate to support the amount" you're claiming, the IRS adds.  Millions of people who don't need to file a regular income-tax return are eligible for the refund. If that includes you, look for a special new form: Form 1040EZ-T, "Request for Refund of Federal Telephone Excise Tax."
