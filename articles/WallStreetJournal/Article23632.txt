 MarketWatch: Getting Advice in a Rough Market   By Burton, Jonathan, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 3, 2008  An adviser should make sure you have cash or other short-term assets to cover living costs for a year or more, and then invest the remainder in a diversified portfolio. Financial advisers should have regular, straightforward talks with their clients about a choppy investing environment and -- based on past experience -- outline what could happen next.
