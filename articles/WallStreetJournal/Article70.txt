 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 1988  May Department Stores Co. -- $175 million of debentures, due Sept. 1, 2018, priced at 10 7/8% at par. The debentures are nonrefundable for 10 years. In the 11th year, a sinking fund is scheduled to begin to retire 5% of the issue annually. In any one year of sinking fund eligibility, the company has the option to increase the sinking fund to 15%. The debentures were priced 140 basis points above the rate of the Treasury 8 7/8% securities of 2017. Rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.  Continental Illinois National Bank & Trust Co. of Chicago -- $100 million notes due Sept. 15, 1992, priced as 9 7/8% securities at 99.905 to yield 9.905%.The notes are noncallable for two years. The issue was priced 97 basis points above the rate of the Treasury four-year note. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Shearson Lehman Hutton Inc. The issuer is a unit of Continental Illinois Corp.  Jacksonville Health Facilities Authority, Florida, postponed its $65.3 million issue of hospital facilities target rate refunding revenue bonds tentatively priced Tuesday. An official with lead underwriter Smith Barney, Harris Upham & Co. says the issue was withdrawn "due to market conditions." The municipal market saw a heavy influx of new issues Tuesday, with over $1 billion in new notes and bonds unveiled. The bonds, for the benefit of the St. Luke's Hospital Association Project, had been tentatively priced to yield from 6% in 1989 to 7% to a mandatory put date in 1994 for bonds due 2016 and 2018. The issue was backed by a letter of credit from Morgan Guaranty Trust Co., a unit of J.P. Morgan & Co., and rated triple-A by S&P. No future date for the sale has been specified.
