 Newspapers in Detroit Are Ordered to Rehire Workers From Strike   By By A Wall Street Journal, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 3, 1998  The ruling means the Detroit News and Detroit Free Press may be forced to lay off hundreds of permanent replacement workers. They also face possible financial liabilities totaling tens of millions of dollars in back pay. The News is owned by Gannett Co., and the Free Press by Knight-Ridder Inc. Both companies said they would challenge the ruling; the case would then go before a federal appeals court.
