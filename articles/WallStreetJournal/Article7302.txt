 Sovereign and Eager to Prove It   By Hay, William Anthony, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 10, 2012  The United States declared war in June 1812 lacking both the money and military to fight, and Britain, mounting a response, risked overextending its forces at a critical point in the Napoleonic wars. Behind the ostensible casus belli -- e.g., the impressment of sailors from American ships by the Royal Navy -- was a clash between America's expansion and Britain's efforts to avenge an earlier defeat by making a former colony a client state.
