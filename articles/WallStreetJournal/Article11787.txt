 Renaissance Plans $80 Million Offering Of Stock, Giving Warburg $58.5 Million   By By Neela Banerjee, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 1993  Television station operator Renaissance Communications Corp. plans an $80 million initial public offering that will yield its majority owner Warburg, Pincus Capital Co. Limited Partnership $58.5 million.  Since 1988, the Greenwich, Conn., broadcasting company has acquired six "underperforming" TV stations and teamed up with Fox Broadcasting Co. to aim for a "young adult" target audience.  The company filed a registration statement with the Securities and Exchange Commission for an offering of 4.7 million shares, estimated to be priced at $16 to $18 a share. Of the total, 3,760,000 shares will be offered in the U.S. and 940,000 overseas, through Goldman, Sachs & Co. and Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.
