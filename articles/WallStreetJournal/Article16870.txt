 A Vintage Strategy Faces Modernity; The Antinori family wine business has thrived for six centuries; Here's how they did it   By Gabriel Kahn in Florence, Italy, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 2008  In between, the Antinori family survived outbreaks of Bubonic plague, the invasion of Napoleon, two world wars, the arrival of globalization and the birth and death of the wine cooler. According to company figures, 2007 revenues were 137 million euros ($214 million), with a net profit expected to be around 18 million euros ($28 million) -- or more than double the margin of most other major wine producers in Italy.
