 TV Preview, Feb. 5-18: Sleuths, Painters and Skaters   By By Martha Bayles, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 5, 1987  Thursdays, Feb. 5-Mar. 19, 9-10 p.m. EST, on PBS (PBS air dates and times vary, so check local listings): "The Return of Sherlock Holmes." Jeremy Brett is back, snaking his eyebrows and twitching his lips in an expert imitation of deductive detecting.  Friday, Feb. 6, 9-10:30 p.m. EST, on PBS: "The Ebony Tower." This dramatization of the John Fowles novel stars Sir Laurence Olivier as a lecherous but gifted elderly painter, visited at his home in the French countryside by a young art critic who tries to woo one of the old man's girlfriends. Only the young women know exactly what they're doing. This is the first of three "Great Performances" films showcasing older actors, and each of them is a gem.  Saturday, Feb. 7, 4:30-6 p.m. EST, and Sunday, Feb. 8, 4-6 p.m. EST, on ABC: The U.S. Figure Skating Championships. Watch Brian Boitano attempt the first ever quadruple toe loop, and other blade-flashing feats.
