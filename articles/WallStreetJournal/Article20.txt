 Seaman Furniture Says Third-Period Net Rose Between 30% and 35%   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 25, 1986  Seaman Furniture Co. estimated that fiscal third-quarter earnings increased between 30% and 35% from a year earlier, largely because of higher sales in new product lines.  But based on calculations from Securities and Exchange Commission filings, net income in the quarter ended Jan. 30, rose to between $3.2 million and $3.4 million, or 48 cents a share to 52 cents a share, from the pro forma $2.5 million, or 40 cents a share, a year earlier. The company expects to report quarterly earnings today.
