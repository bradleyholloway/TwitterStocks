 Malaysia's Rulers Inflame the Opposition   By By Leslie Lopez, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 7, 2000  PAS came to power in Terengganu in November after scoring a decisive victory in state elections over Mr. Mahathir's United Malays National Organization, or UMNO, which previously administered the state. The loss reflected an erosion of support among ethnic Malays for Mr. Mahathir's government stemming from the sacking and imprisonment of former Deputy Premier Anwar Ibrahim in 1998. The defeat was a particularly heavy blow to Mr. Mahathir because it gave PAS economic clout -- in the form of oil and gas royalties -- that had long eluded it.
