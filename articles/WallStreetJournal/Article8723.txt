 Bonds rebound in thin trading, but investors worry about what might follow Treasury sale   By McGee, Suzanne, The Wall Street Journal,  May 7, 1996  Bond prices rallied for the first time in more than a week on May 6, 1996, despite the Treasury's looming sale of $33 billion in three-year and 10-year notes scheduled to begin on May 7. The 30-year Treasury bond gained nearly 5/8 point to 86.26, pushing the yield to 7.05% from 7.11%.
