 Brazil's Franco Is to Take Over Presidency Today   By By Thomas Kamm, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 2, 1992  BRASILIA, Brazil -- Itamar Franco will become Brazil's president today, three days earlier than planned, after the Supreme Court and Senate agreed that impeachment proceedings against President Fernando Collor de Mello should begin immediately.  Yesterday's unexpected decision threw Brazil's political transition into confusion, as vice-president Franco risks taking office before he can form his cabinet. Mr. Franco, 61 years old, spent all day yesterday meeting with politicians, but by late afternoon the key post of finance minister hadn't been filled because of disagreements within the coalition that supports him.
