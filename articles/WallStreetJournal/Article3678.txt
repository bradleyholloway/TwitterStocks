 Crash Probe Reveals Maintenance Gaps   By By Andy Pasztor, The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 2003  The investigation of a fatal crash of a U.S. Airways commuter flight four months ago has revealed major gaps in maintenance oversight: The plane's mechanics, inspectors and safety supervisors all worked for different companies, while the airline's commuter affiliate performed little hands-on supervision. At a National Transportation Safety Board hearing, investigators said an outside mechanic improperly adjusted a flight-control cable, precipitating the crash of the twin-engine Beech 1900 commuter plane, operated by Air Midwest, in Charlotte, N.C.
