 Financial-Services Trade Talks Make Officials Optimistic   By By Bhushan Bahree, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 1997  Talks to open the world's financial-services markets to more foreign competition enter their final phase this week, and trade diplomats are taking an optimistic view that a deal can be clinched before the Dec. 12 deadline.  Trade officials reckon that previously recalcitrant nations, some of them in financially troubled Asia, will see the merits of an agreement that could, according to some estimates, establish the framework for gains -- resulting from cost savings and quality improvements through increased competition -- well in excess of $1 trillion in a little more than a decade. More immediately, a pact could help restore confidence among foreign investors who have fled some Asian financial markets, senior trade officials contend.
