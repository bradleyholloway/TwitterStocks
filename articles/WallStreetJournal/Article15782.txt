 German Lower House Approves Taxes Despite Warnings   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 1999  German insurers and utilities have launched a public attack on the plan, some threatening to move large parts of their operations abroad because of the billions of marks in extra taxes the reforms will cost them. Even after German Chancellor Gerhard Schroeder and Mr. (Oskar) Lafontaine tentatively agreed Wednesday to limit the two industries' burden, the rhetoric on both sides showed no signs of abating.
