 Chinese Jetmaker Gets Western Help; Eager for a Stake in Regional Jet Project, U.S. Firms Contribute Half of Equipment   By Bruce Stanley in Shanghai and J. Lynn Lunsford in Dallas, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 3, 2008  The Federal Aviation Administration, citing safety and the participation of so many U.S. firms, opened a bureau in Shanghai last year to help the Chinese win certification for the ARJ21 to fly in the U.S. Canada's Bombardier Inc., which makes competing regional jets, is angling for a role in ACAC's development of a larger version of the aircraft.
