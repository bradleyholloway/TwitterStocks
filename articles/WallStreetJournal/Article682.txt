 Woolworth, Macy's Report Big Profit Gains   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 14, 1985  F.W. Woolworth Co. and R.H. Macy & Co. reported sharply higher quarterly earnings.  Macy's reported that its fiscal first-quarter earnings rose a better-than-expected 36%, reflecting strong sales during the fall shopping season. In the quarter ended Oct. 27, the big retailer's earnings grew to $42.7 million, or 83 cents a share, from $31.4 million, or 61 cents a share, a year earlier. Sales increased 8.7% to $1.1 billion from $1.01 billion. Meanwhile, sales for stores in operation at least one year rose 6.1%. But the retailer's stock price fell sharply.  Woolworth, citing an increase in pre-tax profit margins and lower interest costs, reported that fiscal third-quarter earnings soared 46%. In the quarter ended Oct. 31, profit increased to $38 million, or $1.20 a share, from $26 million, or 82 cents a share, a year earlier, the big retailer said. Sales rose 4.7% to $1.47 billion from $1.4 billion a year earlier.
