 The Aggregator: What a New Congress Means for Your Money   By Edited by Cristina Lourosa-Ricardo, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 2010  Citigroup is wooing new customers with up to $300; Capital One is offering $200; J.P. Morgan Chase is doling out up to $125; and online bank WTDirect.com is paying up to $300 for opening a new savings account. [...] in the last month, more than half a dozen small banks also have rolled out similar incentives of up to $220. Even after you get the money, you could lose it to other new and increased fees, such as those for overdrafts, failing to keep a required minimum balance or not making a specified number of debit transactions per month. --
