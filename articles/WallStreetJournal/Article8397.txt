 Nippon Credit to write off bad loans totaling $1.92 billion over three years   By Williams, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 7, 1993  Nippon Credit Bank Ltd reported it will write off about 200 billion yen ($1.92 billion) in bad loans over a three-year period ending in Mar 1996, which is one of the largest write-offs to date in Japan's banking crisis. As of spring 1993, Japan's 11 giant city banks had y8.43 trillion in nonperforming loans. Analysts say it may take the industry up to 10 years to recover.
