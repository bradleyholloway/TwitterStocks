 Gillette Co. Stock Surges On Rumors of New Suitor   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 5, 1986  Gillette Co.'s stock, the most actively traded on the New York Stock Exchange yesterday, jumped $6.75, to $54.75 a share amid rumors that the razor-blades and personal-products giant may have another suitor.  The sharp rise on volume of 3,172,600 shares came just 10 days after Gillette blocked a $4.12 billion, or $65-a-share, takeover attempt by Revlon Group Inc. and its chairman, Ronald O. Perelman. Gillette's stock had plummeted to $45.875 a share after Gillette bought Revlon's shares for a premium.
