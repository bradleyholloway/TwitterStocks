 Midlife Career Change Leads to Rude Awakening   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 10, 2004  Switching gears and going into a new field, as Jerry A. DeMartino suggests in your article, may be fruitless. I operated my own business for 28 years before taking time off at age 50 to attend law school. Despite graduating with honors from the University of Michigan and having two articles published in a national periodical, I was unable to find a law firm that was interested in hiring me.
