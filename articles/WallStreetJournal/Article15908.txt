 Rent Board Settles Lawsuit, Eluding Court Scrutiny   By By Sara Kehaulani Goo, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 2000  David M. FitzGerald thinks so. A lawsuit filed in 1992 in the Suffolk County Housing Court by the board against his client, the Franciscan Children's Hospital in Boston, was set to begin trial tomorrow before the state's highest court. Instead, Mr. FitzGerald just accepted a settlement offer from the board.  The deal came about, Mr. FitzGerald believes, because the city agency is operating on uncertain ground. Even though rent control was abolished in Massachusetts by a voter referendum in 1994 and several housing-court judges have ruled that as a result the rent board no longer is viable, the board keeps meeting monthly.
