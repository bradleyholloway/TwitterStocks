 Earnings Digest -- Conglomerates: Berkshire Net Jumps, Led by Insurance Unit   By Gibson, Richard, The Wall Street Journal,  May 5, 2007  Berkshire's "float" -- largely insurance premiums that eventually will be paid out in claims, and which Mr. [Warren Buffett] invests -- rose to $59 billion as of March 31 from $51 billion as of Dec. 31.  Between 25,000 and 28,000 shareholders are expected to attend Saturday's annual meeting, where Mr. Buffett and Vice Chairman Charlie Munger field questions for nearly six hours. Shareholders will be looking for details about the company's acquisition plans and its plan for a successor for the 76-year-old Mr. Buffett.
