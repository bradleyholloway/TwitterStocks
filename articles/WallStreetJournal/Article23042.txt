 Letters to the Editor: Behind the Fierce Battle On Mortgage Closing Costs   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 3, 2003  Finally, you say that the "1974 federal law gives the power to change the closing-cost system." We dispute that. The 1974 law referred to, RESPA, does not give HUD the power to change the closing cost system Congress adopted in RESPA. The only lawful approach to change that regime is for HUD to seek legislative amendments to RESPA to authorize packaging or whatever other reforms in RESPA that HUD may believe are needed. In the legal memorandum we provided to HUD, we argue that the substantive changes in regulations that HUD was proposing were not authorized by -- and, indeed, were in conflict with -- the approaches adopted by Congress.
