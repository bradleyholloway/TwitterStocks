 Lumber futures continue to rise, but cash price may be high enough to cut demand for wood   By Taylor, Jeffrey, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 25, 1993  Lumber futures prices continued to surge on Feb 24, 1993, but there are indications that the cash price of lumber is so high that demand for wood is declining. Lumber for March delivery has surged nearly 150% recently to the closing price on Feb 24 of $445.20 per thousand board feet in Chicago trading. In other commodity markets, crude oil futures prices rose, wheat prices settled mixed and precious metals futures fell.
