 Stocks Go Higher, Though the Pace Of Their Gains Slows   By Browning, E S, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 2003  "You are getting comments from some chief executives that are helping this move," said Warren Epstein, head trader at Kabrik Trading. "People are getting a sense that the market isn't coming in and that, if they don't buy, they are kind of left behind."  John Chambers, chairman of network-equipment maker Cisco, said orders were stronger than expected last month but added that he didn't know if that improvement is sustainable. Credit Suisse First Boston forecast strong sales growth for software companies.  Outside the U.S., stocks advanced in dollar terms. The Dow Jones World Stock Index, excluding U.S. stocks, rose 1.5%, or 1.83 points, to 123.96.
