 Japan's brokers clean up act with new laws   By Steiner, Robert, The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 1996  Although Japan's stock market remains more opaque than Wall Street, the nation's stock markets say they have been cleaning up their act. The new Securities and Exchange Surveillance Commission has prosecuted six securities-law violations in the past three years, compared with seven prosecutions in the preceding 43 years, on issues such as insider trading, market manipulation and "circulating rumors."
