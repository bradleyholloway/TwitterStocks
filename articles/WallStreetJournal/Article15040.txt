 Bond rally ignores economy's signs of strength   By Vogel, Thomas T, Jr, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 1993  On Jan 4, 1993 the 30-year Treasury bond finished the day at 7.32%, a three-month low, as the price rose 7/8 of a point, or $8.75 for each $1,000 face amount. A discussion is offered of why Treasury and bond prices have continued to climb steadily as yields edge lower, despite statistics that suggest the US economy is gaining strength. Economic, psychological and seasonal factors are cited for this enigmatic chain of events.
