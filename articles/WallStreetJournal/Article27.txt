 U.S. to Step Up Drive to Deregulate Most Oil Pipelines   By By Andy Pasztor, The Wall Street Journal,  May 23, 1986  The Justice Department, stepping up efforts to prod Congress into deregulating most oil pipelines, is about to issue a report asserting that such legislation wouldn't create antitrust problems or hurt consumers.  Industry lawyers and government officials familiar with the report said it is likely to identify only a few large pipelines -- among them the Trans-Alaska pipeline that brings crude oil from Prudhoe Bay to the port of Valdez -- for which continued federal regulation of rates is appropriate. Officials who have reviewed earlier drafts of the study said those versions placed somewhere between five and 15 oil pipelines in that category.
