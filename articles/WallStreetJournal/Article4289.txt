 British Elections: Conservatives Appear Set to Capture Comfortable Majority in British Voting   By By James M. Perry, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 1987  Prime Minister Margaret Thatcher's Conservatives are likely to be popping champagne corks when the votes in Britain's general election are tallied tomorrow night. But the campaign has left a bad taste all around.  The latest public-opinion polls show the Conservatives on course to win a comfortable majority in the House of Commons that should see Mrs. Thatcher's programs safely through another four or five years.  The Marplan poll published in yesterday's Guardian, a morning newspaper, showed the Conservatives with 45% of the vote, the Labor Party with 32% and the Alliance of the Liberals and Social Democrats with 21%.
