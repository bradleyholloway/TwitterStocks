 Argentines Engage in a Labor of Love On a Railroad to Nowhere --- Volunteers Fight Weeds, Wasps and Highways In Hopes a Train Will Come Back, Someday   By Moffett, Matt, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 2010  Over several years, the train buffs have labored indomitably to restore about 100 miles of broken-down, weed-choked railway -- enduring extremes of weather, station-house squatters, scrap metal thieves and a variety of intrusive animals. In 2002, when rains washed out the dirt road that was the town of Espora's sole link to the outside world, a local farmer named Victor Rodriguez had the inspiration of making his way out on the tracks in a reconditioned railway motor car, clearing debris off the rails as he went.
