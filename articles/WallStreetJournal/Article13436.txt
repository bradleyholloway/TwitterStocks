 Politics & Economics: In Brief   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 6, 2007  In an interview with The Wall Street Journal, Japan's new finance minister said growth is his first priority as he tries to deal with Japan's biggest financial dilemma: Hacking down the large debt the country has been saddled with since the turn of the century. "If there are no tax revenues," Japan can't improve its finances, said Fukushiro Nukaga, who is taking over as Japan's finance minister after his party suffered a bad election defeat. "So first we have to get growth on course."
