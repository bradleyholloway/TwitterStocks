 Object of the Week: It's a Duesy   By By Alexandra Peers, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 1998  At its heyday in the 1930s, the (Fred) Duesenberg was the most politically incorrect of automobiles. Too rich, too big, too lavish for the deprivation of the Depression-era, it was a car favored by the Hollywood elite -- Gary Cooper owned one -- and the insulated wealthy. It was a car that cost more than $10,000 at a time most cost a few hundred dollars.
