 Ridge Issues New Terrorist-Attack Alert, Warning of a Possible Strike This Month   By By Yochi J. Dreazen and Gary Fields, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 4, 2001  U.S. intelligence officials say they also have been frustrated by the vague warnings, but stress the difficulty of evaluating potential threats. Before U.S. intelligence agencies issue a warning based on general threat information they assess the credibility of the source. An informant who has provided reliable information in the past is considered more reliable than someone who walks into an embassy warning of a possible threat; intercepted telephone calls are evaluated for signs that the speakers know their conversations might be tapped.
