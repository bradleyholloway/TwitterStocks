 Tiny Namibia settles into its democracy just fine, thank you   By Wells, Ken, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 1994  Five years after the South West Africa People's Organization, or SWAPO, won a landslide election victory and gained control of the newly formed legislature in Namibia, the party seems posed to capture its second easy victory. Despite a history of failed democracies in Africa, Namibia, led by Prime Minister Hage Geingob, seems a good-natured, self-assured democracy with pragmatists at its helm and the wind at its back.
