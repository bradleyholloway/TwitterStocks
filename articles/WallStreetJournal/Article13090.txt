 Hynix Sets Chip Pact With a Citigroup Arm; Deal to Sell Some Assets For $828.4 Million Cements The Turnaround of Firm   By Nystedt, Dan, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 6, 2004  Citigroup is buying Hynix's nonmemory-chip businesses, with assets including five semiconductor factories; more than 15,000 patents on chips, including those used in camera phones and LCD screens; and research-and-development centers in several countries. Citigroup and Hynix reached a preliminary agreement for the sale in June after lengthy negotiations, but finalization was delayed because of a spat over the final price and negotiations with minority shareholders who didn't want the division sold.
