 Notable & Quotable [3]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 3, 1986  High-tax countries have reduced imports by amounts varying from 20% to 50% between 1980 and 1984. . . . In the same period, low-tax countries have shown a corresponding increase in imports. Between 1980 and 1984, high-tax Sweden reduced its imports by 40%, the Netherlands by 35%, Belgium by 19%, Italy by 28%, Denmark 29%, Norway by 34%, France by 48%, Germany by 30%, U.K. by 17%, Greece by 39% and Ireland by 36%.
