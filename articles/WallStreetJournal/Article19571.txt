 Deals & Deal Makers: Regulators to Team Up to Build Plan for Analyst Abuses, IPOs   By By Charles Gasparino, Kate Kelly and Judith Burns, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 4, 2002  The accord comes amid a flurry of regulatory activity involving both analysts' conflicts and questionable practices involving the sale of initial public offerings of stock at some of the largest Wall Street firms. Wednesday, congressional investigators revealed that prominent executives at 21 companies personally received hot IPOs from Goldman Sachs Group Inc., which pocketed lucrative investment-banking fees from those companies during the market bubble of the late 1990s. Other firms alleged to have engaged in similar IPO activity are Citigroup Inc.'s Salomon Smith Barney unit and Credit Suisse Group's Credit Suisse First Boston unit. All three firms say they did nothing wrong.
