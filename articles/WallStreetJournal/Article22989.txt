 Airlines Seek to Cut Asia Services   By Reuters News Service, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 3, 2003  Airlines are stepping up pressure on the European Union to allow them more flexibility in slashing services as the outbreak of a virus in Asia spurs deeper cost-cutting, the International Air Transport Association said.  Airlines have lost $30 billion in the past two years as demand has been squashed by a global economic downturn, war, and now a killer pneumonia curtailing travel to and within Asia.
