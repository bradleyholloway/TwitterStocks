 Strawbridge to Pay $40.3 Million for Stake Of 17% Held by Group   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 1985  Strawbridge & Clothier said it agreed to pay $40.3 million to buy a 17% stake from a dissident holder group.  "We feel that by buying the shares we are protecting our independent status, and the company feels strongly that our independence is our most important competitive asset," said Francis R. Strawbridge III, chairman.  The company will buy the dissidents' 779,500 shares for $51.75 each, the executive said. Strawbridge shares closed Friday at $47.75, down 25 cents, in national over-the-counter trading.
