 Experts Advise Routine HIV Tests   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 10, 2005  Since the early years of the AIDS epidemic in the 1980s, the U.S. government has recommended screening in big cities, where AIDS rates are high, and among members of high-risk groups, such as gay men and drug addicts. But two large, federally funded studies found that the cost of routinely testing and treating nearly all adults would be offset by a reduction in new infections and the opportunity to start patients on drug treatment early, when it works best.
