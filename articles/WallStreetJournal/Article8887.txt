 Letters to the Editor: An Insular Attitude In Making Loans   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 1997  American Jews have a perpetual religious and historical obligation to devise programs that affirm their communal role as partners in our society's economic well-being. It is an error to think that in order to avoid depleting loan capital for the community's own needs, Hebrew Free Loan Societies and the American Jewish community must lend exclusively to Jews rather than also make loans to poor non-Jews.
