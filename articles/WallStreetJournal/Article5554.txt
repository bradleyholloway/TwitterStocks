 Business and Finance   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 9, 1998  INDONESIA FACED hoarding and fears of social unrest, amid concerns the IMF will suspend its $43 billion rescue package if Jakarta doesn't conform to IMF policies. Indonesian shares plunged 12% and the rupiah plummeted 26%, roiling markets throughout Asia. Meanwhile, the IMF, struggling to keep its regional rescue effort on track, is sending different messages to Indonesia and Thailand.  AT&T said it will acquire Teleport Communications for $11.3 billion in stock, an expensive but crucial move to penetrate local-phone markets. The long-distance giant's stock surged on a report the deal was coming.
