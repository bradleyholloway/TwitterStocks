 Italian officials target prosecutor after his remarks on corruption   By Bannon, Lisa, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 1994  The cabinet of Italian Prime Minister Silvio Berlusconi sent a formal complaint to President Oscar Luigi Scalfaro asking that the Superior Council of Magistrates, the country's judicial regulatory body, investigate whether Milan's chief prosecutor, Francesco Saverio Borrelli, broke the law by insinuating the prime minister may be ensnared in the country's epic two-and-a-half-year corruption investigations.
