 The Count: Manning's Great Even When He's Terrible   By Smith, Michael David, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 7, 2010  Mr. Manning is legendary for putting in long hours of practice and film study to perfect his timing with his receivers, but this season injuries have left him throwing to the likes of the undrafted rookie Blair White, who has started the past two games, and the previously little-used tight end Jacob Tamme, who had just six catches in 28 career games before having to take over for the injured Dallas Clark six weeks ago.
