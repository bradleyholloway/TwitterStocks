 Late Mortgage Payments Declined in First Quarter   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1988  WASHINGTON (AP) -- In another sign of improving economic conditions, the number of Americans late on their mortgage payments during the first three months of the year fell to the second-lowest level of this decade, according to a trade group of lenders.  The Mortgage Bankers Association reported that the number of mortgage payments late by 30 days or more dropped to 4.78% of total mortgages in the first quarter, down from 4.95% in the last three months of 1987.
