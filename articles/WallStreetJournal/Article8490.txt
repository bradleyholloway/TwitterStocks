 Medicine: Why specialists won't switch to primary care   By Anders, George; Cooper, Helene, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1994  Although many experts believe that the US's shortage of 100,000 primary-care physicians can best be met by retraining the 100,000 surplus specialists, there are a number of problems with the idea. One problem is that the technical, precise nature of a specialty suits the personalities of those who go into the field, and they would not be as good as generalists.
