 Ahead of the Tape   By By Jesse Eisinger, The Wall Street Journal,  May 7, 2002  That may be true, but is less important to those who invest in individual companies, as opposed to macroeconomic trends. The faith of these people has slowly been eroded by concerns about company accounts. These predated Enron, but now people really care. And it isn't just tech and telecom companies that had aggressive bookkeeping, but the Xeroxes, Dynegys and GEs. It isn't just one accounting firm or one investment bank's analyst operations, but the twisted cultures of accounting and Wall Street.
