 How a Disputed Insurance Claim May Mean Bankruptcy in Indonesia   By By Michael Schuman, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 21, 2001  Last year, an obscure company disputed Manulife's purchase of shares in its local subsidiary from its bankrupt partner, and in the ensuing investigation, one Manulife executive was held by police for three weeks without being charged. Last December, another unpaid beneficiary used the same tactic of threatening Manulife with bankruptcy, and the company was forced to pay.
