 Financing small business: As buyers woo smaller concerns, acquisition costs rise   By Gupta, Udayan, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 1995  According to a survey by Buyouts, a New York industry newsletter, buyers are paying more for small and midsize companies, as demand grows. Buyers are paying an average of 6.9 times earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT), which is 19% higher than the price paid in 1993 and 9.5% lower than the high during the booming 1980s.
