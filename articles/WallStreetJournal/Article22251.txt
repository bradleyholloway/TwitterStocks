 Hanbo puts strain on South Korean banks   By Schuman, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 3, 1997  The bankruptcy of Hanbo Steel Industry Co has severely strained South Korea's banking sector. Hanbo Steel received court protection from its many creditors after amassing nearly five trillion won ($5.8 billion) in debt. Worst off is Korea First Bank, which lent almost 1.1 trillion won to Hanbo. But the Hanbo crisis is only the latest straw. In the last two years, nearly 25,000 bankruptcies have added mountains of bad debt to the banks' books.
