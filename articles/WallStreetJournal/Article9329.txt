 Foreign Investment To Buy U.S. Businesses Rises 17% to a Record   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 2001  The report didn't contain information about specific deals, but it said some of the largest foreign purchases were in the food, petroleum, telecommunications and finance industries. Foreigners spent $144.87 billion last year to acquire U.S. manufacturing companies, including $43.9 billion for computer and electronic-products firms, and they spent $62.2 billion to buy information companies.
