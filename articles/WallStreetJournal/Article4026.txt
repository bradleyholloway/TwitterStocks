 Consumers Power Debt Restructuring Pact Seen Ready   By By Amal Nag, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 10, 1985  Citibank reached an understanding with financially ailing Consumers Power Co. on restructuring the utility's $1.2 billion debt, Citibank sources said.  The proposed debt restructuring follows the Michigan Public Service Commission's recent offer to allow Consumers Power to raise electric rates by $99 million annually for six years. The rate increase depends on the utility's meeting several conditions, including repaying the $1.2 billion bank debt and $700 million non-bank debt without any increase in current interest rates on the debts.
