 Your money matters: Weekend report: New medical plans for small businesses carry investment options, but also risks   By Jeffrey, Nancy Ann, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1997  After years of debate, tax-free "medical savings accounts" for the self-employed and people who work for small businesses finally arrived on Jan 1, 1997. Under a four-year program, more than one million people could use MSAs to pay for routine health-care expenses in connection with special high-deductible health-insurance policies. But some financial planners and consumer advocates worry that people could put their health at risk by sinking funds intended for medical expense into the stock market.
