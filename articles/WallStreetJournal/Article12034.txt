 Are bonds headed for disaster again?   By Chopra, Rajan, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 1995  Rajan Chopra warns that the arguments for a continuing bullish bond market are "riddled with overstated expectations and flawed assumptions," and ignore key economic fundamentals. Chopra says that if the Fed fails to ease interest rates, the "ensuing market rout" will be "swift and sharp," and the arbitragers will "flee the market at the first hint of trouble."
