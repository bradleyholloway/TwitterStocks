 In France, it's unemployment, stupid!   By Lavin, Douglas; Kamm, Thomas, The Wall Street Journal,  May 23, 1997  As the first round of French legislative elections on May 25, 1997 approaches, much of the populace remains unconvinced that either the right or the left has an answer to France's No 1 problem: a record 12.8% jobless rate. The governing center-right coalition wants to reignite the economy through spending and tax cuts, whereas the Socialists want to stimulate demand by raising wages and cutting the workweek to 35 hours without reducing pay.
