 The San Diego Zoo Has Become A Veritable Hot Bed of Activity   By By Deborah Yaeger, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 14, 1985  That's what the folks at the San Diego Zoo did when their Komodo dragons, the only ones in the Western Hemisphere, slithered away from any romantic entanglements on the cold cement floor of their cage. The 300-pound Indonesian lizards now share a king-size (8-foot square) 100-degree-Fahrenheit bed. Though the lizards have yet to scale the heights of passion, "they have gotten more active," says John Andy Phillips, a research physiologist at the zoo and inventor of the beds.
