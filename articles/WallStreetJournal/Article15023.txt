 Election '92: The Presidential Vote, State by State   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 5, 1992  A table provides a breakdown of the voting pattern for all 50 states, including the District of Columbia, for the 1992 presidential election. President-elect Bill Clinton received 370 electoral votes, compared to President Bush's 168, and Perot's 19. Of the popular vote, Clinton received 43,728,375, Bush 38,167,416 and Perot 19,237,247.
