 Phillips Petroleum Sells Oil and Gas Interests to Shell   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 5, 1986  Last month, Phillips said it was negotiating with Houston-based Shell, a unit of Royal Dutch/Shell Group, to sell the California properties, which include both onshore and offshore interests. The properties include Phillips's stake in the offshore Huntington Beach Field, which produces 12,600 barrels of oil a day; the offshore Beta Field, 1,650 barrels of oil; Lion Rock Unit, an undeveloped offshore property; Purisma Point Unit, another undeveloped offshore property; two fields in the San Joaquin Valley, one of which produces 2,000 barrels a day. Phillips had received cash from its interest in the second San Joaquin field.
