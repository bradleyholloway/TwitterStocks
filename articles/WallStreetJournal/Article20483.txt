 U.S. News: Drug War Dims Texas Town --- For Years, McAllen Profited From Ties to Mexico, but Nearby Violence Is Reversing Gains   By Campoy, Ana, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 2010  At a time when Mexicans usually flood McAllen's malls, northbound vehicle traffic from Reynosa's main bridge fell 31% from the same vacation period last year, according to the Mexican agency that operates federal roads and bridges. Hotel occupancy rates were down to 70% from 73% last year -- well below the 94% rate during the Easter holiday period in 2008, according to data from STR Global, a research firm that compiles hotel information.
