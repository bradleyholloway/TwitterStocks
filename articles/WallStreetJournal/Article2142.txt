 Business and Finance [3]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 20, 1990  BUDGET NEGOTIATORS are considering proposals to curb itemized tax deductions, including those for mortgage-interest payments, charitable giving and state and local tax payments. The idea of restricting itemized deductions has emerged because of Democratic demands that any tax package hit the wealthy harder than other groups.  AT&T's profit slipped 6% in the second quarter, reflecting lower longdistance revenue and sluggish equipment sales. But the company said it still expects record 1990 earnings.  American Express earnings rose only 1.1% in the second quarter, weighed down by results at its Shearson brokerage unit. Shearson's revenue slumped 20% in the quarter.
