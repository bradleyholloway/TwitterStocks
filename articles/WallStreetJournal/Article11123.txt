 Foreign Investors Allowed In Taiwan Securities Firms --- Special to The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 1988  Under the new rules, foreign investors will each be allowed to hold as much as 10% of the shares of a local securities firm, with total foreign ownership of a local firm limited to 40%. Foreign securities houses also will be allowed at a later time to set up branches in Taiwan. But the liberalization of the local market will proceed gradually to allow local firms time to adjust to foreign competition, said Lu Dauny Yen, vice chairman of Taiwan's Securities and Exchange Commission.
