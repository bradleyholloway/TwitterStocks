 Gillette Net Rises 19% With Help From New Products   By Christina Cheddar Berk, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 2005  Gillette said net income rose to $449 million, or 45 cents a share, from $376 million, or 37 cents a share, a year earlier. Analysts, on average, expected Gillette to earn 43 cents a share, according to Thomson First Call.  For example, at its razor and blade business, Gillette began shipping its M3Power razors to additional European countries and to Australia, and rolled out a new version of the razor, the M3Power Nitro, in the U.S. The company also launched its Venus disposable razors for women in North America and Europe in February.
