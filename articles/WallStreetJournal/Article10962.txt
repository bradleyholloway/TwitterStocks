 IRS Bids to Shrink Estate-Tax Loophole Created by Provision in Overhaul Bill   By By Alan Murray, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 6, 1987  Under the tax law, an estate can sell securities to an employee stock-ownership plan and then deduct half the proceeds from the taxable value of the estate. The law contains hardly any restrictions on this ploy, thus creating the possibility that employee stock plans could become huge tax-shelter mills for people attempting to escape estate taxes.
