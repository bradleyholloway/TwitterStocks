 REVIEW & OUTLOOK (Editorial): Patch, Patch, Patch   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 1985  The tragic proportion of New York City's housing crisis is borne out by daily reports of evictions of vulnerable tenants, particularly the poor and the elderly, from the most miserable of that city's domiciles, known in city parlance as SROs. That stands for single-room-occupancy hotels. For many of the city's most beleaguered residents, they are the last stop before the street. Owners of SROs, buildings that often sit on prime Manhattan land, try to evict residents so they can upgrade their properties -- to luxury condominiums, for example.
