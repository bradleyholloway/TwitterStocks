 Regional Phone Concerns' Costs to Rise For Short Term Due to New FCC Rules   By By Leon E. Wynter, The Wall Street Journal,  May 2, 1986  The Federal Communications Commission adopted new accounting rules that will produce a short-term increase in the operating expenses that the regional telephone companies report to the agency.  But John Curry, the FCC's accounting director, said, "It would be very misleading to say that this will be translated directly into higher rates for the ratepayers." Mr. Curry said that the immediate increase in expenses amounted to a small percentage of the telephone companies' total costs, which can be influenced by many other factors.
