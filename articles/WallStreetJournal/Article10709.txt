 Eyebrows Go Up As Auditors Branch Out   By Rapoport, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 7, 2012  The rise in Deloitte's nonaudit revenue spotlights a recent resurgence in consulting and other nonaudit work by the Big Four accounting firms, a decade after conflict-of-interest concerns and corporate scandal sharply limited such work. The growing focus on consulting and other nonaudit services "threatens to weaken the strength of the audit practice in the firm overall," James Doty, chairman of the Public Company Accounting Oversight Board, the U.S. government's auditing regulator, said at the conference.
