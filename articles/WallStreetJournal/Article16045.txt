 Futures & Options: Making Mother's Day Memories   By By Robert J. Hughes, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 2001  BRUNCH AND A BOAT RIDE? This romantic restaurant in the New York borough of Queens offers spectacular views of the Manhattan skyline across the East River and special brunch and dinner menus for Mother's Day. Manhattanites can skip the traffic and take water ferries back and forth to the restaurant from an East River pier. Brunch runs from 11:30 a.m. to 1:30 p.m., and costs $45 per adult, $25 per child. Dinner is 3 to 8 p.m., and it is $55 for adults, $27.50 for children.  THIS 1902 HOTEL in northern New Hampshire, built in a Spanish Renaissance style, features crystal chandeliers and Tiffany stained-glass windows, plus a private railway that carries passengers to the top of Mount Washington. For the hotel's special Mother's Day brunch, a musical performance will accompany the eggs Benedict, pastries and desserts. Brunch is from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. With the purchase of a ticket for another adult or a child, Mom will ride free. Trains leave hourly. Fares are $44 per adult and $30 per child age six to 12.  THIS MOTHER'S DAY walking tour begins at the Charles Gates Dawes House, a national historic landmark. Mr. Dawes was vice president under Calvin Coolidge, and he won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1925. The tour also takes in the homes of six current Evanstonians near the Northwestern University campus and several historic university buildings not usually open to the public. Refreshments will be served during tour hours. Tickets are $30 beforehand, $35 on Mother's Day. The tour begins at 1 and ends at 5 p.m.
