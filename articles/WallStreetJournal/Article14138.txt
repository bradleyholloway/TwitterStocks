 Sallie Mae Agrees On 5-Year Yen Loan Equal to $40 Million   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 5, 1985  The agreement is the latest effort by the association, also called Sallie Mae, to diversify its funding sources. In the past 12 months, Sallie Mae, which acts as an intermediary in the market for government-insured student loans, has made four securities offerings purchased largely by Japanese and European investors. Proceeds from the yen loan, to be exchanged for dollars, will be used for general purposes.
