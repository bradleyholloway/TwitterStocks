 Ahead of the Tape   By Lahart, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 12, 2007  The rapid growth of London's financial markets has raised alarm bells among New York officials, who worry that Wall Street is losing its edge, if it hasn't already lost it. It's hard to say that London's prominence hasn't brought anything but good things to the U.K. But good things could come to an end some day. A financial episode like the 1994 Mexican peso crisis, the 1997 Asian financial crisis or the 1998 Russian debt crisis would be a tough blow for financial centers around the world.  The U.K. economy has always been dependent on London's financial sector. Greater London -- the area that covers London and its boroughs, but not the "commuter belt" that surrounds them -- accounted for 17% of Britain's economy in 2005, according to U.K. government statistics.
