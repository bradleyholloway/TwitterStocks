 Grain Prices Fall Moderately on Theory Bush May Allow Soviet Cash Purchases; Metals Decline   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 21, 1991  Future prices settled down a bit on Aug 21, 1991, the second day after the coup in the Soviet Union, but traders remained sensitive to developments. Grain futures moved over broad ranges but finished mostly lower. September corn was off four cents a bushel and September wheat lost 3.5 cents.
