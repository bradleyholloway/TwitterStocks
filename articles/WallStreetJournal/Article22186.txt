 Economy accelerated without inflation in second quarter, but pace may slow   By Duff, Christina, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 2, 1996  The US economy soared during the second quarter of 1996 without inflation riding on its coattails, but economists question how long such a momentum can last. The Commerce Dept said that second-quarter GDP jumped at an annual rate of 4.2%, far outpacing the 2% first-quarter gain and the meager 0.3% rise in the final quarter of 1995.
