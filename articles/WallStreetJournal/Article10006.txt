 S&P Buys Goldman Commodity Index   By Cote, Jim, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 7, 2007  There are several commodity indexes, but GSCI is one of the biggest. According to S&P, about $60 billion in institutional investor funds track this index, which will be renamed the S&P GSCI after a transition period.  The GSCI, which dates to 1991, is a global production-weighted commodity index that is based on 24 exchange-traded futures contracts for energy, industrial-metals, precious-metals, agricultural and livestock products.
