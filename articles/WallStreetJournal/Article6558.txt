 Noted ...   The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 2006  AXIS CAPITAL HOLDINGS Ltd. (Pembroke, Bermuda) -- This insurance- and-reinsurance provider appointed David B. Greenfield chief financial officer, effective in the third quarter. Mr. Greenfield, 43, succeeds Andrew Cook, who is leaving the company for personal reasons. Mr. Cook, 43, couldn't be reached for comment. Mr. Greenfield will join the company's executive-management group. He joins the company from KPMG LLP, where he served as global-sector chair for insurance and a member of the global financial-services leadership team.  MANATT, PHELPS & PHILLIPS LLP (Los Angeles) -- Claudia Callaway was named a partner in this law firm's Washington office. Ms. Callaway, 39, was most recently a partner in the Washington office of Paul, Hastings, Janofsky & Walker LLP. She continues to serve on Bryn Mawr College's president's advisory council, and as chairwoman of the diversity-in-the-courtroom subcommittee of the American Bar Association's trial-practice committee.  SKADDEN, ARPS, SLATE, MEAGHER & FLOM LLP (New York) -- Dave Robison, former national chief of appeals at the Internal Revenue Service, was named a senior adviser of tax-resolution strategies in the tax group at this law firm's Washington and New York offices. Mr. Robison, 55, served as the IRS liaison to President Bush's advisory panel on tax reform in 2005.
