 Sony Agrees to Pay $1.8 Million to U.S. To End Price Dispute   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 9, 1987  NEWARK, N.J. -- Sony Corp. of America agreed to pay the federal government about $1.8 million to settle a pricing dispute.  The agreement, announced here by U.S. Attorney Thomas W. Greelish, is the result of a dispute between Sony and the General Services Administration concerning four government contracts the company held between 1980 and 1984.
