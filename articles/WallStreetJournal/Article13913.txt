 WEEKEND INVESTOR --- Taking Advantage Of the Downturn --- Market Selloffs Are Never Fun; But There Are Ways for Smart Investors to Capitalize   By Levisohn, Ben; Saunders, Laura; Greene, Kelly, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 6, 2011  Yet there are good reasons to expect the market slump to ease, at least in the short term. Since 1962, the Standard & Poor's 500-stock index has seen 25 corrections of 10% during a bull market -- but in only nine of them did the losses grow to 20% or more, according to Birinyi Associates Inc. That means there is only slightly more than a 1-in-3 chance that the market is going to keep cratering.
