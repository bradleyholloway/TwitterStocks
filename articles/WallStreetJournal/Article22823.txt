 Royal Dutch/Shell Says Net Income Fell 20% in Period   By By Bhushan Bahree, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 2, 2001  The Anglo/Dutch company's return on average capital employed, an important oil-industry performance measure, rose to 20.8% in the 12 months ended Sept. 30 from 16.6% a year earlier. J.J. Traynor, an analyst at Deutsche Bank in Edinburgh, Scotland, noted that Shell's capital spending was falling behind its targets, boosting its return on capital but also indicating the company was having trouble spending as much as it had intended for growth.
