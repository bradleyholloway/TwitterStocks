 IBM Will Pay the U.S. $14.8 Million to Settle False Claims Charge   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 1993  ARMONK, N.Y. -- International Business Machines Corp. will pay the U.S. government $14.8 million to settle a potential False Claims Act civil suit.  The computer giant admitted to providing reconditioned or remanufactured equipment, instead of new gear, to the government. Some IBM contracts had required it to ship only new equipment, or to notify the government if it intended to ship anything else, according to the Justice Department.
