 Summer Olympics 1992: Value of Precious Medals Fluctuates   By Thurow, Roger; Forman, Craig, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 1992  Although athletes from North America or a wealthy European or Asian country who win an Olympic medal often gain some measure of celebrity status in addition to opportunities in the business world, athletes from poorer countries such as Tajikistan, Romania, Suriname and Namibia cannot always count on parlaying their medals into fame and fortune.
