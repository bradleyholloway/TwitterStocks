 Jobs aren't the problem   By Liscio, John, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1993  John Liscio discusses recent revisions in Labor Department statistics which indicate that job growth in the US is solid. He says that the economy is doing better than most people realize and that the main problem at the moment is that consumers are holding back because of President Clinton's proposal to boost income taxes retroactive to Jan 1, 1993.
