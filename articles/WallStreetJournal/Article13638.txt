 Corporate News: GM Logs Record Sales in China --- Beijing Incentives Boost Demand for Small Cars, but Effect Is Expected to Lessen   By Patricia Jiayi Ho, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 2009  GM, which posted China sales of 151,084 units in April, has two joint ventures in the country: passenger-vehicle maker Shanghai General Motors Corp., a 50-50 venture with Shanghai Automotive Industry Corp.; and mini-commercial vehicle maker SAIC-GM-Wuling Automobile Co., a three-way partnership with SAIC and Liuzhou Wuling Motors Co. The popularity of GM's smaller vehicles in China underscores the impact of government measures to boost demand for autos, said CSM Worldwide analyst Yale Zhang.
