 Banking: Small accounts feel squeeze by big banks   By O Brien, Timothy L, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 1995  First Chicago Corp and other banks are introducing more and more fees aimed at small depositors, who are frequently the least profitable customers, leading consumer advocates to worry that policies favoring wealthier customers will leave low- and middle-income depositors with inadequate, or unbearably expensive, bank services.
