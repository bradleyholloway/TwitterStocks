 Anthrax Vaccine Is Called Effective, But More Reliable One Is Needed   By By Jill Carroll, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 7, 2002  The National Academy of Sciences' Institute of Medicine, in a new report, said the current vaccine is "relatively crude" but effective against the three types of anthrax infections: inhalation, cutaneous and gastrointestinal. The review committee also said it was unlikely anthrax could be genetically altered to resist the vaccine and still retain its ability to make people sick.
