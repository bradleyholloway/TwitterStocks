 Letters to the Editor: Why TV May Get Even Duller   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 2002  It is this personal dynamic that is missing today, and blame must fall largely on effects of consolidation; if the recent appeals court ruling holds up permitting companies to own cable companies and broadcast stations in the same market -- and the FCC lifts its rule limiting broadcasters to a 35% share of the U.S. television audience -- well, it's just a further capitulation to the industry giants who already control most of the programming package. The viewer, then, may expect to see more demographic targeting, expansion of commercial time, more program cloning and a further glut of sitcom reruns, for these concomitants of corporate communications programming are fast becoming the industry's trademark.
