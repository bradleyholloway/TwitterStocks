 Arts & Entertainment: Before Photoshop, Artists Retouched Greatness   By Bortolot, Lana, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 2012  "People then understood that photographs were not always true," said Mia Fineman, a photo historian and curator of "Faking It: Manipulated Photography Before Photoshop," a new exhibition at the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Opening Thursday, the exhibit examines the history of the doctored-up image, from its beginnings less than a decade after the practical camera was introduced, to the early years of Photoshop, the imaging software that made "faking it" as easy as a mouse click.
