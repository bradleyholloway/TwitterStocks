 New Securities Issues [5]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 9, 1995  Citizens Utilities Co. -- $125 million of bonds due July 1, 2035, priced as 7.45s at 99.918 to yield 7.456%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 80 basis points above Treasurys. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and triple-A by Standard & Poor's Ratings Group, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.  Delmarva Power & Light Co. -- $125.8 million of debt priced in two parts through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch. The first tranche consists of $100 million of bonds due January 1, 2025, priced at par to yield 7.71%, a spread of 110 basis points above Treasurys. The bonds are noncallable for 10 years. The second tranche consists of $25.8 million of amortizing bonds due June 1, 2008, priced at par to yield 6.95%. The entire issue is rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A by S&P.  Sears Roebuck Acceptance Corp. -- $250 million of notes due June 15, 2000, priced as 6.5s at 99.583 to yield 6.559%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 60 basis points above Treasurys. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. Sears Roebuck Acceptance is a subsidiary of Sears, Roebuck & Co.
