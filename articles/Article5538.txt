 Mapping the mind: Word processing: Stroke patients yield clues to brain's ability to create language   By Bishop, Jerry E, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 12, 1993  Studies of people who have suffered brain damage that affects their ability to deal with a single part of speech, such as verbs, are revealing a capability of the human brain unsuspected until recently, Neuroscientists say the brain doesn't simply build a mental dictionary in learning language, but rather it sorts and stores words by grammatical category.
