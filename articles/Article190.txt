Music defines the cultural life of New Orleans, perhaps more than it does in any other city in the United States. Whether it's a tribe of Mardi Gras Indians leading hundreds of people, singing and dancing, down the city's side streets, or Sunday brass bands that get entire neighborhoods to supply percussion accompaniment with bottles and cans, live music is part of the city's everyday existence.  Tonight, two of New Orleans's finest bands - if such a simple word is appropriate for the complicated social structures that have produced the groups - come to Tramps. They will also play at Central Park Summerstage on Sunday. It will be a good way to see if the euphoria they make at home translates in New York City.  To see the full article, subscribe here.
