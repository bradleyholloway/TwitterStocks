 Niagara Mohawk Agrees to Pay Plan With Oswego, N.Y.   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 1998  SYRACUSE, N.Y. -- Niagara Mohawk Power Corp. and the city of Oswego, N.Y., reached a payment-in-lieu-of-taxes agreement earlier this month related to Niagara Mohawk's Oswego-based fossil-fuel plant.  Niagara Mohawk, a utility serving upstate New York, has put the plant up for sale as part of a giant restructuring it calls PowerChoice. The company will pay taxes of $2 million by 2003 if it can sell the plant, and $3 million by 2002 if it can't.
