 IBM and Apple Open New Front in PC Wars with Strategic Alliance   By Zachary, G Pascal; Hooper, Laurence, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 5, 1991  The world's two largest PC makers, IBM and Apple, have announced a wide-ranging alliance by attempting to create an operating system that makes computers easier to use, allows users of the company's different machines to link up and swap information, and taps the underused power of the latest microprocessors. The effort is aimed at competitors Microsoft Corp and Intel Corp, and could change the balance of power among computer sellers, software companies and microprocessor purveyors by driving the entire industry toward the goal of complete compatibility of computer systems.
