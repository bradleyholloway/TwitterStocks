 ShopKo Stores, Dress Barn Fall; Ulticom Advances   By Cordeiro, Anjali, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 9, 2005  The Russell 2000 Index fell 3.85, or 0.57%, to 673.47, and the Standard & Poor's SmallCap 600 Index fell 1.89, or 0.54%, to 350.04. The Nasdaq Composite Index of large and small-cap stocks fell 6, or 0.28%, to 2166.03.  Home-construction companies declined sharply, after large-cap Hovnanian Enterprises' fiscal third-quarter earnings missed Wall Street's estimates and the company provided an earnings outlook for 2006 that is below analyst expectations. Cavalier Homes (Amex) fell 41 cents, or 5.9%, to 6.49; Nobility Homes fell 2.63, or 8.6%, to 28.12; and William Lyon Homes (NYSE) dropped 8.69, or 5.5%, to 150.51.  Retailers also declined, after large-cap Sears Holdings posted weaker-than-expected second-quarter earnings. ShopKo Stores (NYSE) fell 38 cents, or 1.5%, to 25.52, and Dress Barn fell 44 cents, or 1.7%, to 25.
