 Comment: Towers of Light   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 2002  Finally, at five minutes of seven, with everyone's head still eagerly tilted at the space between the crosses atop the Catholic Center at NYU and Judson Memorial Baptist Church on West 4th Street, the beams rose up like shooting stars or elevators reclaiming vanished shafts, leaving a bright trace in their wake. They were thin, thinner than the buildings had been, and very tall. Their height brought gawkers out from under trees and lampposts, and heads craned backward in order to look straight up. High in the heavens, the beams seemed to collect themselves into a single circular link, a kind of corona that one could imagine as a symbolic portal to the souls of the dead. The square was quiet. The only sound was the breeze in the bare trees.
