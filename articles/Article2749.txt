 Germany's law on inside trading brings a conviction   By Marshall, Matt, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 21, 1995  German authorities on Aug 18, 1995 announced their first insider-trading conviction, fining the son of the owner of Krones AG $1.3 million. The conviction under the year-old insider-trading law boosted Germany's effort to improve its image as a serious financial center, but many domestic and foreign observers said Germany still has a ways to go.
