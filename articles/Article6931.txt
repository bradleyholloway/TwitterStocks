 Investor's Calendar   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 12, 2005  Changes at Viacom: Shari Redstone is expected to be named vice chairman of Viacom, the media conglomerate controlled by her father, Sumner Redstone, at a board meeting. Viacom's board is also expected to give its final approval to a proposal, announced earlier this year, to split the company in two.  Briefly: Intel slightly raised its second-quarter sales forecast, as the chip maker continued to ride strong demand for notebook computers . . . Citigroup agreed to pay $2 billion to settle a class-action suit over its role in the sale of Enron stock and bonds before the energy company's collapse . . . Washington Mutual agreed to buy Providian in a $6.45 billion deal that will make it one of the top U.S. credit-card issuers . . . Apple and Intel announced an agreement for the computer maker to use Intel chips, paving the way for more-powerful Macs.
