 Yahoo's Net Surged in 2nd Quarter; Internet Company Raises Its Forecast as Demand For Ads Keeps Growing   By Mangalindan, Mylene, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 10, 2003  Shares of Yahoo have climbed steadily this year, up 113% to date, buoyed by investor optimism that the company's diversification efforts and improved relationships with advertisers will pay off as advertising spending rebounds. Electronic-commerce companies Amazon.com Inc. and eBay Inc. also have seen their shares double this year thus far. In comparison, the Nasdaq Composite Index has risen 25% this year.  Yahoo's advertising-related revenue rose to $219.2 million in the second quarter. That's a 44% increase from the year-earlier period. Fee-related revenue was $69.9 million, up 43% from last year, while revenue from listings, such as job postings and personals, totaled $32.3 million, up 29% from a year earlier.
