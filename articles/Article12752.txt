 Retirement-Program Overhaul for Railroads Wins Senate Approval   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 2001  Proponents say the legislation could save as much as $400 million annually in payroll taxes for freight railroads, Amtrak and some commuter railroads, as well as boost benefits for retirees. But some Republican senators say the legislation amounts to a federal subsidy of railroad retirement and could take money from the federal budget.
