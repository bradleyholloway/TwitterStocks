 Letters to the Editor: Airlines Should Give Value for the Price   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 14, 2001  The challenge the airlines face is that a walk-up (read as full-fare) ticket from Chicago to New York is $1,410 whereas a time-restricted ticket is $389. What do the airlines give the traveler in return? No meals, poor service and underpaid frontline employees. Maybe the airlines that gouge the traveler need to provide some value for that price.
