 Legal beat: Setback for Nationwide   By Novak, Viveca; Pereira, Joseph, The Wall Street Journal,  May 5, 1995  A federal appeals court dealt Nationwide Insurance Enterprise a setback in its effort to challenge the federal government's authority to investigate charges of insurance redlining. While Nationwide has repeatedly denied charges of discrimination, the company maintains that the federal government has no authority to investigate such complaints.
