 Rauschenberg's Revolution...   By Rose, Barbara, The Wall Street Journal,  May 14, 2008  Born like Janis Joplin in the provincial oil town of Port Arthur, Texas, Rauschenberg attended the legendary Black Mountain College, near Asheville, N.C., off and on in the early 1950s, because he felt that he lacked the rigorous discipline that the Bauhaus artist Josef Albers, while encouraging experiments with materials, imposed on his American students there.
