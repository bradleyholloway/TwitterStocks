David Wassner, president of the board of trustees of Maimonides Medical Center in Brooklyn, was killed Sunday evening in a two-car collision in the Borough Park section of Brooklyn.  He was 58 years old, and lived in the Flatbush section of Brooklyn.  Mr. Wassner was also executive vice president of Arnav Industries Inc., sportswear manufacturers, in Manhattan. He had been a trustee of Maimonides since 1969, and was elected president of the board in 1980.  He is survived by his wife, the former Mala Weiss; a daughter, Lois Hager of Brooklyn; a son, Leonard of Brooklyn; two brothers, Robert of Manhattan and Joseph of Belle Harbor, Queens, and two grandchildren.
