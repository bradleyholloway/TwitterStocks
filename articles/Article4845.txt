 Business and Finance   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 12, 1988  BLUE-CHIP STOCKS rebounded in the afternoon, though most other shares ended lower in light trading. The Dow Jones industrials closed up 33.82 points, at 1945.13. Bond prices were little changed, while the dollar recovered from a mild decline, helped by resumed central bank intervention. Foreign stocks dropped in reaction to Friday's plunge in New York.  Reagan said the October crash resulted from market factors and not from the U.S. trade or budget deficits or the sliding dollar. The president claimed, incorrectly, that the Brady report exonerated his policies.  Sterling Drug charged in a lawsuit that two Hoffmann-La Roche units illegally traded in Sterling stock and options before the Swiss company began a takeover bid for Sterling.
