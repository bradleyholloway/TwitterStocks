 Chiefs Say Defense Needs Bigger Raise Than Clinton Plans   By By Thomas E. Ricks, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 6, 1999  The Joint Chiefs of Staff told a Senate hearing that President Clinton's promised defense increase is barely enough to address their immediate problems and is far less than is needed in the long term.  The president's promise to effectively boost the military's purchasing power by $12 billion in fiscal year 2000 is "a good start," said Gen. Michael Ryan, the Air Force chief of staff. His service's readiness is "very fragile," and has been plummeting in recent months, he told the Senate Armed Services Committee.
