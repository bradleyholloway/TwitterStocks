 Growth Costs Taiwan in Social Dislocations, as the Big Time Looms   By Lehner, Urban C, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 5, 1991  Like many of its entrepreneurs, Taiwan has come a long way fast but still has a long way to go to reach its goal of moving beyond the "newly industrializing" category to join the exclusive club of advanced economies. Though South Korea has Japanese-type advantages like a homogeneous population and an ability to take the long view, the average Taiwanese is now about twice as rich as the average Korean. Taiwan's government has close to $75 billion in foreign-exchange reserves and no net overseas debt, but it faces problems of diminishing economic growth. Taiwan can no longer rely on low wages and a cheap currency to propel exports and its low rate of R&D investment may hinder it in the long run.
