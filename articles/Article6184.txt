 A Special Weekly Report From The Wall Street Journal's Capital Bureau   By By Ronald G. Shafer, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 12, 1999  The Fed chief appears unmoved for now by soaring stocks, rising long-term rates and an economy that ignores Fed forecasts of a slowdown. He peppers speeches with reassuring phrases like: "There have been no obvious signs of emerging inflation pressures." A change in Greenspan's tone would likely precede a rate increase.  The absence of inflation and persistent weakness abroad hurts Fed hawks' case for raising rates at a March 30 meeting. Greenspan's predictions about a technology-driven surge in productivity draw more support from Fed insiders. "Evidence seems to be building," says San Francisco Fed President Robert Parry.  Kentucky Sen. McConnell, chairman of the National Republican Senatorial Committee, meets twice with New Jersey Gov. Whitman to urge her to run for retiring Democratic Sen. Lautenberg's seat. Nevada Republicans favor Rep. Ensign to seek retiring Democratic Sen. Bryan's seat; they try to discourage GOP Rep. Gibbons from entering the race.
