 Nasdaq small-order plan draws protests   By Harlan, Christi, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 21, 1993  The Small Order Execution System, the Nasdaq Stock Market trading system that was designed to protect small investors, is drawing protests from the NASD, which has asked the SEC to reduce the size of orders eligible for trading on the system due to a flood of orders. The SEC, in turn, has been flooded with letters from traders, investors and others arguing against such limits. The SEC is to vote on the issue Dec 22, 1993.
