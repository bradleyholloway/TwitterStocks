 Stocks Overcome Oil's Sticker Shock, and Finish Higher   By Browning, E S, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 12, 2005  "The great majority of companies now have reported second-quarter earnings, and on average they have beaten expectations," said Tim Heekin, director of trading at San Francisco brokerage firm Thomas Weisel Partners. Money managers "are selectively putting money to work," he said.  The Standard & Poor's 500-stock index rose 0.71%, or 8.68 points, to 1237.81, up 2.1% this year. The Nasdaq Composite Index advanced 0.78%, or 16.74 points, to 2174.55, virtually unchanged on the year.  Outside the U.S., stocks advanced in dollar terms. The Dow Jones World Stock Index, excluding U.S. stocks, rose 0.68%, or 1.23 points, to 183.20.
