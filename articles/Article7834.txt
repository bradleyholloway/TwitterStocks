 Aging baby boomers will swell elderly ranks   By Anonymous, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 7, 1989  Over the next 50 years, the number of people in the US who are 65 or older is expected to more than double the current number of 30 million, to reach 68 million. The first baby boomers will reach 65 in 22 years with the rest of the baby boomers following, raising the percentage for the next 20 years. In 1985, one-fifth of people who died were 85 years old or older and by the year 2040 this number should increase to more than one third.
