 Johnson & Johnson Seeks Approval to Sell Sweetner, Says 4th-Period Net Fell 24%   By By Michael Waldholz, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 1987  NEW BRUNSWICK, N.J. -- Johnson & Johnson said it sought government clearance to market a new artificial sweetener, and posted a 24% profit decline for the fourth quarter because of one-time charges.  But Johnson & Johnson could face months or years of government safety reviews before the new product can be sold.  Based largely on the news concerning the sweetener, Johnson & Johnson shares jumped $3 in composite trading on the New York Stock Exchange, closing at $80 each on volume of 1,024,000 shares. Monsanto stock fell 37.5 cents a share to $78.
