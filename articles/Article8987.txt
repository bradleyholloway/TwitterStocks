 Boston Chicken Inc. Could Be Plucked From Nasdaq Market --- The Former Highflier Fails To Meet Listing Needs; Quarterly Loss Is Posted   By By Alejandro Bodipo-Memba, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 1998  The troubled Golden, Colo., restaurant owner and franchiser has been trying to restructure its debt, raise cash and revive sluggish sales. But investors have become discouraged and the company's stock has become little more than chicken feed, closing yesterday at $1.125, down 43.75 cents.  To be in the Nasdaq National Market listings, however, a stock must maintain a minimum bid price of $5 a share for 30 consecutive trading days. Boston Chicken failed to meet that requirement from midMay to the end of June.
