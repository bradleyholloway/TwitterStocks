 Weather Factor Influences Grain Market Early as Hot and Dry Forecasts Send Soybeans Higher   By Taylor, Jeffrey, The Wall Street Journal,  May 14, 1992  Weather was a major influence on the grain market on May 13, 1992, with fear of the potential for a hot and dry summer sharply boosting soybean futures prices higher. On the CBOT, the price of the soybean contract for July delivery rose $.1450 to $6.1950 a bushel, a life-of-contract high. In other commodity market news, energy futures prices fell, while orange juice futures and coffee futures rose.
