 Berkshire's Derivative Sales Rise   By Richardson, Karen, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 5, 2007  Sellers of stock- and credit-insurance contracts typically bet that the risk priced into the underlying reference securities is overstated. The notional value of the underlying contracts, which include equity-index options and credit-default swaps, has increased by $12 billion, or 52%, to $35 billion since the end of 2006, according to Berkshire's third-quarter earnings filing released Friday. The estimated market value of the contracts was about $5.6 billion, an increase of $2.3 billion, or 70%, from the end of last year.
