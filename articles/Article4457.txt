 Bush Victory Boosts Tokyo Shares, but London And Frankfurt Decline on Feeling of Anticlimax ---- A Wall Street Journal News Roundup   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 1988  Tokyo share prices closed higher Wednesday in active trading, as Republican George Bush's election as the next U.S. president helped activity pick up in the afternoon. But prices fell in London and Frankfurt.  The response to Mr. Bush's victory was muted even in Tokyo. Major indexes were higher during the first hour of the afternoon session but most of their gains were already in place by the end of the morning session.  The Nikkei index added 205.51 points to close at 28212.78, the highest since mid-August. The Tokyo stock price index of all issues listed in the first section rose 24.78 to 2170.51. First section volume was estimated at 1.2 billion shares, up from 891.1 million shares Tuesday.
