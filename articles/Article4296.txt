 Panel Blames Plane Crash In South Africa on Crew --- A Wall Street Journal News Roundup   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 10, 1987  A commission authorized by South Africa to investigate an airplane crash that killed 35, including the president of Mozambique, blamed the crash on errors by the crew.  The Soviet jet was manned by a Russian crew. The plane was heading for Maputo, capital of Mozambique, but crashed just inside South Africa's border the night of Oct. 19, 1986.
