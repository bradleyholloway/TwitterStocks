 Record U.K. libel award overturned in Strasbourg   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 1995  Count Nikolai Tolstoy, a descendant of the Russian novelist Leo Tolstoy, won a long-running battle to overturn record damages ordered against him for libeling Lord Aldington, the former Tory Party chairman. The Human Rights Court agreed with Tolstoy's claim that the scale of the damages was a violation of his freedom of expression, guaranteed in the European Convention on Human Rights.
