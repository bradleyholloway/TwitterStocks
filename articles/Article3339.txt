 Chip Implants Arm Doctors With Data   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 14, 2004  VeriChips, radio frequency microchips the size of a grain of rice, already have been used to identify wayward pets and livestock. In Mexico, more than 1,000 scannable chips have been implanted in patients. The chip's serial number pulls up the patients' blood type and other medical information. Outside of the medical use, nearly 200 people working in Mexico's attorney general's office have been implanted with chips to access secure areas containing sensitive documents....
