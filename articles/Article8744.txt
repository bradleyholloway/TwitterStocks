 Body snatchers find profit in cyberspace   By Gerlin, Andrea, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 1996  An academic research project using cadavers to develop an intricate, three-dimensional map of the human body has taken on a life of its own as a commercial venture. No fewer than two-dozen software products using the project's immense lode of data on the human body are now on store shelves or in the works. However, none of the profits from the sales will go to the Visible Human Project, funded by the National Library of Medicine, because entrepreneurs are downloading the data free from the Internet, repackaging it and reaping the profits.
