 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 21, 1997  BT Preferred Capital Trust II -- $250 million of capital securities via lead manager Deutsche Morgan Grenfell, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Terms: amount, $250 million; maturity, Feb. 25, 2027; coupon, 7.875%; issue price, 99.586; yield, 7.911%; spread, 121 basis points above Treasurys; call date, noncallable for 10 years; debt rating(s), A-2 (Moody's Investors Service Inc.) and BBB+ (Standard & Poor's Ratings Group). BT Preferred Capital Trust II is a unit of Bankers Trust New York Corp.  Idaho Housing and Finance Association -- $25 million of single-family mortgage revenue bonds tentatively priced. All three parts are subject to the alternative minimum tax. They are priced at par, and are callable in 2007 at 102. Lead underwriter is Goldman, Sachs & Co. The $21.3 million in senior bonds have yields ranging from 4.05% in 1999 to 5.95% in 2028. Also, $2.3 million of mezzanine bonds yield 5.85% in 2014. And yields on the $1.5 million of subordinate bonds were lowered five basis points to 5.65% in 2009. The senior bonds carry a rating of triple-A from Moody's. The mezzanine bonds are rated double-A-3 by Moody's. The subordinate bonds are rated single-A-1 by Moody's. The issue isn't rated by S&P.  Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority -- $165 million of notes won by four underwriters. The notes, which are due Feb. 27, 1998, have been rated MIG-2/SP-1/F-1. PaineWebber won $100 million of the notes.The notes are all 4.25s priced to yield 3.6%. The Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority notes all have 4.25 coupons. Bear, Stearns & Co. won $50 million of notes. The securities are priced to yield 3.6%. The $10 million of notes that Baybank won are also priced to yield 3.6%. However, Tucker Anthony's $5 million of notes aren't formally reoffered.
