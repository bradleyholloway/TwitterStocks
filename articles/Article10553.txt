 SmartMoney Preview: Market Rout -- The Sound of Bursting Bubbles   By Pearlman, Russell; O'Brien, Elizabeth; Prior, Anna, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 2011  Agricultural Commodities Even when the rest of the economy looks shaky, prices for agricultural commodities can climb, causing ripples in America's Kwik-E-Marts. Since last year, prices for grocery staples like ground beef, coffee and wheat have risen anywhere from 15% to 50%, according to the Department of Labor. Since the financial crisis, safety-seeking investors and purchases by the Federal Reserve, which bought bonds to prop up the economy, have driven yields to near-record lows.
