 Makers of Diet Aids Soon Must Report Serious Reactions   By Lueck, Sarah, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 2006  The new legislation, meanwhile, took years to move from proposal to passage, as a bipartisan group of lawmakers fine-tuned the provisions and addressed concerns of the supplement industry, which ultimately supported the change. The legislation requires makers of dietary supplements marketed in the U.S. to provide to the FDA, within 15 business days, any reports of "serious" adverse events associated with products. Examples of serious events include death, hospitalization, persistent disability or a birth defect.
