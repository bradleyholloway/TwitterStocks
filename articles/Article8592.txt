 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 1995  Associates Corp. of North America -- $300 million of notes due April 15, 2002, priced as 7.5s at 99.967 to yield 7.506%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 49 basis points above Treasurys. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Ratings Group, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by J.P. Morgan Securities Inc. Associates Corp. is a subsidiary of Ford Motor Co.  Comdisco Inc. -- $200 million of notes due April 15, 1998, priced as 7.25s at 99.768 to yield 7.337%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 58 basis points above Treasurys. Rated Baa-2 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Smith Barney Inc.  Federal National Mortgage Association -- $407.6 million of real-estate mortgage-investment conduit securities priced in seven parts by Bear, Stearns & Co., according to MCM CorporateWatch. The offering is called Series 95-W1, FNMA Whole Loans with first and second liens. The first part consists of $103.2 million of Class A-1 securities priced as 8.4% at 100.59375 to yield 6.79%, 18 basis points above comparable Treasury bills. The second part consists of $80.5 million of Class A-2 securities priced as 8.2% at 101.75 to yield 7.158%, 50 basis points versus Treasury's 6.625s of March 1997. The third part consists of $32.1 million of Class A-3 securities priced as 8% at 101.75 to yield 7.309%, 55 basis points above Treasury's 7.25s of February 1998. The fourth part consists of $56.5 million of Class A-4 securities priced as 8% at 101.65625 to yield 7.479%, 60 basis points above Treasury's 6.5s of April 1999. The fifth part consists of $17 million of Class A-5 securities priced as 8% at 101.5 to yield 7.626%, 70 basis points above Treasury's 6.875s of March 2000. The sixth part consists of $80.8 million of Class A-6 securities priced as 8.1% at 101.375 to yield 7.869%, 80 basis points above Treasury's 6.375s of August 2002. The seventh part consists of $37.6 million of Class A-7 securities priced as 8.2% at 101.6875 to yield 8.039%, 95 basis points above Treasury's 7.5s of February 2005.
