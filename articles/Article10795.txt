 Seagram Net Fell 43% In Fiscal 2nd Quarter   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 6, 1985  Seagram Co. said profit fell 34% in its second quarter ended July 31, mainly because of sharply lower earnings at Du Pont Co., the oil and chemicals company that is 22.5%-owned by the big distiller.  Seagram's earnings declined to $71.2 million, or 78 cents a share, from $107.7 million, or $1.20 a share, a year earlier.
