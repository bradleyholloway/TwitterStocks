 A Stronger Euro Contributes To Fears About Weak Recovery   By By G. Thomas Sims, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 2002  "While the strength of the ongoing recovery is still subject to uncertainty, the most likely scenario is for economic activity to gradually accelerate and reach levels in line with trend potential growth later this year," Wim Duisenberg, president of the European Central Bank, said yesterday. Economists say the bank is unlikely to raise its key interest rate at tomorrow's monthly meeting of governors. They point to Mr. Duisenberg's additional comment that the "recent significant appreciation of the euro" is mitigating other inflationary pressures.
