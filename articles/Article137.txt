THE silver-haired woman standing in line behind me turned to the man with her and said, ''You know, if I get that new card I'll be able to do a lot more with graphics.'' He agreed, a bit glumly, it seemed. His wife was on a shopping trip.  The scene was a computer show, but not a high-powered industry event like Comdex Fall '87, held in Las Vegas two weeks ago. Instead, it was a kind of roving flea market at William Paterson College in Wayne, N.J., mounted by Ken Gordon Productions.
