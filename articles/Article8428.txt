 Tokyo stocks plunge 3.6% after Japan's leaders falter on quick action to find economic health   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 7, 1993  Tokyo shares plunged 3.6% in Dec 6, 1993 trading as the Nikkei 225-stock index sank 618.97 to 16840.38 on disappointment about the Japanese government's faltering commitment to act quickly to battle the recession. In London, the Financial Times-Stock Exchange 100-share index rose 3.1 points to 3237.3, while Frankfurt's DAX 30-stock index eased 1.81 points to 2118.80.
