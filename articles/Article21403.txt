 An Audacious Rebel in Papua New Guinea Shakes Copper Market   By Sterba, James P, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1990  Francis Ona, a villager on the island of Bougainville New Guinea, shut down one of the world's biggest copper mines for nearly eight months with the help of his fellow villagers. Ona shut down the plant after asking for compensation for the environmental and social damage the mine has caused.
