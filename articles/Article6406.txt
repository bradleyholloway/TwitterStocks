 Japan Discusses Possible Sanctions On North Korea   By Sebastian Moffett and Gordon Fairclough, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 10, 2005  Shinzo Abe, acting secretary-general of the ruling Liberal Democratic Party, and the driving force behind Japan's recent North Korea policy, said last month that he believes the government should set a deadline of three or four weeks for a fresh response by North Korea and turn to sanctions. Sanctions could deprive the Pyongyang regime of an important source of revenue from some 200,000 pro- [Pyongyang] Korean residents of Japan. Earlier this month, an editorial in the North's official party newspaper, Rodong Sinmun, said sanctions would be "a reckless and extremely provocative act."
