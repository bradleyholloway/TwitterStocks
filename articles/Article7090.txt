 The Perils and Pitfalls of ETF Investing   By Tarquinio, J Alex, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 2010  Essentially bundles of stocks, bonds or other investments that trade as if they were a single stock, cheap-to-buy ETFs have become a handy way to diversify into several sectors -- a standard defense against a wobbly Dow -- and investors and planners have eagerly embraced them. (By comparison, only 10 ETFs closed in the previous 15 years.) At the same time, studies suggest that many ETFs are having trouble keeping up with their main promise to investors: to stay as close as possible to, or "track," the index they're supposed to follow, whether it's oil, gold or a breed of companies.
