 Barron's Insight: Conglomerate Is Flying High   By Laing, Jonathan R, The Wall Street Journal,  May 14, 2006  Right now, the big manufacturing conglomerate is hitting on all cylinders. Otis, its most profitable unit, has a surging business in China, where torrid urban-population growth is boosting demand for tall buildings and elevators. Carrier recently introduced a "unitary" central air conditioner for homes that has drawn rave reviews because of its energy efficiency and reduced size, weight and required refrigerant charge. In aerospace, Pratt & Whitney has a healthy defense business, as primary provider of jet engines for the F-22 and the costly but controversial F-35 Joint Strike Fighter.
