 Investor's Calendar   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 2006  -- Bonds Without Borders: Starting tomorrow, U.S. individual investors will be able to buy bonds from the Mexican government for as little as $1,000. This is touted as the first time a foreign government has launched a retail bond program in the U.S. The Mexican bonds will be denominated in U.S. dollars and will be available through most major brokerage firms.  -- Growing Gap: The U.S. trade deficit hit a record $725.76 billion in 2005, expanding nearly 18% for the year, the Commerce Department reported. Critics of U.S. trade policy contend that the gap will continue to widen unless the U.S. takes a harder stance against low- wage countries, such as China.
