 Thornburgh Bequests 'Federalizing' Local Crime to Consternation of Critics   By Barrett, Paul M, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 1991  One of the legacies Dick Thornburgh will leave behind as attorney general will be the deployment of vast federal resources to address traditionally local crime problems. Objections have come to the program from academic cop watchers and conservative constitutional purists.
