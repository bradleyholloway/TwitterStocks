 World-Wide   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 2004  A day after an icy Cheney-Edwards duel and ahead of his own second debate tomorrow, the president told a Pennsylvania crowd Kerry is an apostle of "retreat in Iraq" and, echoing attacks by his father on Dukakis, a "tax-and-spend liberal." Bush said a Kerry victory would "weaken America" because he holds a "September the 10th mindset." Edwards pointed to the latest evidence of a "mess in Iraq" and said the president was "in denial" and thus unable to deal with problems realistically.
