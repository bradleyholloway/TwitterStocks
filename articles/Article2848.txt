 Copper trading was lucrative to owners of Winchester firm   By McGee, Suzanne; Calian, Sara, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 1996  Winchester Commodities Group Ltd has made a fortune handling Sumitomo Corp trader Yasuo Hamanaka's trading in copper futures. Winchester was founded by Charles Vincent and Ashley Levett, two young traders who had worked for David Threlkeld, the Vermont trader who alerted the London Metals Exchange five years ago that Hamanaka's trading bore looking into. Their firm soon won Hamanaka's business away from Threlkeld.
