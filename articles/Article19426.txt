 Letters to the Editor: The Verdict of Battle   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 3, 2001  After the defeat of Germany in World War I, the German generals persuaded their populace that German forces had not actually been defeated, but betrayed -- a myth that led directly and inexorably to World War II. The subtext of Rep. [Henry Hyde]'s claim that the U.S. was not "militarily" defeated is a similar myth. It leads directly to Rep. Hyde's unwillingness to accept the verdict of battle and let the Vietnamese work out their own fate.
