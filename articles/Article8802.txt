 Legal beat: Can a law firm be its partner's keeper?   By Starkman, Dean, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 7, 1997  Allen W. Stewart, a former partner of Morgan, Lewis & Bockius, was charged in Dec 1996 in district court in Philadelphia with looting two insurance companies that he controlled, triggering their collapse and causing the second-largest loss in Pennsylvania history. This saga is a cautionary tale for law firms that, like Morgan Lewis, have grown so large so fast that it is difficult to keep tabs on their individual partners.
