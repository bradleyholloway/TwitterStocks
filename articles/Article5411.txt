 Some AIDS cases defy new drug 'cocktails'   By Waldholz, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 1996  HIV, the virus that causes AIDS, has mysteriously managed to muscle its way around a three-drug regimen that has extended the lives of many patients. Doctors and scientists are frantically trying to figure out why some people--perhaps as much as 10% of the 100,000 taking the drug combination which includes the so-called protease inhibitors--aren't faring as well as the vast majority.
