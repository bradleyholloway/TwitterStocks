 Korean Elections Signal Discontent With Government   By By James R. Schiffman, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 14, 1985  The government's Democratic Justice Party kept its majority in the assembly, as expected, but the opposition New Korea Democratic Party scored impressive gains, becoming South Korea's leading anti-government party. The Democratic Korea Party, a government-sanctioned group that had been the largest opposition party, slumped.  The elections were also a victory for Kim Dae Jung and Kim Young Sam, South Korea's leading dissidents, since the New Korea Democratic Party is made up mostly of their supporters. The two Kims weren't allowed to participate in the election, but Kim Dae Jung's homecoming last Friday after two years of self-exile, may have helped the party, which was formed just four weeks ago.
