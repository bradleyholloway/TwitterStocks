 Business and Finance   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 2004  PARMALAT INVESTIGATORS are looking at possible violations of Italian antitrust law over the sale of two business interests to a U.S. buyer. Meanwhile, banks are being scrutinized over whether they were complicit in the dairy concern's implosion. Separately, some of the company's missing money wound up at a tourism group headed by the daughter of Parmalat's founder.
