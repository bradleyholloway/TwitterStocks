 REVIEW --- Books: Rise of a Master   By Hall, James, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 3, 2012  There are around 1,400 surviving letters to and from the artist (Saturday was his letter-writing day), as well as memorandums, contracts, bank-account details, poems -- even the odd shopping list. Michelangelo's rise to artistic supremacy was far from inevitable, thanks in part to the political turmoil that followed the death of Florence's de facto ruler Lorenzo de' Medici in 1492 and the French invasion of Italy in 1494.
