 Yoder & Sons: When a Child 'Forgets' to Give You Change   By Stephen Kreider Yoder; Yoder, Isaac S; Yoder, Levi, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 2010  (Which it apparently was: I was busted once, in sixth grade, after skimming a few coins a week from the family shopping-money purse to play arcade games after running errands to the grocery store.) "Money was so tight," my wife, Karen, told me of her own childhood, "that every penny was accounted for." When her now-grown son and daughter were in grade school, she says, "I would always ask, every time: 'Where's the change?' By the time they were in high school, I didn't ever have to ask."
