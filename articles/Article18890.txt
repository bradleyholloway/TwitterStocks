 Cable Stock Prices Tumble in London On British Sky Plan   By By Matthew Rose, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 4, 1997  In the past two days of London trading, stocks in the major cable television and telephone operators -- Nynex CableComms PLC, TeleWest PLC and General Cable PLC -- have plunged between 10% and 15%. The reason for the decline: Friday, cable and satellite programmer British Sky Broadcasting PLC announced that it had formed a powerful consortium to bid for a license to operate digital TV in the U.K., posing a competitive threat to the already troubled cable industry, which is largely funded by bigger U.S. parents.
