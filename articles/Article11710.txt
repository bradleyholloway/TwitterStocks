 Midsize firms to hire despite glum outlook   By Ehrbar, Al, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 6, 1993  Despite surprisingly downbeat forecasts for the economy as a whole from financial executives, midsize companies plan to add employees and increase capital expenditures in 1993. In a survey of 400 senior financial officers at midsize companies by CIT Group, 44% of those polled said they believe the economy is improving, compared with 71% a year earlier.
