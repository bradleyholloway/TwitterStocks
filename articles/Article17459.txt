 Private Placements During 1984 Totaled Record $53.1 Billion   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 1985  A record $53.1 billion of private placement financing took place in the U.S. in 1984, a 50% increase from the previous record the year before, according to IDD Information Services, a statistical service.  Salomon Brothers Inc. was the largest agent in the private placement market, arranging $7.9 billion of financing last year. Merrill Lynch Capital Markets finished second at $7.3 billion, but was the most active agent, managing 283 transactions. Goldman, Sachs & Co. was third, at $5.89 billion, and First Boston Corp. was fourth, at $5.33 billion.
