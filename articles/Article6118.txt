 Ahead of the Tape   By By Jesse Eisinger, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 10, 2002  Heinz suffers from a problem that, except for a few exceptions, is plaguing the food industry: Sales are growing as fast as ketchup coming out of the bottle. Companies respond by obscuring things with numerous restructuring programs, divestitures, write-downs. "It's a bit of shell game," Mr. [Bill Leach] says. "Heinz is one of the poster children for this phenomenon. In my coverage, only two companies, Wrigley and Sysco, put out press releases where you can figure out what they actually earn." They happen to be the ones that actually have growth.
