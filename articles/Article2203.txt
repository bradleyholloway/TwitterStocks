 Elections, far from ushering in peace, could be prelude to breakup of Bosnia   By Nelson, Mark M, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 13, 1996  The Sep 14, 1996 elections in Bosnia are unlikely to solve any of the problems that are preventing real peace from taking hold and instead are likely to lay the groundwork for the final breakup of Bosnia. Many fear that the three parties likely to win the greatest share of seats in the election will eventually decide to create a Muslim ministate sandwiched between Greater Serbia and Greater Croatia. An EU official says that would mean three intolerant, hostile nations.
