 Lawmakers seek as much as $50 billion more in cuts than the Clinton proposal   By Harwood, John; Rogers, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 1993  In an effort to blunt criticism that Pres Clinton's economic plan will fall short of its deficit-reduction targets, Democrats are seeking additional savings of $40 billion to $50 billion over the next five years. Both the House and the Senate have proposed measures for additional spending cuts.
