 Big Retailers Report Modest Sales Gains for January, Making Fiscal '90 Mediocre   By Trachtenberg, Jeffrey A, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 2, 1990  Jan 1990 sales gains for US retailers averaged about 5.5% to 6%, but the 1989 year-end gain was only about 5.5%. Top performers in the specialty stores. which outperformed the department-stores, were Wal-Mart Stores, Gap Inc and Dayton Hudson Corp.
