 Even as Workers of the World Begin to Disperse, Communist Star Hasn't Dimmed in South Africa   By Thurow, Roger, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 1991  When Pretoria South Africa lifted its 40-year ban on the South African Communist Party, it reckoned that the crumbling of communism elsewhere would soon discredit the party. But Communists have been streaming back from exile and coming out of the closet to organize Africa's largest industrialized working class, and through its long revolutionary alliance with the ANC, it is more popular than when it was banned.
