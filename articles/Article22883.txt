 Interpublic Group Swings to Profit By Cutting Costs   By By Vanessa O'Connell, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 2002  Total revenue at Interpublic, the parent to advertising agencies including McCann-Erickson World Group, FCB Group, and Lowe & Partners Worldwide, fell to $1.42 billion from $1.68 billion, as clients continued to cut back on their marketing budgets.  Interpublic undertook a restructuring last year in order to cut costs and streamline its myriad holdings. The company said that it is continuing to reap savings from the restructuring, and noted that those savings are likely to help it deliver earnings growth this year.
