New York's Dominicans have long straddled two political worlds, casting ballots in United States elections and flying to the Dominican Republic to vote in national elections there. Yesterday, those worlds merged for the first time when thousands of people voted in the Dominican presidential elections from polling centers in New York and across the country, blending an American attempt at order with a distinctly Dominican voice.  Echoes of merengue blasted from windows in Washington Heights as voters crowded polling sites, often locked in loud debates over their candidates of choice.
