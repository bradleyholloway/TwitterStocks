 Russia Grants Western Banks The Go-Ahead For Expansion   By By Guy Chazan, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 2000  With Russia's banking sector still languishing after the August 1998 financial crash, Western financial institutions have been given the green light to expand operations in Russia, after the Central Bank said a quota on foreign capital in Russian banks had been scrapped.  But analysts say it will require more than this to entice foreign money into a lending market still fraught with risk. They say the haphazard way news of the change emerged only underscores the unpredictability of Russia's financial sector.  Russian subsidiaries of Western banks such as Citigroup, Chase Manhattan Bank and Credit Suisse First Boston are some of the most profitable financial institutions in the country, and in rankings of Russian banks by capital, they consistently make the top 50.
