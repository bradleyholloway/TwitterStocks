 A year after U.N. elections, Cambodia continues painful road to recovery   By Wain, Barry, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 21, 1994  Just 12 months after a UN-supervised election brought to power an alliance of former enemies in Cambodia, the country is struggling to keep its head above water. Reverses on the battlefield against the recalcitrant Khmer Rouge, an attempted coup and spreading corruption have demoralized the administration and disappointed its supporters.
