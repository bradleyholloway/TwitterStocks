 Bonds appear to some strategists as good buys, amid fear of possible 'double dip' in economy   By Vames, Steven, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 15, 2002  If Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan is right, it's time to buy bonds. That is because bonds are still priced for a strong and imminent economic recovery despite a warning by Mr. Greenspan on Jan 11, 2002 that recent signs of recovery may prove illusory. At the same time, stocks, which are also priced with a rosy outlook for the economy, may be at risk.
