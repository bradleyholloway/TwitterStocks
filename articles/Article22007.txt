 'Darkness' lifts over California economy   By McCoy, Charles, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 3, 1995  With exports booming, per capita personal income rising and more jobs being created, California's economy appears to have turned the corner, ending the longest and deepest economic downturn since the Great Depression. While some economists warn that the budding recovery isn't yet broad-based, the economy does appear to be moving forward.
