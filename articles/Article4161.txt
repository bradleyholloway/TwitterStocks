 FDA Approves Drug to Combat Digoxin Overdose   The Wall Street Journal,  May 9, 1986  The Food and Drug Administration approved an antidote for life-threatening overdoses of digoxin, a heart drug used by four million Americans.  The intravenous drug, Digibind, will be used to treat thousands of heart patients a year who suffer severe irregular heart beats and other ill effects from an accidental or suicidal overdose. Digoxin overdoses kill 175 persons a year, and represent half of all accidental poisoning deaths in people over 60 years old, according to the FDA.
