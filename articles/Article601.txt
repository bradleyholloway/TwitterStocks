 Investigator for SEC Testifies at U.S. Trial Of Winans, 2 Others   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 1985  Mr. Winans; David Carpenter, his roommate and a former Journal news clerk; and Kenneth P. Felis, a former Kidder, Peabody & Co. stockbroker, are charged with a scheme to profit from advance knowledge of Heard on the Street columns in the Journal.  Mr. Cella said he first interviewed Mr. Winans by telephone on March 1, 1984, as part of an SEC investigation of trading in stocks mentioned in Heard on the Street columns. He said that Norman Pearlstine, managing editor of the Journal, was also on the phone. Mr. Cella said he was told that Robert Sack, a partner in the firm of Patterson, Belknap, Webb & Tyler and general counsel of Dow Jones & Co., publisher of the Journal, also was present in the room with Messrs. Winans and Pearlstine.
