 Few Upbeat Signs as Europe, Jobs Sink Stocks   By Zuckerman, Gregory, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 3, 2012  May was the worst month for the market since September. Since hitting a peak for the year on April 2, the S&P 500 has dropped 9.94%. Some are concerned about a downturn late this year, when tax cuts enacted under President Bush are scheduled to expire, while other changes go into effect to raise taxes, "creating something of a perfect storm to the nascent economic recovery," Evan Roth, a partner at money managers BBR Partners, wrote to his firm's wealthy clients last week.
