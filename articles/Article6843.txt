 Tip of the Week: Advance Fee? Borrower, Flee   By Blackman, Andrew, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 2004  "The overriding principle is, don't pay in advance for a loan," says Steven Baker, director of the Federal Trade Commission's Midwest region. He says legitimate lenders never guarantee loans before reviewing a person's credit. They also don't demand upfront fees; typically, fees are collected when the loan is closed. As for Visa and MasterCard, they say they don't directly solicit customers.
