 SEC Aide Urges Look At Trade in Decimals   The Wall Street Journal,  May 14, 2003  Mr. [William Donaldson] has told SEC staff he is concerned that people aren't making money in decimalization and that the pricing may have become too competitive, say people familiar with the matter. Decimalization means stocks are traded in penny increments instead of increments of 6.25 cents, as was the case with fractions.
