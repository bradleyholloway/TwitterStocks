 This Explains All Those Packets Of Ketchup in the CEO's Office   By By Andrew B. Cohen, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 5, 1988  Call them snooty, call them elitist, but when it comes to eating out, even well-heeled restaurant critics aren't above taking a fast-food repast.  Tim Zagat, editor of the Zagat New York City Restaurant Survey, regularly polls 3,500 professionals and senior executives who are also avid restaurant-goers for their eating establishments of preference. Judging by past surveys, these self-appointed experts would seem more accustomed to the haute cuisine offered at Lutece and The Four Seasons.  But "just as a lark," Mr. Zagat recently asked them for their fast-food preferences and found that their knowledge extends to mass-market fare, too.
