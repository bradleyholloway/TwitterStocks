 Legal beat: Court rejects ban on laws protecting gays   By Barrett, Paul M, The Wall Street Journal,  May 21, 1996  The gay-rights movement won its biggest legal victory ever, as the Supreme Court struck down a Colorado constitutional amendment that banned state laws protecting homosexuals from discrimination, giving gay-rights advocates new weapons in their continuing fight against the military's ban on homosexuals and the nationwide prohibition on same-sex marriages.
