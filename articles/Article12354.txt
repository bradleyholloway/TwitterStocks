 Technology Journal / Health: FCC to Propose New Rules for Calls Abroad From U.S.   By By John Simons, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 6, 1998  The FCC plans to end a requirement that all telecommunications carriers in a given country charge U.S. carriers the same rate when the foreign carriers transmit U.S. calls. The agency wants to lift the restrictions on countries with competitive telecommunications markets. The proposal would affect more than half of the foreign phone calls from the U.S., FCC officials said.
