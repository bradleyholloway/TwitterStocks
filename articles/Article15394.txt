 Revised forecasts by CBO could spur stalled talks on balanced-budget plan   By Wessel, David; Calmes, Jackie, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 5, 1995  The CBO's current review of its economic and spending forecast could bring the bargaining positions of Clinton administration officials and Congress closer together. The CBO review could narrow the $475 billion gap between White House and congressional estimates of what it will take to balance the budget over seven years. Budget negotiators plan to meet Dec 5, 1995 for the first time since the talks broke up on Nov 30.
