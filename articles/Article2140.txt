 Cuba trade ban may anger WTO but U.S. has reply: National security   By Cooper, Helene, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 1996  When President Clinton signed the law aimed at blocking foreign investment in Cuba, he may have broken a basic rule of the World Trade Organization, which the US helped to create. The WTO takes a dim view of unilateral trade measures against member countries that include a cutoff of trade, and Cuba, like the US, is a founding member. But the WTO has an escape clause--an exception for national security--and the US has every intention of using it.
