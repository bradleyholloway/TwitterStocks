 Ahead of the Tape   By Lahart, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 7, 2005  The year 2004 was supposed to be when long-term interest rates broke out to the upside. Amid much talk of a "bond bubble," the yield on the 10-year Treasury note was widely expected to skip from the 4.25% it started the year at to above 5%. Such forecasts were, if not wildly wrong, extremely early. The 10-year's yield ended 2004 at 4.26%.  Merrill Lynch economist David Rosenberg, one of Wall Street's few bond bulls, thinks that what's going on with Treasurys has more to do with a world where nothing seems to offer much return.
