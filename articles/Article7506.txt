 Deere to Restructure Two Foundry Units In Bid for New Orders   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 1986  The company said its foundries at East Moline, Ill., and Waterloo, Iowa, will be operated as a single business making cast products like engine blocks and housings for axles, transmissions and clutches. Those foundries, which used to make castings for Deere farm and industrial equipment, now are operating at well below 50% of capacity and aren't profitable, a spokesman said.
