 Bailout in Japan indicates government isn't yet ready to lift its heavy hand   By Sapsford, Jathon, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 1994  In a move suggesting that the Japanese government's hand in the banking industry may grow even larger, the Bank of Japan, Japan's central bank, announced Dec 9, 1994 that it would join private lenders to set up a new bank to take over the assets and liabilities of two Tokyo-area credit unions, Tokyo Kyowa Credit Association and Anzen Credit Bank The credit unions hold a combined $1 billion in bad property loans.
