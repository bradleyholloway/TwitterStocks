 Issues Recede in '08 Campaign As Voters Focus on Leadership; Campaigns So Far Avoid Bold Ideas; 'Folks Are Tired of Partisan Paralysis'   By Seib, Gerald F, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 5, 2008  In the South Carolina primary on Jan. 26, exit polls indicated that Democratic voters who said the economy was the most important issue voted for Sen. Hillary Clinton and Sen. Obama in nearly the same proportions as did those who said the war in Iraq or health care were the most important issues.
