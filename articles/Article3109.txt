 Short Interest On Big Board Fell for Month --- Figure Declined by 0.69% To 4.10 Billion Shares; Amex Increased by 3.2%   By By Greg Ip, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 21, 1998  Short interest on the New York Stock Exchange fell in the latest month, while activity on the American Stock Exchange increased.  Short interest on the Big Board decreased 0.69% to 4,100,726,367 shares on Aug. 14 from a revised 4,129,392,716 shares in mid-July.  The level of negative sentiment measured by the Big Board's short-interest ratio -- sometimes considered a contrarian indicator, as short-interest shares eventually must be purchased -- fell to 5.98 from 6.6 in the previous trading period. The short-interest ratio is the number of trading days at the exchange's average daily trading volume required to convert the total short-interest position.
