 Career Journal --- MANAGING YOUR CAREER: Healing the Scars of a Long Jobless Bout   By Lublin, Joann S, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 6, 2010  Excess new-job jitters happen the most among individuals who lacked work for at least six months, previously earned low pay or have gone through multiple layoffs, several experts say. Since 2008, "I've seen this syndrome nearly without exception among clients" when they found jobs after lengthy unemployment, says Pamela Mitchell, chief executive of Reinvention Institute, a career-coaching firm in Miami.
