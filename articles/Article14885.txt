 Bond Prices Retreat Slightly as Some Investors Become Less Bullish on Latest Economic Signals   By Torres, Craig; Elliott, Nicholas, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 5, 1991  Prices on most Treasury issues slipped Sep 4, 1991 in light selling as bond investors weighed economic reports and concluded that interest rates would not be lowered anytime soon. The Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond ended at a price of 100 23/32 to yield 8.06%, compared with a price of 100 30/32 a day earlier. The 10-year note fell less than 1/8 point, to 100 13/32 to yield 7.81%. Two-year notes were unchanged at 100 4/32 to yield 6.29%.
