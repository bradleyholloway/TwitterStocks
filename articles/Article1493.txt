 Small Firms Doubt Recovery; SBA Chief Sets Overhaul   By Saddler, Jeanne, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 14, 1991  The latest survey by the National Federation of Independent Business showed that pessimism about the recovery of the economy persisted in the second quarter, one problem being a buildup of inventory at small businesses. SBA Chief Patricia Saiki has vowed to fix the troubled investment-company program. Clarence Thomas won formal support for his Supreme Court nomination from the National Small Business United, a group of about 50,000. The SBA's computerized debt collection system is so proficient that the agency is offering its services to other federal agencies as a collection center for delinquent loans. A mentoring program for defense contractors and minority firms has been set.
