Arcadius Kahan, professor of economics and history at the University of Chicago, and an expert on Soviet bloc agricultureal economics, dies Friday of a heart attack at his Chicago home. He was 62 years old.  He was born in Vilnius, Poland and studied economics and law in Vilnius and Warsaw. He worked after the war as a statistician and emigration official with the American Jewish Joint Distribution Committee and came to the United States in 1950. He recieved a master's degree and a Ph.D in economics from Rutgers University.  He began teaching economics and history at the University of Chicago in 1954. He was on the board of directors of the Yivo Center for Advanced Jewish Studies in New York City.  Mr Kahan is survived by his wife, Pearl; two daughters, Vivian Weston of Los Angeles and Miriam Brody of West Orange, N.J., and a granddaughter.
