 Higher prime rate, consumer borrowing costs are likely in wake of Fed's credit tightening   By Herman, Tom; Murray, Alan, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 1989  Because of the recent credit tightening by the Federal Reserve Board, banks are expected to increase their prime lending rates from the current 10.5% to between 10.75% and 11%. Increases in rates for home equity and other consumer loans are expected to follow suit. The Federal Reserve is expected to hold the interest rate on federal funds to around nine percent or a little bit higher. According to a poll of 13 corporate purchasing managers, the government unemployment rate report will indicate a 0.1 percentage point decline to 5.3%.
