 Evidence of strong U.S. demand fuels surge in gasoline prices amid heavy futures trading   By Tanner, James, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 12, 1993  Evidence of strong US demand for motor fuel caused gasoline prices to surge suddenly in New York and London on Aug 11, 1993. On the New York Mercantile Exchange, unleaded gasoline for September delivery jumped $.215 to $.5578 a gallon, while the October gasoline contract was also up but less, settling $.128 higher at $.5312 a gallon. In US futures trading, crude oil for September delivery rose $.36 to settle at $17.88 a barrel, and on the International Petroleum Exchange in London, Brent blend crude futures were up as much as $.44 a barrel.
