 Growth Is the Right Medicine for Greece   By Samaras, Antonis, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 6, 2012  When Galileo Galilei was forced by the Catholic Inquisition to recant his heliocentric beliefs in 1633, the Italian astronomer was said to have uttered, "And yet it moves." [...] the phrase has become shorthand for defending counterintuitive truth in the face of deep-rooted prejudice.
