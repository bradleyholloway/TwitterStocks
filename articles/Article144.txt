WHILE many other composers have abandoned in recent years the paths carved out by Schoenberg, Webern and their heirs, Thomas E. Barker held the line. Last month Mr. Barker died of bone cancer at the age of 33. As a memorial, several of the works he composed since 1980 were performed Monday evening at Symphony Space.  A quintessential product of the academic circle that maintained a devotion to the 12-tone tradition and its offshoots, Mr. Barker was raised on country music as a child in Atlanta, but moved as a student to New York, training at the Juilliard and Manhattan schools and receiving a doctorate from Columbia University.
