 SmartMoney: Profit From Weak Dollar   By Eleanor Laise With Nicole Bullock, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 6, 2005  The dollar's weakness gives a boost to U.S. steel producers. While the domestic market was once flooded with cheap foreign steel, the currency shift cuts foreign producers' cost advantages, puts a lid on imports and helps sustain prices within the U.S. Commercial Metals of Irving, Texas, recycles scrap metal and fabricates metal doodads like joists and fence posts. When steel prices fall, this business mix offers a natural hedge: Since the fabrication unit sells products under fixed-price contracts, customers pay today's higher prices for fence posts that are made with tomorrow's cheaper steel. This overlooked stock trades at just nine times estimated earnings for the year ending in August, which are expected to jump 82%.
