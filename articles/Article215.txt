By unanimous vote, 98 Senators approved the International Convention Against the Taking of Hostages, adopted by the United Nations in 1979.  The conventions calls for prosecution of extradition of anyone who commits, attempts to commit or aids an act of international terrorism involving hostages.  By similar unanimous vote, the Senate approved a measure calling for protection of nuclear materials used for peaceful purposes in both domestic and international use, storage and transport. Persons who steal, possess or participate in disposing of nuclear materials would be liable to prosecution or extradition.
