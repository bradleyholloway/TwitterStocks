 New Security Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 5, 2007  Danske Bank -- 1 billion euros floating-rate notes was priced with the following terms, lead managers BNP Paribas, Citigroup and Danske Bank said: maturity: Jan. 11, 2012; coupon: five basis points above three-month euribor; price: 99.862; payment: Jan. 11, 2007; ratings: Aa1 (Moody's), AA-minus (S&P), AA-minus (Fitch); denominations: 50,000 euros and increments of 1,000 euros thereafter; listing: Luxembourg; interest: quarterly.  Northern Rock PLC -- $2 billion of floating-rated notes was priced through lead managers Citigroup and Lehman Brothers, according to a person familiar with the deal. Terms: Amount: $750 million; coupon: three basis points above one-month Libor; price: par; yield: three basis points above one-month Libor. Amount: $1.25 billion; coupon: three basis points above three-month Libor; price: par; yield: three basis points above three-month Libor. Common terms: maturity: Jan. 9, 2009; settlement: Jan. 11, 2007 (flat); ratings: A1 (Moody's), A-plus (S&P).  R.R. Donnelley & Sons -- $1.25 billion of debt was priced in two parts via joint lead managers Citigroup, Banc of America Securities and J.P. Morgan, IFR said. Terms: Part 1: maturity: Jan. 15, 2012; coupon: 5.625%; price: 99.86; yield: 5.657%; call: make whole at 15 basis points above Treasurys; spread: 100 basis points above Treasurys. Part 2: maturity: Jan. 15, 2017; coupon: 6.125%; price: 99.21; yield: 6.232%; call: make whole at 25 basis points above Treasurys. Common terms: amount: $625 million; settlement: Jan. 8, 2007; ratings: Baa2 (Moody's), BBB-plus (S&P).
