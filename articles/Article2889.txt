 Lebed charges high-level corruption in potential blow to Yeltsin credibility   By Liesman, Steve; Banerjee, Neela, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 21, 1996  Retired Gen Alexander Lebed, who was fired as Russia's national security chief, has attacked the government of President Boris Yeltsin with charges of high-level corruption. Lebed and his allies have accused the Kremlin of everything from embezzling funds earmarked for the Chechen war to requesting political assassinations. He has struck an alliance with Yeltsin's former bodyguard, Alexander Korzhakov, whose enemies concede he has plenty of dirt.
