 Business and Finance [5]  The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 12, 1985  STOCK PRICES SOARED, fueled by hopes for lower interest rates and improved corporate profits. The Dow Jones Industrial Average leaped 27 points to a record 1431.88. Broader market barometers also hit highs, including the S&P 500-stock index and the Big Board's composite index.  Reagan likely will veto balanced-budget legislation this week, according to sources. Administration officials said the president objects to pending revisions in the bill that could lead to Pentagon spending cuts.  Columbia Gas Systems negotiated substantial price cuts from four of its biggest natural-gas suppliers, a significant step toward resolving its longstanding dispute with suppliers. Nevertheless, Columbia raised its estimated 1985 loss to $100 million.
