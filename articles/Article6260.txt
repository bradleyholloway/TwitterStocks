 The Economy: Consumer Debt Rose At a Greater Rate Than Was Expected   By Lagomarsino, Deborah, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 2003  The September consumer-credit increase was led by nonrevolving credit, which is mostly auto loans. Nonrevolving credit climbed by $12.1 billion in September, after rising by a revised $7.1 billion in August. In addition to auto loans, nonrevolving credit includes education loans and loans on motor homes, boats, trailers or vacations.
