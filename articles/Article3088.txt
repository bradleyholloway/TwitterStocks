 Deals & Deal Makers: Warburg Pincus Plans to Invest $400 Million in Korean Lender   By By Hae Won Choi, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 2003  The U.S. private-equity firm said it agreed to provide a portion of the acquisition capital for Shinhan Financial Group's bid to acquire Chohung Bank. Shinhan had offered a bid valued at $1.6 billion to purchase the Korean government's entire 80% stake in Chohung Bank with a mix of cash and Shinhan stock. Last month, a government committee overseeing the sale of Chohung Bank named Shinhan as the "preferred bidder," giving the domestic lender a big edge over rival bidder Cerberus Partners LP of the U.S.
