 Homestore.com Overstated Revenue From Advertising   By By Maureen Tkacik, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 2002  Homestore.com's ad revenue, which accounted for roughly a third of the revenue the company reported for last year, has been the subject of scrutiny by analysts and shareholders since last October when the firm blamed the Sept. 11 terrorist attacks for a 45% fall in ads booked from the second to the third quarter. Prior to then, Homestore.com's reported advertising revenue rose every quarter, even amid a slowdown in advertising that disproportionately hurt the online advertising business. Homestore.com, which was set up in 1999 to offer online real-estate listings, has yet to turn a profit.
