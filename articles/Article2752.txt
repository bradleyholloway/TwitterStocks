 Hyundai Founder Cuts His Stake in Unit --- Move, Made Under Duress, Will Let Car Maker Gain Its Independence   By By Michael Schuman And Hae Won Choi, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 14, 2000  Mr. [Chung Ju Yung] officially resigned in May from all of his posts at Hyundai and has no management role. But government officials believed he would still hold great influence over the company through his stature as group founder and demanded that he sell down his stake. The standoff with the government further damped market sentiment toward the group, already damaged by squabbles within the Chung family and between Hyundai affiliates.
