 FBI, at White House request, rewrote agency's statement in travel-stuff case   By Birnbaum, Jeffrey H, The Wall Street Journal,  May 25, 1993  George Stephanopoulos, the White House communications director, acknowledged that an FBI official was asked to redraft a statement about the firing of travel staffers, reflecting the agency's view that a criminal investigation was warranted. Critics charged the White House with seeking political cover behind the FBI when the pressure mounted. The White House had accused the seven-member staff of financial mismanagement.
