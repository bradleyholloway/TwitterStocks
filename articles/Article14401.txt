 Tax-Reform Measure Is Approved in Japan By Lower Chamber --- Special to The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 1987  The lower house of Japan's parliament passed a bill that would reduce income taxes but eliminate the current tax exemption on interest from small personal savings accounts.  The tax-reform bill was sent to the upper house for final action, which is expected by Sept. 18. Prime Minister Yasuhiro Nakasone's Liberal Democratic Party, whose 304 members of the 512-seat lower house voted in favor of the measure, also holds a majority in the upper house.
