 Foreign banks lower sights in Australia   By Brooks, Geraldine, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 5, 1987  In September, 1984, 16 foreign banks were selected from 40 applicants for Australian banking licenses. Since then currency declines, rising inflation and stiff domestic competition have forced them to lower earnings expectations and seek niche markets, particularly for capital market deals. Domestic banks remain optimistic about profit opportunities since deregulation.
