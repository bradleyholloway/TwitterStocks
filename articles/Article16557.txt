 Barron's Insight: Intuit's Stock Wins Fans Despite Microsoft Rivalry   By Veverka, Mark, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 2005  While it's no fun to be under siege from Microsoft, it needn't be a disaster, Mr. [Steve Bennett] argues. Intuit managed to stave off Money, Microsoft's 1991 product aimed at Intuit's Quicken personal-finance program. Intuit agreed to be acquired by Microsoft in 1994, but a year later the feds blocked the deal for antitrust reasons. Intuit persevered and flourished, widening its pretax operating profit margins from about 13% to 27%. What Intuit has learned over the years is that competition from Microsoft usually boosts awareness among potential customers, expanding the market.
