 SunTrust's Profit Rises 8% in Spite Of Higher Expenses   By By Carrick Mollenkamp, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 2002  During the call, SunTrust officials insisted that the bank won't take a charge against earnings in order to increase the company's reserves for bad loans. During the past month, other banks such as FleetBoston Financial Corp. have taken such charges. SunTrust officials say the bank is adequately reserved.  SunTrust said its per-share earnings of $1.24 beat Wall Street expectations by two cents. Still, as analysts pointed out, SunTrust picked up eight cents a share from gains involving the elimination of debt.
