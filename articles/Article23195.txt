 Ahead of the Tape   By Lahart, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 3, 2004  Heat is what has the market worried right now. Wednesday's "beige book" release -- a summary of business activity collected by regional Federal Reserve banks -- said the economy continued to expand at a decent clip from mid-October to mid-November. It also indicated companies were having less difficulty passing on higher prices.  "The story is that companies went through all these cost-cutting efforts, but there are limits to those sorts of efficiency gains," says Raymond James economist Scott Brown. "Eventually, you have to start hiring again."  "What we have to worry about is violently higher rates," he says. "A little bit higher isn't going to be a problem."
