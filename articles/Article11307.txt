 Did Fuji Bank Pay Through the Nose In Bid to Keep Face? --- Group Allegedly `Borrowed' $3.5 Million to Keep Quiet About Loan to Gangsters   By By Marcus W. Brauchli, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 6, 1989  In a face-saving land, saving face has its price -- and Fuji Bank, the world's third-largest bank, may have learned the hard way just how much maintaining a good public image can cost.  Police in Japan have arrested four people, including a former political aide, in an alleged scheme to blackmail Fuji Bank out of 500 million yen ($3.5 million). Their threat? To disclose that Fuji Bank had lent money to a group with possible ties to Japan's underworld.
