 Grain Futures Prices Rise despite Bush's Failure in Speech to Announce Credit Package to Soviets   By Taylor, Jeffrey, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 14, 1991  Anticipation of an announcement about a US grain package to the USSR sent grain futures prices higher on Nov 13, 1991. At the CBOT, the soybean contract for Jan 1992 delivery rose 8.50 cents to $5.5750 a bushel. The Dec 1991 corn contract rose 2.75 cents to $2.46 a bushel and the wheat contract rose 5.25 cents to $3.5250 a bushel. In other markets, sugar and cotton futures prices fell, as did crude oil prices.
