 Car-Show Dilemma: Future Isn't Now; Auto Makers, Trapped in a Thirsty Present, Await More Fuel-Efficient Models   By Spector, Mike, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 2008  With gasoline surging above $3 a gallon, auto makers of all stripes will use the North American International Auto Show to trumpet plenty of great innovations they're working on to improve fuel economy, although few are ready for prime time now. Nissan Motor Co. has a thirsty sports car of its own, the 480- horsepower Skyline GTR, and Toyota will sell its new Highlander sport- utility vehicle.
