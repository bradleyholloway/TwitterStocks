 UPS Stock Sale To Net $4.1 Billion, Largest IPO Ever   By By Rick Brooks, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 21, 1999  United Parcel Service of America Inc. estimated that its widely anticipated initial public offering next month would raise about $4.1 billion in net proceeds, making it the largest IPO ever.  In a revised registration statement filed with the Securities and Exchange Commission, the package-delivery company set an expected offering price of between $36 and $42 a share for the 109.4 million shares being sold in the IPO. The deal's estimated total value was based on an assumed price of $39 a share, right in the middle of the expected range set by UPS.
