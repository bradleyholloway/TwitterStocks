 Dollar Is Weaker Despite Increases In U.S. Indicators   By By Stephen Grover, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 1985  The news initially sent the dollar higher. "But there was resistance at 3.0550 (West German) marks," said Douglas E. Hart II, an assistant vice president and manager of the corporate advisory desk of Credit Suisse, New York, "and the failure of the dollar to break through that point led to a selloff. The fact that the dollar couldn't sustain gains in view of these economic indicators could mean we're headed lower."
