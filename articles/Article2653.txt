 Mexican Budget Vote Is Planned Amid a Dispute Over Spending   By By Joel Millman, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 1999  Opposition leaders in Mexico's Congress pledged to complete negotiations today and to vote tomorrow on next year's government budget, despite a hardening of positions on both sides over the $120 billion fiscal package.  What appeared to be an easy ride through Congress for the government's 2000 budget proposal ran into a snag over the weekend, when two opposition factions proposed broad increases in spending. The budget proposed by the leftwing Party of the Democratic Revolution, or PRD, in coalition with the rightist National Action Party, or PAN, adds almost $2 billion to the $120 billion government proposal.
