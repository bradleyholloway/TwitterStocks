 Maybe Schools Should Just Teach Kids in China to Buy Junk Bonds   By By Adi Ignatius, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 1989  CHANGSHA, China -- As a customer steps up to the bank window, the kindly old teller offers some patient advice.  Welcome to the Changsha second branch of the People's Construction Bank of China, probably the only bank in the world that pays interest in toys.  Designed exclusively for children, the savings bank is an unabashed propaganda tool. Leaders in Changsha, the capital of Hunan province, tell children that by depositing their pennies they not only earn interest and toys but also contribute to the nation's economic development.
