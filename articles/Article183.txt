Mayor David N. Dinkins has learned the hard way that rank has its privileges, but sometimes you have to pay for them. Pay is what he has agreed to do after City Hall aides told him he could not charge New York City for first-class air travel or day rooms in hotels on out-of-town trips.  ''I don't propose to change the guidelines,'' Mr. Dinkins said on Thursday after he agreed to reimburse the city more than $2,000 for recent trips. ''I do propose to travel first class whenever first-class accommodations are available, because it is more comfortable, you get more work done and I put in seven days and pretty long days.''
