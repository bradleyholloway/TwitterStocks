In a mysterious accident that may have set back astronomical research by years, the 300-foot radiotelescope at Green Bank, W.Va., collapsed Tuesday night in a tangle of twisted metal girders.  The instrument, operated by the Government's National Radio Astronomy Observatory, was one of the most powerful radiotelescopes in the world. Built in 1962, the 300-foot-diameter dish antenna had been responsible for major discoveries, including the finding that pulsating super-dense stars, pulsars, are created in the debris of exploding stars.
