 Ships' Arrival At China Ports Achieves Little For Taiwan Ties   By By Erik Guyot, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 2001  China's lukewarm response represents a comedown after months of anticipation here stoked by the government of Taiwan President Chen Shui-bian. Beijing is wary of handing any political victories to Mr. Chen, whose party favors independence for Taiwan -- an island Beijing considers a renegade province, although it has been governed separately since 1949. And with President Chen now preoccupied with battling political opponents at home, few expect him to make much headway toward his goal of better relations with China.
