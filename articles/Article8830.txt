 More contracts are settled through cash   By Lucchetti, Aaron, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 1997  Futures exchanges such as the Chicago Mercantile Exchange are considering scrapping the age-old practice of settling futures contracts by delivering the goods. Instead, some new contracts are being settled through cash payments. The CME on Apr 4, 1997 kicked off a controversial switch from physical delivery to cash settlement on its oldest running contract, the pork-belly future.
