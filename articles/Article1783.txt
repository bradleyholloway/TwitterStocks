 Settlements Appear Likely in Car Talks As Deadline Nears   By By Neal Templin and Mark Heinzl, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 14, 1993  Auto-industry labor talks in the U.S. and Canada appeared headed toward settlements before strike deadlines at midnight tonight, company and union officials in both countries indicated.  In Toronto, the Canadian Auto Workers union, which has carried out strike threats the last five times in a row, sounded uncharacteristically optimistic about settling with Chrysler Corp. And in Dearborn, Mich., the United Auto Workers and Ford Motor Co. seemed to be steaming toward an uneventful on-time settlement. Bargainers in both places were set to talk around the clock last night.
