 The U.N.'s 'Invisible Man' --- Ban Ki-moon Struggles to Make Mark; U.S. Urges Stronger Role   By Lauria, Joe; Stecklow, Steve, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2009  Mr. Ban took over as U.N. head in 2007 and pushed for reform, blasting a bureaucracy last year that wastes "incredible amounts of time on largely meaningless matters." Mr. Ban's aides describe him as a master of quiet diplomacy, working behind the scenes to advance causes as diverse as deploying U.N. peacekeepers into a defiant Sudan to enlisting world leaders to fight climate change.
