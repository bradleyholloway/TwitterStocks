 The new preppies: Boarding schools go overseas for students; Money is the object   By Stecklow, Steve, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 1991  Winchendon School typifies many private boarding schools in the US. Faced with sinking enrollment, rising debt and little cash flow, Winchendon turned to well-to-do foreign students, whose tuition provides operating funds and financial aid for American students. Only 5% of foreign students receive financial aid, while 29% of US students do.
