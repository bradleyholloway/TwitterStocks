 Midwest Express Talks With the Pilots Union Enter a New Phase   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 2000  The National Mediation Board, the federal agency that oversees labor relations in the airline industry, this week released the pilots and the regional carrier from bargaining after both sides rejected arbitration following 18 months of talks. The 330 pilots, who in late 1997 elected the Air Line Pilots Association as their bargaining agent, voted in September to authorize a strike if a tentative agreement wasn't reached.
