 Geopolitics: The Reluctant Superpower --- On the eve of Obama's visit, China confronts its tradition of looking inward   By Browne, Andrew, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 14, 2009  The wooden treasure ships commanded by Admiral Cheng-ho, a Chinese Ming dynasty eunuch, were among the largest vessels ever built, nautical monsters that by some accounts carried nine masts. China's achievements have provided a beacon for much of the developing world: its success in lifting 300 million people out of poverty; its fight against disease and illiteracy; its embrace of technology that has put Chinese astronauts in space.
