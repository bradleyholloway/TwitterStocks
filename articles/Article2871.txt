 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 14, 2001  Fannie Mae -- $1 billion offering of global debt was priced through lead managers ABN Amro Securities, HSBC Securities and Goldman Sachs, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Terms: maturity: June 28, 2002; coupon: 4.03%; issue price: par; yield: 4.03%; call: noncallable for six months (one time).  Fannie Mae -- $300 million offering of global notes was priced through lead managers Goldman Sachs and HSBC Securities, according to MCM CorporateWatch. Terms: maturity: July 2, 2004; coupon: 5.125%; issue price: par; yield: 5.125%; call: noncallable for one year.  Conseco Finance Corp. -- $485 million offering of fixed-rate asset-backed securities backed by manufactured-housing loans was priced, according to people familiar with the deal. No details were available on the issue's $402.5 million AAA-rated Class A tranche, with a 6.32-year average life. The $31.25 million AA-rated Class M-1 tranche, with a 10.25-year average life, was sold at 250 basis points above Treasurys. The $25.75 million A-rated Class M-2 tranche, with a 10.25-year average life, was priced at 295 basis points above Treasurys. The $25.5 million BBB-rated Class B tranche, with a 6.46-year average life, was sold at 450 basis points above Treasurys. Lehman Brothers was the lead underwriter on the deal, which was co-managed by Banc One Capital Markets, Credit Suisse First Boston and Merrill Lynch.
