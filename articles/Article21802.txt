 World wire: Wage gap in Japan narrows   The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 1993  A Labor Ministry study found that the difference in pay for Japanese companies' senior managers and new employees has shrunk even further than its already small gap. In companies with more than 100 employees, the typical department chief was found to be paid 3.14 times as much as employees aged between 20 and 24.
