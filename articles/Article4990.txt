 New Securities Issues [1]  The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 12, 1989  California Housing Finance Agency -- $205 million of home mortgage revenue bonds were tentatively priced by a Merrill Lynch Capital Markets group to yield from 7.05% in 1993 to 8% in 2029. The bonds are rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp. The $80 million of 1989 Series A bonds not subject to the federal alternative minimum tax are offered at par to yield from 7.05% in 1993 to 7.50% in 2003. The $125 million of 1989 Series B bonds subject to the federal alternative minimum tax are offered at par to yield from 7.25% in 1993 to 7.75% in 2003. Term bonds include 7 5/8% bonds priced at par due 2009; 7 3/4% bonds priced at par due 2017; and 8% bonds priced at par due 2029 and subject to the alternative minimum tax.  Massachusetts -- $200 million of general obligation bonds, consolidated loan of 1989, Series B, with serial bonds due 1990-2006 and 2009, were won by a First Boston Corp. group with a bid setting the true interest cost at 7.501167%. The bonds were priced for reoffering to yield from 6.80% in 1990 to 7.50% in 2005-2006. The $39.3 million of 7 1/2% term bonds due 2009 weren't formally reoffered. The bonds are rated double-A by Moody's and double-A-plus by S&P, which has them on its CreditWatch surveillance list with negative implications.
