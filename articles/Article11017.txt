 Union Enterprises Ltd. To Separate Its Utility And Non-Utility Assets   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 6, 1987  Under the plan, Union's non-utility assets, which consist mainly of oil and gas holdings, would be transferred to Unicorp. Union holders other than Unicorp would exchange each of their common shares for one new preference share and one warrant of Unigas Corp., a new company that would own Union Gas Ltd., Union's gas utility unit. Unicorp would own all of the Unigas common. The new Unigas preference shares would be valued at 13.10 dollars each and pay an annual dividend of 95 cents a share.
