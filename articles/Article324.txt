 Starbucks Hires Quaker Oats Ex-Manager, Vahlkamp, as Its First Marketing Czar   By By Jennifer Ordonez, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 23, 2001  In the U.S., the Starbucks brand has been built largely though word of mouth, a heavy concentration of stores in big urban markets and countless mentions in the entertainment media that often make light of the chain's perceived ubiquity. With its more than 3,000 stores in the U.S. and plans to triple that number, the Starbucks logo, like McDonald's and strip malls, for some has come to symbolize the homogenization of American cities. Ms. [Cynthia A. Vahlkamp] says she will address that issue.
