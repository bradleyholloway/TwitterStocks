 Charges Filed against Two in Japan Loan Fraud Case   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 3, 1991  Prosecutors filed formal charges against restaurateur Nui Onoue and former Toyo Shinyo Kinko official Tomomi Maekawa in connection with Japan's largest ever loan fraud. Both were charged with fraud and forging documents, and Onoue is charged with using fake certificates as collateral to obtain about $2.6 billion in loans from more than 10 financial institutions, including the Industrial Bank of Japan and Fuji Bank.
