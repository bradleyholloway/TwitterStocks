 Tax rise on airline tickets is proposed by administration to help pay for Nafta   By Murray, William; Davis, Bob, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 14, 1993  It appears that NAFTA will gain more opponents from the airline industry and from consumers since the Clinton administration proposed a tax increase on airline tickets to help pay for the trade accord. James Landry, president of the Air Transport Association of America predicted that if the tax increase is implemented, thousands of additional employees would need to be furloughed.
