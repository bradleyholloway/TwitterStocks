 Steep Hills, Skinny Tires and Guts --- Contenders at Cycling's Giro d'Italia Must Master the Descents; Learning How to Crash Properly   By Reed Albergotti, The Wall Street Journal,  May 12, 2009  Because the tires on racing bikes are so thin and have so little surface area on the ground, riders say they need as much weight pressing down on the center of the bike as possible. Descending is almost impossible to practice. Because roads in big professional races are completely blocked off, cyclists use the entire road to gain speed and turn, using the maximum amount of space.
