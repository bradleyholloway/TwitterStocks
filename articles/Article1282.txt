 Campaign '08: Obama Gains in Party's Top Ranks; Candidate Wrests Lead From Clinton In Superdelegates   By Calmes, Jackie, The Wall Street Journal,  May 12, 2008  Barack Obama's new edge in endorsements from Democratic leaders not only signals the party's establishment is solidifying behind him, but also could allay concerns among party liberals and his supporters that these superdelegates might throw the presidential nomination to Hillary Clinton. The aim is to buy time to show further evidence of Sen. Obama's relative weakness among core Democratic groups, including white blue-collar workers, seniors and women, or to allow for some development that could derail the Obama campaign.
