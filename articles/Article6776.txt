 It's Back to Basics After AIDS Vaccine Setbacks   By Chase, Marilyn, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 2004  Since the human immune system is so poor at containing HIV, the classic vaccine strategy of simply mimicking the immune system "is not the best way," said Dennis Burton, a professor at Scripps Research Institute in La Jolla, Calif. Still, Dr. Burton said he is keen to target gp41, the fusion protein of the AIDS virus, which hides and dodges the immune system by presenting a decoy version of itself.
