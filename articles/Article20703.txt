 More Money, More Risk in Prepaid-Tuition Plans   By Edited by Cristina Lourosa-Ricardo, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 2012  Spooked by the stock market, more parents saving for college are putting their faith in something that could prove to be equally tenuous: the ability of state governments to manage money. Other account options being tested in those states carry monthly charges of $9, $12, $15 and $25 but give customers opportunities to avoid the payments by maintaining minimum balances, using a credit card or taking a mortgage with Bank of America, according to a memo distributed to employees.
