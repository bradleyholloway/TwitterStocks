 Business & race: Muzak changes its tune with a mix of beats   By Wynter, Leon E, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 1996  Muzak, known for soporific tunes originally designed to soothe skittish elevator passengers of the 1930s, early in 1996 expanded to 30 satellite-delivered channels that reflect a shift toward African-American musical styles, and the need for music to fit retail environments where blacks or Hispanics predominate.
