 Whirlpool official realizes goal of tapping into Chinese market   By Hagerty, Bob, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 1995  During the past 15 years, 43-year-old Wah-hui Chu has been in the thick of drives by two major US companies to crack the China market. His first campaign fizzled out, and the second ended in disappointment. At the center of Whirlpool Corp's dash into China, Chu is in charge of drawing Whirlpool's recent joint ventures in China and their 4,000 employees into what the company calls the "global Whirlpool family."
