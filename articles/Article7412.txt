 SEC Charges Firm And Two Officers On Excessive Markups   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1985  The Securities and Exchange Commission accused Anderson & Strudwick Inc., a regional brokerage firm based in Richmond, Va., and two top officers of charging excessive price markups on bond transactions.  In administrative proceedings, the SEC charged that Anderson & Strudwick, George W. Anderson, president, and J. Douglas Gordon Jr., secretary-treasurer, violated the anti-fraud provisions of the securities laws and the fair-pricing and fair-dealing rules of the Municipal Securities Rulemaking Board.
