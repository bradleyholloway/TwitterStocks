 Italy Raises Coupons on the Treasury Bonds Replacing Efim Debt   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 10, 1992  The Italian government, in a bid to satisfy the international banking community, has decided to raise the coupons on some four trillion lire ($3.76 billion) of Treasury bonds that will replace the debts of state holding company Efim. Efim is suffering under the weight of L8 trillion of debts, of which L3.5 trillion is owed to foreign banks.
