 Banks Begin To Withdraw From Their Branch Boom   By Sidel, Robin, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 3, 2010  The total number of retail branches in the U.S. is on pace to decline this year for the first time since at least 2002, according to SNL Financial, a Charlottesville, Va., research firm that tracks branch data filed with banking regulators. According to SNL, there are 98,913 bank branches in the U.S., a decline of about 300, or 0.3%, since June 2009.
