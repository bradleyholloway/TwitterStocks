The union representing New York City hospital security officers sued the city yesterday, claiming that the mayor has decided to replace them with private security guards in retaliation for their asking the State Legislature for permission to carry guns on the job.  James L. Linsey, the union's lawyer, said the decision to replace the officers was the latest stage in the long-running battle over allowing hospital security officers to carry guns. The union says that city officials, including Luis Marcos, president of the Health and Hospitals Corporation, decided to replace the security officers after they repeatedly petitioned the Legislature for the legal authority to carry guns.
