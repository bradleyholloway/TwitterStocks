 Lockheed to Pay $1.5 Million Fine In an OSHA Case   By By Albert R. Karr, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 1989  Lockheed Corp. agreed to pay $1.5 million in fines to settle Labor Department charges that the aircraft maker violated federal safety and health standards at its Burbank, Calif., plant.  Lockheed's Lockheed Aeronautical Systems Co. subsidiary agreed to correct unsafe or unhealthy work practices throughout its operations, including the Burbank, Palmdale and Rye Canyon plants in California, an OSHA spokesman said. Lockheed agreed to the terms of the settlement without admitting guilt as to the alleged violations.
