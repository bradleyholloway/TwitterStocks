 Layoffs, Output Cut At Varity to Result In 4th-Period Charge   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 21, 1991  Varity Corp., responding to weakening demand in its key markets, said it plans to trim its production volumes and inventory levels and to lay off about 5% of its work force, resulting in a fourth-quarter charge of as much as $20 million.  Despite the charge, Varity said it expects to report earnings for the fiscal year ended Jan. 31 were above the company's expectations and higher than the previous year's $92.1 million, or 35 cents a share. However, Varity said it expects to report a loss for the current quarter.
