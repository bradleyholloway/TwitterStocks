 Warning flags: After years of growth, Minneapolis economy enters a risky phase   By Wysocki, Bernard Jr, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 10, 1995  The specter of an unsustainable boom is especially worrying Midwesterners. Minneapolis/St Paul has enjoyed steady, near boom conditions for several years, with the gross product growing at a 4% annual average in 1992-94, soaring personal income and low unemployment. However, many consumers, lenders and entrepreneurs wonder if imprudent lending practices have the economic expansion living on borrowed time.
