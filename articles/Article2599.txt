 Cotton prices rise on rumors of buying by China, but government reports don't support the talk   By Taylor, Jeffrey, The Wall Street Journal,  May 20, 1994  Cotton futures prices shot higher on May 19, 1994 on ill-founded rumors that China was buying cotton, rising the daily limit of $.02 to $.8265 a pound. However, prices are expected to decline on May 20. In other commodity markets, grains and soybean futures prices fell while petroleum futures rose.
