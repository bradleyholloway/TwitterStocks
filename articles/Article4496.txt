 SEC Accuses 5 Men Of Defrauding Firms In Stock-Trade Scheme   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 1985  The SEC, in a suit filed in federal court here, said that the businessmen bought more than $10 million in stock of a land-development company and other securities from E.F. Hutton Group Inc.; Rooney, Pace Group Inc.; Dean Witter Reynolds Inc., a unit of Sears, Roebuck & Co.; Prudential-Bache Securities, a unit of Prudential Insurance Co. of America; Bear, Stearns & Co., and others.  Named as defendants were Texas businessmen Thomas W. Reid and Michael Joe Rogers; New York businessmen Armond Zaccaria and Peter Vito; Los Angeles businessman Maurice Rind and his company, B&M Investments Inc.
