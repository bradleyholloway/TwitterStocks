 Prices of Cattle Futures Climb to Record Levels as Meatpackers Compete for Slim Livestock Supply   By Kilman, Scott, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 3, 1991  Prices of cattle futures contracts hit record levels Apr 2, 1991 amid increasing competition among meatpackers for a tight supply of livestock. The jump in cattle prices signals that losses in the meatpacking industry probably won't end soon. At the Chicago Mercantile Exchange, cattle contracts for Apr 1991 delivery hit a record 82.7 cents a pound before settling at 82.42 cents, up 0.52 cents.
