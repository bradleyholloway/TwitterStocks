 Institutional Money Strays From Tradition; Investments Are Shifting Out of U.S. Stocks, Bonds In Search of 'Alternatives'   By Slater, Joanna, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 12, 2007  The trend toward riskier investment strategies, and away from garden-variety stock and bond buying, looks set to continue. Forty-two percent of the public pension funds surveyed by Greenwich said they are planning to put significantly more money in hedge funds over the next two years. Last year, such investments represented an average of just 0.5% of their total investments. For corporate pension plans, the figure was 2.2%.
