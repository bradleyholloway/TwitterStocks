 U.S. News: Anonymous Ladies and Gentlemen of the Jury --- Concerned About Tampering, Threats, Judges Consider Withholding Identities   By Jones, Ashby; Koppel, Nathan, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 12, 2010  Many courts require that judges find a particular reason exists to withhold jurors' names -- for instance, that jurors have good cause to fear for their safety. Since the mid-1990s, trial courts in Los Angeles county have shielded jurors' names in most criminal cases, a policy initiated by Superior Court Judge Philip Mautino after he found in informal surveys that 98% of them wanted to be anonymous.
