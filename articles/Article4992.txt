 Allied-Signal to Pay $1 Million to Settle Defense Case Charges   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 12, 1989  MORRIS TOWNSHIP, N.J. -- Allied-Signal Inc. said it agreed to pay $1 million to settle civil charges related to the sale to the government of bomb parts employees knew were defective.  The settlement, announced by the U.S. attorney's office in Grand Rapids, Mich., comes in the wake of a three-year investigation of the company's Courter operations in Boyne City, Mich. Seven former Courter employees pleaded guilty to federal conspiracy and fraud charges related to the case.
