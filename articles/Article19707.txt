 Stamps.com, Sylvan Learning Systems Decline   By Schreiber, Cynthia, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 4, 2003  Tire stocks were the session's top percentage gainers. Goodyear Tire & Rubber (NYSE) rose 73 cents, or 11%, to 7.52, after UBS Investment Research upgraded the stock to "neutral" from "reduce," saying a bond deal now seems possible, which would push potential liquidity issues further into the future. Cooper Tire & Rubber (NYSE) added 45 cents, or 2.2%, to close at 20.50.  C-Cor.net fell 1.68, or 14%, to 10.75, after Credit Lyonnais Securities downgraded the stock to "add" from "buy," saying the State College, Pa., communications company's shares offer limited upside after their recent rally.  RehabCare Group (NYSE) rose 1.38, or 7.7%, to 19.25, after J.P. Morgan upgraded the stock to "overweight" from "underweight," saying the St. Louis health-services company should see strong sequential improvement in fourth-quarter earnings because of cost cuts.
