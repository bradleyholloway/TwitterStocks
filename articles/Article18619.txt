 Copper reaches a four-year high in London, but falls in U.S. as traders await job data   By McGee, Suzanne, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 1994  Copper prices hit a four-year high in London as three-month copper futures went for $2,704 a metric ton on Nov 3, 1994. In the US, copper was down 2.60 cents to $1.2370 a pound due to fears of FRB interest-rate tightening. Elsewhere, corn was down in the face of an expected higher US harvest.
