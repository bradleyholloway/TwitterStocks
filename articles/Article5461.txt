 World-wide: South Korea's parliament   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 1997  South Korea's parliament prepared to begin debate on Mar 10, 1997 on revisions to a labor law that led to nationwide strikes when it was adopted in Dec 1996 in a secret session. The revised law suspends for two years a clause making it easier for firms to order layoffs, but unions say it isn't enough.
