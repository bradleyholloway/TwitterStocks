 Some Senators Want to Delay Voting On Nuclear Test Ban Until Next Year   By By Neil King Jr. and Jeffrey Taylor, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 6, 1999  Thrown onto the Senate floor just days ago and with a vote set for Oct. 12, the treaty has sparked intense and deeply partisan debate. Democrats accuse the Republicans of playing politics with an important matter of national security, while most Republicans argue that the treaty would undermine the country's nuclear strength. Underscoring the bad feelings, Mr. (President) Clinton said "it would have been unthinkable before this Congress that such a treaty would not pass."
