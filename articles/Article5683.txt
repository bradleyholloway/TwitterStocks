 Minor health-care steps, such as reforming insurance, may face hard time in new Congress   By McGinley, Laurie, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 12, 1995  The drive for sweeping reform in health care has diminished in Congress, while the drive for reform is slowing on the state level, too. Instead, some GOP lawmakers plan to press less-sweeping alternatives, most of which come under the rubric of insurance reform.
