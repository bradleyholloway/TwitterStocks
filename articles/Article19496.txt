 Report Suggests Global Adoption Of Best Fuel-Efficiency Standards   By By David Bank, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 2002  The report notes that China, Mexico, Thailand and South Korea are likely to reach world-class emissions standards within the next decade. But it says Indonesia, Venezuela, Russia and several African and Middle Eastern countries still allow leaded gasoline, which precludes the adoption of certain emission-control technologies. The report called for an immediate ban on leaded fuel and a steep reduction in sulfur and benzene content. The report also suggested the adoption of on-board diagnostic technologies to identify emission-system failures.
