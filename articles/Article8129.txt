 Yeltsin Changes Style in Race for Presidency of Russian Republic   By Gumbel, Peter, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1991  On Jun 12, 1991, the once-rebellious political outcast Boris Yeltsin is likely to win in the first presidential election ever in Russia, the Soviet Union's largest republic. In an effort to appear presidential, Yeltsin has dramatically changed his rebellious demeanor in recent months to one more conciliatory, though his political platform remains radical.
