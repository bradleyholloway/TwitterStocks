 Securities companies in Japan ready plans for trust bank units   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 7, 1993  Major Japanese securities companies are expected to set up trust bank subsidiaries later in 1993 after laws lowering the barriers between different financial sectors take effect in April. In Dec 1992, Japan's Finance Ministry said that long-term credit banks and foreign-exchange banks would be given a one-year head start over commercial banks in setting up such subsidiaries.
