 No U.S. Strikes At Iraqi Leaders Hit, Group Says   By Jaffe, Greg, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 2003  Many of the senior Iraqi leaders were targeted after senior U.S. officials intercepted conversations that the Iraqis were having on their satellite phones, which carry a global-positioning device. The device, however, is accurate only within a 328-foot radius. Using additional intelligence, the military tried to narrow down the target's location. But too often that intelligence was "unreliable," the Human Rights Watch report states.
