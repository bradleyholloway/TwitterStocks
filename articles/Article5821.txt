 MetLife Reduces Planned Size of IPO To $2.51 Billion   By By Deborah Lohse, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 2000  MetLife, awaiting approval for its demutualization from New York's insurance superintendent, said it will raise as much as an additional $1 billion from a private placement of up to 73 million shares of common stock to Spanish bank Banco Santander Central Hispano SA and Credit Suisse Group. MetLife is a partner with Banco Santander in a Spanish insurance company; Credit Suisse, in which MetLife has a small stake, is co-managing MetLife's IPO. MetLife also plans to raise $1 billion from a convertible bond offering.
