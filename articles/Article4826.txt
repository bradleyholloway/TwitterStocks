 Heard on the Street: Stocks of Companies Announcing Layoffs Fire Up Investors, but Prices Often Wilt   By Dorfman, John R, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1991  A study by business consultants Mitchell & Co found that over a period of one to three years, company layoffs and write-offs are often a strong sell signal for stocks, and that in general, corporate reductions do not ultimately lead to improved profits.
