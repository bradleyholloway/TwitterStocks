 Texaco to Receive Loan of $3 Billion To Pay Creditors   By By Allanna Sullivan, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 1988  Texaco Inc. confirmed yesterday that a group of banks has agreed to provide it with a bridge loan of $3 billion to help pay the $5.5 billion debt the oil company owes its creditors.  The debt will be secured by the stock of Caltex Petroleum Corp., Texaco Canada Inc., Deutsche Texaco AG, oil and gas reserves or other interchangeable assets, the company said in a filing with the bankruptcy court. But that doesn't preclude the sale of any of these assets.
