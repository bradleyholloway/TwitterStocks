 World-Wide   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 2005  Sadr's fighters attacked the pro-government Badr militia when it tried to block reopening of his Najaf political offices. Fighting raged in five cities; Premier Jaafari went on TV to urge calm as Sadr- allied officials threaten to disrupt delicate constitution talks. At the same time, Sunni insurgents hit Baghdad, killing 13 in a series of raids. The U.S. is adding 1,500 troops for elections. Bush's base is sticking with him despite setbacks.  Iran vowed new nuclear proposals and the U.S. said diplomacy "still has legs" after EU nations canceled talks. France said it's eager to revive them.
