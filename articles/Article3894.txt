 Nonprofit Hospitals Leave The City for Greener Pastures   By Martinez, Barbara, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 14, 2008  Close money-losing facilities in poor areas where a large share of patients are uninsured, and build or refurbish hospitals in affluent places where people have private insurance coverage. Shutting down unprofitable operations and expanding profitable ones is a common business maneuver, but nonprofit hospital systems aren't ordinary businesses.
