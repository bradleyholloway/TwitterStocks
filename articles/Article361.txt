Just two days after the police charged a man with drunken driving when he crashed into the scene of an accident on Staten Island, killing a veteran police officer, the New York City Medical Examiner said yesterday that there were no traces of alcohol or drugs in the man's blood.  Although the driver, Ciardi Franklin, 27, of Perth Amboy, N.J., was charged on Sunday with drunken driving and vehicular manslaughter, as well as criminally negligent homicide and assault, it was likely that based on the test results, the first two charges would be dropped, said David W. Lehr, Chief Assistant District Attorney for Richmond County. But he added that the charges of criminally negligent homicide and assault would probably be referred to a grand jury.
