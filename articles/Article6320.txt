 Foreign Exchange: Dollar May Face A Few Hurdles From Rate Moves   By McGeever, Jamie, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 2004  In theory, higher U.S. rates should boost the dollar by reducing the relative yield disadvantage versus other countries, attracting more foreign capital into the U.S. and making it easier to narrow the massive current-account deficit. However, reality is often uglier, with Fed tightening causing a squeeze of liquidity and having a temporary destabilizing effect on markets. If U.S. bond and stock prices both fall in the face of rising rates, as they have recently, that's not a constructive scenario for the dollar.
