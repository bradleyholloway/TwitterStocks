 Marketing and media: advertising: American Express's ad snafu revives spat on creative pilfering   By Goldman, Kevin, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 14, 1992  American Express Co., already under fire from the International Olympic Committee for its recent Olympics-related television advertising campaign, withdrew another TV ad after it appeared only once and European American Bank (Uniondale, NY) claimed it copied one of its advertisements. Chiat/Day/Mojo, which produced the American Express spot, acknowledged that it had inadvertently appropriated the bank ad's concept and images. Claims of advertising copyright infringement usually hang on whether the "copycat" ad thoroughly "looks and feels" like the original ad.
