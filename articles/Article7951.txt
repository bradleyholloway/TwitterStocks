 GM Plans to Lay Off 17,600 of Its Workers At Canadian Plants   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 7, 1989  GM said it plans to indefinitely layoff the second shift of workers at its engine plant in St. Catherines, Ontario, on Jan 15. Workers at the St. Catherines facility build engines for the two-door and four-door versions of the Buick Regal and the Chevrolet Lumina, and for GM's new minivans, generically called the APV.
