 Romanian firm's formula for success: Quality goods, free-market principles   By Beck, Ernest, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 6, 1995  In Romania, where the ravaged economy is still dominated by sluggish state industries, Elvila International is unique. It is a private, start-up operation from which three major factories churn out some 5,000 pieces of upholstered furniture monthly, with about 50% of production exported, mostly to Germany and other western European countries.
