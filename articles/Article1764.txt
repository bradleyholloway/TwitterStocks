 New Securities Issues [1]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 1993  Branch Bank & Trust -- $100 million of notes due May 15, 1996, priced as 4.75s at 99.842 to yield 4.813%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable bank notes were priced at a spread of 50 basis points above Treasurys. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co. Branch Bank & Trust is a subsidiary of BB&T Financial Corp.  International Lease Finance Corp. -- $100 million of notes due July 15, 1996, priced as 4.75s at 99.65 to yield 4.877%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 55 basis points above Treasurys. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's and single-A-plus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Salomon Brothers Inc. International Lease Finance is a subsidiary of American International Group Inc.  10Washington Public Power Supply System -- $505.3 million refunding revenue bonds, Series 1993B, for Nuclear Project Nos. 1, 2 and 3 (Bonneville Power Administration) tentatively priced by a Goldman, Sachs & Co. group. The offering consists of $192.8 million Nuclear Project No. 1 bonds, priced to yield from 2.85% in 1994 to 5.82% in 2012. The bonds are rated double-A by both Moody's and S&P, with the exception of MBIA-insured bonds due 1998, 2004 and 2015, which are triple-A rated. There are $168.1 million Nuclear Project No. 2 bonds priced to yield from 2.85% in 1994 to 5.82% in 2012. Ratings are double-A, with the exception of bonds due 1998 and 2004, which are MBIA-insured and are rated triple-A. There are also $25.5 million Nuclear Project No. 2 periodic auction reset securities due 2010, which are not being publicly reoffered. Also, there are $25.5 million Nuclear Project No. 2 inverse floating-rate securities due 2010, which aren't being publicly reoffered. For Nuclear Project No. 3, there are $93.3 million bonds priced to yield from 2.85% in 1994 to 5.82% in 2013. Bonds due 1998, 2004 and 2018 are MBIA-insured and rated triple-A, while the remainder of the bonds are double-A rated.
