 Dutch Auction Of MCorp Stock Called a Failure   By By Rick Stine, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 12, 1987  The three-year-old Dutch auction preferred stock market had its first "failed" auction when the dividend on a $62.5 million MCorp issue was set at the maximum allowable rate.  Opinion about the impact of the failed auction was divided. There were predictions of jitters in the auction-rate preferred market, while some analysts said that with the failed-auction monkey off the market's back, it now can move forward and continue to function efficiently.
