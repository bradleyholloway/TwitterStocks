 Letters to the Editor: Maybe We Can Learn To Do Without Tests   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 12, 2001  Your June 6 Letters to the Editor "What's the True Worth of Achievement Tests?" reminds me that as of l969 just over one-half of the Ph.D.s in America had graduated in the bottom half of their high-school class. Meanwhile, Harvard studies showed that the B students end up achieving more than the A students, while the Cs often do the best of all.
