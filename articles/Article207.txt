A Tlingit Thunderbird  A monumental American Indian screen emblazoned with a spread-winged thunderbird, to be auctioned next week, is believed to be the first such Northwest Coast tribal artifact ever to be offered at public sale. The heraldic panel, a 24-by-12-foot wood crest of a Tlingit clan painted before World War I in Auke, an Alaskan fishing village north of Juneau, is to be presented at 10:15 A.M. on Tuesday at Sotheby's in New York.
