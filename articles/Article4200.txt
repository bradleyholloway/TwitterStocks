 U.S. Expert Sees 500,000 Chernobyl Cancer Deaths --- A Wall Street Journal News Roundup   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 10, 1986  A U.S. expert on the effects of radiation estimated that the April 26 Chernobyl nuclear accident in the Soviet Union eventually could cause as many as 500,000 cancer deaths, far more than estimates of other Western scientists.  Dr. John Gofman, a medical doctor who specializes in studying the effects of radiation, announced his analysis at a meeting of the American Chemical Society in Anaheim, Calif.
