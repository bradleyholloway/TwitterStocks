 He Sees a Dream Where Others See Something Else   By By Chad Terhune, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 1999  In yet another expansion of the state's service economy, a growing legion of entrepreneurs want to scoop Fido's poop from your yard. A Tampa Bay woman started her Mary POOPins business last summer, and, since September, a couple in Fort Myers have been cleaning up people's lawns.  Now a professional pooper-scooper has swept into Central Florida. Mark Clemens started Doogie-Doo Removal Services with his fiancee out of their Orlando home in February. The two have garnered more than a half-dozen customers so far willing to pay $7 a visit for a poop-free yard.
