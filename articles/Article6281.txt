 Professors Want to Turn Car Chases Into a Science   By By Will Pinkston, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 12, 2000  Two University of South Carolina professors studying police pursuits want law-enforcement officers around the country to break out their calculators and spreadsheets to find safer approaches to chasing crooks in cars.  Criminal justice professor Geoffrey Alpert and marketing professor Thomas Hadden pored over four years of police-pursuit data from the Miami police department to determine why some police pursuits go wrong, resulting in property damage, injury or death.
