 World-Wide   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 2003  They include five Army comrades of Jessica Lynch and two Apache pilots. With Saddam Hussein's hometown close to falling, allies have near-total control of Iraq. Hussein's chief science adviser and a half-brother are in custody, and Gen. Franks said U.S. forces have samples of the dictator's DNA for use if identification is necessary. Baghdad remains restive, though looting has abated somewhat. The Iraq National Museum, repository of much of the world's heritage from the cradle of civilization, was stripped. A grenade injured six Americans; Marines found hundreds of suicide-bomber vests. Bush toughened rhetoric against Syria, demanding that it "cooperate." The U.S. is scaling back on carriers and aircraft. The Army division denied entry via Turkey has entered Iraq.
