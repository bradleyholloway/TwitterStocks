 Bank of New York Gets Lower Ratings On Debt by Moody's   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 21, 1988  Moody's Investors Service Inc. said it lowered ratings on $1.8 billion of Bank of New York Co. securities because of the bank's pending acquisition of Irving Bank Corp.  Bank of New York's senior debt was reduced to single-A-2 from single-A-1; its subordinated debt to single-A-3 from single-A-2; and cumulative preferred to single-A-2 from single-A-1. The bank's commercial paper was maintained at Prime-1. Also, its long-term deposit rating was lowered to double-A-3 from double-A-2.
