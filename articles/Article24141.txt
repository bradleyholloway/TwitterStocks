 REVIEW --- Books: Getting Ready for Overtime   By Stark, Andrew, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 2012  Interviewing them regularly over the coming decades, Harvard scientists aimed to pinpoint the personal attributes that most reliably predict a successful life: that is, a life of superior achievement and income, good physical and mental health, and happy marital and parental relationships. The project was initially dominated by the midcentury preoccupations of academic psychology: extroverted personality traits (whether men with a "rich, exuberant social presence" make the best business leaders); the degree of conformity to social norms (whether "normal" men are more likely to make their "proper place" in the world); even physical attributes (whether broad-shouldered, narrow-hipped men make the best military leaders).
