 Manville Plan On Asbestos Gets Lawyers' Approval   By By Jonathan Dahl, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 1985  A group of attorneys for asbestos victims approved the "basic economic" aspects of Manville Corp.'s plan to pay victims $2.5 billion to $3 billion.  The Denver-based forest and building products concern, which once was the nation's largest asbestos producer, has been operating under Chapter 11 of the federal Bankruptcy Code since August 1982. Manville filed for Chapter 11 shelter after being swamped with thousands of lawsuits claiming damage from exposure to Manville-made asbestos products. Asbestos, once used widely in building insulation, can cause lung cancer and other respiratory diseases.
