 Cross Country: President Obama: The Preview?   By Keller, Jon, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 2008  Having never held elective office before -- though he was assistant attorney general for the civil rights division in the Clinton administration -- it was easy for him to claim that he wouldn't be beholden to special interests or outmoded orthodoxies. Baby boomers, eager to make a permanent mark on the political landscape, also found the idea of electing the state's first black governor appealing.
