 China's War Games Show Round of Political Theater --- Silence Spurs Speculation Exercises Are Meant To Gain Military Favor   By By Charles Hutzler, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 4, 2001  Reports in some official Chinese media bill the exercises as the country's largest since a series of maneuvers and missile tests near Taiwan in 1996, which raised tensions with the U.S. to their highest point in decades. The latest exercises, which may take place as early as this week, involve all three branches of the armed forces and are said to focus on two scenarios: capturing one of Taiwan's outlying islands and attacking an aircraft carrier. That second objective, military analysts say, is meant as a warning to the U.S., which angered China by sending warships off the island's coast during the 1996 war games.
