Relocating offices is never easy and a slumping economy makes it even worse. That's why, when a big insurance company decided to relocate 300 executives from New York to Hartford earlier this year, it hired Bill Herz to help.  Mr. Herz didn't provide psychological counseling for executives afraid of the transition or professional guidance for their spouses. No. He did tricks. Magic tricks. And now the company does not even want him to mention its name because if shareholders were to find out, they might consider it frivolous in light of further bad news it plans to announce soon.
