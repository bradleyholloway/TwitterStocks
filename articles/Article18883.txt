 Lazard Freres sidelines ostensible heir following falling-out with chairman   By Kamm, Thomas; Fleming, Charles, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1997  Edouard Stern, often cited as the leading candidate to one day take over the top job at the Lazard Freres merchant bank, has been sidelined from two key in-house committees following a dispute with his father-in-law, Chairman Michel David-Weill, bank insiders say. The shunting aside of Stern leaves open the question of who will take over coordination of the bank, which has big independent operations in Paris, London and New York.
