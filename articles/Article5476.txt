 More CEOs Prefer Outsiders on Boards, A Study Indicates   By By Joann S. Lublin, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 12, 1993  About 40% of 327 CEOs surveyed last year by recruiters Korn/Ferry International said they preferred being the only inside board member. Just 10.6% of chief executives favored having only one insider in a similar 1991 poll by Korn/Ferry. In that survey, a majority of CEOs said an ideal board consisted of three insiders and nine outsiders.
