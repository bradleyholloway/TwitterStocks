THE quagga mussel, a mollusk found in the northern waters of the United States, turns out to be a native of Ukraine, according to scientists at Cornell University.  Quagga mussels are found only in the Ukraine's Bug and Dneiper Rivers, where they originated, and in North America's Lake Ontario, Lake Erie and the St. Lawrence River, said Dr. Bernie May, director of Cornell's Genome Variation Analysis Facility.
