 Airlines Find Few Suspect Parts In Boeing Fuel-Pump Inspection   By By Andy Pasztor, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 3, 2002  The directive applies to about 1,400 Boeing jets flown by U.S. carriers, though foreign regulators eventually may expand it to 2,100 more planes. The models affected are the latest versions of Boeing's 737, as well as 747 and 757 models. While the FAA order caused minimal schedule disruptions during the weekend, industry officials said some long-range 747s may have to alter routes or reduce cargo and passenger loads due to the FAA's ban on using an extra fuel tank in the tails of some of the jets.
