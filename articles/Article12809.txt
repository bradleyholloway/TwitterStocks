 Ahead of the Tape   By By Jesse Eisinger, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 6, 2002  Wal-Mart said yesterday that same-store sales were up 6.2% in May, compared with expectations of 4.7%. The last week or so was particularly strong. Lawn and garden stuff sold well, which, given the relatively high margins there, bodes well for Wal-Mart's earnings.  Intel's previous revenue expectations were in the $6.4 billion to $7 billion range. According to Thomson Financial/First Call, analysts expect $6.7 billion. But analysts have come out with a flurry of notes in the past several days saying that Intel could guide to the "low end."
