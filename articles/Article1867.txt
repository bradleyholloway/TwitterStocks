 Business and Finance [4]  The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 1994  NORTHROP'S hostile $2.04 billion bid for Grumman could face an obstacle in a 33% equity stake in Grumman that employees hold. The stake is held by a savings and retirement plan, and the plan's trustees, all of whom are Grumman executives, have the power to decide whether to accept the Northrop offer, a rival $1.87 billion bid by Martin Marietta, or to let employee shareholders decide.
