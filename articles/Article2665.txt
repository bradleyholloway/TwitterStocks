 Object of the Week: The Horse's Mouth   By By Robert J. Hughes, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 2000  In the right hands, metal plate could be as fluidly expressive as tapestry. By the middle period of the Ottoman Empire in the 1600s, craftsmen skilled in metallurgy had made such huge advances -- including refinements in forging and molding techniques -- that they were able to create pieces of exquisite detail.
