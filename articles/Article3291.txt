 EU Budget-Rule Debate Intensifies   By G. Thomas Sims and John W. Miller, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 14, 2004  When Europe's foreign ministers meet today, they will come under pressure to cut down the commission's power to police national budget deficits. In a letter reviewed by Dow Jones Newswires, foreign ministers from Germany, Italy, Greece and Poland -- all running deficits above 3% of GDP -- are demanding changes to the proposed EU constitution. The document sent to Irish Foreign Minister Brian Cowan says that budgets are "areas of national competence" and that the commission should have the power only to "recommend," not "propose," sanctions against overspenders.
