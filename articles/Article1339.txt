 The Tilting Yard: Good-Bye to All That   By Thomas, Frank, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 12, 2008  "It reversed the Idea of Progress," writes the literary historian Paul Fussell in "The Great War and Modern Memory," the single best account of the war's cultural impact. By 1930, it was possible for Macmillan to publish a book called, simply, "Colossal Blunders of the War," a history of 1914-1918 made up of nothing but martial mistakes, organized by nationality and with sarcastic remarks inserted at the appropriate points.
