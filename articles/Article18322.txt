 Air Force Admits B-2 Won't Live Up to Initial Promises   By Pasztor, Andy, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 1992  The USAF admitted that the B-2 bomber, the most expensive combat plane in history, will never be able to comply fully with all of its original performance specifications. Air Force brass now insist that the original requirements were too stringent, and that changes in tactics and the types of missions to be expected will compensate for any technical shortfalls.
