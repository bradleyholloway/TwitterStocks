 As EMI Attracts More Bidders, Its Stock Sings   By Daniel Thomas and Jessica Hodgson, The Wall Street Journal,  May 5, 2007  In its most recent set of discussions with Warner Music, EMI said in early March it had received and rejected a "nonbinding " proposal from Warner at 260 pence a share.  EMI's share price has declined significantly since the two companies were involved in a tit-for-tat bidding war last year that was widely expected to lead to a tie-up, sparked by an initial $4.2 billion offer for Warner by EMI.
