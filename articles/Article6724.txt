 Short Selling: Bearish Bets on Nasdaq Fall by 2.7%   By McKay, Peter A, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 2007  Short interest often registers bigger moves in percentage terms at Nasdaq because the tech shares that are the exchange's bread and butter are especially attractive trading vehicles for hedge funds and other players that make bearish bets. They may pile into highflying tech names hoping for a market downturn or, conversely bail out quickly during market rallies that hurt the value of many bearish bets.
