 Bond investors may have to stretch to repeat their double-digit returns   By Vogel, Thomas T, Jr, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 1994  Investors who took on extra risk in the form of junk bonds, convertibles and long-term Treasurys profited handsomely from double-digit returns in 1993, but those who stayed with more staid sectors like investment-grade corporate bonds and intermediate-term Treasuries also did fairly well.
