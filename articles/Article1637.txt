 Petroleum Prices Advance on Rumors of Tensions in Iraq, Lower-Than-Expected Loadings of Crude   By Solomon, Caleb, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 14, 1992  Petroleum prices advanced in light trading on Aug 13, 1992 in response to rumors of Iraqi tensions and lower-than-expected loadings of North Sea crudes. On the New York Mercantile Exchange, light sweet crude oil for Sep 1992 delivery was up $.26 a barrel to $21.34. October crude settled at $21.27 a barrel, up $.25. On other commodity markets, metals prices declined, as did sugar futures prices, while some pork-belly futures contracts rose.
