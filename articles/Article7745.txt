 Business bulletin: banking: a special report: a special background report on trends in industry and finance   By Guenther, Robert, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 7, 1988  The mortgage market is becoming more competitive as demand falls, leading to layoffs and pricing wars. The number of lender liability cases is increasing. Experts believe large US banks will have to strain to meet proposed international capital standards. Manufacturers Hanover Trust Co. helped residents set up a credit union after the bank closed a branch. Japanese banks have nine percent of US banking assets. Creditworthy prospects for Visa and MasterCard are hard to find now that the market is saturated.
