 Newspaper Chain Is Soliciting Offers Amid Deals Trend   By Berman, Dennis K, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 4, 2004  Liberty is already a highly leveraged company. In the quarter ended March 31, Liberty paid more than $15 million in interest expense and posted a net loss of $3.9 million. The company carries about $360 million in debt, say people familiar with the sales materials distributed by the company.  That debt was taken on to fund a roll-up of small weekly and free "shopper" publications begun in 1998 with the purchase of newspapers from Hollinger International Inc. A call to Liberty's chief executive wasn't returned last night.
