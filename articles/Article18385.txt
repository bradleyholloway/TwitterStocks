 Tuesday's Markets Stocks Fall 9.73 Points, Bonds Rise --- Investors Take Profits After Monday's Gain; Oil Issues Take a Hit   By By Steven E. Levingston and Tom Herman, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 1992  An unexpectedly large drop in the index of leading economic indicators weighed on stock prices but gave bonds an early boost. Medium-term Treasury note prices ended slightly higher, although long-term bonds finished mostly unchanged. Prices of taxfree state and local government bonds rose as investors bet that a Clinton presidency will result in higher taxes. Last night, as early returns showed Gov. Clinton with a commanding lead, prices of Treasury bond futures contracts inched lower on the Chicago Board of Trade.
