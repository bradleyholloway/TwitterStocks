 Congress Clears Spending Bill With Tight Domestic Limits; Lawmakers Strike Provision That Risked Violating Rules for Taxpayer Privacy   By Rogers, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 7, 2004  The same pattern is repeated with the Environmental Protection Agency budget. For example, contaminated sediment is a major problem in the Great Lakes basin, and two years ago Congress passed a law promising $270 million in funding over five years. This has been a priority for EPA Administrator Mike Leavitt, who heads a Great Lakes federal interagency task force that held a major meeting in Chicago Friday. But even after Mr. Leavitt interceded in the year-end talks, the final bill gives him only $22.5 million for 2005, half the president's request.
