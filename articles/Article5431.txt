 Some Suspect Breast-Cancer Gene Is Holdover of a Useful Mutation   By Waldholz, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 11, 1992  Scientists believe that by identifying the gene that causes breast cancer they will also discover why the disease is so increasingly common. Some evidence suggests that the increasing incidence of the disease, which occurs in one of every nine American women, is a result of changing lifestyles while others believe the disease may be the result of human evolution.
