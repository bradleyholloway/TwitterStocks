 Ahead of the Tape   By Lahart, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 2006  OK. Beyond the bonus envy that's gripping Wall Streeters who don't work at Goldman, where the average bonus is $600,000-plus, it's been good times all around for brokerage firms. Given the tendency of analysts to underestimate brokerage earnings during good times, nobody will be surprised if Bear Stearns and Lehman beat expectations. More likely, investors will be disappointed if the firms don't top estimates by a wide margin. After Goldman reported earnings per share of $6.59 on Tuesday, rather than the $6 analysts expected, its shares fell.
