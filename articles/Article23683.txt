 Food & Drink -- How's Your Drink? Making Bitter Fernet-Branca Much Easier to Swallow   By Felten, Eric, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 3, 2009  In the 1930 "Savoy Cocktail Book," there is a drink called a Hanky-Panky made with equal parts gin and sweet vermouth with a scant dash or two of Fernet-Branca. The Fanciulli Cocktail predated Volstead by such a stretch that the book's author, Albert Stevens Crockett, was able only to guess at the meaning of the drink's name, assuming that it was just a riff on Italian slang for "the boys."
