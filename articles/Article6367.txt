 Time Off: A Week of Diversions   By By Stuart Ferguson, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 2000  This exhibition tells the story of Russian exploration and colonization in North America. Vitus Bering, a Danish mariner working for Russia, landed on the Alaskan coast on July 15, 1741. Soon the Russians were exhanging sea otter pelts from the region for tea, porcelain and other Asian luxuries. The Russian-American Company gained a monopoly on the fur trade and authority to administer settlements, with headquarters first on Kodiak Island, then at Sitka ("New Archangel").
