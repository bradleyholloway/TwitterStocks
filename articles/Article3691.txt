 PURSUITS; Books: Children's Books   By Meghan Cox Gurdon, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 2007  The title of this book ought to give you an idea of its goofy wonderfulness. "The Neddiad" tells the epic adventures not of a city (the "Iliad") or of a lone warrior (the "Aeneid") but of a nice boy named Neddie Wentworthstein, whose encounter with destiny begins when he expresses the desire to eat in a famous Hollywood restaurant shaped like a derby hat. To Neddie's surprise, his father replies: "Neddie, my boy! It is also my lifelong ambition to eat in the restaurant shaped like a hat!
