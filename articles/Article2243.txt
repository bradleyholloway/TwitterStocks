 Janus, fund manager settle SEC charges   By Gasparino, Charles, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 1997  Janus Capital Corp and Ronald V. Speaker, one of its star bond-fund managers, have reached a settlement with the SEC involving allegations of improper trading at the Denver fund concern. Speaker will give up the $16,000 he made on 'arbitrage opportunities," pay penalties and interest of $21,199 and be suspended from working in the mutual-fund business for the next 90 days and is barred from personal trading of most securities for the next three years. Janus will pay a penalty of $25,000.
