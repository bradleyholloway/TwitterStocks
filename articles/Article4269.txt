 U.S. Leasing to Report 33% Earnings Increase For the First Quarter   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 10, 1987  U.S. Leasing International Inc. expects to post a 33% increase in first-quarter net income because of a "normal growth trend" from continuing investment in its core leasing businesses.  D.E. Mundell, president and chief executive officer of the equipment-rental company, said profit for the quarter rose to about $7.6 million, or roughly $1 a share, from the year-earlier $5.7 million, or 75 cents a share. Revenue grew 11% to $112.9 million from $101.7 million.
