 Superfluous Gibberish Regarding Options Cost   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 2005  Your Dec. 30 "Moving the Market/Tracking the Numbers" column ("Outside Audit: Rule Seeks to Uncover Cost of Stock Options") actually uncovered confusion at the Financial Accounting Standards Board. Advocates of expensing claim the cost of stock options occurs at the time they are granted -- i.e., the "opportunity cost" of not selling supposedly similar options to investors.
