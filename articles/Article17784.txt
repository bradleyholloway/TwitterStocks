 Turner Broadcasting's Financial Chief Is Quitting; Duties of 3 Top Aides Shifted   By By Linda Williams and Betsy Morris, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 1987  Turner Broadcasting System Inc. said William C. Bevins Jr., vice president and chief financial officer, is resigning. The company also announced a realignment of responsibilities for three top executives.  A spokesman wouldn't say whether Mr. Bevins would stay long enough to complete the restructuring and wouldn't give a reason for his planned departure. Mr. Bevins couldn't be reached for comment.  He was the main executive in shaping the financial details for Chairman Ted Turner's expansion plans over the years and he is the Turner insider most respected by the financial community. Mr. Bevins was a certified public accountant for 10 years before joining Turner Broadcasting in 1979.
