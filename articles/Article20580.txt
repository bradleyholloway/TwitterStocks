 Corporate News: Corporate Watch   By Anonymous, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 2011  The situation is clearly reflected in the global airline industry, where carriers are grappling with whether to risk losing passengers by passing on higher fuel costs. Since Feb. 19, one-way tickets from Australia to Europe will incur a surcharge of 145 Australian dollars (US$146.68), up from A$95, a trip to the U.S. will come with a A$115 charge, from A$85, and a journey to Asia rises to A$75 from A$55.
