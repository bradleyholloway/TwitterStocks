 Ford Motor Assigns Top Executives To Luxury Unit Under New CEO   By By Norihiko Shirouzu, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 9, 2001  Ford said Roman Krygier, 58 years old, was appointed group vice president, manufacturing and quality. He had been vice president, powertrain operations. Martin Zimmerman, 55, will handle environmental and safety engineering, governmental affairs and corporate public affairs as group vice president, corporate affairs. He had been vice president, governmental affairs. David Thursfield, 56, also became group vice president. He will remain chairman, president and CEO, Ford of Europe.
