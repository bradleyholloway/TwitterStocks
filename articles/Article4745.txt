 If Prizes Were Awarded for Rudeness, Members of the Media Would Often Win --- Bu Frank Allen Staff Reporter of The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 12, 1987  On the day the shuttle was launched, Concord was brimming with newspeople. Mrs. McAuliffe was scheduled to speak from space with students so reporters and TV crews jammed the high-school auditorium. When the shuttle blew up, "It was difficult for a journalist to know what to do," recalls Michael Pride, editor of the Concord Monitor. "For the most part, the story was in people's houses, but their doors were closed. St. Peter's Church {where an impromptu Mass was being said that first night} was the only place where anything was happening."
