 Music sales jump into the groove after downbeat '91   By Cox, Meg, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 12, 1993  According to the Recording Industry Association of America, overall music sales climbed 15% in 1992 to $9 billion, marking the first year in which CDs outsold audio cassettes in the US. Unit sales were up 11.8%, following a dismal 1991 in which unit sales dropped 7.5%.
