 Wyman-Gordon Files Anti-Dumping Case With Two Agencies   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 10, 1986  WORCESTER, Mass. -- Wyman-Gordon Co. said it filed an anti-dumping petition with the U.S. Commerce Department and the U.S. International Trade Commission charging that imports of forged crankshafts are being sold below fair market price.  The maker of diesel-engine crankshafts and other metal parts alleges that six companies in Japan, West Germany, Brazil and the United Kingdom have used illegal pricing practices, resulting in the rapid expansion of imports.
