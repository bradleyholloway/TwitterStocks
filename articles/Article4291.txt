 MarketWatch: Go Green Before Credits Expire   By Sjolin, Sara, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 14, 2011  People who install biomass stoves, water heaters, central air conditioning and electric heat pumps are limited to a $300 tax break, which includes installation costs. New windows can save homeowners up to $500 on energy costs a year while a new water heater will pay itself off in five years -- not counting the tax breaks -- according to calculations by the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, Energy Star and home-improvement retailer Home Depot.
