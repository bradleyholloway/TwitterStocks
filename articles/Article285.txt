Madeleine K. Albright, the United States representative to the United Nations, told Barnard College graduates yesterday that they are entering an unsettled age in which the United States is moving toward political isolation while civil wars, human rights abuses and economic disasters ravage other nations.  "There is a trend toward isolationism that is running stronger in America than at any time since the period between the two world wars," Ms. Albright said in a keynote address to the 508 graduates of the women's liberal arts college at Columbia University.
