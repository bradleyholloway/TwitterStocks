 Lab notes: 'Natural' interferon joins the AIDS battle   By Waldolz, Michael, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 1993  The anticipated uses of interferon, one of the many substances produced by white blood cells as part of the body's natural immune system, in the treatment of AIDS is discussed. In some laboratory and human studies, it was found that alpha interferon can block replication of HIV, although further research was limited by the facts that interferon must be injected and high doses cause debilitating flu-like side effects.
