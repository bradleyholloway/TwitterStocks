 Japanese Wonder if the Country Has Outgrown Its Institutions in Light of Scandals, War Role   By Chipello, Christopher J, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 9, 1991  Discontent over Japan's role in the Persian Gulf War and the recent scandals involving the nation's financial establishment have made some Japanese question whether the world economic power has outgrown some of its institutions and practices founded when Japan was a small island dependent on the US.
