 Copper futures fall as market tries to regroup   By Vogelstein, Fred, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 1996  After three days of gains, copper prices fell again on Jun 20, 1996, as market participants continued working to stabilize their portfolios in the wake of the Sumitomo Corp trading scandal and the 26% plunge in futures prices this spring. The July copper-futures contract fell 2.4 cents to 94.80 cents a pound on the COMEX. On other markets, corn prices rose.
