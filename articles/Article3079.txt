 U.S. Tracks Cyanide Smuggling   By By David S. Cloud, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 13, 2002  U.S. intelligence agencies have received new reports that Islamic militants in northern Iraq have acquired chemical agents for possible use against U.S. targets, but intelligence officials say the reports are unconfirmed.  A CIA spokesman wouldn't comment on the report, and it couldn't be learned whether the agency had interrogated the prisoner or confirmed he was carrying cyanide. Some officials in the Bush administration consider such reports strong evidence that Iraqi leader Saddam Hussein is forging links with Islamic militants close to al Qaeda and that the Iraqi leader may want to use them as proxies to strike the U.S.
