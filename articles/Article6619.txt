 WEEKEND JOURNAL; The Home Front -- details: Old Wine, New Bottle   By Gunn, Eileen P, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 2006  In general, experts don't favor one shape over another. As long as the decanter gives the wine room to breathe and has a neck that will catch sediment, it will serve its purpose. But Mr. [Marc Lazar] cautions that these shapes can make the decanters tricky to clean: Rinse them poorly and your pricey cabernet could wind up tasting like soap. After a party, he puts his decanter into the dishwasher on a gentle cycle. Or, he says, to avoid using detergent at all, "I just rinse it out right away, so it doesn't get crusty."
