Eastern  A pesky upper-level storm system continues to churn over the Northeast this afternoon. This system is producing showers from central Pennsylvania northward through New York into Vermont. Temperatures are being held in the 50s and 60s in those areas experiencing the shower activity this afternoon. Locations closer to the coast will continue to rise through the 70s with some sunshine. The weather is relatively tranquil across the rest of the East, the Midwest, Plains and the central and southern Rockies with the exception of a cluster of thunderstorms crossing Mississippi and Louisiana. A second storm system remains along the Oregon coast this afternoon. Showers associated with this system are wetting parts of northern California, Washington, Oregon, Idaho and western Montana. Gusty winds in excess of 30 mph are buffeting much of Wyoming and Utah.
