Eastern  A storm system over Missouri is producing a swath of rain from the Midwest through the Ohio Valley into parts of Pennsylvania this afternoon. A frontal boundary stretching eastward from the storm system in Missouri separates cool air to the north from unseasonably warm air to the south. Currently, temperatures are in the 50s and 60s north of the front with some 70s across southern Virginia. South of the front, temperatures are well into the 80s and low 90s across the Southeast. The only other wet weather in the country is located in parts of Montana and the Dakotas where a couple of showers are occurring. The cold front responsible for these showers is producing clouds as far south as northern Utah and Colorado. High pressure is responsible for a good deal of sunshine across the Southwest. Meanwhile, some clouds are dotting the sky in the Northwest.  WEATHER ALMANAC 1982 Alaska — South Coast...greater than 80-mph katabatic winds this afternoon.
