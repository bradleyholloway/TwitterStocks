 Britain Cuts Key Interest Rate by a Half Point --- Stocks Fall on Fear Of Slower Growth   By By Sara Calian, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 11, 1998  Citing a global economic slowdown and the risk of deflation, the Bank of England cut a key interest rate by a greater-than-expected half-percentage point to 6.25%.  The drop in the base rate follows last week's coordinated interest-rate cuts in 11 continental European countries. British stocks fell on the news as investors began to worry that the rate cut, the third decrease in as many months, signals that the United Kingdom's slowing economy could need an even greater boost in the next few months.
