During the recent United States Atlantic Tuna Tournament, an annual competition almost half a century old, GAYLEE ROBINSON hooked into a giant blue fin about 20 miles off Portsmouth, N.H.  She brought it to gaff in a remarkably swift 25 minutes and a later dockside weighing determined that the tuna, at 442 pounds, was 317 pounds heavier than the rod-and-reel operator.
