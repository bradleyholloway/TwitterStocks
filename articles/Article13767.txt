 MarketWatch: Employers Lowballing New Hires   By Mantell, Ruth, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 6, 2010  The job market may be recovering, but some salary offers are still a few years behind. Since the labor market began picking up steam, companies hiring for entry-level or administrative spots with pay that would normally range from $40,000 to $50,000 have been offering workers $28,000 to $38,000, says Randy Miller, founder and chief executive of ReadyMinds, a Lyndhurst, N.J., provider of online career counseling and coaching.
