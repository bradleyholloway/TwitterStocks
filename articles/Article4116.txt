 International Finance: Ireland Cuts Its Payments For Lenders' Bad Loans   By Fottrell, Quentin, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 2010  The chief executive of Ireland's National Asset Management Agency said the agency will pay 43 billion euros ($58.41 billion) of the total book value of loans, not the previously estimated 54 billion euros, based on the discounts on the first tranche of loans.
