 Forceful Educator Gets Teachers and Children to Be More Productive   By Putka, Gary, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 1991  Prince George's County MD School superintendent John Murphy began a policy of making school administrators accountable for students' achievement scores when he took office in 1984 and his efforts have paid off. Murphy calls his philosophy "applied anxiety," and his methods of administration have much in common with the ideas that Education Secretary Lamar Alexander wants to apply nationally. The tradeoffs for aspects of the reform policy, such as an emphasis on test-taking, are examined.
