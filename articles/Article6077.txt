 Plan to Split Up, Sell Hynix Gets Board's Backing   By By Jay Solomon, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 2002  Hynix's creditors also said they are still examining a plan to remove Hynix's board at a shareholders meeting June 25. These creditors, mostly state-controlled South Korean banks, hold three trillion won ($2.34 billion) of Hynix's bonds and are preparing to convert them into a controlling stake of roughly 70% in the company on June 1. The creditors' control of Hynix's management will allow them to dictate the terms and pace of the company's asset sales.
