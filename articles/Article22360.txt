 Congo's Groundbreaking Copper Deal With American Company Is Canceled   By By Robert Block, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 2, 1998  America Mineral Fields Inc., the Hope, Ark., mining firm that made headlines last year as the first international company to do business with Congolese President Laurent-Desire Kabila while he was still a rebel leader, said its groundbreaking copper deal in Congo has been canceled.  The surprise move, announced by the company late Wednesday but not confirmed by Congolese authorities in Kinshasa, could have far-reaching and disastrous consequences for the mineral-rich but cash-poor government of Congo.
