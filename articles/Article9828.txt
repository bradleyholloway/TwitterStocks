 Ask David Wessel   By Wessel, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 2005  A: No. If you were taking an economics class, you'd get points for raising a provocative question but a low grade for your proposed answer. For starters, let's adjust your economic-growth facts to remove the impact of inflation, or rising prices. Between 1940 and 1970, adjusted for inflation, the gross domestic product grew by 4.4% a year on average; between 1976 and 2004, it grew by 3.1%. Then consider this: Every heroin addict in the country drank mother's milk or formula as an infant, but does this mean that drinking milk as an infant has anything to do with addiction?
