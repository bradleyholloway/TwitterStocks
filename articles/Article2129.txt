 U.S. firms lobby against Clinton bid to end tax break for workers abroad   By Lachica, Eduardo, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 16, 1996  American companies are lobbying against a White House proposal to do away with a statute that exempts US citizens working abroad from paying taxes on the first $70,000 of earned income. Critics have called the proposal, part of a White House budget-balancing package, "completely counterproductive and a step backwards in terms of our foreign trade."
