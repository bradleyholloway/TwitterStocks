 Alienated army: Perot voters roam political landscape, backing varied causes   By Seib, Gerald F, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 12, 1994  As America approaches the fall 1994 congressional elections, millions of voters who supported Ross Perot in 1992 have found themselves without a political niche. Many of the 19 million Perot voters now roam the country like a kind of rogue army, its soldiers still angry and energized, but their movements beyond anyone's control.
