 The Morning After for Closed-End Funds; Widening Discounts Give Investors a Hangover After Last Year's Binge   By Salisbury, Ian, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 2008  Investors who bought the Eaton Vance fund at its inception, when the fund enjoyed a marketing push, would see a loss of 8.3% through Friday, despite the fact that the fund's assets have appreciated 7.2%, according to Lipper Inc. Of seven $1 billion-or-larger closed-end funds that hit the market in 2007, all but one, the $3.5 billion Alpine fund, are now trading at discounts of more than 5%, according to ETFConnect.com.
