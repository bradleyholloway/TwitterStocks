 Bonds End Little Changed as Participants Await This Week's Data on Inflation and the Economy   By Donovan, Kevin; King, Sharon R, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 12, 1991  With no fresh economic news on which to focus, bond prices meandered throughout the day on Jun 11, 1991 and ended little changed. The Treasury's benchmark 30-year bond ended unchanged at a price of 96 3/32 to yield 8.47%. The latest two-year notes ended slightly lower at 99 18/32 to yield 6.97%. The discount rate on three-month Treasury bills was 5.57%, while six-month bills were at 5.79%, compared to 5.60% and 5.78%, respectively.
