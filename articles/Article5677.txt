 Recycling soldiers: Poor lands try to cope with residue of war: Their leftover armies   By Carrington, Tim, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 1994  Many cash-strapped, formerly war-torn countries are now ready to turn from militarism to repairing their broken economies. Such countries as Namibia, Chad and Zimbabwe have reduced their armies. However, underdeveloped countries must cope with the fact that though their sizable armies were sapping their economies, their private sectors cannot absorb the cost of retiring soldiers.
