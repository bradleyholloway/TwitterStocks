 Bond Prices Gain; Stocks Are Mixed --- Slide in Industrials Is Moderating After 4 Days, Traders Say   By By Douglas R. Sease, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 1992  But other market observers report some ominous signs. Charles Biderman, editor of a market newsletter that tracks cash levels, cautions that the flow of cash out of bank certificates of deposit and into stocks is slowing. From a peak of $8.05 billion flooding out of bank CDs weekly in January, "the rate of run-off seems to have slowed to a mere torrent, averaging $6.2 billion per week in the three weeks ended Feb. 24," he said, "and at some point the rate will slow down to a trickle."
