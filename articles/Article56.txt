Announcement has been made by Mr. and Mrs. Frederick Bailey Dent of Spartanburg, S.C., of the engagement of their daughter, Diana Gwynn Dent, to Theodore Alexander McGraw Jr., son of Mr. and Mrs. McGraw of Grosse Pointe Farms, Mich.  The wedding is planned for June 6.  The prospective bride, a decorator with Parish-Hadley Inc. in New York, was graduated from the Purnell School and with the class of '77 from the University of Kentucky. Her father, who is president of Mayfair Mills, served as Secretary of Commerce under Presidents Nixon and Ford and as the latter's Special Representative for Trade Negotiations.  Miss Dent is a granddaughter of Mrs. Magruder Dent of Greenwich, Conn.; the late Mr. Dent; Mrs. James L. Harrison of New York and Bedford Hills, N.Y., and the late Mr. Harrison, who was a vice president of the First National City Bank, now Citibank. Her paternal grandfather was president of Joshua Bailey & Company, cotton textile merchants, and chairman of Mayfair Mills.  Mr. McGraw, who was graduated from St. George's School and Yale, is with Johnson & Higgins, insurance brokers in New York. He is a grandson of Mrs. Robert Stoepel of Grosse Pointe, the late Mr. Stoepel; the late Dr. Theodore A. McGraw of Grosse Pointe, a surgeon, and the late Esther Longyear McGraw Murphy. He is a greatgreat-grandson of Henry Uihlein of Milwaukee, president of the Jos. Schlitz Brewing Company from 1879 to 1916.  Illustrations: Photo of Diana Dent
