 A Cultural Conversation / With Jack DeJohnette: Playing Musical Chairs   By Blumenfeld, Larry, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 2012  On Sunday night at Manhattan's Blue Note, leading an expanded version of his working band, he stayed behind his drum set, the primary seat from which he has earned the renown that accounts for both Sunday's packed house and his designation, which will be made official Tuesday night at Jazz at Lincoln Center's Rose Hall, as a 2012 National Endowment for the Arts Jazz Master. "[...] I thought, 'OK, I guess drums are my main instrument.'" Mr. DeJohnette moved to Manhattan's Lower East Side.
