 REVIEW --- A Ride in Dad's Traveling Think Tank --- A generation ago, a man's car was his domain and his kids a captive audience; Who's calling the shots now?   By Zevin, Dan, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2012  To the untrained eye, my father was just another suburban gynecologist driving the kids to his CPA, his lawyer's office or any number of other adult-oriented locales that left no doubt who was at the wheel (him) and who was along for the ride (us). In the rear, you will find their collapsible third row of seats, which functions, depending on the day, either as a sporting-goods superstore for their bikes/bats/balls or as additional seating for their friends and friends of friends.
