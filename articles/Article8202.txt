 Smaller-Than-Expected Rise in U.S. Hog Count Sends Pork Futures Climbing in Active Trading   By Taylor, Jeffrey, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 7, 1992  Pork futures prices rose on Jan 6, 1992 in active trading on a government report that briefly pushed prices up the daily limit but couldn't sustain a full-day rally. The February futures contract for pork bellies settled at $.3575 a pound, while hog futures for February settled up $.0055 a pound at $.3950. In other commodity market news, precious metals prices were mostly lower, coffee fell and energy prices were mixed.
