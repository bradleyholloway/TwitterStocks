Milan C. Kerno, an international investment banker who was the first non-African to become a top official of the African Development Bank, died on July 11 in Abidjan, the Ivory Coast. Mr. Kerno, who lived in London, died of heart failure, his family said. He was 67.  Mr. Kerno joined the bank in 1986 after serving as chairman of Merrill Lynch's international banking group in London and as vice chairman and managing director of Dean Witter Reynolds's London branch.
