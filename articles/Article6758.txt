 Ahead of the Tape   By Eisinger, Jesse, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 12, 2003  Of course, there were the hats. For several years they have been properly viewed as artifacts of a moment we are all deeply embarrassed about, like that Polaroid from the college frat party. But now, acting like Will Ferrell's character in the movie "Old School," investors again are doffing the Dow 10000 hats proudly.  The Dow is made up of only 30 companies, and is calculated with a technique that uses closing prices, not market weightings. It's true that the average person on the street, when asking about the market, is asking about the Dow. But the Dow doesn't tell all about the market, or the economy.
