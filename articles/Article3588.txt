 Ahead of the Tape   By Lahart, Justin, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2006  Proof is starting to accumulate. So far in July, the Dow Jones U.S. retailing-stocks index is down 4.4% and shares of big retailers like Wal-Mart Stores, Home Depot and Target are down for the year. Wal-Mart has reported sales at stores open more than a year were up 1.2% in June versus a year earlier, at the nether end of its earlier forecast of 1% to 3%. Home-furnishings retailer Williams-Sonoma said this week it would reshuffle its executive suite and lowered its sales outlook. Gap, the clothing retailer, said the head of its Old Navy division was stepping down.
