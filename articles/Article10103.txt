 Ask Personal Journal   By Herman, Tom, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 2007  A: Running a daily explanation of how the DJIA is calculated sounds like a great way to put readers to sleep. But here are the basics: When Charles Dow created his industrial average in 1896, he simply added up the prices of 12 stocks at the time and divided by 12. Today, the Dow has 30 stocks selected by the editors of The Wall Street Journal, which is published by Dow Jones.
