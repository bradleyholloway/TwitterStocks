 Investor's Calendar   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 2005  Briefly: The Supreme Court overturned the 2002 criminal conviction that doomed Arthur Andersen. The ruling won't revive the accounting firm, but it could hamper the government crackdown on white-collar crime . . . The U.S. filed a World Trade Organization case against European subsidies to Airbus, and European officials immediately filed a counterclaim against U.S. aid to Boeing in what could be the most expensive trade dispute in history . . . A Dallas Fed official suggested that the campaign to raise interest rates may be nearing an end, saying that after eight rate increases, the next meeting would be the "ninth inning." His remarks boosted the stock market and helped to send Treasury bond yields sharply lower.
