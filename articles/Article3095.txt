 Canada's Red Cross Gets Protection From Bankruptcy   By By John Urquhart, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 21, 1998  Canada's financially troubled Red Cross has been granted bankruptcy protection pending a restructuring of its operations that will include the sale of its blood-supply activities.  The not-for-profit humanitarian organization, which was established in Canada in 1896, is being sued for more than five billion Canadian dollars (US$3.36 billion) by victims of contaminated blood collected and distributed by the Red Cross.
