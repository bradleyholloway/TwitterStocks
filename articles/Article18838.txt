 Seats of power: Reforms in Russia depend on outcome of governors' races   By Banerjee, Neela, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 1996  In the wake of President Boris Yeltsin's re-election victory in Jul 1996, the fight over market reform is raging anew in Russia, as voters go to the polls to elect governors in 52 of Russia's 89 regions. The first elections were held Sep 1 in Saratov, where final results show that the Yeltsin-backed incumbent, Dmitry Ayatskov, trounced his Communist challenger. The threat the governors present to market reform is analyzed.
