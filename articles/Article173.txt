 New Securities Issues [6]  The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 25, 1993  CIT Group Holdings -- $200 million of floating-rate, mediumterm notes due Aug. 31, 1995, through underwriters led by UBS Securities Inc., according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable notes will pay quarterly and float daily at prime minus 245 basis points over Treasurys. The debt will be priced at par. The debt is rated single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp. CIT Group Holdings is jointly owned by Dai-Ichi Kangyo Bank Ltd. and Chemical Banking Corp.  Consolidated Natural Gas Co. -- $150 million of debentures due Aug. 1, 2003, priced in a competitive bid won by Salomon Brothers Inc. The debt was priced as 5.75s at 98.402 to yield 5.965%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable debentures were priced at a spread of 37 basis points above Treasurys. The issue is rated single-A-1 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P.  Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $100 million of PSA Index Bonds issued with a legal final maturity of Sept. 24, 1998, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The debt was priced at par to yield 4.53%. The actual maturity will be determined by the PSA speed of the Federal National Mortgage Asociation's 30-year, 7% mortgages. The expected maturity is based on prepayments at a speed of 100% to 250% of the PSA model. The expected average maturity is 2.5 years. The noncallable debt was priced at a spread of 55 basis points above two-year when-issued Treasurys. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by First Tennessee Bank.
