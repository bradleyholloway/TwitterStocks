 Starting Out: Checking Out Roommates   By Banjo, Shelly, The Wall Street Journal,  May 25, 2008  "A shocking fact of life for renters is that . . . the screw-ups of one roommate are laid at the door of everybody" who is also on the lease, says Janet Portman, managing editor at Nolo, a Berkeley, Calif., publisher of self-help legal information. Once you choose a roommate, you should both sign an agreement specifying the rent amount and due date, how to handle utility, cable and Internet-access bills and terms of potential departure.
