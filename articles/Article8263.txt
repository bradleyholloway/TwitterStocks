 Passing the Buck: In New Pension Plans, Companies Are Putting the Onus on Workers   By Schultz, Ellen E, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 7, 1992  A great number of companies now offer "self-directed" retirement plans that require employees to make decisions regarding their investments. The financial stakes in such a system are enormous, and while many persons take an active interest in selecting an option, most do not. In fact, many employees choose not to invest at all, a decision that financial analysts predict will have serious repercussions at retirement age.
