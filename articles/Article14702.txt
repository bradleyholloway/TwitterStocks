 South Korea Acts To Shore Up Stocks Amid Criticism   By By Joseph P. Manguno, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 1990  SEOUL, South Korea -- The government's latest steps to lift the weakening South Korean stock market are insufficient measures that are bound to fail, according to brokers, analysts and investors.  Among the latest steps, the government pledged to provide funds to trust companies to enable them to buy more shares; to name new institutional investors in a bid to raise the demand base for shares; to increase the ratio of Korean stock owned by institutional investors; and to remove certain restrictions on buying shares in securities companies.
