 World News: U.S.'s Arab Allies Keep Cautious Watch on Iran   By Coker, Margaret, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 2010  Martin Indyk, director of foreign policy at the Brookings Institution, the Washington-based think tank, says Saudi Arabia could persuade China -- which is a big trading partner with Iran and so far has indicated little support for further sanctions -- that a nuclear-powered Iran is bad for the stability of global oil exports and thus Beijing's oil-dependent economy.
