 GATT, in boost to embattled Clinton, details gains likely from trade pact   By Bahree, Bhushan, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 11, 1994  With a canny sense of timing, GATT headquarters announced on Nov 10, 1994 a study that shows the US can expect to rack up some $122 billion annually in net income gains by 2005 if only Congress would ratify the new world trade pact promptly.
