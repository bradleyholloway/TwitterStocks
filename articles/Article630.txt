 Panel Says Holders Of Lilco Must Share Costs of Shoreham   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 14, 1985  ALBANY, N.Y. -- State regulators, in a preliminary vote, decided that Long Island Lighting Co. shareholders must absorb at least $1.35 billion of the Shoreham nuclear plant's $4.2 billion cost because of company mismanagement.  The commission is conducting a similar investigation into the $5.35 billion Nine Mile Point Unit 2 plant being constructed by five New York utilities: Niagara Mohawk Power Corp., which owns 41%; Lilco, 18%; New York State Electric & Gas Corp., 18%; Rochester Gas & Electric Corp., 14%; and Central Hudson Gas & Electric Corp., 9%.
