 Stormy spring: Three-month tumult in bonds lays bare new financial forces   By Wessel, David; Jereski, Laura; Smith, Randall, The Wall Street Journal,  May 20, 1994  An article examines the events which led to the fastest fall in US bond prices in more than a decade in early-1994. In May 1994, with the market finally taking a respite, it appears that the global chain reaction was caused by a confluence of factors, that included the Fed's boost of short-term rates, a strengthening economy, use of leverage by bond traders and the collapse of trade talks with Japan. Then, once the decline started, it was accelarated by a the rush for liquidity by fund managers.
