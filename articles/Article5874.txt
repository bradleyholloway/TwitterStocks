 Heat and demand blamed for outage in Western states   The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 12, 1996  Soaring temperatures, sagging power lines and unusually high demand for electricity shared the blame for an outage that cut power to millions of customers across the West, one of the most severe in US history. Nine states from Oregon to southern California, as well as parts of Mexico, had to deal with outages that began Aug 10, 1996. The problems were not resolved until early Aug 11.
