 EU's Central Banker Stays Mum on Euro Intervention   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 20, 2000  Wim Duisenberg, president of the European Central Bank, said at a closely watched news conference that he would heed the advice of colleagues and critics and stop answering questions about intervention in the currency markets. Earlier this week, Mr. Duisenberg's comments to a British newspaper led markets to believe that intervention was unlikely, contributing to the euro's decline.
