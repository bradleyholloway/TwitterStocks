 Taste: Tony & Tacky   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 20, 2001  Touch and See: Up to now, blind people using computers have had two limited choices: reading Braille one line at a time or listening to a computerized voice. But thanks to an innovative new mouse built by the Israeli company VirTouch, blind people can now draw, read regular writing, "see" pictures and even play tactile computer games, according to CNSNews.com.
