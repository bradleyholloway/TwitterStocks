 Big Retail Chains' Sales Are Continuing at a Sluggish Pace despite Easter Boost   By Agins, Teri, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 12, 1991  Retail sales for the five-week period ended Apr 6, 1991 grew an average of about 2.7% from a year earlier, weighed down by sharp declines at J. C. Penney & Co and Sears, Roebuck & Co. Discounters, led by Wal-Mart Stores Inc and Kmart Corp, generally outperformed the department stores.
