 Growth in Global Oil Demand Is Expected to Ease   By Bhushan Bahree, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2005  The IEA and many other prominent forecasters have underestimated oil demand in the past two years, as fast economic growth, especially in China, led to the largest surge in energy use in a generation. It still isn't clear whether the IEA has a firm handle on the outlook for China's energy appetite, which is difficult to gauge because of the scarcity of reliable data and the country's quirky energy-consumption patterns, which are skewed by price controls and other variables.
