 Disaster-aid bill, replete with riders, pits Republicans against White House   By Rogers, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 4, 1997  Republicans are on a collision course with the White House over a disaster-relief bill that has become caught in a power struggle over tax cuts in the summer of 1997 and the census in the year 2000. Despite repeated veto warnings, GOP leaders said Congress will send the $8.4 billion aid bill to President Clinton with two conservative-backed riders that withhold new funds for domestic programs and deny money to test sampling methods for the census.
