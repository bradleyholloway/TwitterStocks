Grant SmartMoney.com Single Ladies, High Earners The earning power of young single women has surpassed that of their male peers in metropolitan areas around the U.S., a shift driven by the growing ranks of women who attend college and move on to high-earning jobs. In 2008, single, childless women between the ages of 22 and 30 earned more than their male counterparts in most U.S. cities, with incomes that were 8% greater on average, according to an analysis of Census Bureau data released last week by Reach Advisors, a consumer-research firm.
