 Ford Stirs Hope of Car U-Turn --- Surprise Profit Is Driven by Finance Unit, Market-Share Grab; Focus Turns to Toyota   By Dolan, Matthew; Bennett, Jeff, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 2009  Ford's unexpected profit -- the result of renewed strength at its credit arm, vastly improved North American operations and the weakness of its Detroit rivals -- suggests the company's efforts to slash costs and improve its vehicle lineup are beginning to have an impact on the bottom line. Ford, the only one of Detroit's Big Three auto makers that didn't require a U.S. government bailout, reported net income of $997 million, or 29 cents a share, compared with a year-earlier loss of $161 million, or seven cents a share.
