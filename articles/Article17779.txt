 Taiwan's Investors, Angered by Plunge, Take to the Streets --- Government Aides Are Held Responsible for Losses As Market Declines 39%   By By Julia Leung, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 4, 1987  About 200 of them protested outside the lawmaking Legislative Yuan last week against the government's failure to stem the stock-market decline. Others mobbed the Securities and Exchange Commission and laid siege to Finance Minister Robert Chien's home.  A monthlong slide in the stock market, exacerbated by the global drop that began Oct. 19, has lopped 39% off the peak value of the weighted index. A speculative buying spree had pushed the stock index up 350% by Oct. 1 from the beginning of the year.
