 Bank of Boston Unit to Shun Dealer Status --- Plan is Abandoned to Seek Fed Sanction to Trade Government Securities   By By David Wessel, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 1985  As a result, the Bank of Boston Corp. unit is withdrawing an application to become one of the so-called primary, or recognized, dealers with whom the Federal Reserve Bank of New York trades directly, said Peter C. Read, group executive, treasury and investment banking.  The bank expected its application to be approved and hadn't any indication that the Fed might reject it, Mr. Read said. Bank of Boston recently received widespread publicity after pleading guilty to failure to report cash transactions with foreign banks and paying a $500,000 fine.
