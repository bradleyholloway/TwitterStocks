 Japanese insurers inadvertently fuel yen's rise with record cash hoard   By Hardy, Quentin, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 5, 1994  With the yen at record strength against the dollar, Japanese insurance companies might be expected to buy more dollar-denominated securities. However, they are sitting on a 10 trillion yen ($101.5 billion) hoard of cash worried that the dollar might weaken further, and helping it to do just that. In London on Jul 4, 1994, the US unit was quoted at Y98.90 and 1.5970 marks, compared to Y98.70 and DM1.5965 late in New York on Jul 1.
