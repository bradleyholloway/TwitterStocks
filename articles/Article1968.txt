 Business and Finance [7]  The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 13, 1995  THE U.S., pledging to "do what is necessary to restore confidence" in Mexico's battered economy, plans to expand its financial support for the nation, including loan guarantees that could amount to as much as $40 billion. News of the plan, which goes far beyond the existing short-term $18 billion multinational credit line, shored up the peso and sparked a recovery in Mexican stocks and debt securities.
