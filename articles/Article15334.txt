 Binge buyers: Many baby boomers save little, may run into trouble later on   By Wysocki, Bernard Jr, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 5, 1995  While New Englanders have traditionally been known for their frugal ways, baby boomers there and elsewhere in the US are continuing to spend heavily and are sunk knee-deep in debt. Despite layoffs and hard times in the early 1990s, the boomers haven't given up on their big, materialistic dreams and will likely not have enough retirement savings to maintain their lifestyles after they leave the work force.
