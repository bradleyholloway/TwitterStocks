 The Economy: Bush Increases Push to Abolish Dividend Taxes   By By John D. McKinnon and Jeanne Cummings, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 3, 2003  The White House hopes that Wall Street and senior citizens will help nudge members of Congress to approve a tax-cut number that would accommodate most or all of the administration's dividend plan. The House has aligned with the administration, narrowly passing a budget resolution that makes room for the president's entire $726 billion package. But the Senate voted by the slimmest of margins last week to limit its tax cut to $350 billion -- an amount that would leave practically no room for Mr. [Bush]'s dividend break, which by itself costs about $395 billion.
