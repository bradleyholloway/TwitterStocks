 Deft maneuvers: The way a bureaucracy beat back reform says volumes about Japan   By Sapsford, Jathon, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 2, 1996  The Japanese Ministry of Finance became the target of reform-minded politicians in Jan 1996, and they had planned on dismantling the powerful agency. However, the MOF has provided a study in the self-preservation skills of Japan's bureaucracies and has avoided a breakup. The battle over the MOF has only yielded one major change: The Bank of Japan, the nation's central bank, may be given greater autonomy in 1997, thus giving the institution greater credibility and stronger defenses against outside meddling in determining policy.
