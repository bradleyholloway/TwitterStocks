 Proposal to Treat Options as Costs Will Be Laid Out   By By Cassell Bryan-Low and Silvia Ascarelli, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 2002  Many U.S. companies already have voiced their opposition to what international rule makers are proposing, arguing that stock options, while crucial to hiring and retaining talent, don't constitute an expense. "For semiconductor makers, any move that would potentially harm our ability to grant options would severely harm our competitive position," the Semiconductor Industry Association said in a letter to U.S. rule makers. Such companies support as a compromise more detailed disclosure of information about stock-option plans.
