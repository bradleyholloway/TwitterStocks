 Bank-Failure Rate Has Slowed Sharply This Year, Chairman of the FDIC Says   By Duke, Paul Jr; Thomas, Paulette, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 1990  FDIC Chairman William Seidman said that bank failures have slowed considerably in 1990 and that no banks have received FDIC assistance in order to continue operations. The agency's bank deposit-insurance fund had a loss of about $800 million in 1989.
