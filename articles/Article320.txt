The New York City Ballet, in the home stretch of its winter season, continues with a spate of debuts and dancing that has obviously reached a new peak.  An all-new cast of principals in George Balanchine's ''Scotch Symphony'' on Saturday afternoon at the New York State Theater brought in Margaret Tracey as the modern Sylph pursued by Nikolaj Hubbe in the Scotland of Balanchine's inspiration.
