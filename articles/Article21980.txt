 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 3, 1994  Federal Home Loan Bank System -- $155 million of debentures due Nov. 8, 1996, priced at par to yield 6.995%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of six basis points above Treasurys. The issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co.  First Union Corp. -- $150 million of subordinated notes due Nov. 15, 2004, priced as 8.77s at 99.616 to yield 8.83%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The issue, which is noncallable for five years, was priced at a spread of 87 basis points above Treasurys. Rated single-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Ratings Group, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette Securities Corp.  Occidental Petroleum Corp. -- $150 million of medium-term notes due Nov. 9, 2001, priced as 8.5s at 99.4 to yield 8.616%, according to MCM CorporateWatch. The noncallable issue was priced at a spread of 83 basis points above Treasurys. Rated Baa-3 by Moody's and triple-B by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch & Co.
