Alice Woodward DeNormandie and Surendra Vaghji Shah, both of New York, were married yesterday at the home of the bride's parents, Mr. and Mrs. James De Normandie in Lincoln, Mass. The bridegroom is a son of Maniben V. Shah of Nairobi, Kenya, and the late Vaghji Pethraj Shah.  The ceremony was performed by the Rev. F. William McLennan, a Unitarian minister.  Mrs. Shah, who plans to join the New York office of the Omega Performance Corporation, bank consultants, on Aug. 3, was formerly an assistant vice president of the Bank of America in New York. A graduate of the Beaver Country Day School and Wilson College, she received a master's degree from the Simmons College Graduate School of Management. Her father, now retired, formerly was a Republican State Senator in Massachusetts.  Mr. Shah, an assistant vice president at the Chemical Bank in New York, was graduated from the Duke of Gloucester's School in Nairobi and received a B.S. degree in engineering from the University of Manchester in England and a master's degree in business administration from the University of Western Ontario in London, Ontario. His father was a retail merchant in Nairobi.
