 Morgan Stanley, badly bruised, revamps its operations, culture   By Peers, Alexandra, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 12, 1994  Once among the top five in the world of investment banking, Morgan Stanley Group Inc now stands at seventh place in Wall Street's closely watched underwriting rankings. Hoping to reverse its fortune, the company has launched an overhaul of how its operations work together and, more important, of its corporate culture. Among its reorganizing moves, Morgan Stanley has purchased a huge $176 million office building in New York City to combine Morgan operations scattered all over New York.
