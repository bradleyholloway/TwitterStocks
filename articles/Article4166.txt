 Volvo's Big China Gamble --- New Owner Plans to Open Three Plants in Effort to Boost Brand's Global Sales   By Shirouzu, Norihiko, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 14, 2010  Volvo doesn't have a good standing in China's crowded auto market, but the intense media coverage within China about Geely's acquisition of the brand has raised awareness of it among Chinese consumers. Volvos currently are made in Sweden and Belgium as well as in China through a joint venture with Ford, Mazda Motor Co. and Chonqing Changan Automobile Co. Earlier this month, a senior official at China's National Development and Reform Commission warned at an auto industry conference in Tianjin that China's industry may face excess capacity.
