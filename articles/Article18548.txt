 Ding-dong: Updating Avon means respecting history without repeating it   By Hwang, Suein L, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 4, 1994  When Avon Products Inc tried to extend its declining US customer base by instituting organizational changes, Avon's paternalistic corporate culture became divided. Some fault CEO James E. Preston, who saw Avon go through a series of takeover attempts, and the attempt to sell direct to customers, bypassing the traditional Avon lady in the process, for Avon's problems. Currently, the priority at Avon is to rebuild the relationship with sales representatives.
