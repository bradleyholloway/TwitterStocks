 Japan rolls out entries in PCS market meant to rival bigger cellular telephones   By Hamilton, David P, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 1995  While US companies are just now receiving licenses for the technology in the US, Japanese companies have rolled out their versions of small cellular telephones known as PCS, or personal communications services. Japanese companies have signed up tens of thousands of customers for the service known as Personal Handyman Service, or PHS. As evidence of the parade into the new business, DDI Corp, and a unit of Nippon Telegraph & Telephone Corp on Jul 1, 1995 separately launched handyphone services.
