Walker Evans wanted to be a writer, not a photographer. You can almost see the regret seeping into his photographs of signs and posters, window menus and tools embossed with the company name. These pictures of ordinary objects are, after all, also pictures of words.  But Evans the photographer is unrivaled in one kind of writing: list writing. This may sound like faint praise, but Evans's lists -- of his likes and dislikes, of his prospective subjects, of his negatives, of the things he needed and the things he saw -- are powerful bits of vertical writing.
