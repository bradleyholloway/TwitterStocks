 Farmers Face Empty-Nest Syndrome Amid Chicken Housing Crisis --- Poultry Growers Lose Contracts, Can't Pay Off Their Pricey Coops; $200,000 Per House   By Etter, Lauren, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 2009  In the wake of last year's bankruptcy filing by poultry giant Pilgrim's Pride Corp., hundreds of farmers suddenly find themselves unable to make mortgage payments on their pricey chicken coops. Poultry companies typically cut back by reducing the number of chicks given to each farmer, but Pilgrim's finances were so dire that it decided to terminate some contracts, says Mr. Jackson, the president and CEO.
