 Redundancy Is Good For National Security   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 21, 2005  Unfortunately, that is exactly what could happen if we discard "strategic redundancy," a tenet of military doctrine we have lived by for the past 60 years. Reflecting the lessons of Pearl Harbor, the doctrine disperses high-value assets at different locations to prevent their destruction in a single attack. In its March 2005 National Defense Strategy the Department of Defense reiterated its goal of "developing greater flexibility to contend with uncertainty by emphasizing agility and by not overly concentrating military forces in a few locations." But the Pentagon's recent BRAC recommendations do the opposite -- consolidating some of the nation's most valuable air and naval platforms at single installations.
