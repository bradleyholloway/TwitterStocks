 Deals & Deal Makers -- IPO Outlook: Five IPOs on Tap Supply Hope After Flood of Withdrawn Offers   By By Raymond Hennessey, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 2002  Then came a string of formal withdrawal filings Thursday with the Securities and Exchange Commission. Within the space of an hour Thursday afternoon, three companies withdrew their offerings. All told, those companies -- drug makers Corcept Therapeutics Inc. and Protarga Inc. and energy company Power Measurement Inc. -- hoped to raise $258 million. Two other companies also withdrew prospective deals that day, making it the busiest period for IPO withdrawals since six companies bowed out on March 9, 2001, according to data from Dealogic LLC in New York.
