WASHINGTON, April 9â€” Ellis M. Haller, an assistant managing editor of U.S. News & World Report, died Tuesday night of a heart attack in Orlando, Fla. He was 66 years old.  Mr. Haller, a resident of Bethesda, Md., spent 42 years in journalism. He was a graduate of Syracuse University. He was a native of Carthage, N.Y.  While still in college, he worked in upstate New York for The Syracuse Journal-American and The Watertown Daily Times. He joined The Wall Street Journal in 1937 in New York, and spent 18 years on the newspaper. In 1956 he was hired by U.S. News & World Report as a business editor-writer.  Mr. Haller, who was a resident of Bethesda, Md., a Washington suburb, is survived by his wife, Dorothy; a son, Mark, and a daughter, Elizabeth Summers, all of Washington.
