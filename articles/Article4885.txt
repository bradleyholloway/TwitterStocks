 Advertising: Firms Grow Choosier about Film Tie-Ins   By King, Thomas R, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 10, 1992  After a wave of movie-marketing corporate-tie-ins swept over the industry a few years ago, advertisers have cooled a little toward the concept, either turning away or driving tougher and smarter bargains with Hollywood studios. Some of the deals worked for 1992 summer films by such companies as McDonalds Corp, Coca-Cola Co, Choice Hotels International Inc, Burger King and Walt Disney Co are detailed.
