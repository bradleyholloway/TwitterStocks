 Petroleum Futures Rise as Algerian Oil Minister Suggests an Early OPEC Meeting to Spur Prices   By Sullivan, Allanna, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 1992  Oil prices rose on Mar 3, 1992, driven by worry over unrest in Venezuela and speculation that OPEC ministers might meet again earlier than anticipated to spur prices. The April delivery of crude oil climbed by $.30 a barrel to $18.64. Heating oil and gasoline prices also rose. In other market news, grains and soybeans futures fell and coffee and sugar prices rose.
