 Fairfax Makes Bid For TV Stations Owned by Herald   By By Tony Patrick, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 5, 1987  SYDNEY, Australia -- John Fairfax Ltd., attempting to establish a second television network in Australia, offered the equivalent of $239.8 million for the television interests of Herald & Weekly Times Ltd.  Fairfax's offer is the second it has made to Herald, which is Australia's biggest media company, with interests in newspapers, magazines, television and radio. Fairfax earlier offered $1.67 billion for all of Herald, in competition with Rupert Murdoch's News Corp., which is offering $1.53 billion. News Corp. has made provisional agreements to sell Herald's television stations if its bid succeeds.
