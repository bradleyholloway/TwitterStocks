 SmartMoney Stock Screen / Takeover Bait   By Hough, Jack, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 10, 2005  The centerpiece of this screen is the EV/Ebitda ratio, a valuation measure frequently used by merger-and-acquisition analysts. The "EV" stands for enterprise value, which is a company's market capitalization, plus its debt, minus its cash. It's the total amount someone would have to pay to buy a company in full and pay off everything that it owes using what's in the till.
