 Studying How Body Rhythms Help Diagnose Diseases   By Wang, Shirley S, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 2012  Frank Duffy, director of developmental neurobiology at Boston Children's Hospital, in June published a study online in BMC Medicine showing that kids with autism have distinguishable brain patterns. Dr. Duffy and colleagues measured electrical brain-wave activity in 430 children with characteristic symptoms of autism, such as social-skill deficits and communication problems, and in 540 other children without apparent autism.
