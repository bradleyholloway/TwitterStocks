 U.S. May Label as Classified Certain Biomedical Research   By By Marilyn Chase, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 10, 2003  Unusual scrutiny of people in biomedical circles already is taking place. Dr. [Ron Atlas] said he gave a list of the microbiology society's 42,000 members to the Federal Bureau of Investigation during its anthrax probe, but wouldn't supply other information without a subpoena. At Biosecurity 2002, the November meeting hosted by Harvard in Las Vegas, sponsors sought an Federal Bureau of Investigation agent's help in "surveillance" of the list of attendees, said Miles Shore, a professor at Harvard Medical School.
