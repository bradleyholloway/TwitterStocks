 Politics & Economics: As Doubts on Economy Grow, Stock Investors Stay Upbeat   By Harwood, John, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 7, 2007  Investors "don't appear to be particularly shaken," says Democratic pollster Peter Hart, who conducted the Journal/NBC survey with Republican counterpart Neil Newhouse. But lagging spirits about the economy overall, Mr. Hart added, show the broader public mood is "anything but great."  Last week's drop in the Dow Jones Industrial Average was linked to market declines in Asia. That followed remarks by former Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan signaling the possibility of a U.S. economic recession.  Now, the poll shows, just 16% expect the economy to strengthen while 31% expect it to weaken. About half of respondents say the economy will remain "about the same."
