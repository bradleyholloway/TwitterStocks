 Autos: Chrysler's man of many parts cuts costs   By Lavin, Douglas, The Wall Street Journal,  May 14, 1993  Thomas T. Stallkamp, the vice president of purchasing at Chrysler Corp, is profiled for his successful cost-cutting strategy at the big auto maker. Under Stallkamp, Chrysler abandoned unilateral price cuts and competitive bidding and has stopped writing detailed specifications for many parts. The automobile maker actually made money in 1992, something no other US auto maker accomplished.
