Remunda Cadoux, a teacher of French and Spanish in the New York City school system and the author of more than 20 textbooks on the two languages, died Saturday at her home in Manhattan. She was 71 years old.  Beginning in 1933, she served as a high school teacher and supervisor. She wrote textbooks for both high school and college. She also prepared for television a series of training films for teachers of French. She retired from teaching in New York in 1967.  Miss Cadoux was born in New York City, and was a graduate of Barnard College.  For her teaching efforts, Miss Cadoux was named an officer and Chevalier of L'Ordre des Palmes Academiques by the French Government in 1967.  She is survived by two brothers, Alexander Cadoux of Scarsdale, N.Y., and Edmond Cadoux of Weston, Conn.
