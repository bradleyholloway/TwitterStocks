 Coke's Columbia Unit to Dismiss 500 In Plan to Save $40 Million Annually   By By Laura Landro, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1987  Coca-Cola Co.'s Columbia Pictures Entertainment unit will dismiss as many as 500 of its 3,500 employees in a restructuring that is expected to cut overhead costs by $40 million annually, according to executives familiar with the plan.  The layoffs are part of the planned merger of Coke's Entertainment Sector, which includes Columbia Pictures, and Coke's one-third owned Tri-Star Pictures Inc. The new company, Columbia Pictures Entertainment Inc., will be headed by Tri-Star's chairman and chief executive officer, Victor Kaufman. Mr. Kaufman and other company executives said they couldn't comment before the completion of the transaction, expected later this month.
