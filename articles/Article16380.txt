 Dollar's Rally May End Soon, Barring Big Jobs-Data Increase   By Sesit, Michael R, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 5, 2004  Anyone hoping to capitalize on the U.S. dollar's recent rally may already have missed the chance -- unless there is a big positive surprise in today's U.S. employment numbers for February.  In addition, ECB President Jean-Claude Trichet's subsequent news conference and question-and-answer session "was not one suggesting a central bank on the brink of lowering interest rates," said Daragh Maher, a senior economist at ING Financial Markets in London. He said the ECB's failure to deliver a rate cut could undo the dollar's rally, especially if today's U.S. employment report was disappointing.
