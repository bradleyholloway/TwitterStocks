 Ashland Oil Enlists Crawford as Adjuster For Oil Spills Claims   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 14, 1988  Ashland Oil Inc. said it enlisted Crawford & Co., a national insurance claims adjuster, to open an office here and serve those individuals and businesses affected by the Jan. 2 oil spill on the Monongahela River.  In addition, the Ashland, Ky.-based oil refiner said it would work closely with Pennsylvania officials to reimburse local municipalities for their spill-related expenses. Already, the company -- covered by a $400 million insurance policy, less a $2.5 million deductible -- has advanced $210,000 to the Allegheny County Commission to cover its costs from the accident.
