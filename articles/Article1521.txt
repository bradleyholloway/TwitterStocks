 Senate Votes to Cap Credit-Card Rates, Tying Charges to IRS's Interest Rate   By Bacon, Kenneth H, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 14, 1991  The Senate voted 74-19 to approve a measure that would set a ceiling on credit-card interest rates of four percentage points above the rate the IRS charges on the underpayment of taxes. Based on the current IRS rate, credit cards could charge 14%, down from an average of 18.94%.
