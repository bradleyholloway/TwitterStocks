 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 9, 2001  CSX Corp. -- $500 million of notes was priced through lead managers Goldman Sachs & Co. and Salomon Smith Barney Inc., according to people familiar with the deal. Terms: maturity: March 15, 2011; coupon: 6.75%; issue price: 99.719; yield: 6.789%; spread: 190 basis points above Treasurys; call: make whole call at Treasurys plus 20 basis points; ratings: Baa2 (Moody's), triple-B (S&P).  Masco Corp. -- $800 million of notes was priced through joint lead managers Merrill Lynch & Co. and Salomon Smith Barney, according to people familiar with the deal. Terms: maturity: March 15, 2006; coupon: 6.75%; issue price: 99.799; yield: 6.798%; spread: 212.5 basis points above Treasurys; call: make whole call at Treasurys plus 25 basis points; ratings: Baa1 (Moody's), triple-B-plus (S&P).  Wells Fargo & Co. -- $750 million of notes was priced in a reopening through joint lead managers Lehman Brothers Inc. and J.P. Morgan Chase, according to people familiar with the deal. Terms: maturity: Feb. 1, 2011; coupon: 6.45%; issue price: 101.931; yield: 6.185%; spread: 130 basis points above Treasurys; call: noncallable; ratings: Aa2 (Moody's), single-A-plus (S&P). The deal now totals $1.75 billion.
