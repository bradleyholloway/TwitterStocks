 In Brief   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 6, 2007  Advanced Micro Devices Inc. confirmed a delay in delivering one new chip but said a higher-profile product remains on schedule. Monday, computer maker Cray Inc. cited a supplier delay and forecast a shortfall in annual revenue. Yesterday, AMD spokesman Phil Hughes said AMD had informed Cray of a delay for its Budapest chip, for servers that use one chip, but he said its Barcelona chip, for servers that use two or four chips, will launch this summer, as expected. Some analysts say the Barcelona chip is essential for AMDs success.
