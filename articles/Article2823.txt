 World-Wide   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 16, 2001  Last-gasp Mideast talks resume today after a 24-hour break called by Israel over the killing of a Jewish settler in the Gaza Strip by Palestinians. Arafat, meanwhile, responded to an international outcry over recent executions by offering an amnesty to Palestinians suspected of collaborating with Israel.  Yugoslavia's president has declined to meet with the top U.N. war-crimes prosecutor when she visits Belgrade next week. An ally says Kostunica believes the work of the tribunal in The Hague is "politically motivated."  Cambodia's Senate cleared a bill to set up a tribunal of international and local jurists to try Khmer Rouge leaders. Further approvals are needed, but Premier Hun Sen expects the tribunal to begin work this year.
