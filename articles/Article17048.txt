 Economic Woes Let Bears Back In --- Weak Indicators, Profits Crimp Market Momentum; 'Everything Is Not OK'   By Lauricella, Tom, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 5, 2009  By knocking down the Dow Jones Industrial Average almost 350 points since its recent peak, investors appear to be saying that improving but still weak economic numbers and better-than-expected but still weak profits aren't enough to push stocks higher. "People were thinking that companies did a great job of controlling costs and were looking for a decent economic recovery to filter into revenues and bolster profits," says Nick Kalivas, vice president of financial research at MF Global in Chicago.
