 New Securities Issues [4]  The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 7, 1990  Merrill Lynch & Co. -- $225 million of 9.60% spread-adjusted notes due May 15, 1995, priced at par. The notes are so-called because their yield spread against Treasury securities will be adjusted semiannually through an auction process. Rated single-A-2 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Merrill Lynch Capital Markets.  Japan Finance Corp. for Municipal Enterprises -- $150 million of 9 1/8% notes, due March 13, 2000, priced at 99.709 to yield 9.17%. The noncallable notes, guaranteed by the government of Japan, were priced at a spread of 52 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. Rated triple-A by both Moody's and S&P, the issue will be sold through First Boston Corp. Japan Finance Corp. for Municipal Enterprises provides low-cost funds for municipal and local projects.  New York State Medical Care Facilities Finance Agency -- $114.8 million of mental health services facility improvement revenue bonds, 1990 Series A, due Feb. 15 and Aug. 15, 1991-2002, 2010 and 2020, tentatively priced by a W.R. Lazard & Laidlaw Inc. group to yield from 6% in 1991 to 7.81% for bonds due Feb. 15, 2020. Current interest serial bonds are priced to yield from 6.00% in 1991 to 7.40% in 2002. There are $32.3 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at 99 3/4 to yield 7.77%, due Aug. 15, 2010, and $56.7 million of 7 3/4% term bonds priced at 99 1/4 to yield 7.81%, due Feb. 15, 2020. The bonds are rated single-A/single-A-plus.
