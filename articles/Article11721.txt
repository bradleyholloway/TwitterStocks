 Can Texaco win a battle if the Lord is on the other side?   By Richards, Bill, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 1993  The disagreement between Barry Davis, a devout Christian fundamentalist who runs three Texaco service stations in Oregon, and Texaco Corp over his desire to close the stations for the 24-hour biblical Sabbath illustrates the growing number of workplace conflicts involving religious beliefs.
