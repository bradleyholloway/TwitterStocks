 National Airlines Cuts Salaries by Up to 20%   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 5, 2002  The company also slashed employee compensation by as much as 20%, effective Sunday, as the carrier, barred from a federal bailout, seeks concessions from shareholders to stay in the air. National, which began operations in May 1999, has been operating under Chapter 11 bankruptcy-court protection since December 2000, when it said higher-than-expected start-up costs and a rise in fuel prices had drained capital.
