 Architecture & Design: Wrestling for Relevance   By Iovine, Julie V, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 2012  At the main exhibition in the vast Arsenale with its towering brick columns -- where the Venetian fleet was built at the rate of one ship a day in the 16th century -- and at the more than 30 national pavilions that complement the exhibition in the sprawling and dusty public gardens, three disparate notes reverberate insistently: design from the bottom up; the mysterious sources of architectural inspiration; and the art of building.
