 How to Build A Reputation   By Lewis, Michael J, The Wall Street Journal,  May 4, 2009  After being expelled from Harvard in his freshman year (he chose a weeklong party in Manhattan over his midterm exams), he worked at a number of rather humble jobs -- e.g., installing textile machines in Quebec, carting meat for Armour & Co. During World War I, he entered the Naval Academy, but he resigned his commission after a year because he "did not want to be away from his family on assignment for long periods of time."
