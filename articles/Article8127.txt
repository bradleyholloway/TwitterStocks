 Consumer Complaints About Credit Files Rose 50% Last Year, FTC Official Says   By Miller, Michael W, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1991  Complaints about the privacy and accuracy of credit reports shot up 50% in 1990 to 9,000, the FTC reported. Jean Noonan, the associate director of the FTC's division of credit practices, called for stiff new regulations to reform the vast credit-reporting industry.
