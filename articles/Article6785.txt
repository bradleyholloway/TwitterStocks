 WEEKEND JOURNAL; Taste -- Review & Outlook: Question Authority?   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 12, 2004  All too often "zero tolerance" turns out to mean the elimination of judgment and discretion. When the cries for "zero tolerance" become sufficiently loud, the paramount aim becomes not upholding standards but avoiding lawsuits. There was a day, for example, when a kid caught shoplifting from the local candy store might have feared an angry dad more than the police; when a bishop presented with a predator priest wouldn't have had to thumb through his legal team's handbook; when a public school superintendent -- think of it! -- might have actually fired an incompetent teacher. Today a child brings a water pistol to school and gets suspended.
