 Swiss bank secrecy and Holocaust victims   By Brazen, Lionel I, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 7, 1996  Lionel I. Brazen replies to the Oct 1, 1996 World-wide article regarding Switzerland's financial dealings with Nazi Germany and attempts to find the assets of Jews who perished in the Holocaust. Brazen says it is noteworthy that bank secrecy originated in 1934 in Switzerland to prevent the Nazi regime from locating Jewish assets entrusted to Swiss banks.
