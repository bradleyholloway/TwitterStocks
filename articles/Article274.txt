A Brooklyn jury is to begin deliberating today in an effort finally to resolve one of the oldest unsettled cases in the city's courts: murder and arson charges against a man accused of helping set a 1978 fire that killed six firefighters at a Brooklyn supermarket.  In the second trial for Eric Jackson-Knight, who spent nearly 10 years in prison before his 1980 conviction in his first trial was overturned and a new trial ordered, the jury will assess two weeks of testimony and arguments that culminated yesterday with passionate pleas by prosecution and defense lawyers that justice finally be done.
