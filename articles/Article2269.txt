 Dollar Retreats but Retains Its Appeal as a Safe Haven   By Sesit, Michael R, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 21, 1991  The dollar eased against other major currencies on Aug 20, 1991 as traders pocketed profits from the day before's sharp run-up because of the coup in the Soviet Union. In late New York trading, the dollar stood at 1.7985 marks and 137.22 yen, down from DM1.8245 and Y138.12 the day before.
