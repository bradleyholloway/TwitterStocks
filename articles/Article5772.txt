 Foreigners use bribes to beat U.S. rivals in many deals, new report concludes   By Greenberger, Robert S, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 12, 1995  Since 1994, foreign companies have used bribes to edge out US competitors on some $36 billion of international business deals, according to a report that Commerce Secretary Ron Brown is expected to present to Congress Oct 12, 1995. The mostly classified report contains hundreds of examples of bribery and legitimate, often government-assisted, export promotion. The study was conducted to gauge the intensity of competition faced by US companies and develop policy responses.
