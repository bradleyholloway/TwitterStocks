 Stocks' rise helped brokers in key way: They got paid more   By Raghavan, Anita, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 7, 1996  Brokers catering to individual investors received paychecks that averaged $123,839, a 6% rise from 1994, according to the Securities Industry Association. Brokers' pay slumped in 1994, partly because of a muted investor appetite for bond mutual funds and other fixed-income securities in the wake of a crash in the bond market.
