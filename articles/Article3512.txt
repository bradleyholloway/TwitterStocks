 Bookmarks   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 21, 2001  THE NAVY'S sea-air-land commandos, the legendary fighting men known as SEALs, aren't born but made. A grueling 27-week "basic underwater demolition" training regimen takes volunteers and transforms them into warriors. [Dick Couch], who graduated at the top of his training class in 1969, accompanied the members of SEAL Class 228 through their ordeal in 1999 and 2000, chronicling how they learn ambushing, reconnaissance techniques and raid planning.
