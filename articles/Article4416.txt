 Firms In Talks On Bond Trading   By Ng, Serena; Grind, Kirsten, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 14, 2012  The buying and selling of corporate bonds is expected to become more difficult and costly in the years ahead as new regulations limit the trading activities of banks that have been crucial middlemen in bond trades for decades. Since the financial crisis, the amount of corporate bonds that dealers hold on their balance sheets has shrunk to about 0.6% of the total market, from about 4% in 2007, according to a Barclays report.
