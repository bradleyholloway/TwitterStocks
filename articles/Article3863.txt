 U.S. News: U.S. Presses Pakistan to Check Militants; Adm. Mullen's Call For Action Precedes Killing of 9 Soldiers   By Dreazen, Yochi J, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2008  Also Sunday, a suicide bomber on a motorcycle attacked a police patrol in southern Afghanistan, killing 24 people, while U.S. coalition and Afghan soldiers killed 40 militants elsewhere in the south, the Associated Press reported. Mike Mullen, the chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, said he told Pakistan's military and civilian leadership Saturday that they needed to take stronger steps against the Islamist militants operating freely in the country's tribal areas and crossing into Afghanistan regularly to carry out attacks.
