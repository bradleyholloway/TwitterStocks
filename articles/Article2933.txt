 Money funds got more than $1 billion in subsidies from parent groups in '96   By Creswell, Julie, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 21, 1997  Money-market funds were subsidized by their parent fund groups to the tune of $1 billion in 1996. While those subsidies are helping some fund groups quickly attract assets, it may mean an industry consolidation is around the corner as smaller firms find themselves unable to compete, according to industry insiders. Money funds racked up expenses of $6.3 billion in 1996, but only charged investors $5.2 billion.
