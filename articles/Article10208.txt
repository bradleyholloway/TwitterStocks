 Heard on the Street / Financial Analysis and Commentary   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 2008  To revive interbank lending, the Federal Reserve and other central banks could step in as a clearinghouse for unsecured bank-to- bank loans. It follows a similar move by Russian billionaire Oleg Deripaska, who handed creditors his one-fifth stake in Canadian auto- parts manufacturer Magna International Inc. When even oligarchs are having problems, beware the former Soviet Union's formerly booming markets.
