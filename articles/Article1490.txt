 Dollar Ends Mostly Higher on Belief Currency Is Due for Sharp, Short Rally   By Scott, Alwyn, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 14, 1991  The dollar was mostly higher on Aug 13, 1991, lifted largely by the perception that the currency is due for a sharp, short-term rally no matter what happens to German interest rates on Aug 15. In late New York trading, the dollar was quoted at 1.7325 marks, up from DM1.7245 that day before, and was also trading at 136.44 yen, down from Y136.55.
