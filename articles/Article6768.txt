 Bottom Fishers Nibble at Banks, Brokers   By Zuckerman, Gregory, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 10, 2008  Despite the gloom, some smart, aggressive investors -- such as Dinakar Singh, who runs the $13 billion hedge fund TPG-Axon Capital, and Richard Pzena, head of $24 billion Pzena Investment Management -- have been buying financial stocks. Rather than buy an exchange-traded fund or mutual fund holding a broad array of financial companies, focus on companies that have a valuable franchise or limited exposure to credit problems.
