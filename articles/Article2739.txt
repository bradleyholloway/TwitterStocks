 Soft Retail Sales Data in May Fail to Put Dent in Dollar Against Yen and Sterling   By By Tom Barkley, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 14, 2000  Against the Swiss franc, the dollar was at 1.6281 Swiss francs, down from 1.6385 Swiss francs Monday.  While analysts expect the euro and yen to stay well bid on expectations U.S. economic growth and interest rates will converge with the Japanese and euro-zone economies, dollar weakness may be moderate until the Fed meeting at month's end and further data comes out in July.  The dollar weakened on the news, falling to new intraday lows of 106.22 yen and 1.6245 Swiss francs. It also gave up almost half a cent to the euro, which climbed to an intraday high of 96.25 cents a half hour after the data release. The Australian and New Zealand dollars also hit intraday highs, while the United Kingdom pound was given a small push.
