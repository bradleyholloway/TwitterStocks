 Jitters Over North Sea Valuations   By Peaple, Andrew, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 2010  Over the last 40 years, 39.5 billion barrels of oil equivalent have been recovered from the U.K. continental shelf, leaving only 15 billion to 24 billion barrels of oil equivalent still to be extracted, according to trade body Oil & Gas U.K. Production levels have fallen 7.5% a year on average since peak levels in 1999.
