 Denver's airport has novel plan to refinance up to $2.2 billion in bonds; Treasurys steady   By Vogelstein, Fred; Gasparino, Charles, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 14, 1996  Denver's new airport is undertaking a novel plan to refinance as much as $2.2 billion in bonds with a conventional refinancing of up to $300 million in debt and offering holders of another $1.9 billion an above-market price to repurchase the bonds so it can sell new debt at lower rates. On Feb 13, 1996, the 30-year benchmark Treasury bond gained 1/32 point to 99 1/2. Its yield remained relatively unchanged at 6.03%.
