 Wealth of U.S. Households Stayed Flat after Four Years Despite '80s Expansion   By Wessel, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 11, 1991  Despite the economic expansion of the 1980s, the typical US household between 1984 and 1988 accrued no noticeable difference in wealth. Government data suggests that household income grew during this period, but either the money was spent or whatever was saved was offset by additional debt.
