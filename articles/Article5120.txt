 Small stock focus: As interest rates climbed, shares fell; Russell tumbled 3.35; Nasdaq, 9.90   By Mollison, Caitlin, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 1994  The few investors who remained in the small-stock market on May 9, 1994 ahead of an expected move by the FRB to tighten credit dumped small-company stocks as interest rates surged. The Russell 2000 Index tumbled 3.35, or 1.33%, to 248.30 while the Nasdaq Composite Index skidded 9.90 to 722.96.
