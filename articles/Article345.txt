LEAVE it to a chronic collector of classic cars to make one of the biggest killings in the biggest industrial merger ever, the marriage of Daimler-Benz, parent of Mercedes-Benz, and Chrysler, the No. 3 Detroit auto maker.  The $29.8 billion Vanguard Windsor II fund, was one of the biggest net buyers of Chrysler shares lately, according to the most recent portfolio data from Morningstar, the financial publisher in Chicago. It is run by the money manager James P. Barrow, who is known for his collection of vintage automobiles. (Other big buyers were Prudential Equity and Prudential Equity-Income.)
