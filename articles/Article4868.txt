 Bond Prices Jump on Fed Move to Cut Rates, with Short Maturities Outperforming the Long   By Mitchell, Constance; Sherman, Robert, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 10, 1992  Bond prices jumped on Apr 9, 1992 after the FRB reduced the key federal funds interest rate to 3.75% from 4%. The Treasury's 30-year benchmark bond ended at a price of 101 16/32 to yield 7.86% from 100 28/32 to yield 7.91% the day before. Two-year Treasurys ended with a price of 101 8/32 to yield 5.06% from 100 27/32 to yield 5.28%.
