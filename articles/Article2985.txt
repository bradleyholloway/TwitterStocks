 Poverty Economist Is Expected to Join President's Council   By By Jacob M. Schlesinger, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 21, 1997  Rebecca M. Blank, 41 years old, a Northwestern University economist, is expected to be nominated next month, administration officials said. She would join the three-member council pending a formal background check and Senate approval.  The decision comes as Mr. Clinton is placing greater attention on a number of areas that Ms. Blank, director of the Northwestern University/University of Chicago Joint Center for Poverty Research, has studied, such as welfare reform, race relations and income inequality.
