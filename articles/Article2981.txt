 Blue Chips, Nasdaq Fall on Reality Check   By By E.S. Browning, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 2002  Yesterday, the Nasdaq Composite Index fell 1.85%, or 35.09 points, to 1862.03, putting it down 4.5% since the year began. The broader Standard & Poor's 500-stock index slipped 0.99%, or 11.49 points, to 1154.09, leaving it up just 0.5% for the year. The Dow industrials shed 130.50 points to 10501.85, up 4.8% this year.  Outside the U.S., stocks declined in dollar terms. The Dow Jones World Stock Index, excluding U.S. stocks, fell 0.57%, or 0.72 point, to 125.60.
