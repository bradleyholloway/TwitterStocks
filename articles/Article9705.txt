 Russell Index Tumbles as United Online Slides 19%   By Schreiber, Cynthia, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 6, 2004  "The market has been nervous about further growth and unless they get news to offset last month's employment report, they will have reason to be worried," said Barry Hyman, equity-market strategist at Ehrenkrantz King Nussbaum.  Selling was broad. Transportation stocks fell on concerns about oil prices and higher fuel costs. AMR, parent of American Airlines, lost 46 cents, or 5.8%, to 7.52, on the New York Stock Exchange. Continental Airlines fell 58 cents, or 6.4%, to 8.52, Delta Air Lines slid 19 cents, or 4.1%, to 4.50; and Alaska Air Group shed 79 cents, or 3.8%, to 19.91, all on the Big Board. Northwest Airlines lost 29 cents, or 3.4%, to 8.32.  Nektar Therapeutics lost 6.14, or 37%, to 10.45, on an unconfirmed report that Exubera, the San Carlos, Calif., company's inhaled insulin product, may not be approvable by European agencies at this time, a Merrill Lynch analyst said.
