 House of the Week: Swann's Way   By By Danielle Reed, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 5, 2002  Due Diligence: Known as Swann House, this 1920s Tudor-style estate peers out over Birmingham from atop the city's Red Mountain. It was originally built for metallurgist Theodore Swann, a pioneer in the then-burgeoning field of chemical processes. His home contains historic architectural elements that came to Alabama directly from England, including a 12th-century stone [Norman] arch from Castle Hedingham in Essex. The arch lies before the home's Norman hall, complete with a beamed ceiling and decorations that include a shield bearing Castle Hedingham's coat of arms.
