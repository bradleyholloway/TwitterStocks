 Russia Further Dilutes Beer Ads to Deter Drinking --- Offscreen Voices and Frolicking Bottles Will Disappear From TV as Moscow Cracks Down on Alcohol Abuse   By Osborn, Andrew, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 14, 2009  In recent months, public figures here from President Dmitry Medvedev to Patriarch Kirill, the head of the Russian Orthodox Church, have called for urgent action on underage drinking, singling out excessive beer consumption among minors as a particular worry. "Several studies which investigate the relationship between advertising and alcohol consumption show no conclusive evidence that advertising affects overall levels of alcohol consumption or drinking patterns," says Irina Arkhipova, corporate affairs and communications director at SUN InBev Russia, a local unit of Anheuser-Busch InBev that is Russia's second-biggest brewer by sales.
