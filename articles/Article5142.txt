 Industrials Leap 41.83, End 3 Points from All-Time High; Oils Strong   By Wilson, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 12, 1990  Among broader measures, Standard & Poor's 500-Stock Index jumped 4.74 to 361.23, the New York Stock Exchange Composite Index gained 2.35 to 197.11 and the Dow Jones Equity Market Index added 3.85 to 335.79. Advancers led decliners on the New York Stock Exchange by 971 to 506.  The futures price of crude oil for August delivery went up 53 cents a barrel in New York trading, to $17.47, after climbing 36 cents a barrel Tuesday. By comparison, the five OPEC oil ministers agreed that the current priority is to restore oil prices to "at least" $18 a barrel.  Activity was especially brisk in Unocal, which gained 1 1/2 to 27 3/4 in Big Board composite trading of 1.6 million shares. Among other oil issues, Exxon advanced 1 1/8 to 47 7/8, Chevron climbed 1 5/8 to 71, Texaco went up 1 3/8 to 58, Mobil rose 1 3/8 to 61 5/8 and Amoco rose 1 1/4 to 51 3/4. Du Pont, which owns Conoco, rose 1 to 38 3/8.
