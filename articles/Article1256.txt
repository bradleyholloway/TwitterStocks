 Airbus Chief Urges A Global Effort For Supersonic Jet   By By Charles W. Stevens, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 14, 1989  Later, in an interview, Jean Pierson, Airbus managing director and chief executive officer, criticized a Japanese government-financed project to develop a supersonic civil transport plane. In that program, Japan's Ministry of International Trade and Industry has invited some foreign companies to bid for secondary roles in engine development. But for the Japanese effort to succeed, Mr. Pierson said Japan would have to broaden their team further because "they aren't in a position to take the leadership on a supersonic airframe." Airbus, a European joint venture, makes the Concorde, the only civilian supersonic aircraft.
