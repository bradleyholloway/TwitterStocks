 Central Bank cries foul as Yeltsin grabs its profits   By White, Gregory L, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 7, 1996  Russian President Boris Yeltsin's election-campaign spending spree has raised troubling questions about whether the country can maintain an independent central bank, a key ingredient of a market economy. Central bank officials are strongly protesting a law signed late Jun 5, 1996 by Yeltsin that forces the bank to turn over 5 trillion rubles ($991.6 million) from its profits to help cover the government's budget gap.
