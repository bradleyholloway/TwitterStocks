 China takes action against more CD makers in effort to avoid a trade war with the U.S.   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 12, 1996  In a last-minute effort to avoid a trade war with the US, the Chinese government said it revoked the licenses of and fined two CD makers, stopped production at a third manufacturer and sealed 17 shops selling pirated goods in southern China. The moves follow similar actions against four other CD makers in recent days. However, acting US Trade Representative Charlene Barshefsky said China must do more to avoid trade sanctions on Jun 17, 1996.
