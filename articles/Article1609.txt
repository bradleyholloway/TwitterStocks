 Nokia Gambles on a Microsoft Lifeline --- Handset Maker Will Adopt Windows Software for Smartphones as It Tries to Answer Apple, Google   By Lawton, Christopher; Wingfield, Nick, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 2011  On Friday, Nokia Chief Executive Stephen Elop struck a broad alliance with Microsoft that seeks to establish a third major player in the intensely competitive smartphone race where Apple Inc. and Google Inc. are quickly pulling ahead. Nokia will adopt Windows Phone as its principal smartphone operating system replacing its own Symbian software, which has been losing ground to Apple's iPhone and smartphones that run Google's Android.
