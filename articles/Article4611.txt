 Coffee importer is convicted in loan scheme   By Lowenstein, Roger, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 12, 1986  Alberto Duque, a coffee importer who owned Chase & Sanborn Corp. and Colombian Coffee Co., has been convicted of 60 counts of fraud and conspiracy. Duque concocted an "elaborate scheme", based on a grossly exaggerated coffee inventory, to convince bankers at 20 banks to lend him about $105 million. The money was diverted to aid his father's Colombian coffee business.
