 Bond prices fall amid fears that evidence is growing to encourage a rate rise by Fed   By Zuckerman, Gregory, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 1997  Bond prices continued to slide on Mar 3, 1997 on fears that sufficient evidence is building to encourage the FRB to raise interest rates, perhaps as early as this month. The price of the benchmark 30-year Treasury bond was down 10/32 at 97 10/32, to yield 6.83%, up from 6.80% on Feb 28.
