 L.A. Gear Had Loss for First Quarter, Its Sixth in a Row   By Rundle, Rhonda L, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 1992  Troubled sneaker maker L. A. Gear Inc said it lost $6.3 million for the first quarter ended Feb 29, 1992, compared to a loss of $12.5 million the year before. The firm said it would pay no preferred dividends in the fiscal year, giving Trefoil Capital Investors LP, its biggest shareholder, the right to buy more shares.
