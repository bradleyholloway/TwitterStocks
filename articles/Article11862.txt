 Legal beat: Firms' legal costs rose 18% in '93 despite efforts to curb expenses   By Lambert, Wade, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 6, 1994  A new Arthur Andersen & Co study reveals that companies' legal costs shot up 18% in 1993, despite aggressive efforts to strengthen cost controls. By freezing or limiting in-house legal services, the companies may have ironically raised their costs by relying on outside law firms.
