 New Securities Issues   The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 15, 1992  Morgan Stanley Group Inc. -- A $400 million two-part issue of debt securities was priced through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. The first part, which consists of $300 million of notes, due Jan. 15, 1997, was priced at par to yield 7.32%. The notes were priced at a spread of 100 basis points above the comparable Treasury. The size of the issue was increased from an originally planned $200 million. The second part, which consists of $100 million of debentures, due Jan. 15, 2007, was priced at par to yield 8.33%. The debentures were priced at a spread of 130 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The issue is rated Single-A-1 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and single-A-plus by Standard & Poor's Corp.  Gulf States Utilities Co. -- A $300 million two-part issue of first mortgage bonds was priced through underwriters led by Goldman, Sachs & Co. The first part, which consists of $150 million of first mortgage bonds, due Jan. 1, 2002, was priced at par to yield 8.21%. The bonds were priced at a spread of 118 basis points above the Treasury's 10-year note. The second part, which consists of $150 million of first mortgage bonds, due Jan. 1, 2022, was priced at par to yield 8.94%. The bonds, which are non-refundable for 10 years, were priced at a spread of 140 basis points above the Treasury's 30-year bond.
