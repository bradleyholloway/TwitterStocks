 This comic's life is no routine   By Kaufman, Joanne, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 1996  Comedian Alan King, whose memoir "Name-Dropping: The Life and Lies of Alan King" was recently published, is profiled. What has always animated King's comedy is anger, generally directed at institutions. "If a sawed-off shotgun could talk," a critic once observed, "it would sound like Alan King."
