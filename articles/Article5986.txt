 Japan has its own Olympics plan   By Hamilton, David P, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 11, 1997  Much of the money Nagano Japan pledged to spend luring athletes and spectators to the 1998 Winter Olympics has been scaled back or redirected to needs closer to home. Organizers are building an Olympics designed to benefit Nagano long-term, and they appear to be taking it to extremes: Spectators in temporary seating will be crammed into venues, broadcast facilities are minimal and most toilets will be unfamiliar to foreign visitors.
