 Local Phone Upstarts Build Their Networks In Smaller Markets   The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 2, 1999  Defying the conventional wisdom that phone-service competition will first sprout in big cities, a handful of so-called competitive local exchange carriers, or CLECs, are building networks in third-tier and fourthtier markets.  KMC Telecom Inc., Bedminster, N.J., is building competitive local telephone service to communities such as Fayetteville, N.C., and Huntsville, Ala. "We're in markets that no other competitors are interested in," said Michael A. Sternberg, chief executive of closely held KMC.
