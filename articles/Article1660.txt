 Business and Finance [5]  The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 13, 1992  CLINTON SAID his first priority in office will be an economic-recovery bill with a "disciplined" balance of moves to stimulate job growth in the short run and to reduce the budget deficit over the long term. The presidentelect said his plan will include an investment tax credit, but the proposal could result in squabbling over calls for a more generous provision. ---  Ford Motor named Alexander Trotman president of its world-wide auto operations, making him the heir apparent to Chairman Harold Poling, who said he will retire late next year.  Britain cut a key interest rate and unveiled a package of tax cuts and spending to boost the economy. France also lowered rates. European stocks surged on the rate moves. Italy cut rates after the markets closed. ---
