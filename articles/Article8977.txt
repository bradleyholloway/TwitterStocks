 Making the Break From Middle Manager To a Seat at the Top   By By Hal Lancaster, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 7, 1998  WHAT DOES IT TAKE to squeeze through that final, narrow bottleneck between middle management and the exalted ranks of senior executives? It's a career question that has kindled countless debates about leaders vs. managers, nature vs. nurture and performance vs. politics.  Hoping to identify the traits that allow a successful leap, RHR International, a Wood Dale, Ill., consulting firm that does psychological assessments for companies, profiled 200 middle and senior managers at two companies with sharply differing cultures.  Not surprisingly, delivering results matters. So does thinking strategically, being persuasive and politically adroit, and having a "significantly broader organizational awareness," says Al Parchem, RHR's chief executive officer.
