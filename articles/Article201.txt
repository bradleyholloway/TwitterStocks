 Up and over: Optimism about rates and the economy help industrials pierce 4000   By Kansas, Dave, The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 24, 1995  More than a year after the Dow Jones Industrial Average first flirted with 4000, it broke through the mark Feb 23, 1995, laying the groundwork for what some analysts predict will be sustained gains for stock prices for the rest of 1995. The breakthrough came amid optimism about interest rates and the economy. The Dow closed at 4003.33, up 30.28.
