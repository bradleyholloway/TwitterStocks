 Taste -- Review & Outlook: Cardinal Sins   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 13, 2002  In the press the pope's reluctance to relieve Cardinal Law of his responsibility has been presented as evidence of a church too arrogant to accept responsibility. Rarely is it noted that the church does not consider its bishops expendable CEOs. Rightly or wrongly, the Roman Catholic Church considers its bishops successors to the 12 apostles, which surely helps explain (if not entirely excuse) the Vatican's reluctance to "solve" this problem by throwing a bishop to the wolves.
