C. Terence Clyne, a retired advertising agency executive who participated in the development of radio and television advertising, died of a heart attack Monday at his home in Greenwich, Conn. He was 75 years old.  Mr. Clyne retired last year as chairman and chief executive officer of C. T. Clyne Company Inc. after serving as an executive for such agencies as McCann-Erickson Inc., Biow Company Inc. and Clyne-Maxon Inc.  One of Mr. Clyne's major contracts while a vice president at Biow was for the original ''I Love Lucy'' television program for the Phillip Morris cigarette account. His other clients included Bulova Watch, Gillette, General Electric, Chesterfield and American Home Products.  After several years with Free & Peters Inc., an early broadcasting station representative, Mr. Clyne moved to Biow in 1946. He joined McCann-Erickson in 1954 to become senior vice president and vice chairman and a director of its parent company, the Interpublic Group of Companies Inc.  He became president of Clyne-Maxon in 1965 after heading its predecessor, Maxon Inc. Clyne-Maxon later became the C. T. Clyne Company.  Mr. Clyne was born in Philadelphia and graduated from Amherst College. He served as a colonel with the Eighth Air Force during World War II, winning the Legion of Merit, the Croix de Guerre and the Bronze Star with Oak Leaf Cluster.  He is survived by his wife, Hansi; two sons, Terence D. of Amherst, Mass., and Michael J. of Greenwich, and four grandchdildren. A funeral mass will be celebrated today at 11 A.M. at St. Michael's Roman Catholic Church on North Street, Greenwich.
