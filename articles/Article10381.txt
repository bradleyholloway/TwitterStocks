 A March Madness Investing Plan   By Kansas, Dave, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 7, 2010  Anticipating what your fellow poolsters think is a modern variant of John Maynard Keynes's beauty contest illustration for how the stock market works: It is not a case of choosing those [faces] that, to the best of one's judgment, are really the prettiest, nor even those that average opinion genuinely thinks the prettiest, he wrote in General Theory of Employment Interest and Money. The subtle problem is to find the balance between choosing high-probability events (betting on favorites) and going against the crowd (betting on underdogs), wrote Bryan Clair and David Letscher in a 2007 professional journal article, Optimal Strategies for Sports Betting Pools.
