 Dollar Mixed as Foreign Central Banks Move to Bolster Their Own Currencies   By Lee, Charlene, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 12, 1992  The dollar weakened against European currencies on Mar 11, 1992, but firmed against the yen as the Japanese, Swiss and Canadian central banks sold US dollars to boost their respective currencies. In New York trading, the dollar was quoted at 1.6625 marks, down from DM1.6705 the previous day, and at 133.40 yen, up from Y132.85. Sterling was trading at $1.7265, up from $1.7175.
