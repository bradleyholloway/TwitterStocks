 Unions, Government Downed the Airlines   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 14, 2004  Your writers fail to understand two realities of airline management. The first is that the present-value cost of a strike is always many times the present-value savings that might be realized by prevailing in a contract dispute. The second is that airline revenues evaporate completely during a strike and take many months to rebuild; conversely, many airline costs continue undiminished. Thus, airlines can be mortally damaged by work stoppages; any extended strike would threaten a carrier's survival. Indeed, throughout the post- deregulation period, executives who resisted labor demands, myself included, were widely pilloried for having unsatisfactory labor relations.
