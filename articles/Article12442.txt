 NorthPoint's Stock Rockets 68% in Debut   By By Scott Thurm, The Wall Street Journal,  May 6, 1999  NorthPoint is a wholesaler of "digital-subscriber lines," which let users connect to the Internet at speeds as much as 30 times faster than conventional modems. Internet providers and online services market NorthPoint's service, primarily to small businesses and home offices.  NorthPoint's backers include tech powerhouses such as Microsoft Corp., Intel Corp. and Tandy Corp., and Internet providers such as At Home Corp. and Verio Inc. Microsoft agreed to invest as much as $60 million in NorthPoint last month, as part of a deal in which it will use NorthPoint lines for high-speed access to its Microsoft Network online service.
