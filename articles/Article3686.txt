 Delta Air Chief Says Industry Needs More Federal Assistance   By By Evan Perez, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 20, 2003  The executive, Leo Mullin, said U.S. airlines are cutting costs and finding more-efficient ways to operate in order to cope with an aviation slump that began even before the Sept. 11, 2001, terrorist attacks on the U.S. Still, Mr. Mullin, speaking to a luncheon audience of industry lobbyists and government officials, said the carriers need more help from the government, including relaxed regulatory oversight that might allow some airlines to merge and a permanent cut in a security tax levied on airplane tickets.
