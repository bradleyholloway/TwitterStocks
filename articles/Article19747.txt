 Love & Money: Unexpected Expenses=Tough Choices   By Opdyke, Jeff D, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 4, 2004  The challenge to the relationship wasn't just the large chunk of money, "it was the mental anguish of both of us having different views of how to handle this," [Deanna] says. Until that point, they'd never really battled over financial matters and thus, "we had no history of how to handle each other. So we just avoided it, which caused tension."  "I was angry," she says. "We didn't talk for a weekend."  That made Deanna want to accept the settlement all the more. She says she told her husband that "the extra money we might get from a settlement is not worth the mental anguish this is causing me. He just said, 'Trust me.'"
