 Nebraska Escapes Worst Of Outbreak of Aflatoxin   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 7, 1988  Nebraska appears to have escaped the worst of the aflatoxin outbreak in the Midwest corn crop.  Nebraska is the seventh Midwest state to confirm the presense of aflatoxin in the corn harvest. Scientists blame the drought for the unprecedented outbreak. While the fungus that makes aflatoxin is widespread, it usually only produces poison in hot, dry climates such as the Southeast and Southwest.
