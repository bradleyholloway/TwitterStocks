 Prospects Dim For Low-Income Fix in Tax Bill   By By Shailagh Murray, The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 4, 2003  Over the Memorial Day recess, an analysis of the bill by a liberal tax-policy group sparked a minor uproar that poor people had been given short shrift so Republicans could provide income-tax and investment breaks to upper-income taxpayers. Senate Finance Chairman Charles Grassley (R., Iowa) vowed to try to restore the provision when Congress reconvened.
