 New York utilities panel recommends Con Ed revenue cut; Stock falls 5.9%   By Geyelin, Milo, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 1994  New York state's utility regulatory staff recommended slashing revenue for Consolidated Edison Co of New York by 2.3%, or $120 million, in 1995, and further reductions in 1996 and 1997. The announcement sent Con Ed's stock down 5.9% and prompted two major debt ratings groups to consider lowering their ratings.
