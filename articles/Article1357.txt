 Deal Journal / Breaking Insight from WSJ.com   By Campoy, Ana, The Wall Street Journal,  Jan 12, 2009  Last week, Andrew Liveris announced a slew of measures Dow would use to cover the $9 billion cash hole left by a collapsed joint venture, dubbed "K-Dow," between the chemicals conglomerate and Kuwait's Petrochemical Industrials Co. Mr. Liveris's plan included finding new joint-venture partners and selling unnamed Dow Chemical assets, which could partly help the company afford the Rohm & Haas deal.
