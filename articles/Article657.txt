 Warner-Lambert Attorney Is Charged With Offering to Sell Company Secrets   By By Michael Siconolfi, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 14, 1985  Mr. Caparros, 45 years old, an associate counsel in Warner-Lambert's Consumer Products group for more than three years, was fired after his arrest, a company spokesman said. The Morris Plains, N.J.-based company makes pharmaceuticals, chewing gum, mints, shaving products and cookies.  A Warner-Lambert spokesman said the company learned several weeks ago from a "confidential source" at the competitor that someone with access to trade secrets at Warner-Lambert was trying to sell secret information. Warner-Lambert then notified authorities, the spokesman said. The competitor was later identified as Wm. Wrigley Jr. Co., a Chicago-based chewing gum maker.
