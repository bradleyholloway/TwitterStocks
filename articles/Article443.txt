Sure, the brutality of winter could be measured on a thermometer, in inches of snow or the number of ice-induced traffic accidents. But winter can also be judged by the prominence of another scourge, one that is especially irksome in a city that places a premium on being well-groomed and immaculately coiffed: hat hair.  This year, even those New Yorkers who are generally intent on maintaining static-free locks and smooth-looking strands have put their vanity aside, choosing the well-being of their ears and foreheads over the preservation of their tresses and blow-outs.
