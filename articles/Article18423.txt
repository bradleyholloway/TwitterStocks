 The Making -- and Remaking -- of a `Light' Potato Chip [1]  By By Eleena De Lisser, The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 4, 1993  Frito-Lay Inc. found that out when it introduced its Ruffles Light low-fat potato chip thinking it had a blockbuster in the making. After all, the PepsiCo Inc. unit's market-research indicated consumers desperately wanted a healthier alternative to regular chips. And that seemed to be confirmed when Frito-Lay began test marketing the low-fat chip, along with low-fat Doritos and Cheetos, almost five years ago. Sales were strong enough that within 18 months, the company rolled the products out nationally and spent $13 million in 1990 promoting them.
