After struggling for years with a mounting deficit and a shrinking enrollment, St. Nicholas of Tolentine High School in the Bronx will be closed at the end of June, Roman Catholic officials said yesterday.  The closing was announced as Catholic schools throughout the city and the country are closing as archdioceses and parishes struggle with increasing costs and dwindling resources. Only last month, the Archdiocese of New York announced that five of its 312 schools might close this year. It named two, St. Cecilia's, in East Harlem, and St. Columba in Chester, N.Y.
