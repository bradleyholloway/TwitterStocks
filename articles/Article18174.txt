 New Securities Issues [5]  The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 4, 1991  General Motors Acceptance Corp. -- $300 million of 8.75% notes due Feb. 1, 1996, priced at 99.60 to yield 8.85%. The noncallable notes were priced at a spread of 141 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's Investors Service Inc. and double-A-minus by Standard & Poor's Corp., the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley & Co. GMAC is the finance arm of General Motors Corp.  Associates Corp. of North America -- $300 million of 8 3/4% senior notes due Feb. 1, 1996, priced at 99.585 to yield 8.854%. The noncallable notes were priced at 140 basis points above the comparable Treasury note. Rated double-A-3 by Moody's and double-A-minus by S&P, the issue will be sold through underwriters led by Morgan Stanley. Associates is a unit of Ford Motor Co.  Shell Oil Co. -- $250 million of 7.70% notes due Feb. 1, 1996, priced at 99.914 to yield 7.72%. The noncallable notes were priced at 30 basis points above the Treasury's five-year note. Rated triple-A by Moody's and by S&P, the issue will be sold through Merrill Lynch & Co.
