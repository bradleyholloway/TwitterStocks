 Gucci Posts 97% Drop in Profit, But Also Cites Some Recovery   By Galloni, Alessandra, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 3, 2003  The results at one of the world's leading luxury-goods company, which were worse than analysts had expected, gave fashionistas and investors the first glimpse of how sales of branded handbags, shoes and other baubles fared in April -- the height of the SARS crisis in Asia. It was ugly. Gucci Chief Executive Domenico De Sole said April was the worst-ever month for the company, whose brands include Gucci, Yves Saint Laurent and jeweler Boucheron. He said the first quarter of 2003 was the most difficult for the company since it went public in 1995.
