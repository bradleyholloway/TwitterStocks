 Hong Kong Shows Re-Exports, Imports Improved in April   The Wall Street Journal,  Jun 13, 1997  Hong Kong re-export and import trade with most countries increased in April from a year earlier.  The government's Census and Statistics Department said that while the value of re-exports grew by 5.7% in April to 104.5 billion Hong Kong dollars ($13.5 billion), Taiwan trade jumped 20%. Trade with Taiwan was the only double-digit rise in the important re-export sector that includes products that come into Hong Kong and are sent out again with minimum processing. Only German and South Korean re-exports to Hong Kong fell, by 4.2% and 0.1%, respectively.
