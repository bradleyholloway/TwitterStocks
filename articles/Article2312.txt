 Tokyo Share Prices Take a Drubbing from Move by Regulators That Worsened Bourse's Foul Mood   By Hardy, Quentin, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 20, 1991  A decision by Japanese securities regulators to raise margin requirements to 30% from 25% for trading stock futures and options worsened the gloomy mood on the Tokyo stock market, sending the Nikkei 225-stock index down 638.71 points to 21991.19 on Dec 19, 1991. In London, the Financial Times-Stock Exchange 100-share index lost 22.0 points to 2391.6, while Frankfurt's DAX 30-stock index fell 11.98 points to 1561.77.
