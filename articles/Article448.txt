For the past three years, the N.B.A. finals have been a dud, with Shaquille O'Neal pulverizing and Kobe Bryant mesmerizing their Eastern Conference opponents with extraordinary ease. The suspense was nonexistent. There was no realistic chance of the Los Angeles Lakers being beaten.  But the Lakers, who were eliminated by the San Antonio Spurs in the Western Conference semifinals, four games to two, are done, and the sense around the league is that this year's finals may be exciting because of it.
