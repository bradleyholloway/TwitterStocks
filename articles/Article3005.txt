 U.S. Vulnerability On Cyber-Security Is Cited by Report   By By John Simons, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 21, 1997  The U.S. may be vulnerable to a cyberspace version of the Pearl Harbor attack and should spend more on cyber-security research, according to a report by a panel of industry and government officials to be released tomorrow.  Among its recommendations, the President's Commission on Critical Infrastructure Protection suggests quadrupling the amount spent on cyber-security research to $1 billion during the next seven years. It also advocates the creation of a panel comprised of chief executives of industrial infrastructure companies to make periodic recommendations to the president on security issues.
