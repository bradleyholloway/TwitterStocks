 Caterpillar Posts Quarterly Deficit, Sees More Red Ink   By By Robert L. Rose, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 21, 1991  Caterpillar Inc., hit by a deepening slump in markets for its construction equipment, reported a third-quarter loss and predicted more red ink for the fourth quarter.  Caterpillar had a loss of $37 million, or 37 cents a share, after earning $54 million, or 54 cents a share, a year earlier. Revenue fell 16% to $2.36 billion from $2.8 billion.  Although the loss was wider than many analysts expected, investors continued their recent bargain-hunting in Caterpillar shares. With the Dow Jones Industrial Average reaching a new high Friday, Caterpillar stock rose $1.125 to $48.625 in New York Stock Exchange composite trading.
