 Carnival CEO Sees Industry Shakeout On Travel Decline   By By Betsy McKay, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 21, 2001  His comments come as the cruise industry is confronting a drop in bookings at the same time that capacity has increased. While Carnival's bookings are improving, the Miami company said they are still only 50% to 60% of year-earlier levels. The drop in demand is likely to only further widen a gap between the top tier of cruise operators, which includes Carnival, and a smattering of smaller, but less financially stable, players, said Scott Barry, a leisure-travel analyst with Credit Suisse First Boston.
