 Managed Funds Offer Little Cover From the Bear   By Damato, Karen; Gullapalli, Diya, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 5, 2009  Active funds also proved disappointing during the fierce 1973-1974 bear market, which began only two years after the first index portfolios were introduced, recalls David Booth, co-founder and chief executive of Dimensional Fund Advisors, manager of the DFA funds. The best seller in 2008 was SPDR S&P 500 ETF, pulling in $38 billion in new cash, and No. 2 was Vanguard Total Stock Market Index Fund, according to Financial Research Corp. Still, many investors and financial advisers continue to believe they've got a shot at identifying active managers who will beat the market over time.
