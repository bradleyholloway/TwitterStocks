 World Food Security Faces Some Dangers, U.N. Agency Warns ---- Special to The Wall Street Journal   The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 12, 1988  This year's drought in North America, floods in parts of Africa and Asia, and the danger to crops from locusts in Africa all are threatening the world's food security, a United Nations organization warned.  "While there is no risk of an immediate world food crisis, there is every reason for concern for the next year or so," the Food and Agriculture Organization director-general, Edouard Saouma, said Friday in a statement on the current world food situation and its effect on global food security.
