 African Telecom MTN Will Buy Rival Investcom   By Angus Macmillan and Michael Wang, The Wall Street Journal,  May 3, 2006  Investcom's biggest shareholder, M1, an investment firm controlled by the Lebanese Mikati family with 70.6% of the stock, said it has agreed to MTN's cash-and-shares offer. That offer will see the South African company swap $2.08 a share in cash and 0.18 of a new MTN share in exchange for each Investcom share. MTN is also offering an all-cash alternative of $3.85 a share, which is equivalent to $19.25 a share for each global depositary share of the company.
