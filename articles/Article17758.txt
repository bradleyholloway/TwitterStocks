 Criminal Cases, Not Corporate Mergers Are the Priority of New Antitrust Chief   By By Andy Pasztor, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 4, 1987  Suspected bid-rigging and collusion among smaller military contractors accounts for almost 25% of all federal antitrust grand jury investigations, a top Justice Department official said.  Assistant Attorney General Charles Rule, in his first news conference since being confirmed as the agency's chief antitrust enforcer, provided the most detailed breakdown yet of his office's increasing emphasis on criminal, rather than civil, cases.  Prosecuting price-fixers, bid-riggers and other so-called hard-core antitrust violators is "by far the most important thing we do," Mr. Rule asserted. "My No. 1 priority," he told reporters, "is very simple: criminal cases and more criminal cases."
