 Marketing 101: To M.B.A. candidates, the top course today is to land a good job   By Markels, Alex, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 5, 1995  With students even more obsessed with their futures in a rapidly changing economy, business schools have become focused on the job hunt. To some observers, the schools are ceding too much academic ground to recruiters and students. Some educators themselves worry that they are striving too hard to boost their ratings. Although the task of landing choice jobs has always helped define an M.B.A. program's success, its importance has grown as tuition has increased and the average student has become older and more mature.
