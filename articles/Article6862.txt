 Heavy Debt, Fewer Ads Put Radio Firms In a Squeeze   By McBride, Sarah, The Wall Street Journal,  Nov 10, 2008  Similar thinking on how to draw more listeners, and thus get higher rates, is encouraging other station managers to pick up pop personality Ryan Seacrest's radio show, which he started syndicating via Clear Channel Communications Inc.'s Premiere Radio Networks during the summer. While listening hours are declining, most people still listen to an average of 18.5 hours a week, according to Arbitron Inc. Based on the price of reaching each listener, radio generally offers its audience "at the lowest [cost] of any major media," says David Field, chief executive of Entercom.
