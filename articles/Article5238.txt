 Strong Treasury rally pulls yield on long bond below 7% for the first time since March 1994   By Pesek, William Jr, The Wall Street Journal,  May 10, 1995  A powerful rally in Treasurys pulled interest rates down across the maturity spectrum May 9, 1995 as bullish investors stormed the bond market. The yield on the 30-year Treasury bond tumbled below 7% for the first time since Mar 28, 1994, triggering an avalanche of buying as long-idle accounts came back into the market in force. The bond's price was up 1 1/8 points at 108 13/32, pushing the yield down to 6.95% from 7.03%.
