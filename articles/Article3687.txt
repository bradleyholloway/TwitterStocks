 In Brief   The Wall Street Journal,  Mar 14, 2007  Juniper Networks Inc. said Chief Financial Officer Robert Dykes is leaving the company, in connection with what the networking-equipment maker said was a review of its growth plans. The company said in a Securities and Exchange filing that his resignation wasn't the result of any disagreement with the company "on any matters relating to the company's operations, policies or practices." Chief Executive Scott Kriens said Mr. Dykes's departure was mutually agreed to as Juniper plans to double in size in the next few years.
