 Yield on 30-Year Treasury Bond Sinks to 7.78% as Prices Rise amid Hopes of Further Fed Easing   By Donovan, Kevin; Adler, Lynn K, The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 1991  Bond prices rose on Dec 9, 1991 on expectations the FRB will ease policy again soon. However, the yield on the 30-year bond slipped to its lowest closing level since 1987. The benchmark 30-year bond ended at a price of 102 12/32 to yield 7.78%, compared with 102 7/32 to yield 7.80% the previous trading day. Shorter-term securities posted healthy gains with the latest two-year notes ending at 100 26/32 to yield 5.05%, compared with 100 18/32 to yield 5.18%.
