The Chelsea Mini-Storage, a grimy, seven-story brick building with a loading dock at 615 West 27th Street, may well be the nation's largest transit point for wholesale African art.  More than 600 traders, nearly all Muslims from West Africa, cut deals with handshakes, load or unload recent purchases, chat, snooze or plan trips to Africa, where they buy artwork and send it to the United States on cargo ships. While the men trade, two women from Guinea cook chicken smothered in peanut sauce in a back room on the first floor, and the aroma mingles with the scent of smoldering incense.
