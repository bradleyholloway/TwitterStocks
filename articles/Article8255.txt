 Long, Hot Summer?: Miami Looks for Ways to Ease Tension as It Prepares for Unrest   By De Cordoba, Jose, The Wall Street Journal,  Jul 7, 1992  City officials and police in Miami are undergoing training and devising ways to prepare for the racial tensions that are often exacerbated by the city's long, hot summers. However, tensions are expected to reach their zenith in Oct 1992 when police officer William Lozano is scheduled to be retried on manslaughter charges related to the death of two black motorcyclists in a 1989 incident. If acquitted, widespread rioting is expected across the city.
