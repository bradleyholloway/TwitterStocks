 Banks in Minnesota, Nebraska and Texas Shut by Regulators   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 2, 1987  The failed banks were: Western Bank-Westheimer, Houston, which had assets of $290.9 million; Western Bank-Westwood, Houston, which had assets of $46.9 million; Western Bank-North Wilcrest, Houston, which had assets of $44.8 million; Security State Bank, Oxford, Neb., which had assets of $11.3 million; and Clay County State Bank, Dilworth, Minn., which had assets of $10.5 million. The three Houston banks were under the same ownership.
