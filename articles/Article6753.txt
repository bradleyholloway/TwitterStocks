 Questioning Middle East Assumptions   The Wall Street Journal,  Dec 10, 2007  Professor [Bernard Lewis]' sound argument about Arab acceptance of Israel's existence has one technical flaw. The two examples he cites of population exchange (India-Pakistan, Germany-Poland) involved groups that were both long-time residents in the disputed territory. In the case of Palestine however, despite the continuous existence a small Jewish community, the Jews had only recently returned en masse after an absence of 1,700 years. Moreover, this influx occurred under British/League of Nations protection and over the violent objections of the Palestinians, Arabs and Muslims who had lived in and dominated that part of the world for over a millennium.
