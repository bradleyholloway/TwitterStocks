To the Editor:  Re ''Terror in Granada Hills'' (editorial, Aug. 12): That the Congressional approach to ''closing off the avenues of access'' to firearms is guided by a ''narrow vision'' is a gross understatement. While Congressional leaders postpone and dilute essential legislation aimed at limiting the ability of irresponsible citizens to obtain firearms, an emerging firearms market is rapidly developing on the Internet. A simple on-line search shows dozens of gun retailers and private traders. These sites provide children and individuals who would not otherwise be authorized to obtain firearms the opportunity to do so outside of any restrictions on sales.
