 CPC International's Third-Period Profit Rose to $59 Million   The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 14, 1986  ENGLEWOOD CLIFFS, N.J. -- CPC International Inc. posted third-quarter profit that quadrupled from a year earlier, when the food company's results were depressed by a special restructuring charge.  CPC said it had strong volume gains in North America for such products as Mazola corn oil, Hellmann's and Best Foods mayonnaise, Mueller's pasta, Thomas's English muffins and Karo syrup. The company also refines corn into starches, sweeteners, oils and animal feed.  CPC has been portrayed in recent Wall Street rumors as a likely takeover target or a candidate for a major restructuring. In New York Stock Exchange composite trading yesterday, CPC closed at $69.875 a share, up $2.625.
