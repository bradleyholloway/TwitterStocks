 Semiconductor Shares Extend Rally to Fuel Gains   By Schreiber, Cynthia, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 14, 2004  Semiconductor stocks continued last week's rally as investors bet that most of the bad news that had been hurting the group is behind it. Power Integrations climbed 1.96, or 9.6%, to $22.44; Silicon Image advanced 90 cents, or 8%, to 12.16; and OmniVision Technologies gained 86 cents, or 7.1%, to 12.98.  The Russell 2000 Index rose 3.19, or 0.56%, to 573.10, and the Standard & Poor's SmallCap 600 Index gained 1.72, or 0.6%, to 290.39. The Nasdaq Composite Index climbed 16.07, or 0.85%, to 1910.38.  Krispy Kreme Doughnuts (NYSE) slumped 42 cents, or 3.5%, to 11.56. The Winston-Salem, N.C., doughnut chain said its auditor, PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP, refused to complete a review of the company's financial statements for the latest quarter until an outside law firm hired by the company's board is finished performing "certain additional procedures" requested by the auditors.
