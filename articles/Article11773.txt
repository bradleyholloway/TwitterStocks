 Managing your career: Fewer good deeds go unpunished in '90s corporate climate   By Schellhardt, Timothy D, The Wall Street Journal,  Oct 6, 1993  In today's corporate environment, where people are viewed as costs to be cut as much as assets to be developed, it is advised that executives wishing to do volunteer work or become more involved in their community choose their activities so that they do not jeopardize the health of their careers.
