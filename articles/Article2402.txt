 Environment: Wasps Are Enlisted to Fight Lyme Disease   By Stipp, David, The Wall Street Journal,  Sep 21, 1992  Wasps are being tested in the fight to destroy the ticks that carry Lyme disease since wasps are a tick-specific parasite. The wasps lay eggs within ticks and the wasp offspring later hatch and kill the ticks before they reproduce.
