Anna Leah Kohanski, the daughter of Cheryl Spielman Bari of Englewood, N.J., and Ronald Kohanski of Columbia, Md., is to be married on Sunday to Jordan Scott Klein, a son of Carol Klein and Dr. Howard Klein, both of Louisville, Ky. Rabbi Irving Spielman, the bride's maternal grandfather, will officiate at the Pierre in New York.  The bride, 23, is a high-yield-bond research analyst at Lehman Brothers in New York whose focus is the corporate debt of companies in the gambling and leisure industry. She graduated from Harvard.  Her father is the deputy director of the Biology of Aging Program at the National Institutes of Health in Bethesda, Md. Her mother is a partner in Ernst & Young, the accounting firm in New York; she provides tax advisory services to multinational corporations.  The bride's stepfather, Myron Bari, is the founder and president of IPM, a technology consulting firm in New York. Her stepmother, Leslie Pick, is an associate professor and the director of the graduate program in molecular and cell biology at the University of Maryland at College Park.  The bridegroom, 27, is an investment banking associate in the health-care group at Bear, Stearns in New York. He graduated from Indiana University.  His mother teaches sixth grade at the Kammerer Middle School in Louisville. His father is an oral surgeon in private practice in Louisville.
