 The Assault on Iraq: Dinar's Collapse Leaves Many Without Way to Buy Goods   By By Simeon Kerr, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 14, 2003  The 10,000-dinar note has an added problem. The looting that followed British forces' April 6 occupation of Basra now has been followed by speculation that the many 10,000-dinar notes stolen from banks will be canceled.  In February, before the war seemed imminent, the dollar was valued at 180 dinars. The day before U.S.-led forces attacked, the dollar bought 480 dinars. By yesterday, the disparity between the two Iraqi notes was evident as the dollar was buying as much as 10,000 to 16,000 dinars of the large notes and 350 in the smaller notes.
