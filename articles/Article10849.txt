 U.S. Auctions Notes At a Yield of 8.97%, Off From Prior Sale   The Wall Street Journal,  Feb 6, 1986  The Treasury sold $7.01 billion of 10-year notes at an average annual yield of 8.97%.  The department also sold $1 billion of foreign-targeted 10-year notes at an average annual yield of 9.12%, the lowest ever on these securities, which are tailored for overseas investors. When converted to a semiannual coupon payment like the domestic note, the average annual yield was 8.92%, or a savings to the Treasury of five basis points compared with the domestic issue. A basis point is 1/100 percentage point.
