 June Spending On Construction Decreased 1.1% --- Decline in Outlays Blamed On Tax-Law Changes, High Office Vacancies   By By Rose Gutfeld, The Wall Street Journal,  Aug 4, 1987  A drop in outlays for nonresidential buildings pulled construction spending down 1.1% in June, the Commerce Department said.  The decline reflected the continued effects of tax-law changes and high vacancy rates in office and industrial buildings in some areas. Many economists expect these factors to keep construction outlays sluggish for much of the rest of the year.
