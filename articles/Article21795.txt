 Smoking rate in U.S. rises for first time in 25 years   The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 2, 1993  According to a new federal study, the rate of smoking increased slightly in 1991 to 25.7% of the adult population, compared with 25.5% in 1990. The rise was the first in 25 years. The tobacco industry spent $3.9 billion on advertising and promotion in 1991, which some think played a role in the upturn.
