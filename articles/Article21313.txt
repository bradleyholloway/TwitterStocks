 Bombardier Board Clears Developing Stretched Plane   By By Suzanne McGee, The Wall Street Journal,  Apr 3, 1989  Bombardier Inc. said its board voted to go ahead with a 275 million Canadian dollar (US$230.2 million) program to develop a stretched version of its Canadair Challenger executive jet.  Bombardier said it has received orders for 56 of the planes and six options, valued at a total of about US$868 million. Robert Wohl, executive vice president of Bombardier's Canadair unit and head of the regional jet program, said he expects the order backlog to climb to between 100 and 150 units by year end. The company said the average unit price of the jets is about US$14 million.
